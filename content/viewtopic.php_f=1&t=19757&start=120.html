I know plenty of people have expressed plenty of skepticism about defensive WAR, but I want to throw on the pile in the form of a question. Because of some laudatory quotations concerning Willie Mays I came across on Facebook, who I have always thought to be slightly overrated when compared to his great peers (although there was no doubt he was great, but people go on and on and on about Willie Mays and Mickey Mantle, while not so much about Hank Aaron, for example), I decided to check out his WAR, figuring since he was so famous for his defense, that must be something I should be paying attention to. And indeed it is good, not Andruw Jones good but good, but it got me to thinking: how on Earth can defensive WAR from times when essentially no video evidence exists really have all that much meaning? The numbers seem so precise, but what on earth could the really be measuring? I want this explained.

I suppose it has to do with number of balls hit to CF in a given season and how many are put outs and how many land for singles, etc., but I would have to think this data for pre-1970 or even pre- pretty much the dawn of "every out of every game recorded" is sketchy at best.
