in the fangraphs wrc+ list of 385 mlb players with at least 100 pa's this season, revere is in place 205 with a score of 93. that's a bit behind abreu (96, place 188), but ahead of all sorts of interesting names such as...


continuing my neverending quest for measures of the offensive that can be considered comprehensive and valid enough to make useful contributions to our discussions about the relative worth of players we (dis)like...
i just discovered via the fangraphs glossary section that they took baserunning OUT of wrc+. unfortunately, that seems to leave us with only one column in any b-r or fangraph table providing a league and park adjusted rate (as opposed to counting) stat for "pure" total offense without the controversial positional and replacement adjustments: totA from b-r, which has not been validated (via comparison to actual team wins and runs) as thoroughly as the linear weights / war stats.

fortunately, b-r breaks out their owar components well enough to make it apparent that the "pure" offensive rate stat that i want should work out to be simply: (rbat+rbaser+rdp)/pa.

i made a table of that for all major leaguers, then excluded pitchers, then kicked out the no-way-they-should-show-up-anywhere-near-trout crew by excluding those with less than 100 pa's. that left me with 384 players, which is pretty close to 8 or 9 (al) regulars plus 4 primary bench guys per team.

phils have players ranking 73, 79, 88, 92, 133, 203, 213, 235, 293, 307, and 338.
to make jeff happy, i'll confess that the last one is gwynn. to annoy him, i'll let y'all know that b.abreu is well below revere and ruiz. (so are a list of players very similar to the one i provided in my earlier wrc+ post.) and to placate him i'll admit that i don't think either will sustain this unless revere learns to walk and ruiz gets much more rest when he's not on the dl.

i should also point out that the reason that our best players are so low here is precisely because there is no positional adjustment, so utley falls behind a bunch of slugging outfielders and first basemen. given that, i think the rest of the names matching the order above can be guessed.
