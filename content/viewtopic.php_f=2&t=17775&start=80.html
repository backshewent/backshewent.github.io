by Youseff » Tue Feb 28, 2017 21:53:45

saw the first (modern) Steve McQueen flick (of 12 Years a Slave fame), "Hunger." It's about the famous IRA member, Bobby Sands, who was imprisoned for the bombing of a furniture store frequented by British Protestant loyalists, in retaliation for the bombing of a Irish Catholic pub frequented by separatists, which was carried out by UVF (a Protestant loyalists group).

Sands is played by Michael Fassbender, in one of his first big roles.

It's largely about the inhumane conditions the prisoners had to endure, which often times they did not. There's several attempts a protests in an attempt to negotiate with the British government, but it culminates in a hunger strike that in which several prisoners including Sands died.

It's an interesting film to view in our context, when we're a divided people over, I dunno, whose a cuck and who is not, I gather, but in this case they're fighting over their independence as a nation. It's difficult to watch at times, but very good. 87/100
