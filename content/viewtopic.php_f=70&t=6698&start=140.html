by traderdave » Fri Oct 03, 2008 11:03:22

I watched the entire debate and I like Palin even less than I did before the debate (maybe not so much personally but certainly as a VP candidate). I am a Barack supporter but I honestly tried to be open-minded about Palin.

I think Palin held her own last night (i.e. she probably didn't do any major damage to McCain) but she had, like, four talking points and she beat them to death. That might not be so bad but the tax talking point with regard to Obama raising taxes on the middle class was proven to be completely and utterly false months ago.

IMHO, her one answer when she essentially said "I'll talk about what I want to talk about" essentially told me she couldn't comment on the topic actually being discussed. She added absolutely NOTHING to the discussion on health care, she looked like she didn't have the first clue what Biden was talking about when the foreign policy questions came up and what everybody is calling "folksy" struck me as condescending and cartoonish.

I think Biden did kinda sleepwalk through the first 45 minutes or so but I thought he really hit some HRs in the second half. I thought he crushed her when he described (without rebuttal) how McCain was going to pay for the $5,000 health care tax credit (including calling it the "Ultimate Bridge to Nowhere"); the foreign policy stuff wasn't even close and I loved the "he is no maverick on important issues" bit toward the very end.

Overall, I thought Biden did a great job of attacking when necessary but not coming off as "smackin' a ho". I think Obama can wrap the Presidency up with a solid performance next week. Anybody who really watched the debate should have come away from it hoping to hell that McCain lives to 120 should he be elected (God Forbid). Really, Governor? A shout out?
