looks to me like the only way to get fully into contention this offseason without going over the cap to sign at least one more starting pitcher (and trade for another) is to trade segura and prospects for bryant and at least half of odubel's contract on someone. of course, no trade of bryant is going to happen until after a ruling on his service time manipulation case lets teams know whether he is under control for two more years or just one.
while we wait...
is bohm a likely enough short and long-term soluton at 3b that there is no point in getting bryant?
is holding back all of our prospect ammo and payroll space for deals for pitching too much of a priority to allow us to give up lots of both for bryant?
which prospects are worth including if it is for one year of bryant, and which for two years?
should bohm be held back as insurance against hoskins not rebounding? or as trade ammunition for a big midsummer deal for a starter from a team that drops out of contention (odorizzi? someone better).
is bohm ready enough and hoskins still valuable enough that we could finish the deal with hoskins instead of bohm?
do we have prospects other than bohm and howard that could get the deal done?

my answers: i think gettting bryant is more clear path to improvement than trading for sp's; i want to keep bohm for both reasons cited above, even though i don't see him as either a short-term or long-term solution at 3b; i think that hoskins doesn't sweeten the deal, but some combo of grullon/maton and a couple of non-howard pitchers from aa or aaa gets it done. for one year, i would give up just one or two non bohm/howard minor leaguers along with segura. for two years i would give up 4.
your answers?
