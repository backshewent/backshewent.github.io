Happy Primary Day in Arkansas, Georgia, Idaho, Kentucky Oregon and Pennsylvania.

Key Races
Pennsylvania - Democratic primary for governor (Wolf heavily favored); Democratic Primary for lieutenant governor (no idea); Democratic primary for the 13th Congressional District - part of Northeast Philly and parts of Montgomery and Bucks - Marjorie Margolis is Chelsea Clinton's mother in law...there's been no public polling on the race, and whoever gets the Dem nod will win the general in November.

Kentucky - Republican primary for US Senate (McConnell favored over tea party guy Bevin) - likely to be the biggest story of the night nationally even though there are tons of more interesting races

Georgia - Republican primary for US Senate (Perdue, establishment-y conservative, likely to make runoff to face either Kingston, establishment-y conservative, or Karen Handel, less establishment-y conservative. Crazy persons Gingrey and Broun doing surprisingly poorly. Tons of competitive House primaries as well as Kingston, Gingrey and Broun are all vacating their House seats to run for Senate.

Idaho - Our friends from the GOP gubernatorial debate from last week are facing off today. Governor Butch Otter is facing a stern challenge from a tea party state senator because he likes Common Core too much or something, but I'm more interested in how the biker and the curmudgeon do.

Oregon - National GOP favorite Dr. Monica Wehby has had a bad week or two, getting accused of hitting an ex in the face with a notepad or something, and she's facing the more conservative State Rep. Jason Conger for the right to take on freshman Sen. Jeff Merkley. Merkley will be favored regardless, but there has been polling showing he is vulnerable.

Arkansas - I don't care about anything, but TV might
