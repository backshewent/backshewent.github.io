stevemc wrote:
Phred wrote:Harper wasn't going full speed but he was certainly going fast enough to be able to get to first if the ball had dropped in. He wasn't getting to 2nd on that no matter what.


Might have been a different issue had Ben Revere dropped it.



I LOLed.

But seriously, I despise Harper as much as anybody...but time and time again, the "run hard every ball!" people probably didn't play much baseball. And if they did, they were likely horrible baserunners.

On a play like that, "fake hustle" can easily result in you running yourself into an out. What you need to do there is to be around first base by 10 or so feet when the ball lands. If the ball does happen to not be caught, then you're in a position to watch where the ball kicks to. Nothing like being halfway to second when the SS picks the ball up on one hop and throws to second to get you by 20 feet. But if it kicks far enough away, your momentum will be taking you towards second and you can accelerate to get there if you think you can make it.

THAT being said, I have no idea how hard harper ran and if he was even in the position to do what I described above.
