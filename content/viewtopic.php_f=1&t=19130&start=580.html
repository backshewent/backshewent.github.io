I think the color of the Phillies helmets of the 1970-1991 era varied a bit - indeed I once found an article in the Inky from 91 or 92 when they were switching to the current style wherein Bill Giles cited the difficulty in being able to find that maroon shade they used consistently across suppliers and materials as one reason for the change - but I also think the issue is photo color processing of the era. Certainly if you see good footage of the 1980 NLCS and World Series the helmets are unquestionably the dark shade of maroon, so by 1980 at the latest they were dark like the ones they use now.

The thing about him is that he didn’t have a single run of breathtaking dominance like some other greats. He was just really, really, really good, and sometimes dominant, for fucking ever. As just one example, he had 5 season with an OPS of 1.000 or better. Many Hall of Famers had that many or more, bunched together. His first such season was in 1959, and his last was in 1973. From 1955-1973, 19 seasons, he had an OPS of .900 or better 17 times, missing only in 1966 and 1968, his age 32 and 34 seasons. In 1968 he perhaps appeared to be “fading” with an .852 OPS. He then posted 1.003, .958, 1.079, .904, and 1.045 from his age 35-39 seasons, averaging 137 games played per season and 564 PAs. Without performance enhancers.

During those seasons, he recorded 203 home runs, 392 walks - and 274 strike outs.
