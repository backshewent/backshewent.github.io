by lethal » Wed Mar 07, 2012 22:35:58

TenuredVulture wrote:I think the idea that Northern NJ is the best untapped market is untrue. Yankee fans aren't abandoning the Yankees to root for the NJ As. So, really looking at frustrated Met fans as your potential fan base. But given the Mets financial issues, it seems rather likely that most everyone in baseball has an interest in not doing anything that would further damage that franchise. I don't think MLB wants to drive another team into bankruptcy if it can be avoided.


I don't think that there are that many Mets fans in NJ, just because they're further away and its a pain in the butt to get from Jersey to Flushing.

That said, sure, the Newark Rays aren't going to convert many Yankees fans, but maybe a family with kids who lives in Jersey takes their family to Newark instead of the Bronx since the trip is a lot closer and tickets are probably cheaper and you don't have to cross 2 bridges and pay an arm and a leg to drive. Maybe they realize that sure, getting to Yankee Stadium after work is pretty easy to do, but after the game ends at 10 or 10:30, you have to take the packed subway to Penn Station (45 minutes), then take the NJ Transit train to your car (say another 45 minutes), then drive home. You're not home to at least midnight. Instead, maybe you take the train to Newark after work, then after the game ends, you take the train again (but only 30 minutes since you're closer to home). You might be home by 11.

I doubt they ever surpass the Yankees, but in a generation, they might have a similar size fan base as the Mets and if its the Rays who win consistently, you'll get bandwagon fans too.

I don't know how the economics of a New Brunswick or Woodbridge team would be, but I didn't notice Middlesex county on any team's territory list. That team could draw from the Phillies fanbase as well and if a stadium is built close enough to an NJ Transit station, that would help draw commuters.
