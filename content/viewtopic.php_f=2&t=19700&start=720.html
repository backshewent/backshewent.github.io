Lots of choices out there. I might think that Software Engineering or Cyber Security might be a better fit. All the deep down guts stuff I learned in CS are nothing that I have ever used. I certainly understand some of the more esoteric causes of problems, but the fix is at a higher level. I consider SE Degrees when hiring. I do not work in Cyber, but it is a hot hand too.

FWIW, the discussion about Degree over boot camp is provably faulty. If you have an unrelated degree and relevant work experience then a boot camp or comparable reputable course work is acceptable, but if you do not have a degree you can look at the facts of life time earnings to show that there are disadvantages. For instance, my wife and her older brother are 7 years apart. He works in an IT field and discontinued his education with the temptation of gold, my wife works in HR and finished her degree as an adult. She makes ~$50K more annually then he does. The offers he gets when he changes jobs are just not comparable and it has not evened out over time (this is just a fact). In contrast, her earnings took off post degree.
