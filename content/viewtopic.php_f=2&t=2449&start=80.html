Women played a very important role in the early Christian Church. Mary Magdalene, for example, was possibly the most important person in Jesus' crew.

Jesus treated women as equals to men in many ways. He exalted the woman who washed his feet with her tears and dried them with her hair. Jesus was very much a radical. Treating women with dignity and respect was one way in which he was so. In his time, men didn't talk to women in public. But he did.

That stuff is in the bible too. When folks want to portray Christians as knuckle dragging Neanderthals, they can always find quotes in the bible to do so. But it doesn't come close to telling the whole story. (If the subject was baseball here we'd call it a small sample size problem).

There are many, many examples of strong, independent, intelligent women throughout the bible. The early Christians were probably the most progressive folks in the world at that time.

In my religion, the role of women isn't anything like the one portrayed in the bozo article from the above post. Perhaps the name of the article should read The Role of Women in Bozo Religions or something.
