Because of Buck's hot streak I was wondering about guys who generally suck at baseball yet perform exceedingly well against the Phillies. There were obviously some sample size issues so after filtering out guys who had only seen a handful of plate appearances against the Phils, there were a lot of pitchers left over. I ended up setting the minimum at 50 PA. By tOPS+ vs. the Phillies, which makes a player's career OPS a benchmark for their relative OPS+ in games versus the Phils, the best active scrubs against the Phils are:

Miguel Olivo, 151 PA
OPS vs. Phillies: .952
tOPS+ vs. Phillies: 172

Olivo's most similar batter on baseball-reference is Rod Barajas. But against the Phillies, he's basically Mike Piazza.

Miguel Tejada, 138 PA
OPS vs. Phillies: 1.062
tOPS+ vs. Phillies: 166

What's up with Miguels? Tejada has the highest batting average vs. the Phils of any active player at .408.

Scott Hairston, 135 PA
OPS vs. Phillies: 1.025
tOPS+ vs. Phillies: 166

This fucking guy.

Hunter Pence, 114 PA
OPS vs. Phillies: 1.075
tOPS+ vs. Phillies: 159

Nate Schierholtz, 62 PA
OPS vs. Phillies: .949
tOPS+ vs. Phillies: 158

If you can't beat 'em, join 'em? Pence has the fourth-highest OPS vs. the Phillies of any active player and the second-highest slugging percentage behind Ryan Braun.
