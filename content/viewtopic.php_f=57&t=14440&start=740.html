You can nitpick about whether guys like Marson and Donald are real-world MLB starters (they aren't), but the general point is fairly legit. Should change with DOM and I expect d'Arnaud and Gose will be real players. Part of it is organizational draft philosophy that focuses on pitching (and pretty effectively), part of it is the fact that they have had anchors at C, 1B, 2B, SS and CF for the last 6-7 years, which resulted in trades (two bad ones with Houston in my view). Part of it is they have sucked at drafting position players. I don't know how big of a deal it is right now, but it is going to be a big deal soon, so I hope we have the right guys in Quinn, Asche, Joseph, etc....but it would beyond idiotic to expect any of these guys to be anything close to the once-in-a-lifetime talents of Utley/Rollins and to a far lesser extent Chooch and Howard.

JFLNYC wrote:It's hard to find good position players to draft.

I get that you're being facetious, but the argument that they haven't had high first round picks to draft high-end high school bats seems like a fair point.

The organizational philosophy for the last however long I can remember has centered around pitching and tools guys. Some of the tools guys panned out. Brown, even if he doesn't become a star, has to be considered a tools guy that was/is something of value. Bourn and Gose also panned out. Now, obviously, for every one of them, there's a couple Greg Golsons and Anthony Hewitts, but when you either draft in the low 20's or use your pick to sign free agents to help load up the big club, of course it's tough to draft impact bats. Arms aren't necessarily easier to develop, but when they're developed, you can always trade pitching for offense. You can't always trade offense for pitching.
