by RichmondPhilsFan » Fri Dec 09, 2016 17:24:36

slugsrbad wrote:I've yet to get into any of his solo stuff. I wanted to after finishing the first two KK books, but lost steam.

It's excellent. Stormlight will be ten books, so you have plenty of time.

Nearly all of his fantasy books are mildly (and to varying degrees) interconnected by sharing the same universe (the Cosmere), but so far they all work separately. Start with Mistborn and see if you like it--it's the most approachable of his stories IMO. It's the first in a trilogy but has enough of a complete story that if you never pick up another Sanderson book you'll be okay.

What I like about his stuff is that his magic systems are all theoretically based in science, or at least their version of science. But they all have some type of physics/chemistry aspect (e.g., matter/energy is neither created nor destroyed, every force has a counterforce, etc).
