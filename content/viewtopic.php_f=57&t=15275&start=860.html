burnett will be one of the tests the phillies have to pass in order to switch me back to optimism, albeit one of the less significant ones.

i think the rational move consistent with trying to thread the rebuild/compete needle is to sign burnett, but for just this year. that should be achievable. give him $17-18 million plus plenty of incentives, and a no-trade except for pittsburg, baltimore, washington. if he goes elsewhere for just a year, i'll be angry. if he comes to philly for 2+ years of guaranteed big money, i'll be angry. if he goes elsewhere for two years of big money, fine, that's someone else's problem. if he is signed to pitch for the phils for just this season, i'll be very pleased. with health concerns surrounding mag and p-bone and biddle, the huge career inconsistency and age of camora, the age of lee, the loss of our other top prospect starters to injury, and the fact that martin should never be allowed to start, the current staff is too thin for much more than a 1 in 10 shot at the playoffs. at this point on the probability distribution, adding a 2 win pitcher makes a huge difference.

but to have real hope this season i will need to see lots of ruf and galvis (even if howard and rollins are miraculously awesome i want to see them getting lots of rest and the younger guys getting the chance to develop further), rupp as backup catcher most of the year, utley getting regular rest, papelbon and adams on short leashes, and martin being groomed for the backend of the pen and never gettting another start. without that, the old stars are going to fall apart long before the stretch run, and their replacements will have lost much of another year of big-league experience.
