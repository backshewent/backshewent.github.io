I have a portable DeLonghi unit. It may have a dehumidifier mode; if so, we've never used it as A/C dehumidifies by its nature and heat and humidity usually go hand-in-hand. It works great as an air conditioner.


I've often railed on why all A/C systems are designed without independent cool and dehumidify functions. I prefer it a little warmer than most during the summer (around 75-76), but in order to get rid of the Philly humidity during the summer I've got to run the temp down to 70-72. I agree with this. I don't mind the warmth it's the humidity that is awful. I turned the AC on last night for a few hours just to get the humidity out of the air in the house. I have a portable dehumidifier in the basement but it gives off so much heat when I run it that I can't use it for long periods.
