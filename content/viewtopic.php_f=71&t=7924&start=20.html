by swishnicholson » Sun Mar 08, 2009 22:11:47

kruker wrote:So for anyone here that is a runner, is it worth going to one of the specialty stores and getting the stride analysis and all that jazz to get a proper shoe or is it just a ploy to get me to drop $150 on a pair of running shoes?

I'm not much of a runner, 3-5 miles, 2-3 times a week when it's nice out, so I don't know if I'm the type that needs to go to a dedicated store, but if it really makes that big of a difference, I've got no problem dropping an extra $60 or so to make those runs go a little more smoothly.


Yes, probably. Good running shoes are in many ways more important for the novice runner, since a new runner (or an experienced one that is building mileage back up) is less conditioned and also quite likely carrying a little extra weight, and thus more susceptible to injury.

One thing to keep in mind is that the most important thing in choosing the right shoe is still fit. At a good running store they should be able to to come up with three or so styles that fit your stride pattern and body type well. After that, just take the one that feels best on your foot- no slipping in the heel, fitting snugly around your forefoot, the arch hitting in the right place. Don't worry about any supposed technical specs-90% of it's BS anyway. Don't get hung up on size , either. You'll generally want to go a half-size up anyway since your feet tend to swell during running, and many brands are inconsistent in their sizing. Asics, which I always wear, tend to run very small. I generally take a 9 in a dress shoe but a 10.5 in their running shoes. If you can't find one that feels right, go ahead and spend some more time looking around, or wait for them to special order the one that felt right but they only had in 1/2 size too small.

So yeah, I would go to a specialty store for a first pair of shoes-certainly as opposed to just picking something off the rack. Any of the Running Company Stores are good, and there are others. After you know what you want you can look for better deals if you like. I tend to try to stick with the running stores just because they do things I like to have around like group runs and race info distribution and gainful employment for runners (including me at one time), but it's not really a moral decision and these things are less important for the casual runner.
