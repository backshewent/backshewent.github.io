How much do cab drivers make, not including tips?

I'm always bothered conceptually by tipping. Why should I pay people extra for doing what they're already paid to do? I guess the answer is that occupations where tips are common are also poorly-paid, and those people depend on tips to make a decent living. (I know this is especially true of waiters and waitresses.) Fair enough, but then those jobs should pay more. I realize this is tilting at windmills but it's just always struck me as weird that somehow tipping became the norm. Or rather, tipping for unremarkable service. I certainly understand tipping someone who goes above and beyond for you (like the cabbie whose car you puked in).
