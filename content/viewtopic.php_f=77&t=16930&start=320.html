His Wii gamepad charger exploded (i mean almost literally, with a loud POP) and the 'backup', which was the original that he had gnawed the end into almost oblivion, also failed to work. So it was meltdown city- he wanted to go to gamestop immediately to get a new one, and of course today is the 1 day out of 365 where we couldn't do that. At this time, the neighbor from across the street showed up with a gift for him. Luckily, the neighbor just dropped it off and left, and he opened it and it was a couple of cool fossils. So meltdown part 2- i don't want that gift, why don't they know i want video games, and on and on. Some relatives had already arrived for xmas dinner, and it was quite difficult to be there and attempt to calm him down.

Now while the two situations aren't totally congruous, obviously, but my end point is the same for both- don't give up on the kids at this point. Keep doing the right thing.
