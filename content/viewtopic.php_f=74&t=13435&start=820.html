There's a growing body of evidence that people process refined sugar much like they do addictive substances. I'm not saying you don't know what you're talking about when you blandly assert something about "items that aren't addictive and don't inhibit your ability to function", but yeah, I do have my doubts.

But look around you. The stuff you had for breakfast and the whiskey you drink in the evening must satisfy arcane processing and packaging standards. You probably wear a seat belt when you drive, you rode in a child seat when you were a toddler. The things your physician, or pharmacist, or nurse can & can't do for you are hemmed in by rules & strictures you may not understand. Practically all your behaviors & my behaviors and others behaviors are mediated by 'artificial' constraints, many of them emanating from government; many that frankly aren't very effective and more than a few that are positively counterproductive. You may hate it, you may like it, but you shouldn't be surprised by it.

I have many, many reservations about the potential for initiatives like Bloomberg's. At the same time I have zero doubt that if we don't in some fashion diminish behaviors that cost us all a galling and fast-growing share of our social capital for rescue-oriented health treatment, we'll have much bigger problems to quarrel about than whether I can score an extra few ounces of sugar syrup in a single cup.
