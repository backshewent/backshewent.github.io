by Barry Jive » Sun Apr 12, 2009 00:35:56

Dylan, I always thought was just a guy who had a specific audience. I guess I thought it was just a niche that was really popular, sort of how The Dead are (not that they're not good, but they certainly have a cult following).

I remember liking "Like a Rolling Stone" because I think the Stones released it back in the '90s and I probably heard it on the radio when I was a kid. I didn't really listen to Dylan until high school, when I listened to "Hurricane." I thought it was kind of a cool song, but now it's clear that there's way more to him than that.

I tried getting into his "classic" albums for a while in college, but it never really clicked except with Blood On the Tracks, which I've always loved.

I got back into him when the I'm Not There soundtrack came out and there were a bunch of bands I liked covering his stuff. I realized that in a lot of cases, the covers sound better than the originals, but I've always been of the opinion that in some senses the original can never be topped.

And now everything else is starting to click the way Blood on the Tracks first did.
