Sorry, jtk, but that’s just a bunch of cherry-picked horse manure.

Cases a “sham”? What the heck does that even mean? First, if you mean number of cases, there is no reasonable doubt the number of cases is grossly underreported. So if they’re a sham in that sense, it’s directly the opposite of what you meant.

Is the concept of cases a “sham?” “cases as described now have no correlation on any actual meaningful event.” You’re just trolling now, right? You’re just having us on. Because even if some — even many — cases are underreported, over reported, inaccurate, probable or confirmed or however you want to try to explain away some of the cases, there have by any meaningful definition already been millions of cases in the US and over 136,000 deaths.

I can assure you that for anyone who has has, or will have, the virus their “case” is an extremely meaningful event on a health, family, emotional and professional level, to say nothing of the meaningfulness to the 136,000 souls who have perished and their family and friends. It’s been in the
news a lot but my colleague and friend, Amanda Kloots, just lost her husband to to a complications from COVID and his “case” is no doubt going to continue to be pretty damn meaningful to her and their one-year old son for the rest of their lives.

My stepdaughter and her fiancé each had a “case” for which they’ve now recovered but which was extremely difficult for them (and us) as they went through it and we have no good idea what the long-term effects will be for them for the rest of their lives.

“Distorted?” In what way? Too much news coverage of the most significant world health crisis in most of our lifetimes? Too much emphasis on the fact that the US has about 5% of the world’s population but with all our wealth and technology we have 25% of the cases and deaths? Too much fear because the death rate has declined from its high in the Spring but is now higher than it’s been in weeks?

“Unfair” to compare Florida and AZ to other states because the rates of infection and death are different than in other states? What’s “unfair” about it? Are those of us in Florida and AZ no longer American citizens? Are we no longer human beings? Did we make the same sort of distinctions for various regions of China, Italy and other countries? What meaningful difference would it make if we did or did not?

Its about perspective and understanding what ALL the information is telling us.


Who has lost perspective in this case? Here’s what ALL the information is telling us:

. Cases in this country are surging, whereas in virtually everywhere else on Earth they have declined dramatically.
. It’s the Summer, when some suggested the virus would slow down. So it’s actually possible this surge IS a slow down.
. It’s the worst pandemic in a century and shows no signs of slowing in this country.
. The number of deaths SO FAR is equivalent to over 50 times the number who died at the WTC on 9/11 and the equivalent of about 400 jumbo jets full of people crashing.
. The richest, most productive country in the history of civilization still has PPE shortages similar to what we had 4 months ago.
. Once people go back indoors up north in the Fall it’s very possible there will be a deadly second wave, especially if children,teachers, administrators and other staff are forced to go back to school.
. As of yet there’s no guarantee that any effective vaccine can be developed, let alone one which will have a long-term effect.
. Even if a safe, effective and long-term vaccine can be developed, there’s no guarantee that a sufficient number of people in this country will take it.
. In Florida, which reported over 36,000 cases over the last 3 days, people aren’t even required to wear a mask in public.

And the ultimate “sham?” Today Florida reported it’s highest one-day death total: 123 souls.

Of course there are positive signs as you have pointed out. But they are far outweighed by the negative. And, most importantly, NO ONE KNOWS HOW MUCH WORSE THIS MIGHT GET. However, all evidence in this country strongly suggests that, without clear, national policies on prevention (such as those adopted in other countries), it’s likely going to continue to get worse before it gets better unless and until a vaccine is found EVEN IF the death rate declines.

As I’ve wondered before, I have no idea what is to be gained by your quest to downplay the crisis. Doing so is not going to save any lives. In fact, letting our guard down may cost lives as we have clearly seen. Will downplaying it spur us to reopen more things and do so faster? Well we’ve tried that already and it’s been a disaster and terribly counterproductive.

I can’t think of any good reason for downplaying the seriousness of this health crisis except perhaps to either prove some meaningless point or as a worthless academic exercise. It’s a lot easier to think of bad reasons to engage in this exercise and I’d certainly prefer to believe that’s not where you’re coming from.
