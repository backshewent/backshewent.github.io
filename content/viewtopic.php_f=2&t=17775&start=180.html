by drsmooth » Sat May 13, 2017 19:23:21

Some strange, good, but strange movies were made in the '70s. Among them was Winter Kills (1979), a black 'comedy' (definitely not "ha ha" funny, and eerily prescient seen from our current circumstances) loosely organized around a mashup of Kennedy conspiracy theories. John Huston in the Joe Kennedy role (and basically reprising the same patriarch who raised - and razed - Faye Dunaway in Chinatown), Jeff Bridges as Bobby/Teddy/all other Kennedys, and a supporting cast that includes Tony Perkins, Eli Wallach, Sterling Hayden, Ralph Meeker, Richard Boone, and Liz Taylor in a bit part - uncredited! - as a sort of mid-century Pearl Mesta.

But none of that is the strange part. The strange part is that the movie was produced by two drug dealers who found themselves behind on accounts due to mafia lenders to such an extent that one of them was murdered by said mobsters midway through the film's production.

Needless to say that brought production to a halt, and it took two more years for interested & presumably drug-free parties to round up the necessary dough to complete the already waaaaaaaay over-budget film.

It's not any participant's greatest work (but not anyone's worst), it was directed by a guy who never made anything else of note (William Richert, who did portray "Bob" in My Own Private Idaho), and probably was out of synch with its time given that production, uhm, hiccup. Strange, watchable, very much of the '70s.
