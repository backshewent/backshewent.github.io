So I threw this together to update as returns come in because I didn't feel like actually working (though I think I need to start working now) - https://docs.google.com/spreadsheet/ccc ... tZlE#gid=0

Brief description of what's going on here...Columns A-H should be self explanatory. (I threw in some 1s to make sure all the columns weren't showing DIV/0!)

I is the county's share of the total statewide vote this time. J is the county's share of the statewide vote in 2009.

K is what Christie needs to get in the county percentage wise to hit 60% statewide if all county turnout is stable. L is the 66% for Eagleton, which probably ain't happening. Edit: Now L is that thing I babbled about below on updating targets. /no one cares

Everything after that is 2009 stuff, and then me normalizing the spill to Daggett and others so the Corzine+Christie %'s summed to 97% in every county (at least I think that's what I did).

I'm sure there's a way of syncing the reported county vote share and county Christie percentages to update his targets, but it's not coming to me quickly, and I probably spent too much time on this as it is.

My instinct is for the 60% number to come in, Christie is probably going to have run above his targets in places like Bergen, Essex and Hudson because he has more room to grow there and because there are ceilings in some of the other places. For anything much north of 60%, he's gonna need to do that plus have turnout shift so Ocean/Monmouth/Morris and the other smaller GOP counties make up a significantly larger share of the statewide turnout than in 2009.
