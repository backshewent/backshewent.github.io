The real issue is that voter ids aren't going to help at all address the most likely forms of voter fraud. Even Housh's own "argument" here reveals that--how does requiring an id prevent a hacker from going into whatever system exists and manipulating results? If that were the case, then it would also make sense to require an ID whenever you used a credit card, because identity theft. But no one does, because it won't help.

That's not why. It's a cost benefit question, just like with voter ID. Yes, adding an ID requirement would help eliminate fraud. Some merchants do require ID when the incidence/cost of fraud is high enough. But there are other ways that credit card companies reduce fraud that are less intrusive to the customer, so it is not required. It's the credit card company that bears the costs of screwups and fraud, not the customer. With potential voter fraud, who bears the cost of a fraudulent transaction? No one, or everyone, depending on how you look at it.
