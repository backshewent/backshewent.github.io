by Bill McNeal » Sat Jul 05, 2014 11:20:50

I listened to O&A religiously back when they were on in NY on WNEW before they were syndicated etc. and listened up until they got dumped for the Sex for Sam thing. Loved them back then. Then I was an XM subscriber when they came back so I ponied up the extra buck a month to get their channel when they started on there. It wasn't quite as good as it was on NEW, but it was still funny. After a while, they found their stride on xm and went back on terrestrial for half the show. They were still edgy and funny, but they started to get really jaded and cynical. I think that cynicism was always there, but they could use it for their benefit, like when trashing their management or whatever higher power they were hating on because it was funny and relatable, they were their audience. Working class guys, who were pissed off at their job/boss/ex wife/neighbor and could make it funny. They were still kind of the underdog, so it connected. Over the years the bitching just got to be unbearable though, it went from "hey management won't let us do this funny bit!" To "hey management is just wholley incompetent and holding us back from being funnier" to "hey I want to have more guns and the government won't let me!"

I cancelled my xm subscription shortly after Obama was elected, I wasn't commuting anymore and I couldn't get xm in my house, so it just didn't make sense to keep paying for it, I thought I was going to miss O&A, but I really didn't. They were still occasionally funny, mostly when they had Louis CK, Patrice, Bill Burr or even Vos on, but in between the funny segments there was just unbearable levels of bitching they'd even acknowledge how much the fans hated it and didn't care. The "hard core" fans defended them, but I think most folks just kind of got sick of it and they became irrelevant. About a month ago, I googled them to see if they were still on the air, I hadn't heard about them in so long I was just curious if they'd ended the show. I guess now they have.
