JFLNYC wrote:
I may not be the sharpest tool in the shed, but I believe that's what they call a trend.



You always pull this condescending false modesty shtick out of your rhetorical bag of tricks, and it's incredibly aggravating.

Offense has declined league-wide. Polanco's offense is declining at a faster rate than that of the league overall, of course, but that does mitigate the trend somewhat.

Anyway, Polanco is old and he gets hurt a lot, but he's still an incredibly valuable defensive player, and he's not getting paid very much at all. It's not a great situation, but it's not terrible, at least not right now, and I don't think it's wise to compound the situation by making additional mistakes. I'm far more concerned with third base in 2013 and beyond or, if someone like Chase Headley becomes available for the right price, you make a move. I'm just not sure there's a way to upgrade over Polanco in a way that doesn't badly hamstring the team in other ways down the road.

There's one year left on his contract. One. If he proves to be .500 OPSy horrible in 2012, then Wigginton and Valdez will hold it down until July when you look to make a trade. I just don't want to make a move that's going to purge more minor league talent from the pipeline until they know they must.

I like Headley a lot because he's entering his prime and solves the positional problem for a few years. It'd be worth bleeding off more prospects for a guy like that. But the Padres will want a lot for him.
