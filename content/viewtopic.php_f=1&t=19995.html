My mother once made garden flags. Every season they would roll out a new set of designs. These were hand made items, and on the expensive side. After they premiered their new design a month of so would go by then knock offs made in China would show up. Something like the color of the flower would change slightly, and may some subtle shape changes to the art work which would result in something that would pass as 'not the same' thus no copyright infringement. Try as she might, there was no legal remedy, in fact even though you could not tell one from the other at a distance, the close examination show enough change that this was permissible. That seems to be the gambit here as well.
