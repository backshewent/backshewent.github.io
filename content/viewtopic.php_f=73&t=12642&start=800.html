Quick note on refi's. There is a government sponsored program called Making Home Affordable (MHA) that is available through June 2012 (but may be extended). The way it works is, if your loan is currently owned by Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac then you can refinance showing no income or asset info, and there are no minimum credit requirements as long as your payment is going down, staying the same, or increasing by less than 20%. Your new loan can also go up to 105% of the value of your home and there is no PMI or piggyback loan neccessary as long as your current loan does not have PMI. There is no cash-out allowed by the program, so this is strictly for people looking to lower their rate/term or go from an ARM to a fixed, but just about everyone qualifies. If your loan is held by Wells Fargo, Bank of America/Countrywide, Chase, etc. and you are looking to refi, call them and ask about the MHA program, it really is a good deal.

Here is a link to the MHA website, specifically the Home Affordable Refinance Program (HARP) that I described above.
http://www.makinghomeaffordable.gov/pro ... /harp.aspx
