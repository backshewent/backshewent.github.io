milton bernard thompson wrote:people just aren't listening to what i'm saying. dude wants to call me out for saying NO ONE IN PHILLY CARES, when i clearly said THE MAJORITY doesn't care. this should be pretty obvious. i love the phillies more than anyone else in this city, but for every diehard phillies fan i know there are 20 diehard eagles fans. nearly everyone at least kind of cares about the eagles, and a lot of people pay no attention whatsoever to the phillies. this is just reality, you guys must live in a different city than me.

my only point is that it's not surprising, given the way people love their eagles in this city, that a regular season game outdoes a phillies playoff game. i personally am not a big sixers fan and went to at least 2 phillies games this season during sixers playoff games without giving it a second thought. most eagles fans would be considerably more inclined to do such a thing.


Your experience is vastly different from mine then. Yes, I would agree that (1) most people root for the Eagles before any other team and (2) most people "care" about the Eagles on some level, which is more than other local teams can say. And I'd agree that there are far more "diehard" Eagles fans than "diehard" Phillies fans.

Where I think you are wrong is in saying that THE MAJORITY of Philadelphia area folks don't care about the Phillies. From my experience, ONLY SMALL NUMBER of Philadelphians don't care at all about the Phillies. Many are very casual about the Phils or have them as their 3rd or 4th favorite team or are simply bandwagoners that only care when the team is good. Many don't go to many/any regular season games or watch on TV. But I think it's a stretch to extrapolate that the majority of folks don't care .

Yeah, in the mid to late 90's, there were a lot of folks who wouldn't give the Phillies a half a second thought. Winning changes attitudes.

YMMV, but I don't get the same feel in the city that you do.
