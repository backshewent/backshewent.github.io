CalvinBall wrote:CBS- Tie

I mean, what the actual fuck.

Don't get caught up in the topline to the LA Times/USC poll, though the trend is perhaps real. But in other polls that have come out - Reuters (which is garbage), YouGov (pretty good) - there hasn't been any shift since last week.

While I am somewhat amused to see many of you creeping towards the dread that has filled my soul since January, four major things to keep in mind -

1) The 3rd party vote leans Clinton in the head to head ballot, and traditionally many of these voters will choose one of the major party candidates, especially in swing states.
2) The likely voter screens are really hurting Clinton, but a lot of likely voter screens are crude. There was a reason I (and a lot of other Republicans) thought Romney was going to win in mid/late October - he really was winning in a lot of polls, including GOP internals. The Democrats did a much better job modeling voter turnout, and the state polls ended up being better than the national polls. Neil Newhouse, Romney's pollster, said afterwards his biggest lesson in 2012 was an unenthusiastic vote counts just as much as an enthusiastic vote. I think we're seeing a lot of the same things happening in some of these likely voter models.
3) Similar to the above issue, one of the major factors in the fluctuation of polling is in asymmetric response rates. When one candidate's base of supporters is more energized, they're more likely to take the time to talk to someone on the phone about it for 15 minutes. This is one of the major sources of convention 'bounces' - ie the actual vote in the electorate hasn't shifted as much as the polls are sensing. Lotta reason to think after the past couple of weeks this might be true in this race.
4) Donald Trump isn't doing debate prep.
