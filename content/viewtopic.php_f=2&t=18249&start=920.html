It’s a good argument known as the “collective rights model.” The collective rights model was embraced by all the Circuits until this century beginning with the 5th Circuit and ultimately the SC (particularly Scalia in the Heller case) which resolved the then-split Circuits in favor of an individual’s right to bear arms. IOW, the SC decoupled the right to bear arms from the “militia” preamble, saying the preamble was but one of many civic reasons for the Second Amendment.

Another example of the SC not being final because they’re right, but being right because they’re final.
