by FTN » Wed Dec 09, 2009 15:52:02

And much like we discussed this past summer, you have to look at it as "These prospects for Roy Halladay and two draft picks". Im sure he'd love a contract extension, but he hasn't said its a prerequisite to any trade. He wants to win a World Series. And he wants to get paid. But in that order. So if he comes to Philly, I'm sure he'd be told "hey, we'd love to have you back, but our focus is on winning the World Series this year, and if you want to talk extension, we'll listen, and we're interested, but we can't make any promises other than to be fair". Its a win-win for Halladay, because if he comes here and wins a ring, it won't hurt his free agency case. But no one is really going to look at his numbers before he hits free agency, because everyone knows what hes capable of.

The Phillies can afford to trade 2 good prospects + Happ for him, because if he walks, and Lee walks, they are looking at 4 first/comp round picks in the 2011 draft. Those 4 picks have tremendous value, when added to their own first round pick. And because Halladay and Lee are likely to sign with good teams, those picks will be true, unprotected first rounders.
