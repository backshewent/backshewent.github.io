by GunbladeVIII » Tue Jun 05, 2007 09:10:34

smitty wrote:Is Upper Merion connected to Lower Merion? I don't think it is. Upper Darby isn't connected to Darby and there is no Lower Darby. The Phllies will not win a championship until the Greater Philadelphia Area gets its geography straightened out.


Brand new Pennsylvania and east coast resident that she is, my wife is so far unable to wrap her head around the concept of townships.

So Bryn Mawr, which looks like a town, feels like a town, and is what appears on our mailing address, covered by one zip code. All that makes sense.

But when I first told her, back when we were thinking about moving there, that Bryn Mawr wasn't actually a "real" place (i.e. no government, no defined borders, no services, no Bryn Mawr police force) and that in reality, almost none of the "towns" around where I grew up, like Havertown, Broomall, Newtown Square, Wallingford, and so on, are "real" places, that's when she got a little confused. So, wait, she says, you have no local government? No, that's provided by these structures called townships. Okay, then a town is covered by its township for that stuff? Not exactly, since Bryn Mawr doesn't have its township... it has three (Lower Merion, Radnor, and Haverford) across two separate counties (Montgomery and Delaware). That's where I lost her.

I can understand why it's confusing to her, though. The concept of townships is one that's pretty much unique to eastern Pennsylvania and New Jersey, and one that, just from the example of Bryn Mawr, one town across three townships and two counties, is convoluted.
