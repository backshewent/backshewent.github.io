by Wolfgang622 » Sat Feb 20, 2016 12:42:55

Here are how I evaluate possible outcomes tonight, from the GOP perspective:

IDEAL:

1. Rubio
2. Trump

Think Rubio still has a shot to pull this out, as Trump has made himself as obnoxious as possible this last week and there seems to be gathering momentum around Rubio, in SC. I'd rate this as the third most likely outcome.

GOOD:

1. Trump
2. Rubio


Polls suggest Donald still has a pretty wide lead, so it's more likely he closes the deal and finishes first. but if Rubio finishes second, the rest of the race will start to order itself. Kasich may hang around, but Bush will have to drop, even if he finishes ahead of Kasich. Cruz, in this scenario, is relegated to fringe status, even if he finishes third (which he probably would in this case). I rate this as the most likely outcome.

ACCEPTABLE:

1. Trump
2. Cruz
3. Rubio

At least Rubio here re-asserts himself as the clear front-runner among establishment candidates. There is still likely significant pressure on Bush to drop out, which I think he does in this case. Even if he doesn't, his support in the Super Tuesday states probably continues to wane. Kasich still hangs around for Super Tuesday, but I don't see him as a significant player in most of the states participating that day. I'd call this the second most likely scenario.

UGH:

1. Trump
2. Cruz
3. Kasich
4. Rubio

At least this would probably still bounce Bush from the race, but it certainly makes things less clear going forward. Fortunately for the GOP, I think this is the least likely scenario and probably has virtually no chance of happening.

DISASTER:

1. Trump
2. Cruz
3. Bush

I don't think Bush can realistically hope to finish higher than third, and even that would be a yuge upset. But if he does, the waters are hopelessly muddied. He won't drop before Super Tuesday, neither will Kasich, and all of a sudden Trump looks like he could have a big day on that day. I think this is not very likely to happen, only slightly more likely than the "Ugh" scenario above, but it is what the GOP fears, to be sure. Fortunately for them, I doubt very much that Jeb Bush is the last second comeback kind of guy. if this were basketball, and he got the ball, he'd look to pass first, and the clock would run out - that's him, in a nut shell.

Of course it's easy to guess which one I'm "rooting" for.
