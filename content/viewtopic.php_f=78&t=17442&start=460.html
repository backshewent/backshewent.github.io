JFLNYC wrote:
The rent jumped even though he was paying fewer staff in July than he did in March.

The Trump campaign paid Trump Tower Commercial LLC $35,458 in March ― the same amount it had been paying since last summer ― and had 197 paid employees and consultants. In July, it paid 172 employees and consultants.

OK but that says nothing about how many people were working out of the campaign's headquarters. His paid staff through the primaries were mostly field workers who were laid off as their primaries ended (stupidly, leading to a lot of the issues the campaign is having now with building a national field campaign). The field staff who were no longer employed by the campaign never worked out of Trump Tower. In April Manafort became the campaign chair, and they started hiring the normal sort of people on a national campaign - political director, communications director, etc. - who never existed on Trump's primary campaign. I imagine most of these people have been hiring staffs under them as well, and these folks all need office space. In the wide world of things to attack Trump for, saying the campaign paying himself $170k for office space is phony and him making money off the campaign when he gave the campaign $2 mil last month and there's good reason to believe the rent increase is just down to taking up more space seems like a weak choice.
