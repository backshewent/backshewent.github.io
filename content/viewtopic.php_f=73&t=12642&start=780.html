by Bucky » Mon Sep 26, 2011 15:06:03

So I gotta learn how to drive a train. Well, not really, But sorta. The Kid found the "train simulator" called OpenBVE, and hoodwinked me into installing it on his PC. I gotta tell ya, it's pretty amazing. It's not just "stop and go", but has all the operator systems that the real trains have. There's many, many different trains- electric and diesel- to choose from, as wekk as lots and lots of routes. To start up, you have to run through the exact self-test sequence. Raise the pantograph if it's not up (there is a panel indicator in the exact simulation of the real console). Work the accelerator- apparently called a "reverser" on a train- and "gearshift" to navigate inclines and hills, without going too fast. Run over electronic "checkpoints" in the track, which can automagically slow you down or apply brakes if you're not compliant with the speed limit. Took me about an hour to get moving and to the first station.

Pretty cool stuff.
