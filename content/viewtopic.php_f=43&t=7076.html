Ruben Amaro Jr. Named GM. Official Announcement Monday

by Fusilli Jerry » Sat Nov 01, 2008 20:44:52

As per Philly.com

This just in. . .

Ruben Amaro Jr. is the new Phillies general manager.

Sources close to the situation confirmed to the Daily News' Paul Hagen that club president Dave Montgomery met with assistant general manager, scouting and player development Mike Arbuckle yesterday and informed him that he had lost out to Amaro in what had been considered a two-man sweepstakes.

Arbuckle, who was hired by former general manager Lee Thomas before the 1993 season, then informed Montgomery that he will be leaving the organization to pursue other opportunities, the sources continued.

An official announcement is expected Monday.....

by swishnicholson » Sat Nov 01, 2008 22:18:45

I'm pretty accepting of this. It was pretty clear that when he was passed over last time it was because the Phillies wanted him to train under another, experienced GM before they considered him ready, a situation Gillick was willing to accept. I think the only reason they would not have promoted Amaro is if Gillick had said no, this guy can't do the job. For better or worse, it's obvious that Gillick didn't say so.

I think the perception of Arbuckle is that he's more valuable when he's actively involved in scouting than as a supervisory manager, something a GM has to do a lot. So, if he wants to advance he's obviously going to have to do so with another organization. I'm not so sure he will, and we may see accepting a lateral move which at least means he doesn't have to work for Amaro.

We don't have any track record with Amaro as leader, and I don't think the Phillies organization is transparent enough for us to really have an understanding of what roles he's been performing. Hopefully they won't give him a burdensome contract and hopefully he'll show he's capable of taking in other points of view and incorporating them in his management-and I hope even more he'll know which points of view to listen to.

At any rate, it will be interesting to see how he does when it's his show to run.
