Um, you've obviously never studied game theory. You've gotta think like a nerd, man.

538 has over a 10% chance that Clinton wins the popular vote but has 269 or fewer electoral votes (and I'd assume any scenario where Clinton wins Utah outright has her winning well over 300 electoral votes). As such, it may make statistical sense for Clinton supporters in Utah to try to block Trump (by voting McMullin) because there's a chance this throws the election into the House--rather than having Trump win narrowly outright. In the House, a compromise could be reached that would see Clinton become President when she would've otherwise lost--or a compromise that seriously weakens Trump.
