by Bucky » Mon Mar 16, 2020 07:13:00

i guess it depends what you're after. the ford has some advantages for some: it has more 'real' controls instead of everything done through the display (i kinda like that to be honest); it has a more aggressive style (which seems to increase drag and thus decrease range); it will have the $7500 tax credit for the foreseeable future.

The tesla has better range for the buck at all price points (like 20-25%); faster charging potential at superchargers (but realistically 97% of charging will be done at home where both will max out to a residential 220v/50A connection, so the supercharging only matters on long journeys); the tesla is quicker 0-60 at all trims (doesn't matter one lick to me); their software ecosystem, with OTA updates, is revolutionairty in the industry, and i haven't heard ford even trying anything like that); and tesla almost certainly has a huge huge lead if autonomy is on your wish list.

I hope the mach-e is wildly successful. there's a large untapped ev market- those turned off by the cult of tesla and loyal to the motorhead brands of their ancestors. the mach-e should fill that niche nicely and thus help tesla's mission of accelerating the world's transition to sustainable energy.
