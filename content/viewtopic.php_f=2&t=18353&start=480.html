by traderdave » Thu Nov 02, 2017 12:34:32

Please correct me if I am reading this wrong but the Tax Bill sets the brackets as follows (for joint filers):

12% = $0 to $90,000
25% = $90,001 to $260,000
35% = $260,001 to $1,000,000
39.6% = >$1,000,001

So that a couple filing jointly with total taxable income of $130,000 would incur an income tax of $20,800. Currently, that same couple would incur an income tax of $29,382. So, the total savings is $8,582.

What about a couple with total taxable income of $60,000? With the new brackets, the tax due would be $7,200; under the current law, the tax due would be $10,739. So, the total savings is $3,539.

In other words, the rich get richer. Obviously very simplistic examples but is maybe some food for thought.
