The only positive with the buyouts is the buyout figure has already been counted against the tax. So Robertson, Phelps, etc would not count anything against the 2021 tax.

Neris's buyout, if declined, still defaults back to his final arbitration year which would require an increase over this year's $4.6M. So he either needs to be non-tendered or paid something in that range.

By my calcs (estimating arb figures which Spotrac doesn't do), the Phils have $62M under the cap to spend on a catcher, middle infielder (to upgrade the projected Bohm/Segura/Kingery/Hoskins combo), probably a starter to join Nola/Wheeler/Eflin/Howard and then a bunch of bullpen guys. Velasquez is going to be paid $5M+ to do something unless he's non-tendered. A CF upgrade might also be nice.
