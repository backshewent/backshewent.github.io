by swishnicholson » Mon May 15, 2017 15:39:38

Foot pain that subsides with exercise is generally associated with tendinitis. Pain in the the arch or heel that's most severe in the morning will often be plantar fasciitis, but it would be unusual for that to disappear when running. Could also be a bone bruise, a small muscle strain, or an incipient stress fracture (stress reaction).

So, lots of possibilities, with different treatments, so you should probably get it checked out by a sports medicine guy.

If you want to do some home treatment, though, increasing the blood flow to the area, and perhaps breaking down some scarring through massage (a lacrosse ball is good for this, or there are various sorts of rollers) can't hurt. Well, it hurts, but in a way that helps. Can also use alternating ice with heat to increase blood flow. anti-inflammatories can reduce swelling, of course, and sometimes that's the cause of the pain. But I hesitate to recommend them if there's a possibility they mask the pain and lead you to cause further damage, which can happen without a good diagnosis.
