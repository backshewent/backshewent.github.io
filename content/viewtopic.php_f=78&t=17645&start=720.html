I posted a comment this morning to Paul Krugman's column in the Times. It was designated a "NYT Pick" so I'll post it here:

We need to be prepared for a hard truth: It's going to get worse-- probably a lot worse -- before it gets better. And, paradoxically, it will only get better if it does get a lot worse because that's the only way many, if not most, Americans will be able to see the consequences of what we've just done.

We, as a species, have an extraordinary inability to learn from our predecessors' mistakes. Each generation it seems must learn the same, hard lessons over and over. Across each generation we retain a deep-seated fantasy of an all-powerful father figure who will make all things right.

It's only when we are personally faced with the disastrous consequences of that infantile fantasy that we wake up to the fact that we as individuals are responsible for the world in which we live and must ourselves do the work necessary to make the world a better place.

I'm old enough that much, if not all, of the remainder of my life will be spent in the coming storm. For their own sake and the sake of generations to come, I hope our younger generations have the strength, courage and wisdom to weather the storm ahead and to find their way back to the light as soon as possible.
