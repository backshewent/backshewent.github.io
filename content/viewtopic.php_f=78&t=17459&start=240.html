by RichmondPhilsFan » Mon Aug 29, 2016 09:57:09

Insurance companies can't just cancel your policy like that. They have to comply with the terms of their own policies, which are often governed by state law and almost always use universal language.

Generally, once the policy has been in place for 60 days, homeowners insurers cannot cancel your policy unless you have failed to pay, committed fraud, or made material misrepresentations during the application. There's a few other ways that they can cancel a policy due to material changes in the property condition, such as vacancy of the house, substantial deterioration, etc. But even then, they have to give a Notice of Cancellation with an opportunity for the insured to cure the problem--typically this is a 30-day period. If they continue down this route, file a complaint with your state's Bureau of Insurance.
