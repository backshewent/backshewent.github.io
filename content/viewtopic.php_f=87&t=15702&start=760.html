Re: Footgate, Aussies and Wiglans. Sixers Draft!

by JFLNYC » Thu Jun 26, 2014 10:26:11

I'm very excited to see how it all plays out. But I don't even know what to hope for anymore. This draft and the top guys in it have been hyped for so long I don't know what to make of them. There are some really good players in this draft and some really, really good players. If the goal is to get that franchise player, I just don't know if it's attainable in this draft. It's often been said how deep this draft is and it is deep. But, if your goal is to get that one transcendent guy to build your franchise around, the depth of the draft is largely meaningless if that one guy isn't there.

Wiggins is going to have a long and successful career. He's a very good defender already, with a good (but not great) shot. Combined with his athletic prowess, he's going to help any team he plays for. But the combination of a poor handle and questions about his mentality work against his becoming the alpha dog for a championship team. The most likely scenario is he ends up being a really good second or third guy on a team.

As I've said before, I think Parker is the larger version of James Harden: A scoring savant with little interest or ability defensively. He'll be a star because he can put the ball in the basket consistently. Questions about his athletic ability or struggling with size don't concern me. He'll find a way to score. But his defensive deficiencies plus questions about his weight suggest to me it's unlikely he'll be the number 1 guy on a championship team.

Everybody knows I love Embiid. Of all the players in this draft I think he's got the greatest chance to be "The Guy." I'm not saying he will; just that he's got the best chance. The problem, of course, is his health. It's perfectly possible to come back from his injuries with no recurring effect. But, if his bone issues are congenital and/or the result of poor nutrition and/or his size, the issues may get worse, not better, with time.

Like Wiggins, Exum will probably enjoy a long and successful career. His shooting and turnover issues will undoubtedly get better with age and experience. But will it all come together enough to make him "The Guy?" Possible, but I have to believe the odds are against it. And if the Sixers (or any other team) plan to move him off the ball, that's just another complicating factor.

The next tier of guys (Vonleh, Smart, Randle, Gordon) will all be good pieces, but it's hard to see any of them moving the needle significantly. And even the next tier (McDermott, Stauskas, etc.) are likely to have very good careers.

So, there's lots of talent to be had and I trust Hinkie's evaluation and wheeling & dealing skills to get the most from the draft. But, if the goal is to get "The Guy," I'm not sure this is the draft for it.
