azrider wrote:I know there are a couple of first responders here... But I do question the decision made by the fire department after the 911 call was transferred to them for me with very little training to attempt to resuscitate a victim of a trauma, which was attempted very briefly until the paramedics finally arrived.



That's SOP. In lots of situations, even poor help is much better than no help. 911 dispatchers (I'm not sure if "all"; if Barry Jive were here he'd give a more authoritative answer) are trained on how to walk people through CPR and basic first aid over the phone.

All that said, I know very little about the subject so take this as only an uninformed opinion, but I often wonder why CPR/AED etc. is performed on victims of mass trauma. I remember one incident where a dude's brains were on the road and the cops were hustling to get an AED on him. But there's people who study this sort of thing and I imagine there's data that shows that it happens that someone like that gets kickstarted and they're able to survive and eventually overcome their trauma. I guess one time in a thousand is enough to keep doing it.
