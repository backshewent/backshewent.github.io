It’s not just that they had a guy without an overpowering throwing high fastballs but that they had him/them throwing 4-seam FB’s, i.e., FB’s without movement. If you miss high with that pitch it’s a ball. If you miss low it’s a mediocre FB down the middle without movement, otherwise known as a batting practice fastball.

Maybe it’s being reported incorrectly or it’s just not true. But if it’s correct and you add it to the previously-reported news about having our hitters guessing for curveballs instead of looking for fastballs you’ve got an organizational philosophy which is so ridiculously wrong-minded it’s unbelievable — especially when you’re applying it to guys most of whom have beaten the odds and made it to the top rung of the baseball world by using other methods. And, btw, having better organizational approaches to hitting and pitching doesn’t preclude getting better players, too. That’s a false dilemma.
