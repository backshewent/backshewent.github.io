by dajafi » Mon Apr 06, 2009 16:01:09

It occurred to me today that a place like Brooklyn is probably the worst of all worlds in terms of taking books with any sort of cachet to them out of the library. I went online to our catalog and checked the David Simon ("The Wire") books, the Roberto Bolano novels, Saul Bellow's "Dangling Man" and one or two other titles; with maybe one exception, everything was either out, "in transit" (whatever that means) or on hold, despite their having multiple copies of most of those titles. For most of last year I was waiting for "Kafka on the Shore" to get returned.

I think the reason must be that New York City, like maybe a few other places like Boston or San Francisco, has people who are "literary" enough to want to read things like this (not super-abstruse textbooks, but not airport novels either) but not rich enough to just go out and buy copies. Other than college towns, there can't be too many American communities with a lot of people who fit that description.
