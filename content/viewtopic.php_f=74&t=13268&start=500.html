by TenuredVulture » Fri Feb 10, 2012 14:07:29

CFP--what's not clear is that the depression/anxiety is caused by student teaching, or if it's just a coincidence, or some combination of the two.

That being said, teaching isn't for everyone, and it's hard (though it certainly gets a lot easier once you've been at it a few years) and to pursue a not especially lucrative career because you've put in a few years toward pursuing those educational goals is probably not a good idea. Fighting through it at this point however probably isn't a terrible idea, but it's probably worth talking through all this with someone you trust. Are you having issues with class room management? Kids can smell weakness and will take advantage of it. If they seem to be doing what they're supposed to be doing most of the time, you're probably doing ok.

Also, don't worry about ruining someone's education. While a good teacher can inspire kids and change their lives in a positive direction, I would guess only a truly vindictive and malicious teacher can really damage a kid. Mr. Speiser sort of sucked as a high school English teacher, but the only consequence was that we were all bored for 5 hours a week.
