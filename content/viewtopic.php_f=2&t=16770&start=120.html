This discussion prompted me to look up Sanderson's timeline for releasing Stormlight #3. (His website is awesome and he gives constant updates about the status of what he's working on.) He's halfway done with his first draft and thinks he'll have a draft completed by the middle of the year. He estimates that rewrites, art, etc., will push the release into early 2017. But he's making up for that by writing a Stormlight novella that will be included in a short story collection later this year.

He discusses his progress a bit more in this reddit thread: https://www.reddit.com/r/Stormlight_Archive/comments/41r099/oathbringer_spoilers_stormlight_three_update_2/ (minor spoilers but not really)

Sanderson is great to his fans and has a prodigious work ethic. It's really nice to see a guy like him make it big.

jamiethekiller wrote:yeah, read that reddit thread when he first posted it. the books are absolutely ginormous so it doesn't bother me waiting longer than we would have liked. the 'getting 4 books for 1' is a good way to put it. dudes an animal with his writing abilities.

Not to mention that they're so loaded with worldbuilding details--the maps, sketches, the appendices, etc.--that you can get multiple readings of them and damn near feel like you're reading them for the first time, especially if you read all of his Cosmere works. For example, one of the interludes in Way of Kings (the Purelake) features worldhoppers*, one of whom is featured prominently in the Mistborn trilogy. I didn't pick up on his description until my second read of WoK, which came after my second full read of the Mistborn trilogy.




* Worldhoppers are individuals who have figured out how to travel between Cosmere worlds--Hoid/Wit is one.
