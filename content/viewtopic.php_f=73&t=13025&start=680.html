Wow, one week ago one of the members from my former congregation died. I know you are not supposed to speak ill of the dead, but the obituary is interesting- it speaks of him as having a "happy and positive outlook on life", and that he was "an inspiration to many."

The dude was a surly guy who fought with me about changing worship times, wouldn't come to church when he disagreed with things, always complained about my sermons because I actually talked about current events and tried to link scripture themes with ideas that could be applied to everyday life. The week the Virginia Tech shooting happened, I preached on not understanding the world's brokenness in light of that event, and he complained that "he heard about it all week, he didn't wan to hear about it in church." He pretty much hated me.

The last conversation I had with him was one of my last Sundays, when he turned to me and said, "I want to thank you for having the good sense to get out of here before you bankrupted the church. When we voted for you, I voted no!" That was the last thing he ever said to me.
