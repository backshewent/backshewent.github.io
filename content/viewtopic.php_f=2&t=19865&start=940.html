so what's the buzz in the US about this new coronavirus? The chinese are taking it VERY seriously. It appears to have originated in a Wuhan seafood market, making the jump from bats to snakes to humans (not confirmed yet, but that's the theory). They have temperature checkpoints and you can't get on a plane with a temperature. During the SARS outbreak, these checkpoints were even set up outside cities, limiting travel in and out of the cities and even rural areas.

I find this stuff pretty fascinating. These markets have tons of different animals in close contact and a simple mutation in one of the viruses can cause a jump from animal to animal or animal to humans. It doesn't help that the hygiene in the markets is often bad. Even the local supermarkets have very poor cleanliness. We used to go to one market when it first opened because the produce was good and cheap and we could get decent cuts of meat there. But now I have trouble even going to the market because the smell of rotting meat and fish is so strong. We still get some produce and processed food there, but now we get meat at the new Sam's club.

Anyway, I'm just wondering if it's on your radar yet. It's already made it's way out of China, so it will probably spread. I've heard we won't be allowed back into my school compound if we have a temperature. In other words, I won't be able to go home if I have a temperature, since that is where I live. I imagine I'll have to go to a clinic until it's confirmed I don't have the virus.
