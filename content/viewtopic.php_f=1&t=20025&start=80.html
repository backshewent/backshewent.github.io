Looking at these old Phillies games is fun, so many from 1993 up there. But as fun as that is, watching the 9th inning of some of these games, it's just a reminder of how maddening the closer situation really was. Looking at Mitch's line from that year, which I'd never really done before right now, he had a 1.613 WHIP - 1.613 as a fucking closer. I find Hector Neris to be basically intolerable and the highest WHIP he has ever had is 1.301. Mitch struck out 60 batters in 62 IP in 1993 - a respectable but not world-beating 8.7 K/9 for a closer - but also walked 44 batters, for a K/BB ratio of 1.36. For comparison's sake, Brad Lidge in 2008 struck out 92 in 69.1 IP, and walked 35, an 11.9 K/9 and 2.63 K/BB ratio.

And the splits - oh the splits. After the ASB, Mitch was effectively finished but somehow skated by so that not enough people - or at least one person - didn't notice. Yes, his second half ERA was less, by almost half a run, than his first half ERA (3.55 - 3.10). But his WHIP was an unsustainable 1.828. He struck out only one more batter than he walked (29/28).

And then the playoffs... close your eyes:

8 IP / 8 R / 7 ER / 8K / 6 BB / 11 H / 2.125 WHIP

The World Series is dramatically worse than the NLCS of course, but honestly although he got away with it he was 2-0 in the NLCS because he blew two fucking saves, the 1.69 ERA be hanged. His WHIP even in the NLCS was a not-good 1.5, helped enormously by his improbably 1-2-3 in Game 6.

Just for fun, I wondered how long it took Mariano Rivera to surrender 8 post season runs. The answer was 7 years, from his first postseason appearance in 1995 until 2001, when in the World Series he gave up 2 runs that got him to 9, the last of those being Luis Gonzalez's blooper over the drawn-in infield. That was 52 postseason appearances and 79 postseason innings to pass Mitch.

Ay carumba! They should have maybe given the job to Donn Pall or somebody like that at a certain point because it was CLEARLY a time bomb waiting to go off.
