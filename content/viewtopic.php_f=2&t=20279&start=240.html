Not sure if this belongs here or the politics thread but a really good place to start for police reform in PA is reforming Act 111 which has given police unions the uneven and unprecedented power they have.

https://www.inquirer.com/news/pennsylva ... 00619.html

Effectively, when negotiating contracts with municipalities there is a 30 day window of time. If a deal is not reached it goes to binding arbitration. 3 arbitrators are chosen. One by each side and a neutral one. The neutral one is never neutral. The municipality also has to pay for two of the tree arbitrators. The police union in theory is supposed to pay for their handpicked one but that person is on their staff already so it costs them nothing. The negotiations are never public either.

Municipalities basically just give the police union whatever they want because it is likely the results in arbitration will be worse and as mentioned above, expensive.

It seems to have bipartisan support because Republicans will do anything to dismantle any type of organized union.

We need to move to extending the window of time for negotiations, cost share the cost of arbitration equally, expand the pool of picking the neutral arbitrator from 3 people to 7, and make the negotiation open to the public. There are some other nuanced things in there that are important to change too. But if Act 111 is reformed it will give municipalities an equal footing in these processes which have led to immense power for police.
