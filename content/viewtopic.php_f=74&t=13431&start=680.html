SK790 wrote:
Houshphandzadeh wrote:that's terrible

what happens after you take half of someone's brain?

partial paralysis on one side of your body, some loss in motor function, and mild retardation. surprisingly, you can live a somewhat normal life, especially if done at a young age.

i feel vulgar even chiming in, but i can vouch for the fact that it's amazing what a young brain can do to re-structure (for lack of a better term) itself in order to function.

my son was born two months premature, and within the first 24 hours of life contracted e coli menangitis in the hospital. this led to incredible brain bleeds and long story short, we were told all kinds of terrible things to expect and that his brain was essentially "swiss cheese". we were told he'd be deaf, blind, and severely retarded. one particular blow was being told he would never laugh or even smile, which was devastating to us as parents as joking and laughter is a big part of our game. i'm happy to report going on 5 years later, virtually none of that came to fruition. mose jr has his issues, but is a highly functioning child who happens to have cerebral palsy. and we're treated like rock stars wherever we go because of his infectious smile and social skills.

i say all this only to pass on some advice to never listen to what doctors tell you. i can only imagine what the parents are going through, but again am certainly praying for brighter days ahead. stay strong.
