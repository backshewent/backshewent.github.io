Re: Fat, drunk, and RANDOM is no way to go through life

by JUburton » Sun May 17, 2015 14:21:57

Get someone that drives stick, go to car sense, have them drive it off the lot, go to an open lot and practice a little. I don't think it really does much, if any, damage. Honestly there's not a lot of 'learning'. You can get the basics down in 20 minutes. Once you know how to get it moving from start, changing gears is no real problem and after that it's just getting used to your clutch/transmission. Each car will be different.

Why do you need to learn? My first car was stick and I had about 10 minutes in a parking lot on a different car before that and then I just took my car around the neighborhood and went for it. Just don't get on any steep inclines to start out on since the car can roll. New cars may have hill assist though so you wouldn't have to worry about it.
