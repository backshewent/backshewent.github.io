by Soren » Mon Feb 15, 2016 16:45:42

They just announced a new reprint set full of cards that cost an absurd amount of money that haven't been printed in like ~15 years. A great way to lure people into a format called Legacy which, although it's stupid expensive, is probably the safest investment. Cards that cost $$$ in legacy tend to not lose too much value. Also, people actually play Legacy unlike Vintage.

[no one really cares]
There are a few things issues:
1) Early in the game's history they had no idea how good certain cards would be, so there are cards that are just disproportionately powerful, therefore desirable. Additionally, these cards being printed before the game took off means there are less of them. Additionally it's been a long time since they were printed so there's a decreasing supply.

2) At some point WoTC, the company that makes mtg shot themselves in the foot and decided they'd create a "restricted list" of cards they can never ever ever reprint in any way what so ever. So, again diminishing supplies + steadily increasing demand.

3) There is a relatively new format "Modern" with a restricted card set which WoTC has had a really hard time policing. So, if you aren't going to cynically suggest this reprint set is a cash grab it could be an attempt to lure players who are frustrated with Modern (because it sucks) into Legacy events.
[/no one really cares]
