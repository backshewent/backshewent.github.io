Re: Phillies thoughts of the random persuasion

by JFLNYC » Wed Sep 21, 2011 13:30:18

The article is either blatantly narrow-minded or (if indeed it was not meant to be taken seriously), a poor attempt at levity:

First, this:

Through last night's game, Hunter Pence has been with the Phillies for 49 games. Here's a simple comparison of the basic team metrics for the 49 games with Pence and the immediately preceding 49 games without Pence.

Code: Select all
Without    With
Runs Scored    226    225
Runs Allowed    154    150
Record    32-19    32-19


Now, I may not be a math whiz, but when I went to school, 32+19 was 51, not 49. So there may not be any "Bernie Madoff accounting," but there does seem to be a little shortchanging going on somewhere.

Second, you can't just look at the exact same period of time before the Pence trade as after it. You have to look at all the games the Phils played without Pence. Without him, they were 66-37, for a winning percentage of .641. With him -- in games played before they clinched the Division -- they were 32-15 for a winning percentage of .681.

Yes, I left out the last 4 games/losses because they weren't trying. There, I said it: "They weren't trying." I've no doubt each individual player was trying his best but, as a team, they weren't trying as hard. Charlie has used Savery, Schwimer, Bowker, Kratz, Moss, Gload, et al, in situations he never would have used them had the season been in doubt. I mean, do we really think Howard would have been rested and then gotten a cortisone shot if these last 4 games were "must win?"

I'll go even further, after such a grueling season, including this stretch of 33 games in 31 days, there's no doubt in my mind that certain regulars, subconsciously or not, eased off the gas pedal a bit. If you don't believe that professional athletes can give a bit more than even they thought they could in critical situations, read this article, published 2 days ago in the NYT: A Little Deception Helps Push Athletes to the Limit.

Third, if you're saying the Phils were just as good without Pence as with him then, logically, you're also saying the Phils would have been just as good without him as they were with him. Where's that analysis? Who would have gotten his AB's? Brown? Francisco? Mayberry? And, if it's Mayberry, who gets Mayberry's AB's?

Fourth, the Phils didn't get Pence just to help them get to the post-season. They also acquired him to help during the post-season. Among other benefits alluded to in the previous paragraph, the lineup is going to look a whole lot better in WS DH games when Pence is getting the AB's instead of Francisco or Gload.

And, finally, the Phils didn't get Pence only for this season. They got him for next year, too. Even if he leaves after 2012, he will have played ~220 regular-season games for the Phils (and who knows how many post-season games). Isn't it just a wee bit premature, if not selective, to judge the trade after the first 49 (51?) of those games?
