Re: Ceci n'est pas un random thread

by MrsVox » Thu Nov 15, 2018 13:57:46

CalvinBall wrote:They wouldn't even take me without insurance.

And yes, this is an outpatient surgery center. Didn't realize it was cheaper. So that's good to know. Also, it looks like there are a bunch of fees on top of the quoted amount they gave me which is just for the doctor. I don't think anesthesia or facility fees and stuff like that are included yet.


Yes, the fee your doctor office quoted is the physician cost. Anesthesia will be separate, as will the facility costs. You can and should contact each party to find out their cost, and also make sure the anesthesia service provider is in-network.

There shouldn't be "more" on top of what the doctor or facility charges, unless there are special circumstances requiring extra care, like, you're diabetic or asthmatic or have some other condition which makes the surgery more dangerous, and these things have to have a significant impact, it's usually things like, under 12 months, over 80, multiple systemic conditions.

Trying to not write a book here, but basically, the doctor charges $x for "surgery, R arm", and $x for "surgery, left arm", and is reimbursed $y for surgery, arm, times 2 (sometimes at a reduced cost for one of the surgeries, because they are happening on the same day). Included in that is the surgery prep, the supplies, the surgery itself, and follow-up visits in his office for X period, probably 90 days. Not included are things like x-rays, pre-admission testing, etc.
