So, if the Phillies do wind up going over the tax threshold next year, they will become the first NL team to go over the tax threshold. Right now, the only teams who have exceeded it are the Red Sox (5 times, most recently 2010*), Angels (once, 2004), Tigers (once, 2008), and Yankees (every year since it's inception).

Back in 2003, when the luxury tax was implemented, we were 9th in payroll at $95,338,704, $12,661,296 below the luxury tax threshold. Who woulda thought that ten years later we'd double the payroll and be the only team, other than the Yankees, paying the tax? Pretty crazy stuff.
