by phorever » Sat Jan 25, 2014 12:34:48

cshort wrote:Even if Howard does return to his 2010/2011 numbers, that's still not enough to compensate for the other holes in this lineup.


though i've become convinced that rube's stupidity will result in neutralizing the phillies positives this season, i still take exception to statements like this. it is not consistent with the most reasonable projections. i think rollins and howard are just about dead, but for ruiz, asche, brown, revere, byrd, and utley there are recent numbers that demonstrate that it is reasonable to expect >2.5war performance out of each. revere, 3war in 2012, looked on track for that when not hurt last year and isn't old. utley is utley. brown was a 3 win guy last year and isn't old. byrd is old and maybe a cheater, but put up 4war in 2013 post suspension and there is video evidence to support an successfully changed batting style as a reason to believe some of the improved hitting will stick. ruiz is declining and asche still evolving, but the projection systems like them both.

moreover,
cesar and freddy and ruf and rupp and frandsen are all good enough that they can deliver above-replacement performance when used just enough to keep the starters healthy and rested and take advantage of platoon splits. do that (instead of rubes keep-my-lineup-together-so-they-can-prove-me-right-unless-they-all-die-of-exhaustion-so-i-can-blame-it-on-bad-luck plan), and the odds are that performances of the regulars come in on the high side of the projections rather than below. that gives an average of 3 wins from every regular lineup position.

the roster problem heading into the season is pitching, not hitting. only two starting pitchers i feel comfortable with, and one of those is alarmingly old. two are cuban mysteries. all the younger sp options are coming off of injuries. paps is at least as dead as rollins/howard. adams is injured, bastardo comes off a drug suspension, and the rest of the pen consistently inconsistent, though i like their upside.

so, normally, i would be expecting the offense to deliver more war than the pitching this season on the way to around 80 wins, or couple more with optimal usage.

the other problem is that the aforementioned grind-down-the-proven-vets approach is almost certain to result in most of the regulars underperforming their projections.

but it really, really doesn't have to be that way. this offense could and should be a decent one, even though it probably won't be.
