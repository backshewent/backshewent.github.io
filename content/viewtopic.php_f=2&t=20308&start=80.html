by thephan » Tue Jul 28, 2020 09:20:06

Bucky wrote:there's no host component to google maps via chrome, is there? I get a newer satellite view from my PC at home than I get on the PC at the firehouse. Can't figure out why.


I got lost in the wordslaw. Are you asking if you can download GIS from GMaps for use at the firehouse? If so the answer is no. Google has agreements with the remote sensing vendors and they encapsulate the image in a layer that maintains copy right as best they can (i.e., what you can get is a static image that is fairly unusable, especially if you want GIS or image manipulation capabilities).

We tried to digitally watermark SpotImage sensor data years ago to maintain copyright control in image data served to Google via limited use license and Google broke it when they reprocessed the images for their wrapper. In the end Google roughed up the images enough that Spot was good the the copyright was being honored (and I had to go find a different source of project funding).

If you want street data, OpenStreets might work for you. You have to deal with ESRI and ARC, but there was some type of available data access in OpenGIS. Its been 5 or 6 years since I worked with that, so hopefully it has gotten better, but knowing ESRI/ARCGIS it likely has gotten worse (because it is a company that needs to make money).
