by thephan » Wed May 17, 2017 17:40:29

Rewinding to Chelsea Manning's release, and maybe this should be filed under unpopular opinions, but I am not a fan. She is painted as a whistle blower that released information with a clear interest by the American people. What she released was almost 500,000 files, more then 80% of which were file on Iraq, with the remainder being about Afghanistan, becoming known as the Iraq War logs,and the Afghan War logs. Much of it carried some classification. She took these documents to The Washington Post and The New York Times, but was rejected so sent the information to WikiLeaks, making the man that is Julian Asange. If we want to talk about impeachment for Trump over his loose lips, you have to think of the value of nearly a half million documents.

She was spared the treason charge, which could have carried death, perhaps still by firing squad. She was assigned to military prison and, in my opinion, unfairly placed in solitary confinement for nearly a year. There is a chance that this was for her safety, but there is an air of cruelty to that as well.

To me, her 7 years were not enough. I am sure that Trump would have done nothing, but the amount of data transmitted, and the effort taken to put it into the hands of anyone who would take it when respectful organizations would not is its own tell. It does not really pass the test of mishandling of data and theft to say that she served longer then anyone else, and that might not be true either because I doubt many of these cases see much day light.

So I am not lined up to raise a toast on justice served this day.

FWIW, I could care about her reassignment, except that it gives her some status with a disaffected community, and one instance should not cloud judgement of the other.
