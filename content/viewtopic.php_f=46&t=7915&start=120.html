by SideshowBob » Sun Mar 08, 2009 13:05:53

iladelph53 wrote:What's your beef against the World Baseball Classic? Baseball is the greatest sport in the world and it deserves an international tournament. This is exciting: the best players in the world playing simply for pride and patriotism.


Agreed. Baseball has long been missing a premier international tournament and it deserves one. It's a great sport playing in countries all over the world and it's a crying shame that we have only had minor tournaments like the Olympics and Baseball World Cup over the years (and we are losing the Olympics). The WBC is long overdue IMHO.

FTN wrote:Its still a farce. Its an exhibition with depleted rosters and pitch counts.


I'll agree that the competition is lacking somewhat in its structure, but this is harsh and untrue. "Exhibition"? Do you think the people competing aren't interesting in winning? Or that they aren't trying?

I actually think the biggest problem is that it isn't taken seriously enough. I find it really sad that top players are unwilling to "risk injury" -- and that even more the fans of baseball in this country support those decisions -- to play. What is really lacking in the WBC is that too many top players opt out. You don't see that happen with the FIFA World Cup. Playing in -- and winning -- the WBC should be the pinnacle a player's career, but it's not and not even close. I understand that this kind of stuff takes time, but I hope it gets there because baseball really has the potential to have an awesome national team tournament.

Grotewold wrote:
iladelph53 wrote:What's your beef against the World Baseball Classic?


The timing. It basically becomes the MLB players' Spring Training, so the pitchers can't go long and the hitters' timing isn't there. It's like a tournament of all star games, but worse

If they must do it, they should do is do it after the NLCS and ALCS end. The guys in the World Series couldn't play, but the pitchers from the other teams would be stretched out and able to threw a few full games. I know that it will never happen because of the TV conflicts and MLB and money, but it would be a lot better. Or just use minor leaguers


I think it's a good thought about how the timing could be improved. It's annoying seeing the pitchers on such strict pitch limits. Now, I do think it's possible to start spring training earlier in WBC years to help that (the Japan team started training in January, I think) or at least have a "national team" camp that starts early featuring the specific players on the team. Of course, that would cut into the vacation time of major leaguers, but I'd love to see it being such an honor some day where players would want to be involved.

But if you are using minor leaguers and not the best players in the world, what's the point? We already have the Baseball World Cup. Hmmm, you've never heard of it? Exactly. The whole point of the WBC -- and it's a void that should be filled -- is to have a competition featuring the best of the best.

As an aside -- but kind of related -- I really hope the MLB Network broadcasts some baseball from around the world. Stuff like the Japan and Korean leagues and the European Cup in baseball. They did the Carribean World Series which was cool. And I think the more they expose people to this stuff, the more Americans might recognize the global extent of the sport and become interested in the WBC. Or maybe not, we'll see.
