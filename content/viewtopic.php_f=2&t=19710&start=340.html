by JFLNYC » Mon Sep 02, 2019 06:43:31

TenuredVulture wrote:
JFLNYC wrote:Doesn’t the Declaration of Independence say something about inalienable rights bestowed by a Creator?



Declaration of Independence is not the Bible. It's a statement of enlightenment humanism, with reference to a creator (not God) reflected its deism.


“the separate and equal station to which the Laws of Nature and of Nature's God entitle them”

The point being, as JH alluded to, that the most fundamental rights of our form of government are bestowed upon us by a Creator/God (a distinction without a difference in this context) and it is the purpose of government to “secure these rights.” So, a reference by a Republican Congressman to “God-given right” does not represent some fringe view but is, in fact, quite a mainstream idea for our country and it’s government.

As uncomfortable as some of us may be (and I include myself in that number) with the notion of God in our government, we are wise to tread carefully on the subject. Without certain rights being bestowed upon us by a Higher Power and being, therefore, “unalienable” we would be left with our fundamental rights being determined and conferred upon us — or taken away — by other humans. You can decide yourself which is the more reliable source of our most fundamental rights.
