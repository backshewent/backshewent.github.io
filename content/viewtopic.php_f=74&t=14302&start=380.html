by TenuredVulture » Sat Dec 08, 2012 22:38:10

It depends in part on the speed limit of the road and the state and how heavy the traffic is. If the limit is 70, then 75 isn't too bad for the left lane and that probably holds for most interstates on the east coast, and it holds anywhere the traffic is fairly heavy. But in any event, you should move to the right if someone comes up behind you. But in places like Texas and in much of west, you're probably better off going closer to 85 if you want to stay in the left lane.

However, in general, you should travel in the right lane and use the left lane for passing. If the traffic is heavy enough that the right lane just isn't open enough to make that practical, then you really probably should stay 5 or so miles over the speed limit.
