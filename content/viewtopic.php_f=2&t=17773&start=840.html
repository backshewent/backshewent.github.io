The speech was as I expected. Some red meat for the base and reinforcement of objectives. It was lighter in substance than I had hoped, but there will never be any substance from this administration directly as its leader works in 140 characters and sound bites. Because he stayed reigned in, I'd give him an A for effort, and a C for execution with a D- for usefulness.

I liked Ryan laugh about the wall behind him. There is no way to manage debt, take on audacious projects (loved how it was said that the infrastructure was left to rot in the past w/o recognizing that was via his party's obstructions), and cut taxes for everyone (recall that businesses are people too now).

The first speech out of the gate is always more reinforcement of message, and that is what you got. It was nothing great. It failed to meet the build up and chorus of striving for 'unity' because it was colored with some of his ugly campaign rhetoric that got a fresh coat of paint. It is important to see what happens next to include an actual investigation in to the Russia affair, that battle over entitlements (insurance for everyone on the cheap whereas the speaker called said that insurance is an entitlement on the block), promises of job and economic growth (not that fact bending he touted), and a looming showdown over what the military realistically needs (there are needs, but the focus over big projects to put a name on versus replacement parts to keep our forces ready will be interesting).

Buckle up.
