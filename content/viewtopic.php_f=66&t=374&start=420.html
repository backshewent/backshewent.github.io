by momadance » Tue Mar 06, 2007 13:37:08

It probably vectored NW. Planes never fly in direct route. Depending in the traffic at the ZLC ARTCC, it was probably routed to a different airway or sector. It also depends on the gate and current configuration of the destination airport. If SLC was using a north configuration then the aircraft from Boise would have to vector NW. SLC only lands North/South.

Since Boise and SLC share the same ARTCC (Center Controllers), arrivals from Boise were probably assigned to a specific arrival gate to accommodate the current active runways (not an airport gate) that needed a NW turn.

Also, since SLC uses a system called time-based metering, they'll have aircrafts make random turns to eat up time. If the plane is ahead of its scheduled time of arrival and sometimes its ETA, the controllers will start vectoring the aircraft to kill some time.

The on-line flight trackers get their data from the FAA's host system, so its fairly accurate.

I designed a good amount of the software that controls the arrival flights into SLC.
