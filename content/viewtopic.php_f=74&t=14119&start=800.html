that reminds me of an aggravating situation I wanted to rant about. Was at the movies with The Kid. Of course, as soon as the movie starts he wants popcorn. So I go out and there's two lines- both pretty crowded. I pick the shorter of the two. In that line, there's four young adults- early 20s, I'd say- all standing side by side. My mental line calculator counted them for one, maybe two people.

Then it's their turn. They order ONE BY ONE. EACH ONE OF THEM GETS THE SAME EXACT THING- a small popcorn and a small soda. And each any every one of them pays with cash. Which they don't start retrieving until the clerk tells them their total, which, if you've been following, IS THE EXACT SAME PRICE FOR EACH ONE OF THEM. So the clerk tells them "$8.60" or whatever it was. At this point each and every one of them opens up their purse, or gets out their wallet and starts hunting through their pocket for change. IT WAS MADDENING as I watched the other line move along. HEY YOU'RE ADULTS- WHY DON'T YOU ORDER A COUPLE LARGE POPCORNS AND ALL YOUR SODAS TOGETHER AND ONE PERSON PAYS AND EVERYONE GIVES THEM MONEY. If you're familiar with movie theater prices, one large is about 3x the size of a small and about two bucks more. I was ready to strangle.
