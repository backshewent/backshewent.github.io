Brooks has one of his thoughtful pieces for Inauguration Day.

As a guy who's fundamentally a conservative but understood what happened to his party during the Bush/DeLay era and might have voted for Obama, Brooks should be interesting to watch through the next year or two. It's becoming clear that he honestly wants Obama to do well, but retains the skepticism that you'd expect (and want, IMO) a conservative to have about the big government moves that are on the way.

The premise that really changing the tone has to precede huge changes and painful choices seems sound to me. Whether Obama even gets to try some of those things like entitlement reform probably depends on how little political blood is on the floor after he's done taking on the current problems, and how successful he is in all that.
