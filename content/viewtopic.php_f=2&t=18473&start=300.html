Less weird if you make contact before knocking on the door.

I drive by the house I grew up in every time I go up home but never thought to knock on the door. The asbestos singles are going strong almost 60 years after dad built the house!


My grandfather used to own something that was once called The Sleepy Hollow Ranch in Quakertown, PA. It was a bit of a thing, look it up. He owned it after its days as an entertainment venue were over, but people used to stop by all the time and ask to look around, because they met their wife there, or proposed to their wife there, etc. He, being a showboat (not unlike myself), loved this, and would give grandiose tours of the place.

I have always had very fond memories of the place and my current job is one where I often knock on strangers’ doors for a living. Therefore, a few years ago, when I found myself headed up that way to have lunch with a friend, I decided to look up where it was and discovered it was just a mile down the road from where I was. I went, knocked, and the people could not have been lovelier, gave me a little tour and everything.

Do it.
