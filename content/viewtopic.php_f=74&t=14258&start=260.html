pacino wrote:Then why are the 6 and 7 abominations if either party can win?

also, those were different districts thant hey are now

If PA-06, 07 and 16 weren't a jumbled mess, Democrats would regularly win another in the Philly suburbs, while the remaining two districts would be a lot safer for Republicans. All five of the Republican held districts in the southeastern part of the state, as drawn, are competitive for Democrats in a decent year with good candidates (especially if one of the Republicans were to retire). But they should favor Republican incumbents in nonwave years, which is a huge built in advantage. Redistricting/gerrymandering absolutely gives Republicans more seats from PA than they would otherwise have won this time around, but the point I'm trying to get at is because of the geographic concentration of Democratic strength in the state, the GOP would overperform its statewide vote share in seats even if the lines were drawn without regard for partisan politics. I think PBP's guestimate of 11-7 is probably fair.
