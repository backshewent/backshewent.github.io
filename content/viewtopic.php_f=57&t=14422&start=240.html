Re: Phillies sign Delmon Young

by dajafi » Wed Jan 23, 2013 11:20:49

Squire wrote:Plus, Bourn still costs you the 1st round draft pick.


This. Plus the weaknesses in his game--his power is slightly better than Revere's and his strikeout rate is slightly better than Howard's--are more easily overlooked in January than they would be in July.

But the draft pick is the big thing. I love how so many of you deplore Amaro for having a too-old, too-expensive team, then whine when he doesn't sign someone on the wrong side of 30 to an eight-figure deal that also would cost the highest pick they've had in more than a decade. The last two times the Phillies drafted in this neighborhood, they got Hamels and Utley.

There are no great options right now for the Phillies. The seeds of this problem were planted when they whiffed on Savery and Hewitt half a decade back, and signed Howard to that abomination of a contract. I don't love having Delmon Young on my favorite team either, and Amaro's evident disregard for on-base percentage is baffling and infuriating. But this move is most likely to be a non-story, and next-most likely to be a net win. When you have an old, expensive, injury-prone team and some really strong opposition, you need to catch some breaks. There's more possibility of an upside break with Young IMO than with any other viable option they had.

to be honest, this draft looks pretty weak.

i appreciate the value of the first round pick in a vacuum, however, i think you have to look at all factors there. this is not an elite college pool draft. the phillies pick 16th. that is their highest pick in a while. but the last time they picked close to that spot (2007, 19th) they whiffed on savery. outside of the top 6-8 picks, you're taking riskier guys or guys with lower ceilings and higher probabilities. losing the pick also removes that money from the signing pool, which is the bigger downside than losing the actual 16th overall pick.

if you can get bourn for 3 years and 30m, isnt that something you explore? he was a 6 win player last year, and a bit more than a 4 win player the two years prior.

if you get 5, 4, and 3.5 WAR from him over the next 3 seasons, that's about $50m of value, and a huge win. no one here wants him for 5 or 6 years or for 18m per year. but when you start getting in to shorter deals for affordable money, then the pros start to outweigh the cons.

bourn in cf and revere in rf also gives us elite defense in 2 of 3 spots and might help mitigate the bad defense in lf, especially considering our short left field and the butchers we've run out there in recent years.
