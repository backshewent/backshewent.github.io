Yea, it does have a scale if they're using PCR tests. Depends on the test they're using, but CDC guidance is to cycle the swab 40 times.

Some machines can spit out at which cycle the swab came back positive. If it comes back 1-25, then you have a bad infection and are contagious. If its 35 or higher then you aren't contagious and don't have an infection any more. Just that they found dead virus in you.

https://science.sciencemag.org/content/370/6512/22.full

https://www.nytimes.com/2020/08/29/heal ... sting.html

Edit: your body can shed dead virus for 90 days after contracting the virus. So just looking at a positive test or not doesn't tell you much, but the amount it cycles does. That said, I doubt he has any clue what he's talking about.
