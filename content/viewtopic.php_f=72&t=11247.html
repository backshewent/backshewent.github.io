by cshort » Fri Sep 17, 2010 10:25:02

Watched Morning Joe this morning, where they were discussing O'Donnell's debate last night. Consensus was that she seemed to carry herself well, and is actually better than Palin now on camera, since Palin is going overboard with all the "don'tcha knows" etc. Eugene Robinson of the Washington Post commented that the Democratic Party would be foolish to take her lightly ("whistling past the graveyard").

On the one clip they showed of the debate, it looks like she's received some coaching - Coons questioned her about her extreme views (especially from the comments in the 90's). She handled it well, chuckling and chalking it up to youthful enthusiasm, and stating her intention to vote based on the Constitution and not her personal beliefs. I didn't see the entire debate, so I don't know how she did on more substantive issues. She certainly looked a lot more comfortable than Coons in front of the camera, and that will certainly help with some voters. Not saying she's going to win, but it may be closer than people think, especially with the funding she's getting now (close to $1 million in the last day or so).
