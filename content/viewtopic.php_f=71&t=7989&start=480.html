Being in your 20's sucks. No matter who you are or what you are it just plain sucks I think. If you are married you see your single friends and their seemingly endless freedom and one night stands or casual dating and you remember back to the days not so long ago when you yourself were doing that and you think it kind of sucks not doing that anymore and it's all you want. Then you have your friends that are still in that phase of their life, looking at you and wanting that stability in their life, that commitment and everything that goes along with it. Basically I'm stating the old grass in greener argument, but in this case, and I know sometimes I think longingly back on my teens and early 20's dumbassery and miss it, the married life IS better for you in the long run (of course this is generalization, sometimes a marriage is just not gonna work).

I don't really know where I'm going here, but if I'm allowed to give some advice here to MCM, take a step back from your relationship for a moment, and what I mean is, try to look at the forest instead of the trees. You might be in a rough spot which is fixable, but it's hard to see it because you only see these dead ugly trees around you with sticker bushes all around the base. Try to look through that, see if there is a nice, lush rain forest beyond the ugly and painful stuff you have surrounding you. It might make it easier, or at least possible for you to wade through the dense sticker bushes that are your relationship now if you can see the "paradise" that awaits you on the other side.
