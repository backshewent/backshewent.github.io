Barry Jive wrote:Dan Duquette signed a South Korean high schooler and got the Orioles banned from scouting in South Korea



yup, DD screwed the pooch on this one. Basically, KBO/MLB have an agreement where if a MLB team wants to ask about a player (pro or amateur), they gotta get MLB to officially ask KBO about the status of the player. O's didn't do this and signed Kim Seong-min anyways. (from what I've been told from baseball guys in Korea and heard, he wasn't about to get signed by any other MLB team...)

KBO is using this violation by the O's to protect future prospects leaving. KBO's rule is similar to MLB's rule when it comes to high schoolers, they can't be signed/drafted until they graduate. But since Korea is not a US Territory, MLB's international signing age of 16 applies.

I have more about this on my site, but yeah...O's messed up, KBO is now going to extreme lengths to protect their country's players and now O's are banned from Korea for now....
