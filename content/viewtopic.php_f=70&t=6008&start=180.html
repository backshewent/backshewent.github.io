I think this would be a mistake for McCain, as Romney's particularly egregious flip-flopping tarnishes McCain's straight-talk brand. (The brand is BS, but his media fan club will never tell...) Huckabee's line about The Mittster from the primaries--"people don't want to vote for someone who reminds them of their boss"--would seem to retain some relevance. And of course there's the Mormon thing. I don't know if that would tilt any states toward Obama, but I could see it further closing the "God gap."

I don't think his religion is an issue for most Republicans. It wasn't in the primary. The people who might hold it against him voted for him in swing states. It might be a problem in the south, but those people already aren't excited by McCain. He won't help, but probably won't hurt. Do you really think that independents will be discouraged about McCain because Romney is a Mormon?

Romney reminding people of the boss should help McCain with fiscal conservatives. A lot of Republicans dislike McCain's stance on immigration. They might think that having Romney's voice in the room would temper McCain's guest worker ideas.

Romney doesn't address McCain's biggest need, IMO. He's going to win if he can increase his share of independents, not by going after the "base." Going after the "base" could hurt him with independents. Going after independents won't make the "base" any angrier than they are now.
