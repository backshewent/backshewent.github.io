by Werthless » Tue Jun 23, 2020 00:07:04

If it's one of those businesses where I don't interact with the owner, I'm struggling to understand the principle/rule that tells me this makes sense. Are Hispanic business owners better off in the hierarchy here? How about non native English speakers? How about low income businesses?

Secondly, did the newspaper ask permission of each business? Verify? I could see businesses with multiple partners having reasons to claim black ownership, or not claim ownership, so taking that choice away from the business seems off. What if a business was vandalized after the publication? What if it's a racist area, would the publication of the list then be irresponsible? What if the publication of the business leads to vandalism? From my perspective, having a newspaper publish a list of black-owned businesses is something that surprised me. Even from a liability standpoint, I'm somewhat surprised they'd be willing to publish a list. Would we support a list of Jewish owned businesses being published? Maybe that already exists. How about a directory of businesses by race and cover all our bases?

I don't know what the answer is, obviously, but I find that asking the same question in different ways is producing different answers in my head. And that leaves me struggling to understand whether I think it's a good idea. I realize it's an unpopular opinion, but I thought I'd share anyway.
