by CalvinBall » Wed Oct 16, 2019 14:42:23

Friend who I did the bike trip with had his bike (from the trip) stolen from outside his office on Sunday. Through a friend of a friend we saw it posted on the app Let Go.

My friend was able to get this guy to meet under the guise of wanting to buy it. It was all incredibly amateur on the thief's part. Post d it three times on the app under three different listings, all at different prices, and he had no idea how much the bike actually cost.

Was supposed to meet at 8 this morning in a public market in DC. Around 9 a few out of place black teenagers show up. The guy selling the bike still hasn't come. Keeps saying he is on the train and getting close. The group sits down at a table. My friend moves to the opposite end of the market.

Kid finally arrives. My friend offers him 300. Kid says no we agreed to 400. They go back and forth. My friend asks where he got it. Kid says selling it for a friend, and that's when he tells him that it is actually his bike and he knows the kid is full of shit. Shows him the receipt and a photo with the bike.

At that point the kid motions to the table and his buddies get up and walk towards them.

Friend ends up getting him down to 320. Hands him 300. Kid says hold up let me count it. You trying to play me, the kid says. Friend hands him another 20 and rides away.

Pretty frustrating and potentially dangerous encounter. He had the cops there but the kid was so late the cops left. He just wanted his bike back. Sucks to pay 300. He thought about asking to take it for a test ride and then ride off but had no idea if they had weapons not anything. With them being complete amateurs who knows if they could have just gone off and stabbed him or fired a gun. Wild stuff.
