Whether for Mitt Romney or anyone else, this is really a terrible measure of whether someone is "good" or not. Sure, you are BAD if you treat your family like garbage, there is no question about it, but this is a little like that old Chris Rock routine: you are SUPPOSED to love your family. Why should you get any credit for it? On a psychological level (and on a very practical level, for ultra-rich people like Romney who are worried about passing on fortunes and doing whatever they can to avoid the tax man), the family is really, at bottom, the extension of the self. Loving one's family is, therefore, little more than loving oneself, and not a mark of any tremendous character. The ABSENCE of love for one's family, or (and especially) any active nasty behavior directed at one's family, can certainly be used, quite reasonably and quite fairly, as a mark of someone's lack of character; but love of family is no mark of true human kindness or morality. How you treat and think of people whom you don't know - who mean nothing to you - is a much better guide to what kind of person you are.
