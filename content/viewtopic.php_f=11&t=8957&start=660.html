by dajafi » Tue Jul 28, 2009 12:50:35

Just as an exercise, I think it would be interesting to negotiate this way:

--JP starts by naming a Phillie he wants among any big-league rookie and prospect in the system.

--Ruben then picks among the same pool one player whom the Jays can't have.

--JP goes again

--Ruben goes again

This continues until Toronto has four players in a package for Halladay, or five if it's Halladay plus another useful part. (In this variant, maybe Ruben gets to protect two or three more guys before JP picks the fifth player.) A variant might be that each GM gets to select two at a time.

For one thing, this might reveal if the two teams see eye-to-eye in terms of valuing the Phils' prospects. For me, the top eight go: Drabek, Brown, Taylor, Happ, Marson, Carrasco, D'Arnaud, Gose. But presumably the Jays' needs would leave us (at least) one of the two catchers, and they might put Donald in there.

Almost certainly in this scenario we'd lose Drabek or Brown, but get to keep the other. And at this point that's all I'm focusing on--save one of those two guys, and get Halladay.
