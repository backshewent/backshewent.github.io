My grandmom would have been 74 today (5/6). She died 12/1/04. Lung cancer- by the time they found it, it had already spread to her spine. It was so painful- she suffered so much.

Everybody knew she had no chance yet her doc at the U of Penn sold her 3 rounds of chemo and turned her into a skeleton. I wonder how much they charged her insurance for that. It's so sad to know doctors have to be salesmen at the same time. And for what? Why couldn't they let her die with some dignity? What did chemo do- extend her life and excruciating pain for a few extra months? My parents had booked a trip to Florida a year prior to finding all of this out. My mom was going to cancel but the doc told her to go to relieve some stress and assured her she would be OK and that she had 4-8 months to live. As it turns outs she died 4 days later so I guess he was a little off. We had to call them on the third day and tell them to take the first flight home. As soon as my mom walked into the room, my grandmom opened her eyes looked at her, and took her last breath. It was like she was waiting for her- I know it sounds crazy but I witnessed it with my own 2 eyes. Her dogs in the their pens began howling out of nowhere for at least 10 minutes. I know it sounds crazy but I witnessed it with my own 2 ears. Some pretty crazy coincidences.

Nobody deserves to suffer like that but my grandmom was a saint and such a free spirit. What other grandmom do you know that would call her grandson at 11:00 on a Tuesday night and ask if he wanted to go to Geno's or down the shore to the casinos? That's just how she was. And if we did go to Geno's she would end up buying all of the homeless people a samich as well. She was a typical South Philly Italian with her big black Caddy and all kinds of bling. She bailed me out every time I got in trouble and she always took my side no matter what. If it was because of a fight she would say, "Well, why did he bite your hand?" and then look at me and give me a little wink. Every time I got kicked out of my house she took me in and mediated the situation. I could call her at 2:00 and tell her I could go for steak and shrimp- she would curse me out in Italian and tell me she didn't feel like going to the store. An hour later she would call me and say, "Be here by 6:00 and bring some of your friends." Every Sunday she made a big pot of gravy- the best macaroni and meatballs you ever tasted. 6 or 7 of my friends always came over to eat and watch football- that was always the recovery day for us after partying from Thursday to Saturday. She even took in 3 Vietnamese kids/young adult immigrants that had nowhere else to go and put a roof over their heads, clothed them, fed them...etc... until they could get on their feet. All of her 8 brothers and sisters and their kids always came to her house for any holiday like Memorial Day (Even though my grandmom lived in Jersey, the rest of my family still lives in South Philly- I literally have ~75 cousins in a 20 block radius). FYI People from South Philly act like they have never seen water before when you have a pool- they go buck wild. I guess you can say she was the patriarch of the family. She always helped everybody. She didn't deserve to suffer like that. I'm just glad she saw me get married and I was able to dance with her on my wedding day. I'll always have that. They didn't find the cancer until 6 months later.

Anyway I'm just rambling now. Happy Birthday even though you're dead.
