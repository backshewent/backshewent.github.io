No. Absolutely not. Speaking as a father of a girl who wrestled in middle school and of a boy who currently wrestles on his high school team.

The best possible solution, of course, would be to give the girls the opportunity to wrestle on girls teams, but throughout large parts of the country this is simply not possible at this time. Some places it is-I took my daughter to an invitational in north Jersey and it was something to see the hundreds of girls who turned out, most currently wrestling for clubs and schools in the area. But in the south Jersey area the only option was to join boys teams, a surprising number of which had one or two girls, all of whom wrestled without incident.

Should we also ban homosexual guys from wrestling, since, y'know, they might get excited? It's a sport, it has rules, and this situation has been handled thousands of times without any problem. The people who have a problem with it aren't familiar enough with it and let their imaginations get the best of them. As for the "boys will be traumatized by losing to girls" argument, competition between sexes is going to take place throughout life and the sooner kids learn to deal with it the better. Wrestling's a tough sport-it's always just you out there alone against your opponent. But if you're fixated on who that opponent is, you're halfway to losing the match. Learning to do your best whatever the circumstances is one of the great lessons of the sport.

OTOH, I haven't actually read the article, so there may be external circumstances I haven't considered. I reserve the right to withdraw any of my opinions in this particular case based on any inconvenient facts.
