so if someone is in chestnut hill hospital and their condition needs more specialized care, they sometimes transfer them to Temple Hospital. (I have no idea what condition(s) dictate this). If the condition is considered grave enough, they transport via helicopter. the thing his, CHH has no heliport. so they dispatch whitemarsh ambulance to drive to chestnut hill hospital, load up the patient, transport the patient via ambulance to miles park in lafayette hill, where the helicopter has been dispatched to to be on the ground waiting the arrival of the ambulance. they then load the patient from the ambulance to the helicopter for the flight to temple hospital.

from CHH to TUH is 7.2 miles by road. it's 3.2 miles from CHH to miles park. it usually takes about 5-10 minutes for the handoff from the amberlamps to the heliochopper. Now if it's rush hour, i think maybe some time may be save (even lights & siren gets you stuck in traffic at rush hour). but over the last couple weeks we've landed helicopters at miles park IN THE MIDDLE OF THE FREAKIN' NIGHT! Google says by car it will take 22 minutes to get from CHH to TUH at 1AM, whileit take 7 minutes to get from CHH to miles. So, considering the time it takes for transfer, and likely the longer time it takes to get the patient from the roof of TUH to wherever they need to go, I'm pretty convinced it takes LONGER to use the helicopter route. OK, even if there is some time savings, it has to be very minimal.

bottom line, i think there's big bucks in medevac transports, and they will be milked for all they're worth.
