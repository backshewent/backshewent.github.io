stevemc wrote:How is Huff an everyday player and why him especially considering the amount of LH OF bats we already have?

It's true that Huff doesn't meet the preferred RH requirements for filling Burrell's shoes, but in every other way he's pretty similar and demonstrably better than either Jenkins or Stairs. He has a split, but is a .280 hitter against LHP, better than what either of the other two can do. In reality, a steady LH bat in RF to hit in the 6th spot would balance the order nicely and allow you to put Victorino back up top with Werth sliding into Burrell's position and batting slot.

Concerns about a glut could be alleviated during ST when teams looking to beef up their bench will be calling about Stairs for sure
