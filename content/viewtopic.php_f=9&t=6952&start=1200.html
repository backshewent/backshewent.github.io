I wanted to get something in here about Ryan Howard that apparently isn’t coming through in my writing this week. Howard should be platooned, because his combination of offense and defense at first base when a lefty pitches is basically replacment level. And the reporting around his performance in the postseason has a huge hole in it; he was hitting righties just fine and lefties not at all when he came to the plate midway through Saturday’s game. He simply wasn’t getting any chance to affect a game by facing righthanders.

I keep harping on this point because there is no more dangerous situation in this series, perhaps in the NL, than Ryan Howard with a right-handed pitcher on the mound. I’m killing Maddon for his decisionmaking precisely because I have so much respect for Howard’s ability to destroy righties. He’s a limited player in that he doesn’t do a lot of things baseball players can do to help their team win, but what he does do-hit for power and draw walks against righties-is very valuable. Not “Most” valuable, not by a long shot, but enough so that not taking that opportunity away from him whenever you can is probably the biggest mistake you can make against the Phillies.

Ryan Howard kills righties. That doesn’t mean he does anything else well. The gap between those two notions is where we’ve lived this week.
