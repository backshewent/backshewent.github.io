Bill McNeal wrote:Saw this on reddit. 10 minutes of the best calls in Phillies history. I enjoyed it.



Striking to me how much the Franzke call on the J-Roll walk off in the 2009 NLDS lands for me as the best of the group. I remember someone complaining about it at the time, and my response then rings perhaps even truer to me today: it starts off relatively-low key, because I am not sure how immediately clear the implications of the hit were to Franzke, but his excitement builds throughout the call, reaching a real crescendo that lasts and lasts at the end. Because of this, it still feels as exciting now as it did then. I was fist pumping listening, cheering J-Roll around those bases, gleefully waiting for the throw home that never came. Awesome.

Missing from that group IMHO:

- Scott Graham on the David Bell HR in Cincinnati in 2005. It turned out not to matter so much as the Phillies missed the postseason, but in the moment that game was critical and that call was absolutely electric. They had been up, I think 6-1, their 2005 pitching coughed that up so they were down 10-6 in the 9th, Utley hit a 3 run bomb to make the comeback possible, and with two outs, "David Bell did it!" Amazing. I have posted that call here on occasion.

- In the 1986-produced "Phillies Centennial" tape, there is footage of Bo Diaz's ultimate slam from 83 - bases loaded, down three, two outs. Kalas goes absolutely ballistic. Here it is:



Slide to 1:25.
"I'm in a bar with the games sound turned off and that Cespedes home run still sounded like inevitability."
