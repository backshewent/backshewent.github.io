by jerseyhoya » Tue Jan 31, 2012 02:13:06

The American economy has generally boomed out of downturns, but we have not on this occasion. The tepid growth the economy has experienced over the past year+ has not been nearly enough to repair the damage inflicted upon it by the recession. The extent to which this is Obama's fault is limited, just as the original recession wasn't much Bush's fault. But he's in charge and people tend to hold the president accountable for economic issues regardless of his direct impact on them. If you think the fact that the economy is barely treading water means the GOP doesn't have an argument to make against Obama this fall, more power to you. But you're wrong.

If the growth in jobs from Nov to Dec becomes a trend and we see a string of months over 200k new jobs and GDP growth becoming stronger soon, that changes the narrative, and Obama will win reelection. I don't think that's all that likely, although I guess it might happen. The country seeing actual improvement wouldn't be the worst outcome.
