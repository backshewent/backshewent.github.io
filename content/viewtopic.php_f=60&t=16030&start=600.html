by rolex » Fri Nov 21, 2014 17:36:53

CFP wrote:That appears to be Hextall's first mistake.

Maybe not exactly a mistake in the long run.
I'm down on Hextall for other things than the Hartnell trade. (1) The roster that he constructed looked weak with the bottom six forwards and that has proven to be true. VdeV and Akeson stand out as guys that don't belong here. (2) The defense looks too slow and unable to handle the puck either on the stick or passing it. There has to be a way to put together a defense where both Schenn and Grossmann are on the blue line for a game. One of those guys per game is enough. (4) IMO Ghost deserved more of a shot than he got. Was he up to NHL standards? Not in all aspects of his game but in some ways he had skills that the club needs. He certainly didn't deserve having the GM stating to the press that he was going to watch him like a hawk for his deficiencies. (5) Emery is not good enough to be a NHL back up goal tender. His lack of lateral movement is a killer. I admire his heart but his hips say no.
(6) Berube is not skilled enough as a coach to be the head guy. He gets schooled constantly by other coaches-ex. Vigneault on Wed. nite. The Rags knew every tendency that the team had and took them apart with that knowledge. Berube had not counter to anything. This happens too often.
Obviously Hextall knows all about the roster deficiencies. Hopefully he will be able to make moves to help clean that up. Also the way that he has discussed Laughton's call up with the press shows that he knows that he was to harsh with Ghost. I suspect that he'd like a re-do with the entire situation.
After working with Sutter in LA, Hextall knows good coaching when he sees it. When he is incited to go ape on his players in the locker room after a game, questioning the capabilities of your coach has to have entered Hextall's mind. He'll probably move Berube to another position; better sooner than later.
