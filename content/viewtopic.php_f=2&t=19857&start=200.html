I believed then, and now, in low taxes and smaller government. Maybe it's always been a ruse to get "low taxes for billionaires" and it's just more obvious under Trump.

I believed then, and now, in open trade. That has been a GOP issue, even though Bill Clinton co-opted it with NAFTA and got it done with a GOP congress. I still recall my glee in seeing Al Gore destroy Ross Perot in a televised NAFTA debate. You don't create American jobs by killing trade. Trump is completely against free trade, and somehow Hillary is the one who got it right (even though she couldn't say it too loudly).

I believed then, and now, in government policies that encourage self-sufficiency over policies that encourage reliance on a government subsidy. I felt that the GOP was, in its heart, people like Evan McMullin, Nicole Wallace, Max Boot - people of principle. What Trump has done is reveal that 90% of Republicans are simply greedy racists, and I'm pretty sad about that, and no longer a Republican.

I can't envision a GOP that will ever have me as a member again, but I'd like to see a movement where fiscal conservatives align with social libruls, to reign in some of the excesses of the well-meaning but financially clueless Democrats.
