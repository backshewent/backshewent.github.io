First of all, I don't care for Sixers ownership. I fully resent their caving to the league on Hinkie and accepting Jerry Col rammed down their throats. Then, the nepotism of his son, who I never wanted here - do not think is the right guy for the job - nor an elite GM.

Add in now, that what would this do to team locker-room and overall culture and confidence in the org?

I realize it's being played out partly in the public arena. There are legal and financial layers to it all. But I just hope that it ends with him gone - and someone better qualified to massage this team in to a true title contender.

I was totally anti-lebron before - but I totally have changed my mind. If that can get it done, get it done. Lakers stole Kareem, Wilt and Shaw to win titles. Durant was OKC core - and now winning rings for GS. F it. Lebron went to Miami and did it. Why should I care. Will it be a circus? Yes. Can he hold up many more years? Not likely. But even 1 title would be a start. I don't think it will happen, I don't think he will select Phila., and I'm not sure he has it in him to bail on his home town again... but I'm good.

In the current NBA you have to be able to rain down some 3's. I just don't see a lot of low-post game anymore. That said, I'd like to Joel get better with the inside moves and use his size inside - it will open up a lot of stuff and if he gets more Moses/Olajuwon type moves - he'll be very difficult for teams to stop and the Sixers can control pacing too. I liked this team this year. I liked the end of the season run, and the playoffs and despite Ben having zero shot - it was fun. They have some nice pieces but they need more.

Frustrating how things played out with Fulz but he was what, 19? No way to know that young except in rare cases it they will be special. In theory the game he was supposed to have, we need - but man that kid on Celtics was a beast as a rook.

Let's get it right and assemble the right talent - the culture really got good this year. Get rid of the toxic GM.
