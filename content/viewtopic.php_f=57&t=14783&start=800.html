chiming in late on a couple of conversations...
on amaro:
if not for the howard gaffe, he would get a b grade from me. bad luck with health in the 2009 series makes it too easy to discount his amazing acquisitions of lee and pedro that year. charlie running halladay, rollins, and utley into the ground before the playoffs makes it easy to discounts the great value rubes got for the victorino, werth, and ruiz contracts and the cleverness of the lee-halladay-oswalt-lee sequence, all of which should have made 2010-13 better than they were. yeah, some of the disappointment came not only from a unnecessarily worn down core, but also from the chain reaction consequences of raj´s extra years for ibanez and polonco and the weak trade of lee. still, the non-howard positives from ruben outweight the negatives, and the howard effect only hit in 2012. once it became clear that halladay and utley were near their expiration dates, he started retooling, and i think he´s done a pretty good job so far. he could still end up failing (the longer evil remains on the team the lower the grade, and making the right call on a papelbon trade is huge), but he´s well positioned to both dump salary and fill holes with trades.

on asche:
i think he´ll be a solid mlb regular soon, but he needs to have at least to the trade deadline to get more reps at aaa. if the phils aren´t legit contenders at the deadline, young should go as well as papelbon, so asche can go through his mlb growing pains in a meaningless august-september. if he flops, fransden and galvis can hold the fort. if the phils are in a playoff race, they won´t trade paps till the offseason and young is probably better than asche this year so its a good excuse to hold asche back till september.
