by Wolfgang622 » Tue Aug 05, 2008 15:54:47

dajafi wrote:The hell of it is that the "WWRJD?" poster probably was designed by a lefty, and meant ironically.


Yeah, that's a good point. Whatever, I don't care that much, but there is a world of difference between wacky left teenagers looking to find themselves and wacky right teenagers doing the same. The central tenets of the most lefty-hippy types generally promote peace (to the point of embarassing naievete) and a sense of "social justice" wherein everybody has enough and nobody too much. While some may find these ideas overly flowery or unrealistic or whatever, they are generally indicative of a heart that is in the right place. Even revolutionary figures like Che Guevara and Mao Tse-tung really represent the idea of a society where nobody has too much before everyone has enough for most kids. As soon as they actually bother to find out exactly what kind of violence these people helped to perpetrate, most people in this category get a little less enamored of their heroes.

"Slavery gets shit done" and confederate flags all over one's web site, however, are indicative of a young mind already cynically convinced of social darwinism, and, more to the point, already trained to think certain ways about minorities, etc. It's also mean-spirited in a way that lefty teenage fascinations tend not to be. To see a young person so jaded about the world is kind of sad in its own right. So, when a Republican congressman's son is found to have a lot of such stuff all over his facebook page, it raises some questions about what kind of home the senator keeps, and what his true beliefs really are --- all too often, kids get their cues for this sort of bile from those closest to them, after all.

This is to say nothing of how poor a job the senator has done in teaching his son about politics: if you want Daddy to STAY a senator, try keeping certain inflammatory ideas to yourself!
