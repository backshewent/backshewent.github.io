Zo article on the Penn draft pick Bossart:

"It was a little bit of a surprise. I was very happy to have my name called by them. I've been in Philly, I've been to a few games there, and I'm excited to be a part of it."

Bossart hopes to become the first Quakers player to make the big leagues since Mark DeRosa (1998-2013) and Doug Glanville (1996-2004).

"They were two really, really great players. I hope to have a career like they did."
.
.
"I've always considered myself more of a defensive player first. I've improved offensively year after year. I've been very happy with that. This year I went in with the mentality of being a professional baseball player, so I sat down at the beginning of the year and said I'm going to take a professional approach to everything. It really seemed to work out for me."
.
.
"I didn't really hear anything from the Phillies throughout the season, then I sent them a little bit on information late in the year. I hadn't heard anything from them until this morning when [area scout Paul Murphy] told me to give him a call. He said he really liked me and he was going to throw my name in the mix. And before you know it, the Phillies had me."

Bossart is eager to get started.

"I went to Penn with baseball on my mind from Day 1. That's what I've always wanted to do since high school. Education was always a big thing for me, so it gave me the best of both worlds. But I've always had baseball on my mind."
