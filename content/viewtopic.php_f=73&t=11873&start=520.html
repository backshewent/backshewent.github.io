No. See final count in Brian Lenihan's constituency tonight. On the first count, Joan Burton gets in. Second count redistributes all her votes. It's the last gasp for the bottom 3 candidates, but if they get second preference from her surplus votes, then perhaps they hang in a bit longer and get sufficient votes to get back their deposit (about 500 euros). third count they're gone, and some of their votes (pretty small amount) redistributed to the remaining candidates. In the end, Brian Lenihan (former minister for finance) makes it in by virtue of having more votes then everyone of the remaining candidates, even though he doesn't make quota.
