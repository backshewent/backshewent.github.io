A few things:

1. He is so sensitive to criticism or even perceived criticism I believe that he will really lose it at some point, and I'm not just talking some twitter storm or something. It could be really bad, or it could be good in that it will end his presidency.

2. It's pretty clear given his cabinet appointments, especially his secretary of labor, that his solution to competition from China is to make it possible to create Chinese-like factory conditions here--low wages, and no health and safety requirements. I think it will take awhile for the Trumpies to recognize this, if they care at all. On the other hand, accelerating this shift combined with the dismantling of what remains of our safety net and you'll see real misery that I don't think can be obscured by rhetoric. When grandma dies because Trump allowed Ryan to dismantle Medicare, people may notice.

3. Too many people think you can run government like a business. You can't. Being President is not like being CEO. (It's not like being Senator either, but I think Senators understand this.) It is sort of like being governor. I think this fact is likely to frustrate Trump and his appointees. I do worry that he appears to have enough enablers that at least at first he may be able to get his way on things. But eventually, as things go south, his grip on them is likely to weaken. The fact that he doesn't seem to intend to spend a lot of time on being President will make this work.
