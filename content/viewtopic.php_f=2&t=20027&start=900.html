Bucky wrote:did that video (that thankfully i wasn't exposed to) talk about fauci holding a patent on a vaccine? that seems to be popping up a lot today.


I have no idea if Fauci holds a patent on anything, but people have no idea how these kind of patents work. It's not like Thomas Edison working in his basement inventing a light bulb (in fact Edison's light bulb invention wasn't like that either). You have teams of people in labs working for corporations or universities or government making incremental improvements in various technologies. Holding "a" patent means very little for someone who has spent a career as a research scientist.

My father was an engineer and holds something 60+ patents. They were useful in his career, and he'd get a small bonus when one was accepted (he was very active around the time I was in college for some reason) but maybe one or two had any practical application. Even if the patent is commercially viable, the employer keeps almost all of the profit. Maybe after a certain threshold, you'd get a fraction of a percent, split of course with everyone else on the patent.
