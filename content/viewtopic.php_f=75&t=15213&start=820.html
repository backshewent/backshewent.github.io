Re: And we'll never be RANDOM...It don't run in our blood

by Bill McNeal » Fri Dec 20, 2013 00:10:35

I wanted to go into TV production as a career back in the day, I've been to and involved in a lot of recordings of shows, I directed a series of educational programs on public speaking for PBS when I was in high school. So my view may be a little biased, but I think its awesome. I mean, you have to understand that it isn't going to be thrill a minute, but being there for the experience is almost always interesting. What you see as a final product is often different than what you see at the taping. You get to see scenes that get cut for time, how things get reworked in editing, cast/crew screw ups. It really depends on what your expectation is going in. Personally, I love it, but I can see how it could be tedious if you aren't into the literal behind the scenes stuff.

I was at a Montell Williams show taping that just went completely off the rails, the episode never aired, but the guests were just a mess, they came in with some crazy cheating story, but got caught making it up, and then some real shit came out where the teenage daughter who was there to denounce her mom's cheating ways got for real pissed about something and told her mom that she'd shed been sleeping with someone, I can't remember who it was, like the neighbor or something, and then the mom had a breakdown and the guy who the mom was supposedly cheating with totally broke and was like I'm outta here. It was pretty awesome.
