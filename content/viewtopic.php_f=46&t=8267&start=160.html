by Wolfgang622 » Tue Oct 12, 2010 20:39:16

I think the Phils have to know that the reality of the situation is that Franzke, and not McCarthy, is Harry's heir. This idea occured to me as I am writing this, but you might argue, in fact, that the Phillies keeping Franzke on the radio is in fact evidence that they know he is the future. By keeping him on the radio while McCarthy's contract plays out, Franzke's tenure on the radio also conveniently coincides with the team as currently constructed running through its prime. Their policy, going back to 2008, has been to let the radio guys continue to be the radio guys through the postseason; Harry was brought in mostly to do the last outs of clinching games, IIRC. You'll notice that they have not extended this policy with McCarthy. The upshot of this policy has been that Franzke has gotten every big call in the postseason, and people have now associated most of the best moments over the past three years with his voice, save for the few (significant) calls reserved for Harry. Franzke would have had the WS-win call last year had the Phils won it, and, for now, he still has a shot at it this year.

McCarthy, on the other hand, is nowhere to be heard in any of these clips. More, Franzke's ascendancy is reinforced every day by WIP and KYW, which will play a significant audio highlight or highlights from the previous night's game, which almost always means using Franzke's call.

McCarthy is on TV, but I actually think that has given him the lower profile in Philly between himself and Franzke.

And now that I've noticed that pattern, I think this has been a conscious decision on the part of the Phillies.
"I'm in a bar with the games sound turned off and that Cespedes home run still sounded like inevitability."
