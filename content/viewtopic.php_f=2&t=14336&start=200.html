by SK790 » Fri Dec 14, 2012 22:48:22

Sorry, just catching up on this thread now, but I wanted share a story from my Catholic school. I had a quasi-friend in Catholic school for years who was always just a really shy and independent kid. Up through 4th grade, he was always just really quiet and shy and I was probably his best friend even through we never really hung out much. In 5th grade, he started to get into trouble a lot. He would ask unusual questions to the teachers during classes and always seem to act out when he didn't get the answer he wanted. I know he started seeing the Diocese' Guidance Councilor in the 7th grade because he was getting put in detention almost every other week(our school only had detention once a week for some reason). I always sympathized with him, but others in my class where relentlessly teasing him and bullying him and when that happened, I would usually just keep my mouth shut. This kid came to me one day and told me how miserable he was at the school and just wanted to leave. I told him he should go talk to the principal about how he was bullied and teased, he did, but nobody really did anything to stop it. He ended up getting kicked out of the school halfway through 8th grade because, i think, he just couldn't take it anymore.

I think about this kid often, I wish there were a way to contact him now, but he's not on Facebook from the searches I've done and I heard his family moved away after he finished 9th grade. I always felt bad for the guy. My point is, sometimes the sense of community Catholic schools build can exclude those who think differently and drive them away. My limited involvement with that kid had a huge roll in me losing my faith in the Catholic church for obvious reasons.
