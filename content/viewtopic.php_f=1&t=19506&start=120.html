Every pick in the first 10 rounds has a slot value which you can see here https://blogs.fangraphs.com/2019-mlb-dr ... ck-values/

Every pick after the first 10 can sign for $125,000. However if a team sign guys in first 10 rounds for less than slot, then they can allocate the surplus to signing picks after the 10th round. So basically teams are going to pick some college seniors who have no leverage and others that they KNOW will sign below slot to then use that extra money to get guys who they may not be sure if they can sign later in the draft. And if you exceed your draft allotment by 5%, you get taxed to hell and back and if you exceed it by 5% or more you get taxed more and lose future draft picks.

Another fun little nugget is that if you lose a draft pick (like the philllies did for signing Bryce) you lose that allotment money from your pool and if you fail to sign a draft pick you canâ€™t use their excess money to sign other guys. So if you don't sign the 1.1 pick, you can't then turn around and use his $8mil allotment to get some late round picks to sign, you lose it.
