Now, aside from shady dealing, lets debate the reality of the need for a nation to invest in R&D. R&D is actually something that does trickle down to citizens making the world a better place, providing jobs, etc. The US was great at this through DOD, NASA, FDA, etc., but we shifted to the idea that good ideas will get investment. That is only partially true in my assessment, which is one that has the prospective of having worked in R&D. Investment came for quick hit tech that could be patented and then exploited, but it dried up for other areas. App for that, hell yes, but a more efficient, cleaner jet engine required a long term deal with GE and United to test fly an algae based fuel, and that work progresses slowly with a very low budget.

Where does this fit with national strategy? The lists are long and storied, but with out ARPA/DARPA funding 'the internet', BSG might be a dial up message board, and the internet is, of course a who lot more now that IOT drive telco, etc. GPS, same same. These investments today are rare. Sure goofy things got funded (mood sensing walls for offices went no where, and is still one of the strangest things I recall - I assume it was more about sensor development to detect stresses in a high stress arena like space), but there were unexpected breakthroughs. Clearly I am a firm believer in Federal Support for Research and Development, especially if it is a public/private partnership where the Government make the agreement iron clad (i.e., avoiding scenarios where the government funds, then fails to meet its obligation creating a scenario where the government then pays full price to purchase the results of the research it primarily funded).
