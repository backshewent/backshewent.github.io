If you want a queen size futon that is actually comfortable to sleep on, it can get kinda pricey.

Metal frame 200
Wood frame 250-450
Innerspring mattress 250-350
Memory foam/gel topper 50-100
Futon cover 50-100

You can go cheaper but you don't want to be sleeping on a sack of flour or feeling the support bars.

by MrsVox » Tue Nov 13, 2018 10:02:30

Not sure about age of kids and stuff, but for the middle (11), I use a moto e4 phone. We connect it to our google play family subscription for music, and audible for audiobooks. Our library has a couple of free services for music and audiobooks, overdrive and hoopla.

The value-add is that I can throw in a Google Fi data card, and then he can use hangouts to talk to me when he's at a sleepover or a friends for the afternoon, or roaming the neighborhood where there isn't wifi and I can get in touch with him if I need to. Google kid's management lets us control apps he has access too, at night I shut off Hangouts and games so he's not up playing games all night.

Downside to that particular model phone - there's a security issue with bluetooth, so it has been shut off, so you can't use bluetooth devices with, including game accessories like pokemon go watch. Over the summer, we upgraded to a moto g4.
