The disconnect of Romney's answer on the pay equity question is that by and large it's not professional women of the type he had in those binders (who knew?) who need legal protection. Like most very highly educated people, they have much more leeway to set the terms of their employment. If they can't get paid at one gig, they'll go somewhere that will meet their terms. It's the ones that don't have grad degrees and a ton of other good options who benefit by the Ledbetter law.

As for the questions, one of my favorite alt-debate scenarios (though not quite at the level of "drink a shot every 15 minutes") is asking the candidates to answer questions from the smartest partisans on the other side. It would be fun to hear Ezra Klein or Matt Yglesias query Romney, or Ramesh Ponnoru grill Obama.
