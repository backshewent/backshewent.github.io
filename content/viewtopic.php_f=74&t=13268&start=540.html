Stick with it to the end of the course and at least get the credits. And then decide if you want to continue. Many teachers and student teachers have a moment or moments where they feel they can't continue. If you can fight through it, you'll feel a lot better by the end of the semester.

I had my days where I would come back from student teaching and I would just break down and sob. And I consider myself pretty level-headed. I eventually found out that even the most well adjusted and talented of my friends and classmates who were in the same program would do the same thing.

Keep at it. Constantly refine and make improvements. And understand that you're not going to ruin your students. That was a big fear of mine too, and I didn't understand how overblown that fear was until after I was finished.

Add another vote in there for sticking with it. Right after college, I lived with 4 friends, one of whom was student teaching. The rest of us were in our first jobs, going out to bars constantly and being general jerks. My friend who was student teaching was a really confident, fun-loving guy, but while student teaching he shuttered himself in his room the whole time. The rest us would make fun of him for being lame, and eventually the one time, he basically told us that he was scared to death getting in front of the class every morning. And if he had a beer or two the night before, he was almost certain that he would literally shit his pants in front of the class from the nerves. Like I said, he was not the type of guy you'd expect to hear having that type of anxiety. He stuck with it, got the hang of it and now, by all accounts, he's a pretty awesome history teacher and totally loves it.

I'm not going to say that happens with everyone, but I do think you'll regret quitting if you're that close.
