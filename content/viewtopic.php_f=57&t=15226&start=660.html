ReadingPhilly wrote:It makes a lot of sense to deal Lee. Hamels, not so much.



No. It does not make ANY sense.

Amaro has constructed a roster with old veterans. He's banking on them being healthy enough and productive enough to compete. If they were not seriously thinking they could compete, he wouldn't be shopping Dom or acquiring Byrd. So IFFF RAJ believes he is setting up a roster to compete, you don't dump perhaps your two best players. How many pitchers in all of baseball are better than those two? Who would be an upgrade? Who is cheaper for same production? Lee and Hamels at their best are about as good as it gets.

Lee for a big bucket of return prospects, means you are rebuilding. But then you play Asche and Ruf and Dom and Hernandez and look to bring up other kids, Biddle etc... that's NOT what they are doing. There's no point in putting JRol, Ryho, and Utley w Byrd now on the field and not keep your top 10-15 in all of Baseball starters. There are teams that would kill for 2 pitchers of that caliber. Anyone remember a couple guys named Maddux and Glavine? What's to say the Phils can't make a run with great starting pitching and a lights out bullpen? Maybe the old-as-dirt wheez-kids could do just enough.

My point, isn't that I'm not open to a total rebuild, it's that you don't front like you think you can take the division and make the playoffs and then dump perhaps your two best players. One you just signed last year who is not over the hill, and one who has been consistenly good and healthy and isn't overweight and has great mechanics.

C'mon.
