I think the distinction is that Bernie has actually addressed those votes and acknowledged how he would govern differently. His gun votes he attributed to representing a rural state with a high percentage of gun owners. He expressed lots of reluctance about the crime bill at the time, but voted for it because it included provisions to fund the Violence Against Women Act. These were all wrong votes, but he fully expresses regret about it, unlike a Bloomberg who hides behind NDAs or a Biden who reflexively defends nearly all of his record.

Stuff you did 20 years ago matters. If you can show how you’ve evolved and grown and will make amends, there’s a seat for you at the table. If you can’t, you should be held accountable. I see no reason to believe that a President Sanders would veto gun control legislation. I also see no reason to believe that a President Bloomberg wouldn’t bring deeply toxic ideas about women and people of color to the White House.
