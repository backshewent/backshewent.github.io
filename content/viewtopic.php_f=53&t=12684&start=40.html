by The B1G Piece » Mon Sep 12, 2011 17:23:33

Thinking about all the 9/11 coverage yesterday got me thinking about the 2001 team and as the Inky article a few days mentioned, how far the organization has come in those 10 years.

In the post 9/11/01 world, the Phillies were sitting in 2nd place in the NL East, 3 1/2 games back of the Atlanta Braves, at 75-68. Their last game before 9/11 was 12-4 defeat of the Expos in front of 5K-odd fans in Olympic Stadium.

Since the fateful day, the Phillies have gone 901-718, a very respectable .557 winning percentage.

The only team with more wins? The Cardinals, who are 904-718 AND have played three more games than the Phillies.

The Barves are 894-729.

My favorite part, 10 years later the Phillies are 94-49, holding a 12 game lead in a division they're about to win for the fifth time in a row, while holding the best record in baseball by a very comfortable margin.

Awesome.
