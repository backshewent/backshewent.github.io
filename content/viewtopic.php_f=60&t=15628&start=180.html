Rd 7 = Emmanuel Marrero, SS, Alabama State
bio



Alabama State hasn't had a player drafted since 2002, and none of the six Hornets selected have been taken before the 24th round. That will change this June, when Marrero's defensive prowess should make him a second-day pick in a Draft light on legitimate shortstops. The 2014 Southwestern Athletic Conference player of the year, Marrero has all the tools to play shortstop in the big leagues. He has smooth actions, quick hands and a strong, accurate arm. Though his pure speed doesn't grade as better than average, he still offers plenty of range at shortstop. The Puerto Rican-born Marrero stands out much more in the field at the plate. He's a switch-hitter who's better as a right-hander, though he'll need to tone down his approach to hit for a decent average. He won't provide much power and probably will fit toward the bottom of a lineup.


MLB.com article from Feb 2012 about Marrero playing at PR Academy
