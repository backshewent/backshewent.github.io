by Bucky » Tue Sep 02, 2008 00:25:38

TenuredVulture wrote:
EndlessSummer wrote:Well, this is weird. Half the power in my house is out. Half the power on my street is out. The power in the basement is on, which is where all of our internet stuff is hooked up, so I can at least dick around on here. But our good living room TV is out, while our old basement one works. So basically tonight I've learned that we need batteries in our flashlight and rediscovered that 27 inch TVs suck.



Do you have two meters? Is the meter running now? You might be getting free electricity.


Nah, the meter will be running as long as juice is flowing. What it is is that pole transformers break down a single leg of the 13.3KV primary power into two legs of 120V single phase power, which flows into residences in two separate wires making 240V (and there's a third neutral wire). So either the transformer's hosed, or there's a break in one of the legs of 120V power on the poles.

If there's absolutely something you want running, and you know your way around a breaker box, every other row in the box will be alive. Just move the circuit from a 'dead' row to a live row and it will be good to go. No 240V appliances (stoves, dryers, large A/C, etc.) can be used on a single leg, though.
