it seems that lots of support from folks here (including me) for overpaying one or more of the imperfect free agents is based on the assuption that the phillies don't have the chips to trade for a significant upgrade.
so i want to ask:
who is the best player we could acquire via trade of a phil who is actually tradeable?
for the purpose of answering, please make the (hopefully reasonable) assumption that the untradeables are halladay, lee, hamels, papelbon, howard, utley, rollins, ruiz, and last year's draftees. i'll accept treating biddle, franco, and one of valle/joseph as untradeable as well. if you think one of the first group can be traded or wish to add others to the second group, please explain.

my own answer would be that the best we could get is span, which would require sending the twins worley plus one or two of kendrick/cloyd/pettibone/may/artin and a reliever, and then signing free-agent pitchers to fill the resulting holes. olt is a little out of reach, and justin upton and wright are way out of reach.

if they can get span, and sign uehara plus a well-chosen undervalued pitcher (liriano? haren?) i would be content with platoons of current phils in rf, lf, and 3b.
if they can't get someone at least as good as span in a trade, i would rather keep our current crop of starting pitchers and sign one of the outfield free agents.
