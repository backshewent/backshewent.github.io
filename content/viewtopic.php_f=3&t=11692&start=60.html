by Bucky » Sun Jan 23, 2011 15:14:55

I would tend to think that the continuity is a lot less important in football than baseball. I could be wrong. I think baseball has a lot more old-timers still associated with the franchise in an official capacity (the Phillies are the prime example of this old school thinking), and the org runs much much deeper than the Big squad, since each baseball franchise has 5-6 minor league teams, and a roster of hundreds of players under their control. With football you have the 50-some pro players, and a handful of practice squad guys. And a new NFL owner is likely to gut the front office and replace it with "his" people anyway, so even if they had stayed in Cleveland, the only think remaining would have been the 30-40 guys who carried over from the previous season. And within 5-7 years, that's mostly gone too.
