Not complying with RealID is stupid. I worked in that world for a while and the items put in place raise the bar on the security of a default national ID. Sure, we don't have one, we just have this plastic (polycarbonate in many cases) cards that behave exactly like one would. Standardization of security feature, and having X number (you don't need them all), changes the game on counterfeiting to a place of significant difficulty. Pricing out legitimate enough ID replication is the game. As these become machine readable, and verifiable, it quickly becomes an impossibility.

So why would a state 'opt-out'? Well, it is all political showmanship. Although I have been away for a good while, most 'non-compliant' states generally came up just 1 security feature short, it does not matter which one, so that they can make the political claim of keeping 'big government' out of your wallet.

So, stop being stupid and get that ID up to par. You've only had 11 years to figure it out and plenty of time to repeal a terrible, headline grabbing law.
