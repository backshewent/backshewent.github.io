At the least, I think it's fair to assert that modern primaries are almost all id. Conventional wisdom now is that Obama beat Clinton in 2008 because he ran a tactically superior campaign, and that's almost certainly true. But that wouldn't have mattered if not for the fact that Obama had opposed the Iraq War and Hillary had supported it. As a unit, the Democratic Party by 2008 was opposed to the Iraq War at a visceral level; that had to be the decision point for enough voters otherwise relatively indifferent between the two candidates to swing the nomination to Obama.

But I also think it's fair to argue that the epistemic closure on the right has rendered a large chunk of those voters resistant, if not downright impervious, to big swathes of objective reality.
