A few misc. responses to the above conversation:

* These rule changes are all about pace, not game length. The MLB's SVP for Economics & Ops literally says "One of our principle objectives is to reduce the amount of dead time in games" in the article. I don't think anyone can objectively argue that the pace of the game has slowed to a crawl. Glad to see the MLB legitimately exploring ways to fix it.

* MLB (and NFL) should be more worried about the NBA, whose average fan is only 42. Ratings are (way) down this year with LeBron moving out west + being out for several weeks, but overall they are crushing it with younger fans.

* This quote is spot on:
“The game has undergone changes throughout its history, but it’s been locked into its current configuration since the DH was introduced in the 1970s,” White said. “Meanwhile, in every other popular team sport we’re aware of, rules change year by year by year. Part of baseball’s charm is the resolution to stay the same, but in the world we’re living in now, with the difference between pitchers and hitters, things don’t have to stay the same.”
