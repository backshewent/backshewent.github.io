by Soren » Wed Feb 13, 2013 09:14:28

so I was thinking about 300 K in a year for a starting pitcher.

No one has had more than 300 Ks in a season since 2002. Randy Johnson led the league in IP with 260 that year and had a 11.6 K/9 which was actually his lowest of the four consecutive years he won the CY. The top guys in IP the last few years have been in the 230-250 IP range. So, splitting the difference, a pitcher throwing 240 innings would need a K/9 of 11.25 to hit 300.

Since 1992, there have only been 4 players who have come close to hitting that mark. Randy Johnson twice in 1995 and 1997. He had 294 and 291 K on the year respectively. Pedro Martinez in 2000 had 284 K across 217 IP. That was his pornographically good 291 ERA+ season. Kerry Wood racked up 266 K in 2003, he only pitched 211 innings that season.

Since 2003, Verlander in 09 came the closest with 269 K across 240 IP.

Strasburg obviously has a really good chance. He had a K/9 of 11.1 last year. He'd need to bump that up closer to his 2010 mark of 12.2 and pitch about 230 innings.
