by Philly the Kid » Fri Aug 29, 2008 03:27:00

As most of you know by now, I'm for systemic change -- not "change" as a marketing slogan. I'm for a new Constitutional Congress, fix some stuff up. I'm far more left than the DNC or current Dem machine -- and I don't think Obama is a visionary -- but I will say that he accomplished a lot of his mission tonight. He generated some excitement -- he called out Bush 8 years and McCain. He got somewhat specific, and his best line was that it's not about me its about you. It wasn't perfect, as one pundit remarked he's made more eloquent and erudite speeches, but he got down to a real level, he personalized himself and did not in any way come off as some ivy league. He got the point to some people, that the real elite is the guy who has been doing it for all these years, with so many homes he can't keep count. Experience or is it really being a Washington insider.

I'd also say the Dems and the event at the stadium came off more polished for once. It wasn't quite Dems as-usual. He helped himself tonight in what will still be a close race, and I'm still sure there will be some Republican cheating, so I htink he needs to win by more than 50.7 to 49.3 plus pop vote doesn't count really anyway...

Debates will be interesting and McCain and his camp have a week to come with their comeback. They will get on that business about the cave, probably not the best move when you don't have military background the other guy is a former pow, that one wlll come back to bite him. I don't like it when Dems try to act tougher than Repubs, never gone win that one.

Obama did what he needed to do. He threw the Reps for a loop and the pundits were favorable, only the Ben Stein's of the world who can't be taken seriously tried to pooh pooh it.

I wish it meant more than it does, and that if he wins, more of the "changes" I'm looking for would be implemented (repeal the Patriot Act, close down Gitmo, shrink Homeland Security, stop the no-fly lists, go after monopolies, polluters and union-busting -- and really come up with a viable quality single-payer health care system ) but maybe he can do some good and get us out of this dark period in national history.

PS

I don't get the whole "experience" slogan anway. W had no national experience, and it showed! He manufactured a boon-doggle of a war for no reason. It's not about experience, it's about ability and sense. If I were the Dems I take control of this bit. Hilary tried to use it too. Need to come with better comebacks. I suspect Obama and Biden will prevail in the debates if not dominate, but it may not matter...
