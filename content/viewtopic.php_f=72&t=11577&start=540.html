by Bucky » Mon Dec 13, 2010 11:41:00

The Kid has researched and found out about a build-your-own-Mario game for th PC called "Super Mario Brothers X". After he "earned" it, I downloaded it. And I must say, I'm quite amazed that this thing is out there and free. Basically you can drag-and-drop elements from five or six of the Mario franchise games and create your own levels. And it's pretty extensive- you can create warps to other levels; animate objects such as platforms to make them move; hide things and reveal them with trigger events; etc. All in all, it's pretty amazing, especially considering it's FREE. I also believe that there's a way to upload custom levels to a Wii, but the Wii must be "hacked" first- so if he really gets good, I'll get a cheap barebones used Wii to hack as to not risk our "primary" Wii...


I'm not much of a gamer (or really a gamer at all), but this is pretty impressive- and it's pretty neat watching an 8 year old's brain churn as he learns all about object attributes, triggers, events, and other facets of OOP. Now hopefully he won't make the same mistake his dad did and go into IT, but of course learning the tools will likely enable him in any endeavors he chooses....
