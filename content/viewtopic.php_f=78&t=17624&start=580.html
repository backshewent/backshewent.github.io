Youseff wrote:https://twitter.com/jshkatz/status/793553664724140032

interesting graphic tracking Clinton probabilities across all the main forecasting sites. 538 seems to have the largest swings.

Think 538 and the Princeton and Huff Post models are showing they're flawed with how they handle uncertainty. This race is reasonably close - if it was held today I would bet a tremendous amount of money to win a modest amount of money that Hillary would win - but there's time for one more dip. Anything showing HRC with a greater than 95% win probability seems essentially flawed to me. Meanwhile while Trump *could* win it seems very likely that he will not. There are 272 electoral votes that he has shown no ability to reach, which is problematic for one's chances of winning a majority of the 538 in play. I appreciate 538 hyping uncertainty in two respects - 1) the public polls are truly not good, and placing an excess of faith in them is unwarranted, and 2) I think Nate is right about polling errors generally being systemic in the same direction and states being correlated so if one domino falls chances are many others will as well. However! this is not a 1 in 3 sort of chance. It is ridiculous to think it is. Now, maybe black people don't turnout, maybe 18-29 year olds don't turnout, maybe there are shy Trump voters, maybe everyone's turnout models are wrong. I think given how close the race is with public polls some degree of nerves are fair (especially compared to a week ago), but jesus christ this isn't something Trump wins 1 in 3 times.

That Upshot model looks really good. It moved enough when it was appropriate, but has stayed confident as well.
