by Eem » Tue May 20, 2014 15:58:54

No, there are 14 balls in the shuffler. 1-14. No repeats. The guy then draws four of the numbers to assign a selection... for instance, 6-11-3-9. In that order. They then match that combination with a team -- each team is assigned a list of number combinations using 1-14, with the worst team getting the most combinations and the "best" getting the fewest.

So in this scenario the team with 6-11-3-9 would get the #1 overall pick. They then draw combinations for the #2 and #3 picks. If the combo drawn matches a team that already got a selection, they redraw until they match a team that hasn't gotten a selection.

Doll Is Mine wrote:
CalvinBall wrote:the little balls make the combinations.

But what is a combination and how does one combination differ from another? Is it a bunch or numbers? And what do those numbers mean?

It's a combination of four numbers in a specific order. Think of how many of these you can make with 14 different numbers, and imagine how long a list of a team's potential combinations are:

1-2-3-4
1-3-2-4
1-2-4-3

No numbers repeat in a combination. They use 14 different numbers for the 14 different teams, but it's a way of making the process completely random. And as I said above, it's a way of using a normal sized machine instead of trying to shove 30,000 team logo balls into one machine.

Also, only one team is assigned each combo. So there's a team that has 1-2-3-4 IN THAT ORDER, but no other team has it.
