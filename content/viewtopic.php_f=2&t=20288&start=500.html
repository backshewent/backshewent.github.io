One of the things that always gets left out of what's happening in academia is that many religious institutions have explicit policies regulating what their faculty can and cannot say. There's a lot less academic freedom at those places than even at the Oberlins of the world.

So, yeah, I'm glad I teach in a place where I can in fact say "evolution is good science" and explain how a scientific theory works according to Karl Popper's philosophy of science.

One thing though--as we increasing move to an on-line environment, there's going to be a lot more documentation of what happens in a class. Not sure what the effects of that are going to be. But as chair, I have heard complaints like "prof so and so tends to dismiss comments made by women" and though I can talk to prof so and so, there's not a lot that I can do about something so vague--but if we start recording classes on video that changes things.
