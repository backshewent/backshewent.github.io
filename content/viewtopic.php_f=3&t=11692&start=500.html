My story is a little long, and a bit boring. But you asked. I grew up on the northern part of the Jersey shore. I was a Giants fan during the 70s, when they were stinky, and as a kid was a bigger football fan than a baseball fan. I was a lukewarm Yankee fan, but getting to either Yankee Stadium or Shea from that part of NJ was pretty tough. Getting to Giants stadium was a breeze by contrast. I went to several football games as a kid, but my first baseball game was at the Vet, because getting to the Vet really wasn't any tougher, and of course tickets were dirt cheap. The Yankees of the 70s and early 80s were of course good, but really unappealing. In 84, I started college in the Philadelphia area. Around that time, the unappealing Yankees started sucking. And I really began fall in love with Philly, staying there after college until I started grad school. So, while I could never quite give up rooting for the Giants, I decided if I was going to root for a sucky baseball team, it might as well be the Phillies. At least no one could accuse me of being a front runner.
