I've never waited tables, so I will not weigh in on what it is or is not harder than.

What I can say is that, shockingly, I understand Jeff's point. It's not particularly easy to run any major business concern, and I am sure Mitt Romney wasn't coming home at 5 every night when he was the head of Bain Capital, just as I am sure that when he was "home," he was often still "at work" mentally. you guys are comparing apples and oranges. i spent a year working 60+ hours a week splitting time between a bakery and a warehouse. i was physically tired all the time, didn't make great money, and barely had any free time to spend with my friends/gf outside of watching tv for an hour or two. however, i'd go to work high all the time and you could do the jobs without thinking too much. now i work 30-50 hours a week depending on the season, i sit behind a computer during all that time trying to look over and decipher data into a forecast. i'm taking up to 100 calls a day from clients and i'm always on my toes. on busy days, i leave work and my brain just feels like it has no capacity to process any more information and i usually just want to lie down because i'm so mentally tired. however, when i leave i have ample time to meet up with friends/workout/hike/etc...
