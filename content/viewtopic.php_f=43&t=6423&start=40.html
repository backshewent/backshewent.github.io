by dajafi » Thu Sep 04, 2008 00:28:16

After the miracle win over the Mets last week put us back in first--holy fuck, was that really just eight days ago?--I was talking or e-mailing with one of my (all too many) Mets-fan friends, and I said that last year, the Phils suffered what seemed like dozens of devastating losses. Just soul-crushing defeats, usually of the come-from-ahead, bullpen-meltdown variety. The one that stands out for me was that afternoon loss in Atlanta about a year ago, but there were many, many others. Losses, in other words, like the one we hung on the Mets that night.

And it seemed like every game the Phils played after one of those losses, they won. That was the most resilient Phillies team I've seen in 30 years of following them.

Unfortunately (and I said this to my friend), I think the Mets are resilient like that right now. Their bullpen without Wagner is fairly similar to our 2007 bullpen pre-September. That loss at CBP was far from the first excruciating defeat for them. They keep bouncing back.

But I also think this Phils team has some bounce left. I'm pretty sure they won't roll over, and I suspect they have one more run in them--whether at the division or the wild card.

(An aside: the other thing I'm feeling right now is real rage and loathing at the Milwaukee Brewers. If we fall short and the Mets get them in the first round, I hope the same thing happens then as happened this week.)
