Re: Biden our Time until 2020 - Politics Thread

by TenuredVulture » Wed Jun 12, 2019 10:14:28

I'm increasingly annoyed at how both those who favor and especially those who oppose things like universal health care, higher minimum wages, and so forth (basically, the left of the Democratic party) are labeled "socialists". That's an incorrect usage of term, most likely stemming from an confusion regarding social democracy and democratic socialism. Socialism, whether democratic or otherwise, stands for more government ownership of the means of production--it would involve the nationalization of major industries and centrally planned economy. (Corbyn does seem to support this somewhat, so if you want to call him a socialist, I guess that's fine.) Social democrats by contrast seek policies like increasing the minimum wage, providing more funding for education, and providing universal healthcare, because the main goal is to mitigate the inequalities inherent in the free market system. It's also inherently democratic--it reflects the classic republican insight that substantial inequalities of wealth result in the imposition of anti-democratic oligarchy or aristocracy.

To be fair, I cringe when people who should know better like Bernie and AOC bring this label on themselves. The distinction matters because socialism (like pure free market capitalism) does not work. It allows opponents of progressive politics to say "but look at Venezuela!" But social democracy can and does work.

Two other important points--social democracy is conducive to incrementalism--because it is democratic, it is self-correcting in a way that a political system based on an economic ideology cannot be. This is most evident when it comes to the environment. Neither capitalism nor socialism protects the environment very well. (I mean, you all saw Chernobyl.) Democratic accountability is essential for the protection of the environment (and any other collective good.)
