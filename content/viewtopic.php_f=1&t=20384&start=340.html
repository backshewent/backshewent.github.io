by JFLNYC » Mon Aug 31, 2020 23:14:22

Jimmy may not be the one but he’d be a damn sight better than what we (don’t) have now. But he’s gone, so it doesn’t matter.

But the discussion of a closer informs my take on what I believe the Sixers should do moving forward. I expect little agreement with my take but here it is: I think we should definitely consider trading Jo or a Ben or both for the following reasons:

1. Each has a fatal flaw: Jo either can’t or won’t get in acceptable playoff game condition now for a couple of years. Ben still refuses to shoot enough 3’s and other jumpers to keep defenses honest enough to help Jo’s game and that of other teammates.

2. Neither is likely to become the closer we need. Jo lacks the conditioning and can’t be trusted with the ball in crunch time. Ben hides during crunch time for fear of being fouled.

3. Both are not particularly durable. Jo’s had a broken bone in his back, a couple of navicular fractures, a torn meniscus sand bone bruise, and now has arthritis in his knee as an inexorable result of meniscus surgery. Ben has now had both significant back and knee problems this year.

Can one or both overcome his/their fatal flaw? Maybe, but maybe not. Can one become a closer? Very, very unlikely a big man like Jo can become the primary crunch time ball handler and creator even if he’s in top shape and learns to take care of the ball. Ben? Without a willingness to shoot jumpers and a desire and ability to shoot and make FT’s, I think it’s also unlikely. The injury situation can’t really be controlled but will continue to be worrisome.

I’ve heard the argument many times that a new coach should be allowed the chance to make it work with Jo and Ben and that argument might be right. But, first, even if a new HC makes it better I doubt either of them becomes a closer and perhaps the only way to get one is by trading Jo and/or Ben.

Plus, giving a new coach the opportunity does not come without risk. If a new coach can’t make it happen and Jo and Ben continue to show their fatal flaws and/or an unwillingness to correct them and/or injuries continue to haunt them, their value could well be significantly lower in a year. At some point each could cross that line between unproven ability and proven inability.

It’s just my take and I could be proven wrong — as it will no doubt be pointed out to me. Time will tell. But even if we haven’t yet reached it, we’re getting near the point where doing the same thing over and over — specifically trying to win it all with Jo and Ben — and expecting a different result becomes insanity.
Jamie
