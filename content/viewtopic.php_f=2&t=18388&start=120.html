by Wolfgang622 » Tue Oct 29, 2019 15:22:47

CalvinBall wrote:think part of it is that a lot of that creativity is in television these days.


Yeah, you're right. TV has caught up to movies thanks to the digital age, in a few important ways: for a relatively modest amount of money, it is possible to have a functional home theater, with a surround sound system and a pretty damn big television that is a wide screen; and digital effects have made convincing-looking special effects, particularly set in space, within a TV show's budget to produce; and streaming services have dramatically increased the money available for TV in the first place. All of this makes TV the perfect space for the new, the exciting, and the risky; movies cost so much anymore that they almost have to have built-in audiences in order to minimize the risk of catastrophic losses.

This I find depressing, because a good movie is still a helluva thing.

Maybe it is time to ask: are movies, at least fictional, non-documentary movies, as a form, a dying breed? Is the future all serialized world-building now?
