Barry Svrluga of the Washington Post reports that Stephen Strasburg's seven-year, $175 million extension includes $70 million in deferred payments.
Strasburg will make $15 million per season from 2017-2023. As for the deferred money, which has become typical with the Nationals, it will be paid without interest at $10 million annually from 2024-2030.

That doesn't matter for luxury tax purposes. If they don't care about the tax then, good on them. But the deferred payments to guys like Scherzer and count for payroll purposes during the life of the contract, not afterwards. My understanding is that the deferred payments are only permitted now if they take the form of a deferred signing bonus, and payroll is always calculated as

Payroll value = (Signing Bonus/Years of Contract) + Annual Salary

The personal services contracts given to Pujols and Ryan Zimmerman back in the day are now prohibited by MLB.
