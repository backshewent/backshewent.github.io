Hah! Surprised to see this come up, since I finally watched it just tonight. At first I really wanted to see it, then was pretty turned off by some reviews and opinions that made it sound a little too creaky and obvious and predictable.

Maybe it was all those things, but it was also very enjoyable (some reviews I had read had described it as intentionally humorless, in an effort to go against expectations for a Jordan Peele project. I don't know about Oscar-worthy. Don't think it tries to be anything other than a well-constructed horror-comedy with a bit of social consciousness thrown in. Maybe in some years that's all it takes. Visually it has some nice images, but it's no Birdman.
