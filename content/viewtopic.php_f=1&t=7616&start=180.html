Just to get the chronology right, a pulmonary embolism occurs when a blood clot (or other material)travels to the lungs and eventually lodges in the vessels there, causing a blockage.

I've had it happen twice, with actually showers of clots causing multiple emboli. I didn't die, which is good, and was able to dissolve the clots through medication. I've gone on both times to fully recover and pretty much be able to run faster than ever, with allowances for the fact that I'm an ancient old coot. Serena's clots were apparently generated after surgery on her foot following a laceration of it after a still pretty much unexplained nightclub accident. She then also required surgery to have the embolism removed. Why this was either I don't really know, since it's almost never used.I assume it's this surgery that caused her prolonged absence, since otherwise treatment is a bit of a pain in the ass but not really incapacitating. Embolisms themselves are scary, but if they don't kill you and are treated there's really no lingering effect, except perhaps if have a pulmonary infarct which can leave a little(or more)lingering back pain.
