by JFLNYC » Thu Jan 24, 2013 19:16:23

allentown wrote:
JFLNYC wrote:The only problem with rebuilding is that our prospects range from "meh" to "suck" and there are no vets on the roster likely to get us a haul of prospects. Absent some sort of alchemy, there's gonna be some lean years ahead.

I don't agree at all. The Phillies have a lot of vets who could be extremely helpful to a contender in a deadline deal. They are over-flowing with such players. They have to be healthy and playing tolerably well for a sucky Phillies team for deals to be likely, but I can't really think of anyone who would be a more attractive trade target than Halladay, Papelbon, Utley. If one of the Youngs turns it around by mid-year, he could bring back a little in return. If the Phillies are really going to pack it in and go with kids, Ruiz would also bring a big, big return from the right pennant contender. Adams could bring a decent return. With that you've written off 2014 into total rebuild mode, but 2015 could look pretty good. For 2014 you go with the kids: Brown, Ruf, Galvis, Asche, Martin, Biddle, Pettibone, Aumont, DeFratus, Rosenberg, Joseph/Valle, Hernandez, Gillies.


There's a Catch-22 you're missing. If those vets you mentioned are healthy and productive, the Phils might be in the race, so they won't trade the vets for the prospects they need for the future. If those vets aren't healthy and productive, they bring little or nothing in the way of quality prospects. Either way, you don't get what you need to rebuild. And you can't trade Halladay, Utley or Chooch in the off-season since their contracts will have ended.

By the way, Michael Young has a FNTC.

If the Phils focus on that, they're getting themselves into a death spiral. You worry about the lower ratings, so you cut back on payroll. Cutting back on payroll yields an inferior team, which both doesn't generate much buzz and performs also performs poorly, so ratings dip lower. With ratings lower, you cut back on payroll even more, which yields a still worse team, etc., etc. Worrying too much about those lower ratings becomes a self-fulfilling prophecy.
