however, i continue to point out that the phils roster is good enough to be a playoff contender.
contrary to the interpretation of swish, i don't even think everything needs to break right for that. sane use of the full existing roster (which i now accept is extremely unlikely) should give 81 wins with neutral luck. in that scenario, i think the offense will be better than decent, while pitching will be the problem, mostly because 3/5 of the rotation and the closer spot are highly suspect. many on this and other phils boards/blogs seem to think it will be the other way around. since i disagree and like to argue baseball, i keep pointing out my counterarguments when i get the chance.

81 probably isn't what you would call decent, but i think they need add in only about half of the available lucky breaks (e.g. mag and hernandez and the pen pitching well and cole and cliff staying healthy on the pitching side, but neutral luck on the offensive side) to be in the 85-90 win decent/contender zone. need to land another good starter or have everything go right (i.e. the above pitching scenario plus asche and outfield all match best projections, and ruf+howard merging into a two-headed 1b monster, while rollins and utley and ruiz all hold on for one more decent season), to make the playoffs.

In theory, I don't have a problem with holding money when the FA class is bad. The problem is that it isn't likely to get much better because teams are locking up their good young players. That said, I do think we'll be a bit better this year. Last year was pretty much a disaster with nearly everything going wrong. I think the defense and bullpen will be better, if for no other reason than they can't get worse, and then the OF will also most likely be better.
