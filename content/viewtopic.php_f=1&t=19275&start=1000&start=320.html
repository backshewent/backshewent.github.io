1.) Giants - why SF? Bryce and Kayla absolutely love the city of San Fran. There's plenty of public evidence and comments about it, they vacation in the area often. Can they actually put up the big numbers in a contract? No. Are they being used as leverage for the Dodgers? probably. Would he sign with the Giants if everything were equal? Maybe. Can the Giants equal up to the Phillies. Unlikely.

2.) Dodgers - why LA? Dodgers are 3 hour drive or a 40 min flight to Vegas. It's incredibly close to home, it's what he ultimately wants. Can the Dodgers put up a bigger number offer? I think so. Are they being used as leverage to the Phillies? I don't believe so. Dodgers are coming off back to back WS appearances, he wants to win now and thinks this is his best shot. It checks off most if not all of his wish boxes. Is it his preferred destination? 100%

3.) Phillies - why Philly? They can give the most money and at the end of the day the player and the agent are trying to get paid. Were the Phillies the only team going incredibly high on terms for recent weeks? Yes. It's why I was so confident in him going to the Phillies. What I knew was NO ONE was even close to what the Phillies had put out there. Literally a lock at that point. Is it his first choice in places to go? Definitely not. You could talk someone into the rebuilding roster and offseason moves and throw a bunch of money at them and convince them to come play sure if it's the only team giving you $350.



With no one else jumping in to the mix this decision was incredibly easy and was merely when not if. I unfortunately think Middleton flying to Vegas set off a chain reaction of other teams perking up and jumping in. If the Phillies offer a significant amount more money than the other two teams, he's wearing red. If the offers are close in the slightest between LA and PHI he's going to the Dodgers and from what I gather the Dodgers are talking like they have money in convos, thats not bias BS. Like Shadow said above, it'll probably take the Phillies going to the $365-370 range to get it done. Problem I think is the Phillies will be too stubborn and let Harper walk instead up bumping the offer, but that last statement is my opinion.
