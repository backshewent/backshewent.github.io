I've been seeing references to the DSA (Democratic Socialists of America, right?) pop up frequently over the last few months. Now, as far as I know, they've been around forever, but relatively marginal. (I mean, I've actually read Michael Harrington, so...) In my day, the left wing of the Democratic Party was the ADA (Americans for Democratic Action) Is the current organization the remnants of the Bernie Sanders people? Are they doing much useful? (In Arkansas, they held an event where they helped people repair broken taillights so as to avoid getting randomly stopped by police. I guess that's fine...but what about working to elect some Democrats? Their facebook page shows no actual political activity.)

Looking at their strategy page, they see 2016 as a year of rising rather than one of defeat. I guess in some ways, by embracing left-wing populism, they share certain characteristics with Trumpism.

Anyway, I skimmed their "strategy" document on their website, and it doesn't really seem like much of a strategy. Kinda a feel at best they'll suck energy and resources from left wing entities that might actually be effective.

I mean, nothing about getting people registered to vote, little about union organizing, no knowledge of already existing political structures in the various communities where they should be forming coalitions, no lobbying, little that could actually translate effectively into actual political change. In some sense, they seem even more politically clueless than animal rights organizations. (Who in fact have some successes they can point to even in places like Arkansas.)

Re: Hypocrisy is dead. Seize the politics

by pacino » Thu Dec 28, 2017 14:39:45

I've been on record here being hesitant to embrace the movement but it seems to me that the energy and work being done has mostly been positive. there's a few outliers but the overall work is pushing unionization in the South, getting elected, and forcing politics to include the left perspective. There are different strains of thought within the group, as with any group, and some learn much more Marxist in nature, but PA had two candidates (John Fetterman and Aryanna Beringer) from the Democratic primary race for Lieutenant Governor try to get Pittsburgh's DSAs endorsement. Larry Krasner is going to be Philly's DA!

There's no astroturf money flowing into this; it's all people power. It's not an organization I'll be a part of but I respect those who are and don't think most of them are acting in any kind of bad faith to ruin Democratic politics. Yes, they rail on neoliberalism, and I think it tends to be misapplied, but that's a minor quibble. Them and other leftist groups, immigrant justice groups, progressive unions, etc are creating a new block on the left, especially in Pennsylvania politics (Pittsburgh, Philly and the Lehigh Valley area are seeing a huge shift). if you don't see that, I don't know what you tell you.
