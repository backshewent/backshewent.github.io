by jerseyhoya » Tue Jun 03, 2014 11:28:26

Election day in the great Garden State. Heading home over lunch to vote for Tom MacArthur against Lonegan. Race has been pretty nasty, as I'm sure people who live locally have seen on TV. MacArthur is the establishment-y rich guy who has the backing of both the Burlington and Ocean County Republican Organizations. Lonegan is the movement conservative fellow who lost to Booker in the special senate election last year, and he's done well raising money nationally from the tea party, small donor crowd. Neither man is from the district, but both have beach houses in it to which they have moved full time recently. I think MacArthur is favored, as this is one part of the country where you don't bet against the machine, and I really hope he wins.

The other marquee event in New Jersey today is the NJ-12 Democratic Primary to replace Rush Holt. The race has been pretty civil for the most part with three liberal Democratic state legislators (Bonnie Watson Coleman - Mercer; Linda Greenstein - Middlesex; Upendra Chivukula - Somerset) from the three biggest counties in the district as the main contenders. It'll come down to Watson Coleman and Greenstein, and whether Greenstein can hold down Coleman's margin in Mercer since she represents part of it in the senate already, while running up the score in Middlesex. Greenstein seemed smart and no nonsense when I met her at Rutgers, so I'm hoping she wins.

The big primary nationally today is Mississippi Senate, where Sen. Thad Cochran (R) is running for his seventh term and being challenged from the right by State Sen. Chris McDaniel. By all accounts the race could go either way. While if McDaniel wins it will rightly be talked up as a big W for the tea party win and a blow against an establishment/bipartisan type, Cochran has run a race worthy of losing. He's old and out of touch either due to not caring enough to ramp up or losing a few miles an hour on his fastball.

There are also some unpredictable House primaries in California today due to their top two system. CA-17 is an interesting one with an up and coming Indian American Democrat taking on an incumbent Japanese American Democrat in the only Asian-American majority district in the country. Mike Honda has represented the Silicon Valley area since 2001, and he's being pushed hard by Ro Khanna, who is an IP lawyer and served as Deputy Assistant Secretary of Commerce in the Obama administration. The drama tonight centers on whether both will beat out the top Republican in the field (who has no chance in the general in this district), or if Honda's labor allies who have been boosting the GOP guy will help him into the top two, making his reelection much easier.
