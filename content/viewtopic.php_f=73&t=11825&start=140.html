mozartpc27 wrote:
phatj wrote:It also doesn't say it has awful lyrics. It says it uses archaic language. Well, no $#@!, it was written 200 years ago.

That's a pretty bad criticism.


I go back and forth on the anthem. The music, to sing, is difficult, but it is pleasant enough when played instrumentally. Has a certain majesty to it. The lyrics, when read, are really awful - it is a third rate piece of poetry - but have the distinct advantage of not referencing God, where admittedly better songs (America the Beautiful, Battle Hymn of the Republic) do reference God. I'd really rather not force one more God reference into the group of cultural artifacts that make up the backbone of American mythology, thank you very much.

I don't have a problem with criticism of the lyrics of the Anthem as bad poetry (though I think you'd have to go further than that to show that it's a bad anthem), but the column that Housh linked didn't actually do that. It just made fun of the archaic language, which is pretty dumb. Should we adopt a new anthem every fifty years or so to avoid having archaic language?

I also think a case could be made that a national anthem shouldn't be difficult to sing for most of the people whose anthem it's supposed to be. However, the article doesn't really make that point either, though I suppose it hints at it. I'm not sure I agree anyway; the article points out that most Americans can at least fake their way through it when it's played at ballgames and so forth, and how much more than that is required of the average citizen?

I agree with you on the "God" issue, as I'm pretty sure I posted in a recent thread specifically about the National Anthem.
