Take this example:

NFL Sunday Million 2662/5555 48% full ($200 entry)


DraftKings has a $1m guaranteed prize pool. $200 entry fee. They need 5000 entries to break even. They open it up to 5555 entries, so if they fill every seat, they make around $110k.

90 minutes before kickoff, they only have 2662 entries. My guess is that they only ended up with around 3000 or so (maybe 3500). The prize pool doesn't change whether there are 3000 or 5555 entries, so your odds of winning are increased significantly at DraftKings' expense. They probably lost 300k to 400k just on this contest.

It's a top-heavy prize pool, but based on 5500 entries, they probably still paid down to 1000 spots, which means for $200, you probably had a 1 in 3 chance of winning 5x your money (since only 3000 entered) and a better shot at the big money (1st place was around $100k).
