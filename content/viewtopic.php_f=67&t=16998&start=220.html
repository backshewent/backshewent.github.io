So the vid of Wentz --

impressive. now - i realize he was at a small division school - but one that has dominated their level -- he sure looks the part -- height, arm strength and surprisingly agile for a guy of that size and body type. He looked half Big Ben and half Mariotta.

It's hard to predict how a guy translates to the pro game. Needs good protection, good coaching, and good QB IQ and instincts. You don't know til you put the guy in the position.

I'd draft him on that tape alone if we could have gotten him in 2nd round (and had a 2nd round pick) but he's clearly climbing the board and could even be the 2nd QB taken after Goff. I'd be stunned if the Browns don't take Goff. I could also see Chip liking this Wentz kid too. Dal is there before us. What if Houston tries to move up?

I won't complain if we end up with this kid. I won't complain if we end up with Bradford and use #13 on a serious OT/DE/S/Corner that can start as a rookie.

If 19 QBs make 15mil a year or more I have a hard time believing Bradford is going to take 15-16, he's going to want that 20mil range. He was at 13mil last year on his rookie deal. I'm sure Condon will argue he had 3700 yards and set the franchise completion % record -- showed he could take a hit. Dealt with the chaos of Novacare /Chip/Howie /Media - and had an inconsistent line, running game and league leaders in drops. They will argue on a good team he'd have been a 4000+ yard guy with 25 TDs, etc and he came on strong showing full recovery. That he warrants at least 20.

Will be interesting to see how it shakes out. And whether the Birds believe in him, or really want to do a hard-reset and find a rookie prospect to build with over next 5 years?
