Re: And I took the Thoughts less Random by

by JUburton » Mon Oct 16, 2017 11:11:19

Werthless has it covered but a sort of easy way to think about it is to think about all possible combinations. You have 29 chances to share a birthday with the 29 other people in the room. Say you don't, then the 29th person has 28 chances, the 28th person has 27 chances, etc (because you've already exhausted some of those chances) all the way down to the last two people sharing one chance. Odds get pretty good that at least one of those chances will be a match even though the odds of you specifically sharing a bday with someone are low.
