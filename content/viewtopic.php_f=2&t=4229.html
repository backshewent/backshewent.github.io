Great list. I don't know why I always say Teen Wolf is my favorite basketball movie when Hoop Dreams is one of my top ten movies of all time. It's just a phenomenal movie from beginning to end. I remember seeing it in the theater back in 1995 and I remember being disappointed at the end because I wanted to see more. I've never had another three hour movie give me that kind of feeling.

I went to college with a kid who was a freshman on the St. Joe's hoops team the year that William Gates was a senior. His name was Rodney Horton and you can see him ever so briefly at the end of the film. What I found most interesting in talking to him about Hoop Dreams (he had lived the same life of an urban Chicago kid being recruited to play hoops in the suburbs at St. Joe's) was that he felt that Coach Pingatore was unfairly portrayed in the film. He thought that he comes off as kind of harsh, but that he really was a coach who cared greatly for his players.

I know that American Beauty isn't a cheerleading film in the traditional sense, but it features enough cheerleading that it works for me. It's my game so I'll play it how I want. I didn't set any rules for this. If people want to say that M*A*S*H was a football movie because it featured a football game, they can. It won't bother me.
