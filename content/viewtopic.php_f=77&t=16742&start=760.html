by TenuredVulture » Thu Oct 15, 2015 09:54:59

There are a lot of people out there who seem to think we need someone who "isn't a politician." Like political parties, there's a strong sense in political rhetoric that "politician" (and thus by inference politics) are pejoratives. (I'd say at least 50% of the criticism here launched at JH reflect this attitude.) So, we get either no chancers like Bernie and Rand, or we get people who are absolutely unqualified like Trump, Carson, and Fiorina gaining a lot of popularity. The latter is really dumb--you wouldn't hire someone to fix your car because "she's not a mechanic" or someone to operate on you because "he's not a surgeon". That would be idiotic. (The irony here is that I think one legitimate criticism of Obama's Presidency involves his lack of experience. And yet, how many of those people are now voting for Fiorina?)

I can't think of one example of someone who achieved anything politically who was not in some sense or other a real politician. Indeed, many great political leaders had little success in any other field.
