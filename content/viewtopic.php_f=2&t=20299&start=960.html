slugsrbad wrote:The 538 forecast is out, has Biden at 71% to win.

The write up is worth reading - https://fivethirtyeight.com/features/it ... trump-out/

I am most surprised I think by Biden only being 93% to win if the election was held today. Like, I understand there's a chance everything is very wrong, but a 'chance' feels like it should be 2-3%, not 7%. That might be minor quibbling.

In 2016, in the model wars, I liked the Upshot the best, which had Clinton at 85%. Thought throughout the campaign 538 was too rosy on Trump. Mostly not for statistical/methodological reasons, but because the percentage felt right based on what I saw and knew. I think 538's approach to errors not being independent proved prescient, so probably not worth getting too far out on a limb tearing apart what they're doing now.

Trying to model uncertainty moving forward is something I do not know how to do technically, and intuitively where they have it feels too uncertain, but some of the other models (e.g. the Economist model has Biden at 97% to win the popular vote) are absurdly overconfident. Nate's write up seems correct to me, but the numbers seem a bit low. I dunno.
