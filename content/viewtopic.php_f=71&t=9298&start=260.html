by Phan In Phlorida » Fri Sep 18, 2009 18:38:06

Werthless wrote:
phatj wrote:I'm trying to imagine the circumstances under which a guy who grew up in, went to college in, and currently resides in PA could possibly become a Yankees and Cowboys fan.

You find it inconceivable that a front-running tool could exist in this state?

Not necessarily. It can depend on how old the person is, where they grew up, where their family was originally from. IIRC, in NEPA the Cowboys were always on TV in the 1970's. Same for baseball. My folks had NY stations WWOR and WPIX on their cable lineup years before any Philly station, so this fostered plenty of Yankees and Mets fandom in the region. IIRC, the Phils didn't really make a move into the NEPA market until the late 1970's (the first time I met Harry K was at a NEPA car dealership in the late 70's as part of a Phils publicity campaign to expand their market north).

Location and travel routes played influence too, as before the NE Extension, New York was quite closer to NEPA than Philly. And even with the NE Extension, it took the Blue Route to make it easer. So more old timers up in NEPA are NY team fans than Philly. I only knew 1 Eagles fan growing up, and he had relatives in the Philly area. Everyone else were Steelers, Cowboys, Dolphins, Colts, Jets... the teams we (and our parents) got on TV/radio.

Now, if the kids are from the Philly region... well, it's human nature to avoid things that make you sad and disappointed
