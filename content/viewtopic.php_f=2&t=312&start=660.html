by Bucky » Mon Mar 04, 2019 13:04:25

It's quite the interesting story. Here's a primer: https://www.prosoundweb.com/channels/li ... ir_bros/3/

It grew up as a sound production company. Then acts started touring with their own sound and light shows and, eventually, staging. They built a huge auditorium style soundstage out there to make sure their gear work in the environments they were intended for. I think Bruce was the first to say hey, you've got this place with the sound system you put together for us- why don't we just do the tour rehearsals at your place? Then as they (and other surrounding support companies) designed and built the staging and lighting, it became almost a necessity to do the rehearsals out there. Fun fact: The crew even rehearses once the set has been finalized. At the end of a day's rehearsal, they will break down everything and load it in to trailers. Then the next morning they will set it all up again for the next day's rehearsal.
