by Wolfgang622 » Mon Apr 13, 2009 14:38:20

I was at a great Phillies game in 1998 which they won 8-7 with a brilliant rally in the ninth against the Pirates, resulting in one of the great Harry Kalas calls. There had been a rain delay, and there were so few people left at the Vet y the seventh inning, the broadcasters could hear and acknowledge individual fans. My firend and I watched as two dudes got Wheeler's attention, and he looked out from the booth and waved to them. So, naturally, we decided we need to get Harry Kalas. We screamed and screamed his name, and, sure enough, he poked his head out, looked dead at us, and waved.

No big thing, but it meant the world to me when I was 19.

RIP Harry. RIP. I think I will cry, eventually.
