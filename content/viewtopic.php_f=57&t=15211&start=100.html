by joe table » Tue Nov 12, 2013 14:02:51

Good numbers for Byrd:

-412 foot average HR distance: 7th in MLB last year, suggests it was not a fluky HR year with a bunch of wall scrapers

-13.2% HR/FB at Citi Field last year...again, showed legit power in a cavern of a park

-37% FB% last year (32% career)...if he can sustain this, no reason he can't get to 20 bombs again

Scary numbers:

-Obviously the BB rate, but also K rate: 25% (vs 18% career)

-Swung at 56% (!) of pitches last year, vs 50% career. His contact rate went down to 73%, vs 79% career

He could be really really bad if he loses the power stroke from last year. But at 2 years, no draft pick, and ostensibly a big defensive upgrade in RF, this is a fine move IMO
