by Bucky » Thu Sep 10, 2015 16:48:49

"common core"; i.e. "least common denominator" core

I've been in many debates about this and have seen some good arguments on why it's good. Bottom line (IMO) it is a good way to help those who have a real hard time grasping numeric concepts. However, to those (i.e. a good majority of kids) who can pretty easily assimilate said concepts, it only service to confuse and frustrate. My kid was doing 5th grade math problems in his head at 5 YO and when he finally got to fifth grade he struggled with the way he was being taught. But I do totally see what they're trying to do and how it could help some.
