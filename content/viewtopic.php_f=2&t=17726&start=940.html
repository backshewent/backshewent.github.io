Sending a nominee to the floor without a committee's recommendation isn't SOP because committees don't shoot down nominees who'd otherwise have a shot at confirmation hardly ever. I'm not sure what the mechanism is, but Rubio might be required to vote to send the nomination to the floor without recommendation. I'd think he would do that as a courtesy regardless of his position on Tillerson's confirmation, as might some Dems. In a normal administration, this level of resistance usually prompts a withdrawal of the nomination. But if Trump is committed to the nomination, it should get a vote of the full Senate. There is a tremendous amount of default deference to a president filling out a cabinet, and the randomness of committee assignments probably shouldn't be the thing to derail a nomination.

If Rubio votes no in committee, would strongly expect McCain and Graham at least will join him on the floor. Would guess Sasse and a few others would be possibilities. Some Dems might cross over so he could be confirmed anyway, but he'll need Dem votes.
