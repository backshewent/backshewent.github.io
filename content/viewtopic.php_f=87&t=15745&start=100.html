by azrider » Tue Jul 08, 2014 23:41:42

First, I'm getting pretty excited for the embiid, Noel tandem. As I've said months ago, and after watching a couple of games, I like Noel at the 4. Right now, he's holding is own and definitely doing positive things, but at I can see him actually blossom sooner at the 4. I love this guy's hands. Couple that with his ability to cover so much ground so quickly, I think he may be able to terrorize the opponent better in space than down low. He can disrupt passing lanes, challenge the 3 and race back in for weakside shot blocking/rebounding, all the while not taking a pounding constantly fighting for position on the blocks against players 20-40 lbs heavier. This is not to say that he is not capable at the 5, I just think he will need the game to slow down, hopefully gain a few more pounds, but most importantly pick up on the nuances of the game that come from experience. And I could easily see that 6+ years down the road when he slows down a tad he will be most effective defensively at the 5.

I really like what I've seen from both mcdaniels and Thompson so far. It will be nice to see which one can win that d and 3 spot on the wing. Neither are or will be scorers, but given time to get set they can hit the open 3, what will separate them is what they do on the other side of the ball and how well they can fill up the stat sheet.

I really didn't bother to read too much, with I don't know what was going on here in this thread... But don't be surprised if hinkie comes out of the free agent period with a team much closer than we think. Hinkie knows that building through the draft is a gamble and while it may initially be the plan, he has created the flexibility to do a lot of different things. I was pretty sure the lebron was staying in Miami, now I'm far from sure. If he moves, this may create a ripple effect throughout the nba and one where hinkie with tons of cap room and assets can get things done. Two teams that have come up are Cleveland and Houston. I could see him taking on Lin and getting a Clint capela aka Jerome moiso, and a 2nd or maybe a first and then packaging some of that or whatever we have to Cleveland for jack and waiters. Suddenly, they could field a rather exciting team, albeit inexperienced.

4/5 Noel,sims,embiid, moultrie?
4 Capela
3/4 young, grant
2/3 McDaniel,Thompson,Richardson
2 waiters
1/2 Lin,wroten,jack
1 mcw
Granted pieces will be moved in and out and the backcourt is a little crowded and front court a little thin if moultrie is out and until embiid comes back. Richardson would most likely be bought out and jack would take on a veteran leadership role for the next few years backing up at the 1 and 2. But most importantly you're now looking at a core of mcw, waiters, Noel, embiid which has a strong possibility to flourish into something special.

There are probably a dozen other scenarios and I am so confident that hinkie has gone through them all will do what is best for the team.
