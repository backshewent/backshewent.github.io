by phillychuck » Mon Jan 28, 2008 21:26:43

So you guys who hate this signing, read the following from one of the top working sabermetricians, Tom Tango:

Pedro Feliz:

His fielding is sensational, Gold-Glove caliber. And I say this without ever having seen him (or noticing him). I’m no expert, which is why I rely on Giants fans. Fans call him +1.5 wins as a fielder. John Dewan has him as the top 3B from 2005-2007 (+61 runs). Clearly, he’s fantastic with the glove.

His hitting on the other hand is at the -1.0 or -1.5 win level.

Overall, he’s somewhere between a league average 3B to a somewhat above. Add in the aging, and he’s at league average, or a bit below.

That puts him right around a +2 WAR player.

I’d sign him for 2/16, or 3/21.

My guess is that good fielding, no-hitting 3B are undervalued. Let’s see who goes for him.

#43 —tangotiger, 11/10 @ 10:24 AM


We have all wanted the Phils to be more of a "Moneyball" team. Today's signing, contrary to popular opinion, pushes them in that direction.

Moneyball isn't about OBP, it's about figuring out which assets are undervalued. As published in MLewis's sequel to MB, Beane said that everyone was getting on the OBP bandwagon, and he thought defense was now the most undervalued (and poorly evaluated) asset. Tom Tango's comment above that he is worth $8 million per year as a FA tells you just HOW undervalued he is.

He's a slightly worse hitter than Helms figures to be this year. He's probably the best defensive 3B in the major leagues. The defensive improvement from worst to best is probably worth 2.5 to 3.5 wins (25-35 runs) if he plays 70%+ time. Maybe more, if he's taking Helms' time, who was probably 10 to 15 runs below average defensively. It's probably a 2 or 3 win upgrade, and with wins going for about 4.4 million in the FA market, he was a discount signing, and a good one. As much as it pains me to say it.

Of course I'd prefer Zimmerman or Wright, but that's not an option.
