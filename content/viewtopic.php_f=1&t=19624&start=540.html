I am in some despair about this team. They can't play consistently and our stars aren't playing like stars. Offense sucks. Pitching sucks.

I wonder a lot about "buying" a competitor. Maybe that works for some teams, but man, it sure doesn't seem to work for us.

We are told that clubhouse chemistry is great (Stolnis just talked about this on the latest Hittin' Season podcast). sports psychology is fascinating. the mccutchen loss definitely hurt, but he's still been hanging around the team. that said, the team definitely hit a wall at that point in early june. so it's easy to point to that.

i wonder how the players feel about playing for a guy like kapler? is he someone that they play in spite of, or someone they wanna make proud? i know he's mr calm and collected, but he also comes off as mr macho. players, since their little league days, strive to make their "dad" proud on the ballfield. players may perceive kapler as less of a father figure and more of an intimidating male rival given how young and ripped he is, idk. it sounds ridiculous but there's something clearly bothering this team and i'm trying to figure out what it is. something psychological is getting in the way of this team's ability.

it could be something stupid like maybe the team is getting up too early everyday for whatever reason (or staying up too late playing fortnite) and by gametime they're sleepy

or maybe they have too many off-the-field charity commitments like showing up at hospitals for sick kids

anyway, who knows, it's pointless for us to speculate. we don't know enough about the dynamics of the clubhouse. a sports psychologist hanging around the team all the time may be able to pick up on it.
