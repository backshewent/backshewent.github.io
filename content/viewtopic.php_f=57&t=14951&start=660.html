kruker wrote:To McNeal's point above, and it's been said on here hundreds of times, but all those years when we were giving up high draft picks we should have been flexing our financial muscle in the international market.


After the good work done by several folks here, this point looks to be real solid. I do remember last decade the Phils tried to go cheap with Aussies and Canadians. It didn't work because all those guys were no good. They were late to the party. While the Phillies low minor league teams and to a certain extent, their upper minors as well, are lousy with internationals, the big leagues are semi-lousy with star international players.

The Phils have only a handful or home grown international big leaguers. And only one can be considered a star so far. The second best of them just got suspended for 50.

More money, more scouts perhaps, building the best academies in MLB all were probably doable and probably would have paid off with at least a few big leaguers and even some stars.

Hopefully this has and will be changing. The fact that there are some good looking young Latins and Arubans in the system is encouraging. The contract for Gonalez, even if it goes belly up, at least shows the Phils are players for defectors and such.
