by dajafi » Wed Jun 24, 2009 10:15:26

As someone who's had a lot of blood drawn, I can tell you it's not a big deal. You sit in a chair and chat with the phlebotomist, she ties a rubber thingy around your arm and asks you to make a fist, you look away (I recommend this, from the first few times I had it done and was nervous about it) and relax; you feel a tiny pinch as the needle goes in, and in maybe 30 seconds (which bring no additional discomfort or pain) you're done. She puts a small gauze pad over the entry point, and a band-aid over that; you give your co-pay and you're out of there.
