Likely voter screens are not always clear cut. Different poll firms use different methodologies--Monmouth actually appears to be using at least two methods. In some elections, a lot of not likely voters show up, in others, even the likelies don't show up as much. I'm not certain, but I think 2008 and 2018 were two elections where lots of voters who were not considered "likely" showed up.

One way to tell--if you can compare likely voters to registered voters (in the same poll), you should see a big drop in undecideds. At this point in the election, the most unlikely voter is one who has not made up their mind.
