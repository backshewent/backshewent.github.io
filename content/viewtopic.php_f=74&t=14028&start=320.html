There's one ingredient for success that few really acknowledge--an almost delusional sense of optimism. The problem is, that's also an ingredient for spectacular failure. The difference is successful people have a knack for self-promotion that gets others to share their delusion.

It's not all about money--Mrs. Vulture is that way to an extent. We live in a pretty redneck town of about 11,000 (though there is a university here which makes a difference). There's an empty building on our town square. Mrs. Vulture gets it in her head that the building would make a good arts center. It had a leaky roof, in adequate HVAC, and lots of other problems. Two years later, there's an arts center there. She put in a ton of work, but she also got a lot of other people to put in work and got other people to write some pretty fat checks. The art center has been open about a year, and it's already considered an important community asset.
