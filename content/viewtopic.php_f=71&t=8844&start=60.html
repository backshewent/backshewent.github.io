kruker wrote:
Barry Jive wrote:my friend got pissed at me because i said "that's just wrong" to my other friend, like i was being rude for saying something wrong was wrong. his argument was that it was all opinion and that i can't call someone's opinion wrong. well, my explanation was, "in my opinion" is implied by anything that comes out of my mouth. obviously "that's just wrong" is my opinion and it's a waste to preface it.

but you know and i know that it's not actually opinion in this case. but regardless, i don't know why i bother in such cases.


Where have we heard this before?


The problem is in ordinary usage opinion has two different meanings. One is subjective--in my opinion, Sushi tastes good. Or in my opinion, Bubba Ho-Tep is a better movie than Godfather II. You can't really argue with me that I like Godfather II better than Bubba Ho-Tep, because I don't.

On the other hand, there's the usage of opinion that deals with things that may or may not be subjective. In my opinion, the Phillies were the best team in major league baseball in 2008. But someone could say that the Red Sox won more games with a tougher schedule, blah blah blah. The argument there of course is over the metric. I use WFC, while the other guy is using wins over a season.

But we really should not use the word opinion if we're talking about things that are clearly objective. Someone who says in my opinion, Elizabeth II is the Queen of England isn't really using the term correctly. The more appropriate term in that context is belief.

This issue also comes up when discussing morality--if we do not have certain knowledge of morality, are moral views beliefs or opinions?

So there are statements where the term opinion is clearly correct, and others where belief is the correct term, but a whole lot of statements where either could be used. "In my opinion, the Phillies will win the NL East in 2009" and "I believe the Phillies will win the NL East in 2009." have the same meaning.
