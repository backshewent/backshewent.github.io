by phorever » Mon Nov 16, 2015 15:16:20

on the hellickson trade:
agree with those who consider the prospect traded away to have insignificant value. guy with an terrible k rate when repeating gcl is about as long a longshot as it gets, no matter what the upside. this means that we paid just about the lowest possible price in a trade for a major league starting pitcher.

with respect to the possibility of other, better options being out there, just looked at the steamer projections on some of the names. i used the list of guys someone likely to sign short deals posted by one of the guys in the crashburnalley discussion of this issue.

kennedy is forecast to be about 0.3 run/9 better than hellboy. colon is forecast to be about 0.1 run/9 better than hellboy. buerhle about equal. fister worse, harang worse, kendrick much worse, lohshe worse, simon worse, guthrie much worse

fangraphs also has crowdsourced contract estimates. average years and aav for kennedy 3 and $12m;
colon 1 and $13mil; buerhle 1 and $14mil; fister 2 and $10.5mil. kk 1 and $3mil (hey, look... a bargain!)

and, concerning the innings eating... only 90 mlb starters logged 146+ innings last year...3 per team. of those, about 50 or so performed at least as well as colon, and will thus be more expensive and very unlikely to sign for just a year or two or be available via trade without giving up a top-20 prospect. of the 40 sub-colon, hellickson was at about the mid point in actual performance. leaving only around 20 in the eat as many innings, pitch as well group. most of those are under contract and half of them are over 30 and thus ticking injury time bombs. there just plain aren't many relatively cheap arms available who can be expected to be able to pitch as many above-replacement innings as hellickson.

good deal.
