Trump Blasts FCC for Not Approving Sinclair’s Deal with Tribune Media

Hunt Valley, Md. – A week after the chairman of the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) raised “serious concerns” about Hunt Valley-based Sinclair Broadcast Group’s proposed $3.9 billion acquisition of Tribune Media, President Trump on Tuesday night expressed his frustration in the form of a tweet. “So sad and unfair that the FCC wouldn’t approve the Sinclair Broadcast merger with Tribune,” Trump wrote on Twitter. “This would have been a great and much needed Conservative voice for and of the People. Liberal Fake News NBC and Comcast gets approved, much bigger, but not Sinclair. Disgraceful!” Ajit Pai, who was appointed FCC chairman early last year by Trump, said he couldn’t support the transaction in its current form. “Based on a thorough review of the record, I have serious concerns about the Sinclair/Tribune transaction,” wrote Pai in a statement. “The evidence we’ve received suggests that certain station divestitures that have been proposed to the FCC would allow Sinclair to control those stations in practice, even if not in name, in violation of the law. When the FCC confronts disputed issues like these, the Communications Act does not allow it to approve a transaction.” Instead, Pai said the proposed deal could become the subject of a hearing in front of an administrative law judge involving certain proposed divestitures. Sinclair in a statement said it was “shocked and disappointed” by the news, denying allegations of misrepresentation and lack of candor “in the strongest possible manner.” The acquisition would give the company 215 TV stations in 102 local markets.
