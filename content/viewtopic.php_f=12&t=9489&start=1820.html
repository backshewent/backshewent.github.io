by TheDude24 » Mon Oct 12, 2009 22:34:55

This series was so hard fought on both sides. The Phillies never trailed, series-wise, but it felt like their backs were up against the wall more than a few times. I don't remember feeling quite like that during last year's playoff run. Game 3 was epic and I'll never forget it. That weather influencing such an important game, and the postponement affecting the starting pitching rotations. From game 2 on, I noticed that Charlie was managing like every inning was the most important one of the year, and doing things differently than he did in the regular season. he had to, since this team is a little banged up, bullpen-wise. The state of the bench also makes it more of a challenge than last year, but you gotta work with what you got. I never feel like this team is out of it, and I love how they prove me right- this is why I love baseball, anything can happen. The Phillies beat the Rockies in 4 games, but this was not the same kind of NLDS that the Brewers gave us in 2008. I think we can all agree that this was much more intense. The Phillies in October. Yeah, this will never get old.
