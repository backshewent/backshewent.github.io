NYTimes' Joe Nocera (?!?!) writes two pages on Anat Cohen.

Everything about it, from the title ("Jazz's Skinny Stepchild" which refers of course not to Ms Cohen, who is zaftig, but to her instrument), is off.

I've gone on & on over the months & years about how fantastic Cohen is. And she is. And of course Nocera finds her so. But he's bafflingly ignorant of other contemporary clarinetists (he does get around to mentioning Don Byron and Ken Peplowski, but nary a peep about Evan Christopher, or Chris Speed), and of contemporary jazz, and of pretty much everything, that his piece cloys, rather than coaxes readers to go find out more.

So read the article anyways, but don't like it too much. Mostly go get you some Anat Cohen
