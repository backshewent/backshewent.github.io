by Philly the Kid » Fri Apr 17, 2015 20:08:37

I hope this is the right thread -- but serious question about Howard --

Is there a precedent for a guy having such a steep decline like this? I had always expected (hoped) that he would age like Big Papi. Not be the fearsome MVP candidate of his mid-20's, but a reliable slugger well in to his 30's.

Has anyone done a deep dive of theories on what happened to Ryan? I know the injury, I know the family stuff -- but that's not enough. I know he always struggled some with left-handed hitting and the shift has hurt him. But often-times a veteran hitter with power rep, is good at getting walks too.

Did he just "lose it" completely? Is it a mental thing? Did he lose bat speed. Is there a predominant theory about him?

I just feel terrible for the guy. To be an MVP and slug 58 home runs, and then struggle to bat .200 - while making top 15 slugger in baseball money -- ouch.

I'm over the pain for us fans -- of not having a competitive team. Or Ryan and Chase keeping their numbers up to be in HOF discussions. I'd just like to see the guy hit .260 with 27 hrs and 93 rbi. -- Anything can change I guess - but it really doesn't look like he'll get above .220 and get more than 10-11 hrs if that...

What happened to this guy?

If he ultimately is benched this year -- would he stick around for future seasons just to collect the pay-check? Is there such a thing as shaming a guy to retire or cut a settlement deal? What's the psychology of being demoted to bench duty and collecting 25mil ?

I think he should get paid for this year, and if he can't play anymore - just let the Phils make a reasonable offer to buy out the rest of his contract - maybe another 10mil or 12mil paid as 2mil a year for 5-6 years - and retire enjoy his life. No team is going to trade for him, not even a team with DH if he can't show any power anymore and or get up to .250

This is sad.

If Utley remains sub .250 all season,I think he should be benched or let go too.

I'd rather see suckitude from AAAA-ers or journeymen I have no emotional stake in. It's cruel to see our heroes like this...
