by TenuredVulture » Tue Jun 30, 2015 12:42:13

One interesting fact about much of the rural south--our public schools tend to be far more integrated than what you'll find in the Northeast. (To be sure, there are some really rural places with tiny school districts where this doesn't always apply.) Of course, in the metropolitan areas of the South, the same or worse pattern of residential segregation results in school segregation. But these days, segregation in schools requires a degree of population density that simply doesn't obtain in much of the rural South.

Our school district has over the last decade or so absorbed three small predominantly African American school districts, and now our school district is majority African American (I'd guess around 60%). There's no tipping point here, and while there has been some white flight, some of that reversed in the last couple of years. We also have school choice, so parents could find a more segregated public school for their children if they so desired. That's not to say there isn't racism and prejudice around here--there is. But for most people, the desire to send their children to the best school in the area trumps their prejudice.

Also, there is certainly a degree of "internal" segregation in the school. That is a real problem with no easy solution.
