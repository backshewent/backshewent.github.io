The Republicans performed well for Trump and his base while the Dems made some progress, but it was small and incremental.

The question is whether this afternoon or thereafter the Dems can reach an inflection point at which a sufficient number of Republican senators would vote to convict at an impeachment trial.

That’s a heavy lift politically.

Even if Republican senators could be convinced that Trump obstructed justice I could very well see them rationalizing that any such obstruction did not rise to the level of an impeachable offense.

However, that’s not the end of the political calculus for the Dems. The more they can credibly and successfully undermine Trump between now and the election, the greater their chances of defeating him.
