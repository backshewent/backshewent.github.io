Houshphandzadeh wrote:not sure if you guys missed it but Thad was at summer league the other day so I can only assume he's a Sixer for a life and there ain't no room in this town for the two of em


This is actually an under reported story that I think goes against a lot of the general thinking when it comes to Thad and the Sixers. How many guys Thad's position (career, finances and standing) are sitting on the bench in Orlando right now? If Thad was checked out, and resigned to trade demands, what possible appeal would Orlando in July hold?

I love the guy, and think his veteran leadership would be a huge asset to the Sixers moving forward. A lot is made about his age and the fact that he needs to move onto a winner- the dude just turned 26 years old. Say the Sixers are sneaky good this year, and win 35ish games? Wouldn't Thad want to stick around, opt into his option year, and see what another year of development with the core, the addition of Embiid and an extra lottery puts them? Hinkie has gone on the record of saying what an asset and influence Thad has had on the young Sixers already- maybe that trumps whatever trade deadline pick-up Thad might be worth?
