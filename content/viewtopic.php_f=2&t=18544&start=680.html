by Soren » Mon May 21, 2018 11:58:19

Mental illness is a touchy thing. I spend a lot of time thinking about blame and responsibility when it comes to getting treatment for it and the consequences of undiagnosed/untreated mental illnesses. Trying to make a comparison to another disease/illness doesn't work so well whenever I try, eg: On one hand, if you have the flu and get other people sick you're an asshole. On the other hand there isn't massive social stigma around getting the flu or around taking care of yourself once you get it. You also do eventually get over the flu. I'm not going to wake up one day and not be bipolar. If I stopped taking my meds it's very likely I would hurt myself or others. As a for instance, the year before I finally went out and got help I had myself convinced I could drive my car into oncoming traffic on 76 and I would be fine because I was invincible. I had enough sense left in the tank left not to try it but I earnestly believed it.

So all the "we need to do something about mental illness!" articles get my hackles up just a bit. Could be paranoia or whatever but I don't think compassion and easy access to care is the first thing people have in mind. I think the first thought is passing legislation to, in albeit narrow circumstances, discriminate against anyone with a diagnosed mental illness.
