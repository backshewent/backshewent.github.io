"Whose little boy are you?” Baldwin, Mongols & other books

by Youseff » Fri Sep 25, 2015 10:07:22

there's this guy Tai Lopez who seems to be a huckster of sorts and his big thing is saying he reads a book a day, which is possible, but you wouldn't have time to make goofy aspirational Youtubes. he said that he's not really an advocate of reading the whole thing, just scanning it and reading chapters or random sections that seem interesting to you. obviously that's fine, but don't say you read a book a day if that's what you're actually doing.

this type of thought has taken hold, I believe, with the Silicon valley type - not all of course, but I just heard an interview with angel investor Naval Ravikant where he says to do the exact same thing.

as a practice I'm not wholly opposed to it - I do it in bookstores all the time, but again, I don't say that I've read that book when I've just read 30 pages or whatever. It leads to this really superficial understanding of the material, and lacks nuance. granted, these people are probably talking about business books, which are generally about 85-98% filler.
