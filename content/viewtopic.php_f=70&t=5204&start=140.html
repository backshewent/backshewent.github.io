by Houshphandzadeh » Fri Apr 25, 2008 13:32:07

I guess my read on it will be pretty warped, because it was a HUGE deal for the museum community. They even asked all of us employees if we could chip in personally to help out. The American Art and Development Departments were total warzones in the two months after the announce of the sales, begging every big donor and looking for any possible grants to help them get the loot together. All other departments were on order to prioritize support for them.

Outside of the museum, there was definitely a lot of news coverage with a handful of front page Inquirer articles, including yesterday's paper. I can't say if Joe Kensington gave a crap or not, but it was a pretty big deal throughout the center city, youth, and arts communities.
