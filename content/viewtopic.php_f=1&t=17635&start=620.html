I was looking at the 2017 hall of fame balloting and besides seeing that old friends Pat Burrell and Matt Stairs got bounced without a single vote in their first years of eligibility (sad but unsurprising), Jorge Posada was eliminated after only one year of eligibility, albeit with 17 votes.

I surely would have thought New York bias alone would have kept him on the ballot, but am I missing something? 17 years, all with the Yankees (a rarity for any player in this day and age), a 121 career OPS+, 42.7 career WAR, and all for a catcher. Looking at b-r's HOF monitor stuff it does look like he would have clocked in below average for hall of famers at that position in most respects, but not exactly out of the argument either. You'd think all the WS rings would have given him the intangible thing.

Just kinda stunned he didn't get enough votes to stay on the ballot a couple of years. I can't think of a better catcher for the "oughts" decade, at least from an offensive point of view, and he stayed clear of the steroid thing.
