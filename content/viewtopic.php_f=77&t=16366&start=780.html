by Phan In Phlorida » Fri Jun 05, 2015 19:20:19

Oh fer cryin'... they're Ludwig Vistalite octobans (these are roto-toms). Octobans were originally a set of 8, melodically tuned to encompass an octave (hence the name), but a set of 2 or 4 is more common nowadays. The shorter ones are called high-pitch and the long ones called low-pitch. The Ludwig Vistalite ones I posted are 2 highs (6" diameter, 12" & 16" long) and 2 lows (6" dia, 20" & 24" long).

Here's a pic of a kit with 4 lows mounted in a cluster to your right...



I think I still have my 2 low 20 yr old Tama octobans (22" & 24") somewhere, probably stored in my mom's attic (NEPA). I thought about making my own set of super long (~36") ones but I couldn't find 5 3/4" OD acrylic tubes.
