Phillies vs Dodgers: NLCS Discussion

by BigEd76 » Sun Oct 05, 2008 17:52:04

For the seventh time in their long history, the Phillies are in the NLCS........and for the 4th time it'll be the Dodgers standing in their path towards a World Series. The Dodgers took advantage of a weak NL West and entered the playoffs with the worst record among the 8 teams, but they took advantage of Cubs miscues, had great pitching and swept the century-long losers. The Phils beat the Brewers in 4 games despite very little offensive production from Utley or Howard, but the starting pitching has been great and the role players like Victorino have stepped up. LA swept the Phils out west (though two of those games should have been Phillies wins), and the Phils returned the favor back home the following week. The Dodgers have Joe Torre's experience, great righty pitching and the lineup has been dangerous since that Manny guy arrived. Should be a fun series. 4 wins until the next champagne party.

(All games on FOX)

Scheduled Starters: (Joe Buck / Tim McCarver)

SUN 8:20PM at Los Angeles = Moyer vs Kuroda

by Monkeyboy » Sun Oct 05, 2008 18:51:25

Well the Dodgers is the team that's worried me since the preseason. They looked lost for a while, but then put it together down the stretch. Their pitching is my main concern (well, that and Manny and a few of the kids). Lowe, Billingsley, and Kuroda are all groundball pitchers with decent to excellent control. Add in the hitters being geared up for the playoffs and it's a recipe for lots and lots and lots of groundballs. I'm more worried about this series than whatever team will come out of the AL.

Taken together, those 3 pitchers have a total of:

595 IP
563 Hits
41 HRs
167 Walks
~ 2.10 GB/FB -- lots and lots of groundballs.

Based on their ballparks, the Phils and Dodgers should probably trade pitching staffs.
