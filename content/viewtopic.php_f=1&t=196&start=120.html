by Monkeyboy » Tue May 12, 2009 10:14:48

I haven't bowled in years, but I was in a league from grades 7-11. I still have my yellow dot ball somewhere. If there's anyone on here from the Middletown area, I used to bowl at Middletown Lanes, which was used by some local bowlers to sandbag local tournaments because the lanes were harder than most lanes. I would have a 180 average there and then go to a handicap tournament and get more handicap pins than I would have if I bowled at a regular lanes. I got a few trophies that way, though I just bowled there because it was right down the street from my house and I couldn't drive yet.

Of course, a lot has changed since then. The balls and pins are different, making higher scores easier, though 800 is still kinda ridiculous. I can't imagine throwing an 800. I threw one 700 in a tournament in Camp Hill. I think I still have a patch somewhere.

Middletown Lanes was also my first job. I cleaned it on weekends in exchange for minimum wage and free bowling. You could smoke in the lanes back then and half the mess was cleaning about a billion cigarette butts and all the other stuff that goes along with it. Disgusting job, but it seemed worth it for the $3.15/hr and free bowling at the time.

Well, I've never had a whole lot of success on Viper(Cheetah and Chameleon are my favorites). Not knowing exactly how you throw the ball, I would say your best bet is to start off playing an outside line, without much swing, and as the oil breaks down move deper inside and swing the ball toward the dry area. Probably don't want to use a whole lot of surface, as with only 37 feet of oil the ball may "roll out" before the pocket and lose hitting power. Probably something with the pin above the fingers, to help get the ball down the lane.
