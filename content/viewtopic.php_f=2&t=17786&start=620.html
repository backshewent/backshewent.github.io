I think there's a weird self-sabotage problem among rural whites, especially in the South. Consider--for non plantation owning whites in the South, slavery was an economic disaster--slavery seriously depressed wages for everyone else. (This actually sort of comes up in the old mini-series North and South, where a Northern Farmer was angry about how slave labor in the South depressed agriculture prices for everyone.) But poor whites fought against their own economic interests. They continued to do so, typically siding with rich whites rather than making common cause with poor blacks. Later on, they proudly resist unionization. Here, the "racist" angle doesn't quite work as easily. Yet, we've got a population that simply refuses to to defend its economic interests over and over again.

Obviously, though, race is a huge part of the problem. But there has to be some way to get people to understand how their racism makes it possible for people to screw them over again and again and again.
