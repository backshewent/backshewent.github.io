by RichmondPhilsFan » Thu Jun 12, 2014 12:10:50

drsmooth wrote:Cantor dropping his Congressional seat is pretty amazingly stupid. It's like losing a cushy job because you accidentally dropboxed your porn stash to your contact list.

I mean, how do you explain that to your spouse: "Uhhh, honey, I forgot to campaign this year, so I got #$!&@"

The national media's coverage of this upset has been extremely disappointing. Cantor DID campaign, that was part of the problem.

Cantor basically had two major mistakes in this election. The first was the one that'd been building up for years... the perception (rightly so) that he was more concerned with national politics than what was best for his constituents. There was no way he'd be able to refute that by working hard for a few months, but he's been successfully fighting that perception since at least 2008 (including defeating a viable challenge from Wayne Powell in 2012 who benefitted from an Obama bump). And let's be honest, it is a House party leader's job to remain focused on national issues... any complaints from 7th District constituents about Cantor being out of touch with them is mostly lunatic fringe type stuff, who are never going to be satisfied unless their guy is a complete parrot of their fringe opinions on every issue.

The second (and much larger IMO) mistake was that Eric Cantor was the one that made David Brat a household name. He started running cartoonishly negative ads against Brat back in early May, during a time in which you NEVER saw Brat signs or ads outside of rural Hanover County (major Tea Party stronghold).

Here was the lowlight:



It's a fundamental rule of campaign strategy that you don't give your opponent name recognition if he doesn't already have it. A lot of you know where I work (hint, I can see the VA Capitol from my window)--even most people I work with had no clue that Cantor had a primary opponent until these ads hit. Mind you, Dave Brat himself did not pay for a single televised campaign ad until the day before the primary.

Cantor never goes negative in reelection campaigns (the last time I can recall it was 2002 against Ben Jones). Granted, he doesn't typically face stiff competition, but I'd argue that Brat wasn't stiff competition until he got name-dropped by Cantor. I can't recall a single time that he's even mentioned his opponent in an ad before this election cycle (and he has been my congressman for over a decade now).

So yeah, Cantor lost this race b/c of people being upset with him, but he mostly lost this race because he announced to people that he had a challenger to be taken seriously.
