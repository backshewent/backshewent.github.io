Uncle Milty wrote:Weren't the majority of those near the end of the contract term where the remainder was likely paid or they were terminated for poor performance (cause)? Most of them were also more narrow in position not GM and president of operations.


Unless front office contracts are completely different than other exec level employment agreements, Cause definition has nothing to do teams performance, and Cause and Good Reason definitions only come into play when determining entitlement to severance and/or whether equity or phantom equity awards are subject to accelerated vesting

Of the "typical" prongs of Cause definition the one that seems most likely to be implicated here is willful misconduct (lying)...assuming they can prove that he lied to Harris et al. Others typically include conviction or no contest plea to felony or crime of moral turpitude, material uncured breach of the contract (this could also be in play I guess depending on what confi provision says), inability to perform duties which persists for X consecutive days, etc
