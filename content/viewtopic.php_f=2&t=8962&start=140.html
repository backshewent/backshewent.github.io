So been plowing through Sopranos again last few days, through Season 4 now. First time I really didn't get into it because all the irrational dunderhead characters bothered me, but its been better this time around (with an eye towards the symbolism...scenes with meat, the Tony/animal empathy, generational differences, etc).

Still my main problem is that I hate literally all the characters, but I suppose that's the point. It's interesting to try to rank the least likeable characters between Christopher, Janice, A.J (notwithstanding Livia who was basically a 2 dimensional foil for Tony's issues, and the other minor characters like Ralph, Jackie Jr., Bevilacqua, etc).

Then again, I can't deny that there are some great characters (especially Junior) and some of the scenes/plot points are hilarious (e.g., the Paulie and his mom's nursing home bullying storyline). Think my favorite episodes in S 1-4 are when Tony and Meadow go to Bowdoin, the Chris and Jon Favreau episode, and when Tony goes to Italy

I think it's probably common for a groundbreaking show not to age well. If one measure of a show's aging well is continued popularity in syndication, even noting that it's tougher for dramas in syndication than comedies, think about how shows that were path breaking at the time are never seen in syndication--Homicide, Hill Street Blues, even LA Law and 30 Something were pathbreaking and popular but as far as I know have never been widely syndicated.

One of the things about the show I thought was interesting is that unlike most "mafia" movies and shows, I found myself rooting for Tony et al to get arrested and convicted. I also figured Meadow would ultimately be the one to take over the family business.
