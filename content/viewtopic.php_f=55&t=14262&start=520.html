Why do you have to have a plan for 2014 CF? Opportunities arise all the time that you don't expect and can't anticipate (see the Blue Jays trade for example). I don't want to lock myself in to someone like Pagan in any event, but doing so prohibits you from being nimble enough to capitalize on those opportunities. If I'm going to lock myself into someone, that person has to be worth it. Pagan isn't worth it to me.

Also, I'm really not hung up on getting a CF as much as I am on getting signficantly more really good offensive players. I would overpay for Hamiilton and try to trade for a defense-first AAAA type CF with limited offensive ability.

The Philadelphia Phillies are one obvious fit for Soriano. Soriano’s presence would be a welcome fit for a Philadelphia outfield that currently consists of Darin Ruf, John Mayberry Jr. and Domonic Brown. Soriano totaled an .821 OPS this year while setting a career high with 108 RBI.
The Phillies pursued Soriano when he became a free agent after the 2006 season, but the Cubs won the bidding with an eight-year, $136 million deal.
