by The Dude » Thu Dec 03, 2009 09:32:55

The only time it was my decision to have to put an animal down was with our cat about 6 years ago. We got him as a kitten, and he was sick from the start. I would have to go home at lunch and feed him heavy cream and ice cream with a dropper b/c he couldn't smell anything. He eventually got over that (kennel cough) but then one day (the day the Eagles played in Atlanta, with Schaub starting b/c Vick was hurt) he just stopped doing anything. He was very lethargic, just sat in one space, and then would go hide somewhere. When cats are going to die, they tend to go somewhere to die alone. Very depressing. So we took him to the vet the next day, and he said the cat was having kidney failure. He said we could go to Jefferson's pet clinic and get him a kidney transplant. Since our insurance barely covered our vision, there was no way.

We set it up for him to be put down in a couple days, and when I left work that day to go pick him up to take to the vet, he was already dead. He was on this blanket in the office, we put him in there so our other young cat wouldn't try to keep playing with him. He was sitting there frozen with his eyes open. I wrapped him up in the blanket and took him to the vet, and they asked if I wanted the blanket back. I didn't think it would be cool to offer someone a cat's death shroud if they ever slept over, so I just let the vet keep it.
