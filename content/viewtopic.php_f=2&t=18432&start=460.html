by JFLNYC » Sat Dec 16, 2017 19:28:56

There is an article in today’s NYT regarding the new tax plan which allows as how tax subsidies for home ownership are a form of social engineering and, worse yet, social engineering which “distorts the economy” in a negative manner.

The notion that subsidies for home ownership “distort the economy” would be laughable if scaling back those subsidies weren’t so punitive and dangerous. It’s not as though our American economic model was carved in stone and handed down from on high. The whole idea of *any* economic model is to engage in social engineering; to encourage some activities and people and to discourage others.

Are lower corporate tax rates not social engineering? How about all but abolishing the estate tax? Or waiving taxes on private jet maintenance contracts? Or taxing investment income less than wage income?

Everything we do as a society is in a real sense social engineering. Civilization itself is social engineering and so is, by extension, taxes paid for the common weal.

The new tax plan is nothing more than its own social engineering. The problem in this case — which is particularly egregious in a democracy— is that, for no apparent reason other than greed and selfishness, it entrenches both winners and losers in their current respective positions both now and for generations to come.

Equally as bad and, I would submit downright dangerous, is the wrenching effect the new tax plan will likely have on the entire housing industry including real estate sales, mortgages and banking, new home construction, home restoration and repair, appliance and electronics sales, etc., all of which form such a large part of our economy.

Instead of engineering our society for the possibility of upward mobility and the accumulation of wealth for the majority of our society, the new tax plan codifies increasing inequality and encourages an increasingly rental society with a few uber wealthy owners and the rest of the society as renters. This new tax plan is, without hyperbole, a giant step back towards feudalism.
Jamie
