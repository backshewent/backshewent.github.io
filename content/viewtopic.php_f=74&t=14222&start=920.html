jamiethekiller wrote:Its probably been talked about a little already. What is everyones opinion for the hurricane effect? is any northeast state thats expected to go blue end up going red?


Really New Jersey and Connecticut would be the only northeast states competitive enough (and destroyed enough) that could possibly flip due to non-voters. The devastated areas of New Jersey are a mixed bag. While there is significant damage in Democratic strongholds across the Hudson from NYC, Republican wins in New Jersey are built on big margins in Ocean, Morris, and Monmouth Counties, and these areas also significantly devastated/impacted (Ocean and Monmouth are along the coast). Connecticut is a bit harder to predict because the devastated coastal areas are primarily Democratic leaning, but Connecticut is also a few points more Democratic than New Jersey to start with.

With this said, I doubt either flip, although it could impact the Connecticut senate race.
