Re: Random Slowhands and other Wheels Falling Off

by phatj » Fri Mar 13, 2020 23:11:34

TV is a TCL 4-series 43-inch. Laptop is a HP EliteBook G3 I think. It's upstairs and I'm lazy. Dunno the graphics card setup but it's some crappy integrated Intel. Still drives the display at 3840x2160 60Hz though. I also hooked up one of the 1280x1024 monitors because I have space for it on my desk so why not?

A few thoughts after using this all day for work:
* If you do screen sharing through Jabber or Webex or whatever, keep in mind that whoever you're sharing with probably has a 1080P monitor. To work around this, if you have a smaller monitor in your setup as well (as I do), move whatever you want to share to that and share it instead of the big one; alternatively you can temporarily set your biggun to 1080P.
* If you use RDP keep in mind that Alt-Tab app switching only works if the RDP session is full screen. I usually do no want my RDP session full screen as it is not my main focus at any given time.
* The corner dimming is definitely noticeable but not really problematic if you're relatively mobile. I roll around in my chair some to better see the corners but this is obviously not ideal. If you can afford it a curved, purpose-built 4K monitor would be a better choice.
* Going smaller than 40 inches would be a mistake, in my opinion, unless your eyesight is top-notch. I checked out a 32-inch 4K monitor at a store going on five years ago and while I probably would have been able to work with it then, I'm pretty sure I wouldn't now without my reading glasses.
* I haven't found a good solution for screen splitting. There's a product which looks pretty cool called UltraView which is $99 and no free preview. For it to serve my needs it would have to integrate with the OS so I could pick the screen I want to share in Jabber or whatever and 100 bucks is too rich for my blood for an experiment.
* Many applications open by default in full screen which is usually absurd on a 4K display. Resizing them is not a big deal on a case-by-case basis but becomes a PITA the 100th time you have to do it.
* Microsoft really needs to build something into Windows to handle this kind of scenario which I imagine is becoming more common. The snapping to the top, sides, bottom or corners that Windows supports is not really adequate with a really large, high-resolution display.

Overall I'm pretty happy with the setup. I did my work this evening with the main display split between three separate windows of the same application displaying different things, that I used to have as full screen windows that I would have to switch between with Alt-Tab, and even then would not have been able to see as much. Meanwhile I had Outlook open on my laptop screen and other stuff on the third monitor.
