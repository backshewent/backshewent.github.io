Anyone who tries to rationalize 2 years, $16M is fucking brain-dead.

We're paying a guy, and starting a guy, who's 36 years old in RF. When he was 33, he was below average. When he was 34, he was below replacement. When he was 35, he was very good. The problem is, Amaro BETTER expect that he's above average the next 2 year, or we shouldn't be p(l)aying him. But if he DOES expect him to be above average, that's just a big an issue.

And, Grote, you're talking about above average outfield defense in RF like it's a given. BBREF lists 2 stats for defense (Total Zone Fielding Runs Above Average, and BIS Defensive Runs Above Average). One of those stats says he was 7 runs below average last year in RF, and the other says he was 12 runs above average. FanGraphs has him at 3.7 runs below average. Why should I bank on above average defense from him?

If you think you're going to compete, you need a better player than this. If you're not competing, you play ANYONE but a 36-year-old, like, say, Ruf, and evaluate what you've got.

This is gonna be a fun offseason.
