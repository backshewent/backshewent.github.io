by phorever » Fri Apr 12, 2013 16:19:30

looking for silver linings...

looking at the fangraphs pitchf/x charts, halladay´s release point moved up an inch or two in game 2, cutting in half the difference between game 1 and his 2010-2011 level. similar story with fastball velocities... ft and fc speeds each about a half mph faster in game 2. one week behind due to flu, another because he didn´t push himself quite as hard in the offseason to try to avoid running out of gas in september october... still reasonable to expect some further improvement.

game 1 pretty crappy all-round. zone % lower than any full-season number in his career underlying the horrible bb/k/pa ratios. and explaining why swing% way down relative to career or even 2012. but weird things were happening when the braves did swing, and it got weirder against the mets. all of the armslot/velocity/movement analysis above might explain the .357 babip in game 1. but not the .800 babip in game 2. and his game 1 swing and miss rate was 14.75, way way above his career norm. game 2 was a little below career norm, but right at the rate he had in his pretty darn good 2008 season.

speaking of game 2, his zone% was back around career norms and better than his good philly years in that game. and the other funky thing about that game was that the fouls/pitch fouls/swing and fouls/contact rate were all off-the-charts high relative to his career. since the zone rates and swing and miss and contact rates were all pretty much in line with his career, that looks an awful lot like bad luck. combine the foul and babip anomalies, and game 2 might well have been the single unluckiest of his career.

or maybe he was just serving meatballs that were being driven deep and foul or missed by overly excited hitters (i don´t watch the games due to time difference and i wasn´t brave enough to read through what must have been horribly depressing game threads).
