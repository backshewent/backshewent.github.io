Random Baseball News/Discussion: Heading into the 2020s

by BigEd76 » Sun Sep 08, 2019 22:17:09

As the 2010s come to a close and we start a new decade:

-- The Rangers (1961) still haven't won a World Series
-- The Brewers (1969) still haven't won a World Series and haven't won a pennant since 1982
-- The Padres (1969) still haven't won a World Series, haven't won a pennant (or reached an NLCS) since 1998, haven't been to the playoffs since 2006 and haven't had a winning record since 2010
-- The Mariners (1988) still haven't won a World Series or an AL pennant and haven't been to the playoffs since 2001
-- The Rockies (1993) still haven't won a World Series, won a division title or lost an NLCS, and haven't won a playoff series since their 2007 NL pennant
-- The Rays (1998) still haven't won a World Series

-- The Indians haven't won a World Series since 1948 and haven't lost an ALCS since 2007
-- The Pirates haven't won a World Series or an NL pennant since 1979 and haven't reached the NLCS since 1992 (the last time they won a division title)
-- The Orioles haven't won a World Series or an AL pennant since 1983
-- The Tigers haven't won a World Series since 1984
-- The Mets haven't won a World Series since 1986
-- The Dodgers haven't won a World Series since 1988
-- The Athletics haven't won a World Series since 1989, haven't won an AL pennant since 1990 and haven't reached an ALCS since 2006
-- The Reds haven't won a World Series or an NL pennant since 1990 and haven't won a playoff series since 1995
-- The Twins haven't won a World Series or an AL pennant since 1991 and haven't reached the ALCS since 2002
-- The Blue Jays haven't won a World Series or an AL pennant since 1993
-- The Braves haven't won a World Series since 1995, haven't won an NL pennant since 1999 and haven't won a playoff series since 2001
-- The Marlins (1993) still haven't won a division title or lost an NLCS, haven't been to the playoffs since their 2003 championship and haven't had a winning record since 2009

-- The Royals haven't lost an ALCS since 1984
-- The Giants haven't lost an NLCS since 1987
-- The White Sox haven't lost an ALCS since 1993 and haven't been to the playoffs since 2008

-- The PHILLIES haven't had a winning record since 2011

See now, and the lesson I was going to draw from BigEd's wonderfully researched post was that championships come along for most teams once in a blue moon. So you'd better appreciate each game on it's own merits. If you become fixated on titles won and the "larger significance" of games, you're almost certain to end up disappointed, grumpy or bored most of the time since, let's face it, virtually all of them are "meaningless" in that context. Yet fans of baseball,even of those teams with even less success than the Phillies, still enjoy the game, since even after a lifetime of watching games it's amazing how often you can still be surprised or pleased by something that happens, and you never would have seen if you'd stopped paying attention.
