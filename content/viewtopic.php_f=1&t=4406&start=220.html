What I find very interesting about this story has been the reporting of it. Deadspin ran a long-winded, constantly updated report overnight Saturday into Sunday. It had the video and the firsthand account as the lead-in. If you had watched the video without first reading the eyewitness account, which later turned out to be a friend of the victim, then you would have struggled to see anything at all aside from a hothead driver running out of his car into oncoming traffic at night. It's a great example of how one's perception can be completely altered or controlled before he or she even sees the evidence at-hand (e.g. see things that aren't really there or vice versa).

I have watched that video 4 times now and see nothing in it aside from a driver running into oncoming traffic. I have no idea if Tony had the intent of doing anything other than doing everything he could to put himself in a position to win (/Andy Reid) because that's what he has seemingly always done.
