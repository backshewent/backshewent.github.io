When MLB does expand (because I think this is a when-not-if proposition), I think the two best and most available markets will be Montreal and Portland. But how do you realign? It opens up a lot of possibilities. I figure there would be some significant league switching. Or you could drop the leagues altogether - since they are essentially fictional vestiges now anyway - and re-align along strictly geographical lines.

Thinking about 8 4 team divisions, and keeping the AL/NL structure, I guess you'd get something like this:

AL North East
Cleveland
Detroit
Toronto
Montreal (expansion, placed here for rivalry purposes with Toronto)

AL East
New York
Boston
Baltimore
Washington (from NL, for rivalry purposes)

AL Central
Chicago
Kansas City
Minnesota
Milwaukee (from NL)

AL West
Oakland
Los Angeles
Seattle
Portland (expansion, placed here for rivalry purposes)

NL East
Philadelphia
New York
Atlanta
Miami

NL Midwest
Cincinnati
Chicago
St. Louis
Pittsburgh

NL Mountain Southwest
Arizona
Texas (from AL)
Houston (from AL)
Austin/San Antonio (Tampa Bay relocated)

NL West
San Francisco
San Diego
Los Angeles
Colorado

The advantage here is that you largely retain the league structure, most traditional rivalries are preserved (Yankees/Red Sox, Cubs/Cardinals, Dodgers/Giants), and restores certain "traditional" markets to their "correct" leagues - Milwaukee back to AL (I know it was NL before that), Houston back to NL, Washington back to AL. Texas moves to the NL to maintain the Texas rivalry with Houston, and you solve the Tampa Bay problem by moving to the, I believe, more baseball-ready Austin/San Antonio area.

Of course, you could go radical realignment and do a full-on geographical thing, regardless of league:

Great Lakes
Montreal (expansion)
Toronto
Detroit
Cleveland

East

Chicago
Chicago

Milwaukee

Southwest
Texas

Austin/San Antonio (moved from TB)

California
Los Angeles
Los Angeles
San Francisco
Oakland

West

San Diego
Seattle
Colorado

Works out pretty well, most choices here were made either to preserve existing or "natural" rivalries (i.e., it might have made more sense geographically for a division of LA-LA-SD-AZ, but keeping the Giants & Dodgers together, and the Athletics with the Giants and Angels with the Dodgers makes "natural" rivalry sense), and again moving TB to Austin/San Antonio not only solves a distribution problem in terms of divisions but also the real-world problem of what to do with that franchise.
