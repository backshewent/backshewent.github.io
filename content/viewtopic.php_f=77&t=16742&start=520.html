figured I'd check in on the Republican polls, seeing as about three weeks ago people started talking in DC about Trump's inevitable decline.

CBS News poll covering 10/4 - 10/8 put him in the lead at 27, Carson at 21, Cruz at 9, Rubio at 8, Fiorina at 6, Bush at 6, Paul at 4, and then the also-rans. Other than an outlier poll from IBD, Trump has been in the lead of every poll for the past two months.

If someone has a second choice other than Trump, it's Carson. Trump is also viewed as the most electable in a general election by Republicans. 81% of Republican voters think Carson is honest and trustworthy. 84% think Trump is a strong leader.

Bush is viewed very unfavorably. He should probably drop out of the race and swing his support to Rubio, but I doubt he does.

This is all as peole increasingly pay more attention, and more people tune in. The new normal is here to stay.
