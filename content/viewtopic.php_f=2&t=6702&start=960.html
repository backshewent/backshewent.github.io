by azrider » Sun Sep 27, 2020 00:59:30

Just bring your driver’s license and registration to the notary. Also bring another form of ID and if you have that letter, but just leave it in the car unless you really need it.

I usually start off these types of situations by saying I’m an idiot. It’s not much of a stretch for me, and it always helps to facilitate the process.

I hate giving people too much information. That’s just more stuff that can usually go wrong. If your registration matches your ID that has to be enough to get the ball rolling.

The notary will then x off the fields to fill in for you with that same form. Any questions or anything that you might have a feeling could go sideways, ask the notary. Might need some contact info from the previous lien holder and use what info is on the letter. If you don’t have the letter anymore, find some contact info online. Doesn’t have to be perfect. They’re not doing some grand theft auto investigation here.

I imagine the notary has a way to request information if there is a lien on the vehicle, but I’d skip that. Just send it into the state. They will tell by the vin who owns it or if there is a lien holder. You already got your personal identification notarized so they should send you a copy of the title in the mail in like 7-10 days. There might be an express service for an additional charge.
