I was in DC at the time, and it was mind boggling how few questions were being asked about what to do once the Iraqi military had been defeated.

I was just a Captain, yet I knew about the military-political linkage...what was the political endgame? I never believed for a second - and Powell is grossly incorrect about this - that the military and political spheres of influence are distinct and separate.

On a tactical and operational level, I was trying to get people to understand that there was no way Saddam was going to fight us the same way. He was obliterated during Desert Storm trying to fight Soviet tactics with obsolete equipment and poorly trained/semi-loyal personnel. I wasn't sure how he was going to fight the US, but I was damned sure he wasn't going to try and mass mechanized units in the open desert.

There was way too much victory disease and cheerleading, and way too little intellectual discussion and discourse. The conduct of Afghanistan and Iraq has motivated me to study as much as possible about the military profession because we sure as fuck screwed up badly as possible with these two operations.
