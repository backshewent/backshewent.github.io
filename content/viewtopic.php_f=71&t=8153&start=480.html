I love the NFL draft- always have... ever since I was a kid.

A few years back my wife's grandmom died during the week of the draft- the year they took Bunkley in the first round- and the funeral was the day of the draft so I missed a good chunk of the first day.

This year her grandpop turned 90, lived a good long life but wasn't doing so well over the past few months or so. Hospice said he had another 1-2 weeks to live yesterday but he died this morning. Sure enough the funeral is on Saturday.

So it looks like I'm going to miss at least the first round again this year. Don't get me wrong. I'm a selfish prick but I'm not THAT much of a selfish prick. I guess I'm just used to there being a 4 or 5 day waiting period before the actual funeral- like send out an obit, mourn for a couple of days, give people time for travel arrangements, go to the cleaners, whatever. They're doing everything in less than 48 hours. I mean were they pumping embalming fluid into his IV or something?
