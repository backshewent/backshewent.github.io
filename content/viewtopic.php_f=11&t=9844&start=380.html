by dajafi » Mon Dec 14, 2009 18:43:41

I'm trying to think this through from the perspective of the teams' motivations.

The Phillies want at the least to break even for 2010 (depending on one's take on Halladay vs. Lee), to stay under $140 million or so, and to have a no-doubt ace going forward. The Jays obviously just want to maximize the return for Halladay--to turn chicken shit into chicken salad. If they can add Canadian players in the deal, swell, but I have trouble believing that's really a big motivating factor.

Seattle presumably sees an opportunity in the AL West with the Angels much weaker and Texas likely to see its pitchers underperform compared to '09. They've got money to spend, at least for 2010.

Lee gives them a deadly top of rotation at a bargain price, takes pressure off King Felix (not that he was exactly struggling under the burden of high expectations), and shows the Zdurencik regime's commitment to contending. I don't see why Seattle would do it if Lee weren't involved.
