As someone who has dealt with pretty bad social anxiety in the past, I do sympathize with Greinke on some level. Harmless, normal things people do can REALLY irritate you sometimes. We can debate how normal it is for a grown man to be seeking autographs, but it’s generally harmless.

And when people request things of you, even nicely, it can feel like they’re targeting you with the intent to make you feel uncomfortable. I often had this feeling. “Why are they trying to make me uncomfortable? Screw them”. And there’s a tendency to want to lash out at them for it and do the exact opposite of what they want you to do, just so that they can feel as uncomfortable as you do, and they’ll leave you alone and you won’t have to deal with them again.

It’s a “natural” feeling for someone with that condition to have, but you have a choice as to how you deal with it, and you have to realize that’s not an effective way to react. You kind of have to do a cost benefit analysis and decide how your actions might be affecting your condition.

I got a lot better when I went against those instincts and “did what normal people do” even if I didn’t want to. This doesn’t mean being doormat or a sheep, but sometimes you need to do things that you don’t want to do in order to get better. Maybe he had a good reason for not wanting to sign, in which case, he probably could have explained it. My guess is he probably didn’t have one.
