Quite a difference between "might have to move to corner OF" and "move to first base." Particularly in his mid-20s.

You don't pay $100m+ to put a guy at first base--or, to put it another way, if they thought they'd have to do that with Tomas, they wouldn't pay him nine figures anyway and it's moot. The Phillies have gotten this exactly backwards, as they have so much else, but unless you have a big developmental score, first base really should be the place you find a misfit toy: some big ungainly dude who draws walks and mashes, maybe just in a platoon context, and doesn't cost much.
