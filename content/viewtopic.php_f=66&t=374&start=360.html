1 wrote:
Wasn't President's Day TWO WEEKS AGO?!? GM needs to get a calendar.

Is it Presidents' Day or President's Day?


I was listening to a radio report on this a while back. A couple of maybe interesting facts:

The official name of the federal holiday is still Washington's Birthday. It's commonly thought that Washington's and Lincoln's birthdays were merged into some sort of official Presidents' Day, but this never happened and the term is pretty much the creation of advertisers. Some states do refer to the holiday as Presidents Day or Presidents' Day, following common practice.

Washington's (or Lincoln's for that matter) actual birthday can never fall on the third Monday of February. This is true whether you use the calendar in use at the time of Washington's birth, or that when the holiday was declared.

President's Day is almost certainly wrong, since if the holiday is referred to this way it is referencing at least two presidents. I would think Presidents' Day would be more grammatically correct than Presidents Day, although you'll see it both ways. PA refers to it as Presidents' Day, NJ as Washington's Birthday.
