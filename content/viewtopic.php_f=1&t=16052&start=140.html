Regarding QBs and winning SBs. Or even "reaching SBs".

Randall Cunningham's core decade was 1988-1998.
The QBs who won SBs in that period:
Montana (2), Aikman (3), Young, Favre, Simms, Rypien, Elway (2)

Donovan Mcnabb's core decade of 2000-2010

Brady (3) P Manning, Rothlisberger (2), Eli, Brees, Rogers, Dilfer, Brad Johnson

If you fill in 98-99, 11, 12, 13 you add Flacco, Warner, Eli again, R Wilson

Here's another way of putthing things with regard to SBs and QBs:

Starr, Namath, Staubach, BRadshaw, Montana, Aikman, P Manning, E Manning, Rogers, Brees, Young, Favre, Elway, Plunket, Dawson

See the pattern? The overwhelming number of SBs were won by HOF QBs.

QBs that didn't win but reached SB include Tarkenton, Marino, Elway, Kelly, Dawson, Favre, P Manning, Warner, Brady

So the Brad Johnsons, Mark Rypiens, Trent Dilfers are the exception, not the norm.

Not sure where one would put Phil Simms or Jim McMahon?

So the question is, where would Foles fall in this spectrum?

Again, I don't think we know yet, but the question for me remains the same -- can he be in the range of Flacco to Brad Johnson, or is more likely a Kerry Collins ? Because he sure isn't Brees, Rogers, Brady caliber... and he's not a running QB who can hurt you like McNabb or RC (or R Wilson, Kaepernick, Cam, etc..)

There is a correlation between elite QBs and reaching and or winning a SB. Though there are a few exceptions....
