I played a good bit as a teen, then took thirty years off (I maybe swung a racket twice in that time), so it was like I was starting over again. Joined an adult school group class and it was a lot of fun. Co-ed, and no one cares what your level is (if you're worse than them it probably makes them happy). Later met up with a lot of the same people either for an adult class in a neighboring town or just for recreational games. A good instructor who keeps it fun and interesting is important, but it's not a huge investment either way. Maybe your gym has an a beginner adult class too? Though an adult school class will usually be very cheap , and you 'll still get a refresher on the basics.

In the meantime you could go whack it against a high wall (PiP)
