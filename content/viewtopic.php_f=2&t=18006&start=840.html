by phatj » Wed Oct 21, 2020 23:08:05

Did I mention on here that my wife and I decided back in August that the Boyertown school board had their heads up their asses and that we couldn't trust them to make intelligent decisions, and so we pulled our kids out and put them in a cyber charter school? I'm so glad we did... the school board decided/realized like three days before classes were supposed to start that they didn't have the staff (teachers, nurses, custodial, bus drivers, etc.) to actually pull off the hybrid plan they had decided on, so everyone was doing full-time remote learning again. Then a few weeks ago they started doing in-person classes in a hybrid model, and while nothing bad has happened (yet), I've been hearing from other parents that the kids who are remote are getting the short end of the stick. Finally, they're planning to go back to in person full-time starting next week, but... our district straddles Montgomery and Berks, and while Montgomery's numbers have been steady, Berks' have not.

The cyber charter school is not something we would ever have considered absent this pandemic and I still have my misgivings, but it's so nice to have the kids in classes designed from the ground up for online instruction, taught by teachers that have been doing it for years (and by a school - PALCS - that has been doing this since 2004). They'll be doing this for sure through the end of this school year, and then we'll reevaluate. The one daughter with anxiety issues surrounding illness may stay in it through high school, who knows.
