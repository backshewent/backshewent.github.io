by JFLNYC » Sun Dec 02, 2012 13:07:01

I don't like Swisher's contract demands either. And he's probably going to get ~6/$100MM. Under other circumstances, I wouldn't bother with him or any of the other FA's in this year's highly-overvalued crop.

But the circumstances.

Halladay and Utley are in the last year of their contracts and it's possible that either or both could be very near the end of his/their careers. With the exception of Hamels and, arguably Papelbon, every one of the best players on the team -- Ruiz, Howard, Utley, Rollins, Halladay and Lee -- are now well into their 30's and past their prime.

I think you've got to go for it this year. The problem is it's a year filled with risky and/or overpriced FA's. But, if you're going to go for it, you're going to have to hold your nose and overpay. So the question is -- knowing that you're going to be overpaying -- where is your money best spent.

Upton's gone, Vic's apparently not an option and Bourn's game is so predicated on speed that he could turn into Juan Pierre overnight. Hamilton's just got too many risks.

I think if you asked anyone here whether they'd take Pagan and Swisher each on 2-year deals, Pagan at $11MM/yr. and Swisher at $16MM/yr., everyone would ask: "Where do I sign?" Swisher is consistently a 4+ WAR/yr. player and Pagan has averaged 3.5 WAR/yr. over the last 4 (although he's been very up and down). Of course it's possible you might be able to work a trade for a Crisp (and I'm on record repeatedly for Crisp) or Choo or Asdrubal Cabrera or somebody else. But, failing that, if you're talking the current FA crop, the 2 guys who probably best fill the Phils' needs and who stand to maintain their production over the next 2 or 3 years at least, are Pagan and Swisher.

The Nats have gotten better. The Braves have gotten better. This is no longer a roster where you can just tinker around the edges. The team needs an impact player or two. With the aging core and expiring contracts the Phils have, you either go for it this year -- with the full understanding that 4+ years down the road the Pagan and/or Swisher contracts could smell to high heaven -- or you might as well start the breakup of the team while they still have value.
