Green Bay was $10M under the cap in 2015 and will be at least $24M under the cap next season. They're struggling to keep talent around Rogers because they refuse to sign any free agents, ever. So if they can't retain their own drafted players, they're relying on developing players and hitting an unsustainably high percentage rate in the draft.

Atlanta also had $10M in cap space last season. Their problem isn't so much Ryan's contract as it is Ryan's contract PLUS Julio Jones's contract. Ryan's cap hit for 2015 was $19.5M, but Jones was an additional $12.4M. In 2016, that will increase to $23.75M and $15.9M. Those are good players to lock up, but 1/4 of your cap figure on two players is quite a constraint. Even still, they have $19M in cap space for 2016 going into FA and the draft.

No, I can't defend Flacco's contract. That thing is an atrocity. He has the second biggest cap hit of any NFL player in 2016 ($28.55M), second only to Brees at $30M. But it should be noted that fifteen QBs will have a cap hit of $17M or higher in 2016. Nineteen are at $15M or higher (although one is RGIII). And three of the QBs who fall below that line are on their rookie contracts (Winston, Bortles, and Mariotta).
