From National Review's blog:

The much-maligned youth vote appears to have showed up for Obama. In the entrance poll I'm looking at,22% of voters were 17-29, and he got 57% of them. Interestingly, Huckabee also did well among (relatively) young voters, scoring higher among voters 17-29 and 30-44 than among older voters. Also, 37% of GOP voters said it mattered a great deal that a candidate shared their religious beliefs. Huck got 55% of them. 30% said it mattered somewhat and Huck split them with Romney, Huck 28, Romney 26. Romeny won 39% of the voters who cared not much/not at all. Huck got 2% of the not at all.

(Re: increase in youth vote between 2004 and 2008) Andy Kohut says it was 17% in 2004, and that up to 22% is a notable bump.
