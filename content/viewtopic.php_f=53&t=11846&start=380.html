by joe table » Thu Feb 17, 2011 14:17:59

Yeah Cole's "straight" 4 seam had about 4 inches of fade. The cutter like you said had almost no horizontal movement but ended up about 5 inches lower than the 4-seam according to the Lefkowitz site. Righties hit HRs off the cutter at a high rate last year but overall it was arguably a tougher pitch vs RHB

Cutter vs RHB: 228 pitches, 23.4% whiffs, 49% GB - 7% LD - 44% FB
Cutter vs LHB: 66 pitches, 13.5% whiffs, 60% GB - 13% LD - 37% FB

It's definitely a weapon for him. If he and Chooch can tighten up the sequencing and figure out pitch patterns that work well vs certain types of hitters maybe he can improve, since he has just as many weapons to work with as any LHP in the NL not named CLIFF IMO
