To be fair, all I'm suggesting that because you can measure a player's offensive contribution by looking at the outcome of an at bat, and those outcomes are entirely objective--hit, walk, strike out, etc. Moreover, advanced statistics tell us pretty clearly how those events contribute to scoring runs and winning games.

Defense, by contrast is tough. At some point, subjective judgment enters into calculations. Errors are usually obvious, but how do you measure how great a great defensive play is? As you note, sometimes a stolen base is the pitcher's fault, and every once in awhile, it's the infielder's fault.

Sometimes, a fielder needs to make a great play because of a bad throw.
Sometimes, a play looks routine because of the fielder's position.
