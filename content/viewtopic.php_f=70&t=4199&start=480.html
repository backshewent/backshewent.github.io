If it's a spider bite you should be able to tell. There should be a place where it looks like the thing sunk his jaws into you (or whatever those creepy things use to bite you with).

Anyway, a great cure for a spider bite is to wet an aspirin and put it directly on the place where the bite is. You do that for a while. Then make a paste out of some baking soda and put it on the bite.

I know it works great if you do it pretty quickly after the bite occurs. It works OK later too. My wife has pretty bad reactions to spider bites and we've used this cure and it worked great. The first time it was on her calf and it swelled up pretty good before we looked up the aspirin/baking soda treatment in some book. Anyway it worked well. The second time we treated the bite pretty quickly after it happened and the thing never even swelled up at all.
