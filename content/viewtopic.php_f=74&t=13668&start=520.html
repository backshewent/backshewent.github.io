by TenuredVulture » Mon Jun 04, 2012 14:21:47

Part of the problem this nationalism discussion is running into is a failure to be precise with usage. Nationalism implies a nation--either a bona fide actual nation like France, or a historic nation where people are linked usually by an ethnic identity as well as language and religion.

Language often is a central issue--Quebecois are generally ok with being Canadians as long as official business is conducted in their version of French. Black South Africans got very upset during the apartheid era about the Boers insisting on using Afrikaans in schools. It's easy to see why this is a problem--one need not assert superiority, but if the main business of the place where you've lived is conducted in a language other than yours you're very much going to be a second class citizen.
