pacino wrote:
RichmondPhilsFan wrote:So has anyone read (or going to read) the book Catalyst that sets the stage for Rogue One? I'm a big fan of James Luceno's books, so I'm biased, but I thought it was good, and sets the stage for the relationships of all of the characters quite nicely.

It helps if you've read Tarkin (came out last year) as well, but it's not a must.

you're the SW book man, what's it about?

Basically the origins of the Death Star project and the relationship between Krennic and Galen Erso (Jyn's father), with some Tarkin-y goodness thrown in. Galen is a genius in all areas, but especially in energy research. He'd been researching Kyber crystals (the crystals the Jedi use to power their lightsabers) and trying to figure out how to make sustainable clean energy for poor planets.

Krennic, who is in charge of the superweapon portion of the DS project, manipulates Galen into refocusing the research in a way that leads to the Death Star's superweapon. He's basically the SW version of Einstein--he didn't build the atomic bomb, but without his unparalleled genius and theories, no one else could've made the weapon work.

We also meet a helpful smuggler (not Han Solo) who turns out to have a heart of gold (again, not Han Solo) and Forrest Whittaker's character Saw Gerrera.

It's especially interesting because it starts one year into the Clone Wars and ends two or three years after Order 66. It also clarifies why it took nearly two decades to make one DS but the second was so much more quickly accomplished (although I still believe that Palpatine was already building the second one before the first was destroyed).
