Just thinking out loud about about Roy Moore and the state of the Republican Party:

I've long said that in terms of policy, Trump is really a mainstream Republican, though he's more inept than the last Republican President in the foreign policy realm and he's not I guess a free trader. But it does seem, despite small differences in policy, the "establishment" style of Republican Party politics is about to go the way of Country Club Republicanism. So, we've gotten rid of dog whistle racism in exchange for the explicit kind, a complete rejection of reality mattering, and a world where being outrageous is considered an electoral asset, not a liability. In such a world, Roy Moore easily gets the nod over a pretty similar Luther Strange. Now, here's the thing--this may not cost the Republicans a seat in Alabama. Roy Moore isn't establishment, but he's not even Ted Cruz. He's more like Loy Mauch. I doubt any of you have heard of Loy Mauch, but he was a Republican State Rep in Arkansas who was a neo-confederate and said things like "If slavery was so awful, why didn't Jesus condemn it?" He was also a member of the White Supremacist organization The League of the South.

But here's the thing that should provide at least a sliver of solace--Mauch lost his bid for re-election. By a considerable margin. To a Democrat. Even in crazy ass parts of the South, there seem to be limits. And if the Republican primary electorate has really gone bat shit crazy, and there's no evidence that they have not, then maybe the party really is poised to become a rather marginal political force again, as it once was.

Now, I've been wrong about the demise of the Republican Party for the last ten years, but one thing I do know about medium term predictions in politics--there's almost no way to predict anything more than an election cycle out. And if you don't believe, just look at 2008 or 2016. Predicting the outcome of those elections two years prior would have gotten you laughed at. So I'm not saying anything about the Senate or the House next year. Because I really don't know, and no one else does either.
