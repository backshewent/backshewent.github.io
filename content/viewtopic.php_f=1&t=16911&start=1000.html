I'm not sure why JU, or anyone else has, to defend themselves and their preferences regarding golfers, or tennis players or whatever.Teams we root for due to accidents of geography, or upbringing or whatever, and only occasionally by a conscious choice. Individual athletes, unless we know them personally, and I'm usually glad we don't, we pick because of the way they play the game, or conduct themselves in matches, or maybe because of some public statements they've made that lead to us to believe they'd be cool to have a beer with, or better having a beer poured over their head. Looking for logic in that seems crazy.I hate Phil who seems the embodiment of Masters of the Universe Republicanism, but that probably describes 90% of the players on the tour. I like Tiger for the excitement he brought, and brings, to golf, and his public pronouncements have always been calculatedly anodyne, so I'm willing to overlook the indisputable indications that he treats the people around him like dirt. Patrick Reed looks, and acts like and overfed frat boy, so I'm perfectly willing to believe the worst accounts of his behavior. As long as we recognize that we don't know any of these people and certainly shouldn't act out any behavior that will affect them in real life, who cares if we pick favorites, and whether these favorites make any sense? Yeah, it's nice to watch a well-executed chip shot, but without rooting interests the sport of golf would die out pretty damn quickly.
