drsmooth wrote:I read this brief essay the other day that offers a brief, mild defense of Machiavelli as something other than a cynical, manipulative Rasputin-by-the-Mediterranean.

Basically its author suggests Machiavelli was a master ironist - he intended its reader to take him literally, and so walk right into taking personally disastrous political steps that would result in his total failure as a leader.


And then I read Rosenstein's 3-pager, and I thought, "hmmmm"....

As a former state AAG and a current in-house attorney at a state agency, let me say this unequivocally: state and federal Departments of Law are very rarely concerned with the political ramifications of their recommendations. They may point out to the client/executive what the potential pitfalls may be, but the political calculus is left to the politicians. Their recommendations are based on the legal/ethical/regulatory ramifications. It's not that those people aren't politically savvy--many of them are far more savvy than the appointed or elected officials they are advising. But the political calculus just isn't their concern.

So this type of recommendation isn't particularly surprising. And I don't know that the timing of the recommendation memo is either--Rosenstein had probably reached this opinion some time ago but wasn't confirmed until the end of April. What's weird/suspicious/insane is that Trump received the recommendation and immediately acted on it within hours.
