Eh, I dunno.

I didn't think it was a great speech, but I wasn't expecting a great speech. My wife was somewhat upset because she thought it was effective. I think it showed the characteristic mean-spiritedness (how do you attack community organizers? Was that just coded racism? Granted, I doubt there's a community organizer alive who's a Republican, but still--you just don't choose to do that unless you've got a pretty big heart) and intellectual vacuity ("drill baby drill"? really?) of the Republicans. But of course I'm gonna think that. She read from a teleprompter without fucking up. Most successful politicians (and anyone at that level is successful) can do that.

Something I read today, can't remember where right now, was that unlike Bush in '04, McCain can't win this election simply by mobilizing the base--though he has to do that. Palin, a faith-based selection if ever there was one, obviously excites the hardcore Republicans. She obviously turns off the hardcore Democrats. I don't think she draws the independents and marginal Democrats, whom McCain needs to win, particularly after Obama's pretty good DNC effort to bring those people home.

Maybe I'm wrong. I didn't think the electorate would reward Bush for his first-term failures, but the Republicans managed to make that election about Kerry's personality (or their caricature of it) rather than Bush's record. As I've written before and as Trent put it in the last thread (damn that mother filled up fast--I guess you all needed to vent after the shit sandwich fed us all by Mr. Durbin), I just don't want to see the Republicans rewarded for the awfulness of the Bush years. But maybe the usual bloody shirt waving, the garbage and distortions thrown at Obama, and the endless willingness of McCain's admirers in the press to carry that message, will obscure all the policy arguments.
