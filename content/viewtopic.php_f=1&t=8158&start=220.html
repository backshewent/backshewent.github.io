by ReadingPhilly » Mon Apr 13, 2009 16:21:37

wow. i had to stop at office during work and decided to check espn like i normally do if i get a few minutes and was stunned when i saw the headline on the sidebar of espn. i literally started shaking so much the cursor just moved from one side of the screen to the other for quite a while.

for this 23 year old lifelong fan i have never known any other voice and i think it is special that those on here 40 and younger can say the same thing. when whitey passed away i was merely a kid who couldn't fully grasp the situation. it took 12 years for me to realize what that day in 1997 meant. i'll miss those summer nights as i typed away here on back she goes when i whipped around as harry shouted "well hit" or "long drive" only to see the outfielder catch it just shy of the warning track. admit it, that was part of the charm. there's a reason i flipped on the radio at end of game five of the nlcs and that reason was to hear harry kalas make the call that i had longed to hear for such a long time. really, it was that call that i lived for. how special is it that we got to hear him crown us as the world champions of baseball once again before he left? harry might be gone, but i know when the phillies hit a homerun i'll think "outta here" for the rest of my life.
