by mcare89 » Thu May 17, 2018 22:56:28

I think the only way that trade makes any sort of sense is conditional on two things.

1) A Kawhi extension. You just can't give up that kind of depth without a guarantee Kawhi's staying. You're in too good a position right now to make that kind of home run swing.
2) Lebron's coming. The only thing that cushions the blow on that sort of trade is that you still have your max cap space, which you would then have to use immediately, because any new Kawhi deal would soak up that space. It would have to be contingent on the idea that you're building an absolute super team of a starting lineup.

Even with those two things, it's still a massive overpay for a pending UFA who barely played this year due to an injury situation that is far from resolved. That's two starters, the #1 overall pick in the draft 11 months ago, another lottery pick and more assets. That's a desperation trade, and the Sixers are not desperate right now.
