Barry Jive wrote:I'm head over heels for three songs by both Squeeze and Cheap Trick, which is just short of the required amount to get me to pay a bunch of money to see two bands.


Well, Squeeze has about three dozen tuneful songs they can pull out their collective asses and have you swearing this was your favorite song by the second verse; so as long as they give them enough time on stage I'm sure this will be worth it. They're definitely there to entertain, too, in a very non-pretentious way. At least they were last time I saw them, and one doubts they've developed pretensions in the interim.

I saw Cheap Trick in a free 4th of July show a few years back and that was about right. They have a few songs I don't mind hearing again, and of course everyone can sing along with Surrender. Among arena rock bands of the 70s they would be about the most palatable to me.

I'm a little worried about the dynamics of the crowd and whether all the Cheap Trick fans will regard Squeeze as wankers and Squeeze fans seeing Cheap Trick as idiots. There's not a lot of commonality between "I Want You to Want Me" and "Pulling Mussels from a Shell". it's too bad Gilson Lavis isn't touring with Squeeze, or at least both bands would have large drummers in common.

I think I've talked myself into going if I can get tickets.
