Werthless wrote:Thought you might remember something from law school about advanced directives and dying.


There was an "Elder Law" class. I never took it.

I'm pretty sure the laws differ from state to state. I think, but don't quote me on this, that the next of kin has full control over what happens to your organs. Even if you have a donor card or mark the box on your driver's license, if your next of kin does not approve, then the hospital won't take the organs. I also think that if the next of kin wishes to donate your organs and you do not, your organs get taken, but I'm not sure about that.

You can override all of that with a living will. That will specify who the person is that has the right to direct the hospital what to do and probably can also specify which organs you're willing to donate and which you wouldn't donate.

I thought the rights of the deceased and the rights of the next of kin were discussed ad nauseam during the Terry Schiavo trials.
