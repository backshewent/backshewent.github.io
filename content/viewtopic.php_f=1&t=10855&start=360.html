by Phan In Phlorida » Tue Jan 13, 2015 18:56:01

When I was a kid in NEPA, NFL fandom was split amongst the Cowboys, Steelers, Colts, Raiders, Packers. An Eagle fan was more rare than an actual eagle. This was before the NFL regional broadcast rules, so the networks would televise the good teams at the time. I was a Colts fan until my interest in football waned around junior high. My siblings are a split of Steelers and Cowboys. There were some Bills and Jets fans because of "OJ" and "Broadway Joe". But I cannot recall a single Eagles fan amongst the kids in school or the neighborhood. Quite frankly, I didn't even know Philadelphia had a football team until I was around 10-12 yrs old because the Eagles were so bad and irrelevant they were never on TV.

As I said, this was before regional broadcast rules. So kids picked their team based on the most important factor... the logo/uni they liked best of the teams most often on TV. And prior to the turnpike & blue route, NEPA and Philly were kinda isolated from each other. NEPA was much closer drive-wise to NY. Hence why NEPA has as many fans of the Mets and Yankees as the Phillies. Actually, from what I've heard, it's more Yankees now since the Phils AAA moved away.
