by Phight On! » Wed Apr 23, 2008 17:20:23

Bill McNeal wrote:My Mom-mom passed away last night, her son was going through the rosary with her and, as soon as he finished he said goodnight mom, and she took a deep breath and then stopped breathing. Her last words were to me on Monday about my son. I miss my mom-mom


I'm sorry to hear that. I was really pulling for her to make it. It sounds like a very peaceful way to go and as much as it sucks that she had to pass on at least you can take some comfort in knowing that.

I certainly don't want to take anything away from your loss but I just wanted to share the story of how my grandmom passed.

After getting assurances from every doctor that my mom-mom was treating with that she had 4-6 months to live, my parents left for a short vacation/business deal that my dad was working on. If they didn't go they would have lost a few thousand dollars so they decided to go. Within 2 or 3 days it was apparent that she wasn't going to make it anywhere near 4 months so we called my parents and told them to get on the next flight home. I don't know if anybody has experienced what doctors and nurses call "the death roll" but it's this awful sound that people make when they have lung cancer and are about to go. She was in and out of consciousness every few minutes and it was like she was talking to her mom, dad, and sister who was murdered (it was like she was having a conversation with them- most likely she was hallucinating from the morphine but who really knows?). Anyway she wanted me to hold one hand and my brother to hold her other and then she kept asking for my mom. So my parents took the first flight home and took a cab to her house and once she heard my mom's voice her eyes opened. She smiled at my mom, brother, my wife and me and then took one deep breath, and she was gone.
