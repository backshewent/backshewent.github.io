by swishnicholson » Thu May 01, 2008 08:39:25

jerseyhoya wrote:What language does a "J" sound like a "Y"?


Are you thinking of Latin? Theories vary, but generally in classical Latin even though a consonantal "I" will often be transcribed as as "J", the pronunciation will still be that of the "i" sound combined with the following vowel.

So, in Julius Caesar, the beginning of Julius is pronounced sort of as (longish I + U), or kind of eeyuu, similar to a y sound.
