CFP wrote:Mechanics of what McCain did: Is that common? Whenever I tune in for votes I just hear "Senator so and so, aye, senator so and so, nay" and just assumed they voted electronically at an assigned area. Was the thumbs down in front of the clerk a bit of showmanship?


No--the whole thing was bizarre from a procedural standpoint. Basically, the Senate was going to pass a something that everyone knows would be a disaster, but then the House and Senate would form a conference committee. Whatever the conference committee came up with would be sent back to both chambers. The idea was the conference committee would basically do whatever with healthcare--know one knows what that outcome would be. But it wouldn't be subject to debate and such. At least part of McCain's objection was to the unusual procedural maneuvers needed to get this far.

And let's not be too laudatory towards the Senator--he could have forestalled this whole fiasco by staying in the hospital. It does seem like he made up his mind at the very last minute, which I suppose is pretty unusual these days.

On the other hand, I wonder if McCain's voting to proceed and then voting to kill the thing hasn't done maximum damage to the Republicans. And the Dems taking the Senate just got slightly easier, now that Heller is pretty damaged and Collins might try to run for governor of Maine.
