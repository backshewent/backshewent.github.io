I have a Yamaha DTX 560 that I bought at Guitar Center as a floor demo. It was crazy cheap, and I wouldn't have ever made the leap to e-drums if I hadn't gotten such a good deal. I love it for playing in pit orchestras and giving lessons to my sons. There are "lessons" built into the trigger module, but I haven't really explored those.

Yamaha has since updated the series to 402, 502, 702, etc. I replaced the trigger module with the newer 502 model, and there's pretty much nothing this kit can't do.

Honestly, go to GC or Sam Ash and sit down and play any of the Roland or Yamaha kits. They're always on display, and they're always on sale. I wouldn't bother with the cheaper Alesis options. Check out this review of what I have.
