by Wolfgang622 » Mon Dec 02, 2019 15:27:49

Since 11/22 came up in this thread, and since I have already apparently embarrassed myself once today by divulging what kinds of things fascinate me with respect to the odds of them happening, I'll share this story here. I thought about calling it in once when This American Life was looking for stories about odd coincidences - or are they coincidences? - kind of thing.

So I was living in Center City at the time, so this would have been 2004, 5, or 6, don't quite remember which. I lived at 22nd & Sansom and at the time there was a Wawa at 20th & Chestnut, which was my go-to. Went in there for whatever I went in there for - cigarettes, probably, since I smoked then - and paid with cash and got some change. Habitually I looked down at the coins I received - at the time I was sort of making it a habit to collect the bicentennial quarters, and I think my Mom was collecting the state ones, so I always looked - and to my surprise I found I had received a Kennedy half dollar.

I don't know how often I have received a Kennedy half dollar as change in my life, certainly no more than ten times, maybe five, hell, maybe just that once. I certainly have never received one as change again. Not sure I have touched one for any reason since.

I looked at it, kind of said, "Huh, that's neat," and was going to put it away when it dawned on me: it was November 22nd. I got spooked.

ANd the more I thought about it, the more spooked I got. I figure there are lots of people who know off-hand tht JFK was killed on November 22nd, but I figure there are also lots of people who don't. Considering I was not alive when it happened, the only reason I know the date so well is because of what I mentioned above: my parents' wedding anniversary, and the day they moved into the home I grew up in, were both also November 22nd. And not to get too graphic, but my parents moved in on 11/22/1977, and I was born on August 27, 1978 - so it doesn't take much imagination for me to figure out why this date would be of especial importance to my particular life.

Then I thought about how uncommon it probably was that this Wawa - or any place - was ever giving out Kennedy half dollars as change. Perhaps slightly more likely in the city than the suburbs because of the coin machines in the Septa stations from when you used to get the tokens instead of the cards they use now. Those machines always gave out silver/gold dollars, but honestly I never once remember getting a Kennedy half dollar from one.

No, it must be kinda rare that this Wawa had a Kennedy half dollar to distribute at all. And when they did it was on 11/22. To me of all people, who not only knew right away and off hand the significance of the date, but precisely because it was of such personal importance to my life.

What are the odds?

[I wish I remembered if it were a Bicentennial Kennedy Half Dollar or not - probably not, I think I would remember if it had been, but alas I no longer remember the year it was issued.]
