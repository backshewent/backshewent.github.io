jerseyhoya wrote:David Shor’s Unified Theory of American Politics - retweets are not endorsements, and there are things in here I don't agree with or obviously would frame differently as I come at many things from a different perspective than him, but this has been getting a lot of deserved love on Twitter today and is worth the read if politics/polling/elections interest you


Shor's obviously a smart guy. On some level, I'm very sympathetic with the overall view, as I have long thought the real cleavage in American politics (and it's not just the US) was between cosmopolitans and parochials and a good strategy for the Democrats would be to emphasize the economic issues that appeal to parochials. Bill Clinton did this, and even Obama did this. I'm not sure though he acknowledges enough how much the long term trend he cites that education levels will matter more, and ethnicity will matter less takes into account how much Trump has transformed the Republican Party, narrowing its base. That is, I think a lot of the "Obama-Trump" voters are going to swing back to Biden and perhaps stay with the Democrats. I don't think young non-church going African Americans are going to stick with the Republican party in significant numbers. He seems to think that for the Democrats to gain Texas, they'd have to lose Pennsylvania and Wisconsin because of age and education. I'm not sure I buy that at all.

I also don't think Shor (or really anyone for that matter) has fully digested the impact that Covid-19 is going to have on all this. It might for example do irreparable damage to the Republican brand in Florida and Arizona, and significant damage in Texas and Georgia. We might be on the verge of something like the 1932 election--a fundamental shift in American politics. I have come to believe that you cannot pull apart Republican ideology and Republican incompetence. The hostility to technical expertise is fundamental to Republican populism. It's as if you decided that modern medicine is some kind of scam, so instead of a doctor you decide that faith healing is the way to go.
