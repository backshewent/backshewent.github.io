I have 2 points here: Republicans spent time and effort building statehouse legislative majorities so they could gerrymander voting districts at the local level starting in 2010. Consequently you have stuff like the 66-34 Republican advantage in the Virginia House, in a state that's consistently voted Democrat for statewide offices and the President over the past decade.

Secondly, Republican local officeholders are very good about supporting other Republicans for other local offices. For instance, the Republican Congresswoman near me, Barbara Comstock, is in a district that went for Clinton, but Comstock is consistently supporting local Republicans for state legislature, county board of supervisors, county school boards and other local offices. She helps make sure Republicans win those offices because then there are no Democrats making a name for themselves at the local level and no one on the bench with enough following to challenge her for her House seat.

I think both situations are happening all over the country.


Also it's much easier to be considered a "rising star" if you're a Republican. Get elected to Congress and vote against everything or get elected governor and fire a bunch of state workers.
