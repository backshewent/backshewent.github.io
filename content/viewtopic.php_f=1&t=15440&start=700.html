Plus, Clippard is a free agent after 2015. They're in kinda good shape for next season in the bullpen without Soriano, but not after that. So they might not mind the 2016 vesting option, especially if it allows them to drive down the asking price. And they could maybe even renegotiate the contract with Papelbon into a guaranteed 2-year deal at a slightly lower price.

Then again, they're going to have to consider whether to re-sign guys like LaRoche, Asdrubal Cabrera (unlikely), Span (no-brainer for $9M club option), Schierholtz (unlikely), and Scott Hairston, plus they have to pay for Fister's final arb year, Strasburg's second arb year, Storen's third arb year (super two), and Detwiler's final arb year. They also have to consider whether to work on an extension for Jordan Zimmerman (2016 FA) and an early extension with Bryce Harper (arb-eligible in 2016). So in addition to the escalating salary of Werth ($20M), the decline of Ryan Zimmerman at the same price ($14M), and the slight increase (but still way reasonable) for Gio ($11M), their payroll is going to start climbing quickly.

And it's already been skyrocketing (per cots):

2014: $136,856,579
2013: $118,289,679
2012: $ 92,534,929
2011: $ 68,306,929


So bottom line is that I doubt they'd want to pick up a guy like Papelbon.
