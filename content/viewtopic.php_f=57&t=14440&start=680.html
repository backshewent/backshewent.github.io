i think you make a huge offer to cano. understanding that there is a good chance he could fall off a cliff in 4 years like every 2b does at the end of their career. but hes a 5-7 win player and worth $20m per year for at least the next 4 years. you're paying for his age 31-37 years if you give him a 7 year deal. because he is a middle infielder, i think he'll get less than that. i think 6/150 gets it done.

we're currently paying utley $15m per. halladay makes $20m per. so if you replace those 2 with cano and one of the young arms, you're "saving" $10m.

i think the other offer should be to granderson. his batting average has plummeted in recent years, but its coincided with a huge power spike. ISOs of .221, .290 and .260 the last 3 years, with BB rates over 10% all 3 years and average defense in CF. move him to LF. move brown to RF.

since he'll be 33 next april, its probably a 3-4 year deal with a high AAV. figure he gets 4/60. thats $15m per. when you factor in the "savings" of $10m from the above cano for halladay/utley swap, we've added $5m total to the payroll.

if joseph replaces ruiz, you've then essentially balanced the books. the rotation is then only the big 2. but we should have some money to get a decent #3. there are always plenty of interesting arms available. and that also assumes only one of morgan/biddle/ethan martin/pettibone is ready to step up. we certainly dont want to use morgan, kendrick and lannan in the rotation next year, but i see morgan as the replacement for lannan, meaning we'd need 1 more SP.

the only downside to this plan is it would make us extremely LH heavy. but im not really worried about that now.
