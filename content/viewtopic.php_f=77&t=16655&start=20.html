Of course I was wrong in saying Romney would win, and in retrospect was also wrong to suggest he was the favorite at any point in October. As has been discussed multiple times on this fair message board, GOP internal polling was far too rosy because it was modeling a universe of likely voters that included too few young people and nonwhite people on the basis of their stated lack of enthusiasm compared to 2008. The public polls and Dem internal polls did a much better job at predicting the outcome. The opposite was true this past November, as the GOP internal polls did a nice job, and the Dems were blindsided. You win some, you lose some.

While I was wrong about Romney winning, I was certainly never sure he would win, so I don't think that's a very good word for you to use in describing my past beliefs or a good comparison with how I feel about Sanders, to whom I give very, very little chance.

I don't really understand why people ought to treat me poorly because I disagree with most people here politically, but then I'm not a member of the tolerant left.
