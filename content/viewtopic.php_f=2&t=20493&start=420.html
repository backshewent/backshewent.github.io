by JFLNYC » Sun Oct 04, 2020 15:18:45

I was originally prescribed 4mg per day in order to reduce inflammation in my lower GI caused by my radiation treatments. I took it for 6 weeks. After the treatments but before I was off the Dex I noticed how much easier it was for me to move (I have arthritis in my shoulders and knees and a bulging disk in my lower back). I went off the Dex and noticed that the pain came back, making even the simplest tasks a chore once again.

When I stopped the Dex I had very difficult withdrawal symptoms for 2 weeks (body aches, occasional spikes in temperature in the evening, fatigue, etc.), so I was wary about going back on. I tried 1 mg/day, four times per week and got the same results. Talked to my doctor and we tried the half a milligram per day and got the same results without having to worry about side effects from such a small dose.

So finding out that Dexamethasone could relieve my arthritis symptoms was a silver lining to my cancer treatments. At such a small dose and at my age (65) my doctor and I agreed it was worth any risks because it’s given me such relief. Talk to your doctor. At higher doses and/or for someone younger it may not be a good choice. For me it had been.
Jamie
