Speaking of new glass, I just ordered a $30 lens. It's a 35mm f/1.7 C-mount lens, which is designed for TV cameras and the like. My camera (Panasonic G1) is able to, with adapters, accept nearly any interchangeable lens for any system ever made. This lens would be nearly impossible to use on any DSLR (unless you only do macro work) as to focus normally it would have to be inside the camera body.

It's not "good" glass by any stretch but I'm looking forward to playing with it and will post some photos once I get it (7-20 business days, shipped from Hong Kong).

Here are a couple of samples from this lens, both taken wide open. The bokeh is very weird at this aperture, and it's not very sharp even in the center, and drops off sharply to the edges.





For flower closeups it produces a dreamy effect which is kind of cool, I think. For portraits it's very challenging, especially if your subject doesn't hold still, as it's strictly manual focus and you have to be careful to keep your subject in the center of the frame.
