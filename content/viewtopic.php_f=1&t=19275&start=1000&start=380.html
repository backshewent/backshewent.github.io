by Shadow » Wed Feb 27, 2019 18:19:34

A couple idle thoughts:

1. When your rebuilding plan is to limit pay roll for several years to plan for a free agency period years away and have teams that suck, you run the risk of your standing among your peers going into heavy decline. It's what makes these kinds of plans so risky. Not only do you have to outbid teams in a sport with no salary cap, but you have to convince them to commit to you for many years. Not as easy to do when you just came off several years of no playoffs, and many of them a bottom 3 team the league. Which puts you in a position to have to overpay and beat everyone else. Not saying this was a faulty plan, I still think we get him, I'm just saying it's incredibly risky putting all your eggs in one basket.

2. We wouldn't be nearly as stressed if we had a superstar bat in the minors waiting in the wings. We haven't developed a superstar bat since Utley and Howard. Hoskins is looking like he can become that, so hoping he's the trend breaker that continues on. But I am tired of seeing some clubs crank out these star minor league bats every few years and we can barely get one every decade.
