At any rate, this list of the Japanese league professional stadiums is kind of cool. Some interesting wrinkles in there, like the one with the all-dirt infield.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_N ... l_stadiums

On a completely different note, I think MLB should ban the shift, but to balance it, experiment with raising the mound back to 18 inches. I have long suspected that the shorter mound height precipitates more injuries as pitchers themselves have to generate more torque to get pitches down in the zone. A taller mound with more of a slope would take the pressure off arms and let pitchers use legs and gravity more.

Just a theory, they should try it at some level of the minors. I can understand it would take some getting used to for current MLB pitchers (I would think), probably would have to be phased in over time by starting it at A ball level and moving it up as the next generation of pitchers move up.
