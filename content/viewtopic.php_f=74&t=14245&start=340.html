Unless you think he's going to outperform his 2008 margin (spoiler: he's not), there's no shot of this.

Then again, a mandate is what you make of it. Bush clearly didn't have one the first time but acted like he'd won in a landslide, and claimed one the second time but couldn't move an agenda, for a bunch of reasons. What might empower Obama, if he wins, isn't the margin but the circumstances around the "fiscal cliff," plus the likelihood of the recovery picking up steam.

That second point is the one that sticks in my head. I'm convinced the only way to rid the Republicans of the crazy is to beat it out of them. If Obama wins today and the economy really starts to cook over the next few years, Obamacare is implemented (maybe with slight/bipartisan reforms) and there's a deal on immigration reform, I could see a scenario in which they nominate a true nutball next time, lose in a landslide, and then maybe, finally start to turn back toward Huntsman/Daniels type conservatives, with a party behind them more interested in good governance than dying on the hill of right-wing dogma.*

*I actually believe this is true of Romney--but not that he has the strength of character to lead this fight within his party. My hope is that if he does lose, Republicans will reach the conclusion that what beat him was how far he had to run to the right in the primaries just to fend off people you wouldn't want feeding your cats, much less running the country.
