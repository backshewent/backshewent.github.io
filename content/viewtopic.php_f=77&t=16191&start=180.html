He doesn't drink. He is very personable though (I've met him). And as far as governor of Arkansas, he mostly did a good job--raised taxes when necessary to confront a serious budget shortfall, took real political risks to reform public education (an effort where he fell short in accomplishing what he wanted to but there's no doubt he tried) and overall did a very good job managing state finances--we aren't one of those states facing a pension time bomb or some horrible debt thing. And remember--most of his time as governor was during a relatively weak economy nationwide.

There were little things that were admirable as well--during Katrina, when many evacuees ended up in Arkansas with nothing but the clothes on their backs, he mobilized state agencies and non-profits and dealt with that crisis effectively.

It's been all downhill since then though--his Fox show was horrific, his PAC seems to be little more than a way for him to divert donor's money to himself and his family, and he moved to Florida.

Like Christie though he can be incredibly thin skinned and does not deal with an adversarial press well at all.
