My in-laws came today with a U-Haul containing their piano, which nobody in their home has played for many years. It badly needs a tune but all the keys work so hopefully not too much maintenance needed.

It's a ca. 1910 James & Holmstrom transposing piano, meaning that there's a lever that shifts the entire keyboard over a bit so you can change keys without having to transpose while sight-reading. However this mechanism is no longer functional, not that I care much. This was originally purchased for my wife's great-grandmother as a 16th birthday present, and was transported ~50 miles by horse and wagon to her home. Pretty neat.

I've been jonesing for a piano ever since my digital piano became unplayable several years ago (the "action" became extremely noisy and many keys would get stuck down). I tried taking it apart to clean and repair but it was beyond my skills. I still have all the parts but calls to piano shops to see if anyone can finish the job have gone unanswered.
