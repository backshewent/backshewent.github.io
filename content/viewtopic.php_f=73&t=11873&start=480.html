Eem wrote:
Barry Jive wrote:
Eem wrote:#$&! a State Patty's Day


I love State Patty's Day. Moreso in theory than in practice.


Can't get behind you on this one friend, but I do look forward to your reasoning


Part of it stems from the fact that I hate St. Patrick's Day for what it's become. It's my family's damned traditional holiday, and I hate that it's this mainstream drinking holiday for everyone from everywhere. No, not everyone is Irish on St. Patrick's Day. It's just the day everyone wishes they were. So that kind of takes away from it for me.

State Patty's Day took that away. It gave students a legitimate drinking holiday. And it's not like people take it easy on St. Patrick's Day as a result, but that's what the damage control should have been: Drive drinking activities toward a controlled drinking holiday and away from St. Patrick's Day. That way, you have one highly regulated drinking day instead of two wags of the finger from Bill Mahon et al.

I'm not saying it'd work but nothing they've done has worked anyway and it was never going to, despite the borough's apparent conclusion that cracking down on bars would keep people from getting hammered in the Valley all day long anyway.

Plus, as shameful as some make it out to be, Penn State has always been a drinking and party school, and making up a drinking holiday out of proclaimed necessity (because we'd be damned if spring break was gonna take away our St. Patrick's Day) is about the most mobile the student body ever got while I was there. Its ridiculous and not necessarily anything to be proud of, but it's representative of Penn State's culture and kind of endearing in that sense.

Cinco de State is trite bull shit, though.
no offense but you are everything that's wrong with America
