I am so ambivalent on the question of impeachment. I get why some Dems want it, but I also get why Pelosi and others are not keen on it. I hate to think this way but the only thing that matter is what is MOST likely to result in President Elect Democrat on November 4, 2020. Impeachment at this juncture is basically a dead letter in terms of actually removing Trump from office before the next possible “natural” expiration date; there isn’t enough time. So the only thing now is will “Benghaziing” him work for 2020?

Some would say sure it will, it worked on Hillary after all. But did it? She won the popular vote by almost 3M and two whole percentage points - a nearly unimaginable margin while still managing to lose. Her team ran an awful campaign and James Comey was a much more proximate reason for the “collapse” of her numbers in the final week.

And Don just seems Teflon to these sorts of things. Polling numbers be hanged, he reminds me of George W. Bush in this one respect: liberals hate him so much you’d think he was the devil incarnate, as likable as if someone already unlikeable, like Ted Cruz, were also a proven child molester to boot. But outside of our echo chamber, I think they miss something critical: Americans love this persona. The big bad boy with a lot of bravado and a lot of glitz. Most (white) people on some level LIKE Donald Trump no matter what they say, his persona is so quintessentially American, and the reality is he’s got a kind of charm, if you look past the bigotry and whatnot. He plays by his own rules, he tells it like it is, whatever you want.

Trying to crucify this cat seems exactly likely to backfire.

It’s clear to me he goes on his tirades not even so much because he believes what he says, but because he is like Bart Simpson: he knows how to play people to get a reaction. And the reaction he wants is liberal hand-washing about him and his positions, which comes across to a significant part of the (white) population as whining. And he gets it. All the time.

In my personal experience, it is better, a la Sun-Tzu and The Art of War, to let people like that just go off, and not respond too much. Let them over-swing, over-kick, over-attack, and use the momentum hey generate against them by simply remaining a mostly quiet and dignified alternative, who can maybe get can get an effective and understated cut in or two against the loudmouth to accentuate the point.

This is another reason for Elizabeth Warren to be my fave. Not only do I agree with every idea that leaves her mouth, I think she truly understands that you cannot win a pissing match with this man, nor can you expect to win by crying going all outrage-machine every time he gets on Twitter and says something else trying to provoke that EXACT reaction. There’s a reason he wants it.
