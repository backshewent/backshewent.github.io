Yeah, it's really terrible. My kids were totally skeeved out and that was the proper reaction. One of the fish was literally dripping blood out of its sores when we pulled it over the side.

Yeah, the specs were around hard from 2012-2014. Not so much the last two years. Have to go over to the Eastern Shore to find them, there are some nice fish around Smith Island and in that general vicinity.

Tens of thousands of cownose rays this year. Really cool to see a school of them glide under your boat.

philliesr98 wrote:
Luzinski's Gut wrote:took my family fishing yesterday on the Chesapeake. Beautiful evening, warm but a nice breeze out of the southwest.

Ended up with a mess of white perch, lots of undersized striped bass, a few spot, and I caught a 40 pound cownose ray. My kids were dumbfounded when I got up next to the boat and they saw it flapping its wings. It actually straightened out my hook so release was easy.

On a sadder nose, we also caught two 28 inch stripers that were badly infected with mycobacteriosis, a terrible infection that can be passed to humans and is lethal if not treated promptly. The Maryland DNR believes up to 70% of stripers have the disease, which ends up being fatal to fish. Brutal stuff, the water quality in the Bay, while the best its been in years, is still in need of major improvement.


Go for rockfish in the chesie fairly often. Last time I was down almost every fish had those sores

The one nice thing is that we were getting speckled trout too which haven't been showing up there for years. That was cool to mix in.
