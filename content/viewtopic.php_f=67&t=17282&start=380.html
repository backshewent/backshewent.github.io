by BigEd76 » Sun Jun 26, 2016 18:03:03

$144,900 saved from signing the top 10 picks, then they went $500K beyond the $100K limit on Stephen, so they're about $350K over their pool and will pay a 75% tax on it. They can spend about $300K more for anything beyond the #11-40 $100K limit before they get lose a 1st-round pick, but that doesn't include whatever #13 Brown got, so they might be done with big deals and #22 Young would head to Hofstra instead.

0-5% = 75% tax
5-10% = 75% tax + loss of 1st-rd pick
10-15% = 100% tax + loss of 1st-rd pick + loss of 2nd-rd pick
15%+ = 100% tax + loss of two 1st-rd picks
