But alot of things did go right last year....Papelbon was quite good, Kendrick gave us alot of value as a starter, Rollins shrugged off his poor start and turned in a better season than expected, Pierre played well, Lee and Hamels were very effective, Frandsen & Kratz came out of nowhere, Ruiz had another good season, Galvis flashed his defensive skills at 2B, Howard & Utley came back and did not suffer re-injury etc.

Sure, Halladay had a tough season and Amaro got unlucky Howard had the infection setback, but alot of the other issues were risky conscious choices made by Amaro that predictably did not pan out i.e. relying on Polanco, Contreras, Qualls, Nix, Wigginton; expecting Howard back in May, signing Thome when he was nothing but a DH, seeming to not understand Utley's injury, not monitoring him during the off-season and being "surprised" 2 days before ST that he was not ready; yo-yoing Brown, not bringing Ruf up and playing him until it was too late etc.

Amaro has largely dug his own hole, jumped in and shoveled the dirt back in around his feet...his approach has always been to win now with little regard for the future or adaptations if the approach did not work. Eventually, everyone has to pay the piper and the bill is coming due. Getting Revere was good in that he is young, controllable and cheap, but unfortunately he's also not that good and certainly not worth Worley/May who were sold low...Young is another in a long string of previously very good players acquired when past their prime, he could be ok or quite bad. Lannan is meh, going from Worley to Lannan is not an upgrade. Adams could be decent and let Manuel sleep better at night, but there are red flag injury issues on a pitcher who will turn 35 in July.
In the little town of Candor, in the last year of my youth, I learned the final lesson of the levels to the truth. -- Harry Chapin

R.I.P. Dave
