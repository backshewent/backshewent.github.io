by TomatoPie » Mon Jul 30, 2018 15:25:33

I don't know you Smitty but I have enjoyed your posts and share others' impressions that you're a good man with a good heart.

Your story touched me because lung cancer was the impetus for my three most recent trips to spring training.

I knew Pete because his daughter and mine were classmates. We'd chat at swim meets and after-school care stuff. He was a quiet guy but a Phillies fan like me.

Well he invited me to lunch and told me about his recent diagnosis. He was in treatment and looked the same as usual.

He didn't make any grand public declarations but decided to start checking things off the bucket list. He learned Italian and took his family to Italy twice.

He wanted to go to spring training and persuaded me to go with him. I had gone alone in 2008 which was a great time to go when the Phils were on the cusp of something big. With Pete, I returned three more times. We found the best places to stay and to eat.

He also suggested we drive down from Yardley to Philly on a string of trips to verify Glen MacNow's cheesesteak research.

His horrible diagnosis led us to some great times that never would have happened.

We went to the Phillies parade and got seats in CPB. We took his young son and I told him that I had waited 28 years since the last championship, so drink it all in.

I'm afraid his drugs weren't as good as yours because he's gone now. I hope we're here discussing the next Phillies championship with you, Smitty.
