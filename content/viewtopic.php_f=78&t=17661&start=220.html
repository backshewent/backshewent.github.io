I also don't believe that he has the stomach to push for unconstitutional ideas... I believe that he will say something, then backtrack and claim to be a uniter who is being vilified by a hostile press.

I may have overstated here(Trump expressed values and morals while campaigning) in order to compare Trump's campaign to something akin to religious dogma. Trump's dogma is a set of ideas around frustration with the status quo, unfairness in our relations with foreign nations, and a sense that the government is not effective at improving the lives of its citizens. It's like religion, because many supporters don't actually want to argue individual ideas point by point; the individual arguments don't really matter. He's an outsider, and he's going to fight the power. It doesn't matter that you have evidence that the earth is more than 6k years old, and it doesn't matter that Trump was questioning Obama's citizenship as late as 2014, and Trump's unchanging position on the Central Park Five, even with exonerating DNA evidence, does not matter.

Yes, he's inconsistent, and it's because he hasn't thought through many of his positions. He didn't need to do so to get elected. It's quite a cliffhanger, ain't it?
