by JFLNYC » Fri May 11, 2018 09:58:05

Look, if you have the opportunity to bring LeBron here you have to do it and doing so could very well lead to one or more championships. But let’s not kid ourselves. We’ll be asking the GOAT to adjust his game, be somewhat less than what’s made him the GOAT, we’ll be asking him to play less minutes and, of course, at some point Father Time is going to catch him.

In addition we’ll be asking one of our two great young franchise players to be a very, very different player, especially when he and LeBron are on the court together. This is not like Harden and Paul who can switch roles seamlessly because they can both shoot. Asking/forcing Simmons to be a different player could end being a good thing by forcing him to grow and get better at his weaknesses. OTOH it could really screw him up. We saw in the Boston series that he becomes way less effective — even in some ways a liability — when he’s taken off his game.

An analogous situation might be when the Sixers had a future HOF forward, George McGinnis, still in the prime of his career but had the opportunity to get Dr J. That acquisition eventually led to our last championship, but not before the team had to come to the realization that Dr J and McGinnis weren’t going to make it work together so McGinnis had to be traded for Bobby Jones. If that were to happen in this case it would be tough to have to trade Ben so early in his career.

Two great players, both of whom are unselfish like LeBron and Ben, would probably find a way to make it work, maybe spectacularly. But let’s not kid ourselves that it doesn’t have risks, including lack of compatibility, LeBron finally slowing down, difficulty in filling out the roster because of lack of cap space and it could also screw up Ben and the franchise long term.
