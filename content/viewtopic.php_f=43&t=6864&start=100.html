Thanks for that link.

It appears the only thing that brings guaranteed ratings is a series that goes 6 or 7 games. The ratings seem to go up in Game 2, down slightly in Game 3, and depending on the outcome of those games, gradually upward in the remaining games. The 1993 series was low compared to previous years, but since then, the only two Game 1s that had higher ratings were in 1995 and 1996. The overall viewers have risen since then, but the actual ratings have generally gone down.

Basically put, maybe the Dodgers would have drawn slightly better ratings than the Phils, but all you really need for success is a storyline. I don't think the Red Sox provide that as well as the Rays (worst to first, first WS for the franchise, young team, etc.) as the 2007 WS didn't outrank its contemporaries by much. The 2004 series obviously was a great success, but even that series was outdone by the 1999 series.

Boston's not as big a draw as everyone seems to think. New York is, and Atlanta was in the '90s, but other than that, there's no consistency in the "big market" theory.

Storylines. The Phils are in their first World Series in 15 years and the Rays are in their first ever. I think lots of people are going to watch. It'll certainly outdraw the 2006 WS, and it'll probably outdraw the 2005 and 2002 World Series.
