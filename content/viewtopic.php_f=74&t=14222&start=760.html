by dajafi » Wed Oct 31, 2012 23:03:46

I still think Kerry could have won in 2004--or rather, that Bush wasn't unbeatable that year, the way Reagan was in '84 or Clinton in '96. Given the fundamentals of so-so growth and some major negatives for the incumbent, a better candidate/campaign could have won that race. Kerry was even or led all spring, then blew it over the summer and couldn't catch up even after winning the debates. I'm not even sure he wouldn't have won had bin Laden not released his tape the weekend before the election, though I doubt many will agree with that.

We've been a 50/50 country since 2000, obviously. Bush temporarily disqualified the Republicans in 2008, but that was the fluke. Until one party is clearly successful to the point that it builds in lifelong loyalties the way FDR did, or the demographics change in ways that would render the Republicans uncompetitive--which I don't buy anyway, because they're too smart not to adjust--this is how it probably will be.
