McCain has staked out a foreign policy position, for good or ill. Ironically Obama, who accuses Bush of concentrating only Iraq, had no position on Georgia until after some briefings. Obama is in a difficult spot. He has to come out as more diplomatic than McCain the war monger, but if he's too conciliatory he comes out as weak and naive.

His stance on foreign policy is diplomacy. He says that Bush has failed in foreign policy because he doesn't talk to other countries. That sounds great, because he can hope that people believe that diplomacy will always work. We're all for that. Yet in situations like this a country isn't interested in diplomacy. Obama can't afford situations where diplomacy doesn't work. He has to sell it that if he were President he would've talked to Putin beforehand and this wouldn't have happened.
