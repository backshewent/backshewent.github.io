When I lived in state college, some friends and I made a film festival of just our own stupid movies. They were all pretty bad, but it was a lot of fun getting together to watch them. The last year we were going to have it, I went back and forth between making two movies. We ended up not having the festival that last year, so I never made either movie, but both are mostly written in my head.

One of the possibilities was a movie called "Scooby Doo, 2032." It was the old gang from the original series, but it's the year 2032 and they are all in an old folks home. Scooby is dead, but he is kept in an urn and Shaggy carries him everywhere. But then the urn is stolen because it turns out the urn (and a few other urns disappearing at the old folks home) is a priceless vase. The owner of the old folks home dresses up as a ghost to try to scare people away from the home and steal the vases. Classic Scooby Doo storyline, except with meddling old people instead of meddling kids. The emotional undercurrent is Shaggy's inability to let go of Scooby and move on. He does move on in the last scene.

I still kinda want to make the movie. It's been in my head for 20+ years. I'm sure it would be stupid, but it would be fun to watch with the old gang.
