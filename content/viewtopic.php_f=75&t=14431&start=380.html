The leverage model is going to be toast at all but the most elite law firms and for the most critical cases. Most low-level work (the work that I did as 1st through 3rd year lawyer) is already being performed by contract lawyers, in many cases in India. Many companies are refusing to pay for 1st and 2nd year lawyers.

What I think you are going to see is a continued decline in summer programs/hiring out of law school by big firms. The elite schools will still do fine, but most new lawyers will basically be forced to "apprentice" at mid-tier, lower paying jobs. The best firms will wait until those lawyers have developed a skillset that they can utilize and poach them at that point.

What you find is that most companies are more than happy to pay $1200 an hour for Ted Wells or a well-connected partner. What they don't want to pay for is a $350 an hour 2nd year associate.
