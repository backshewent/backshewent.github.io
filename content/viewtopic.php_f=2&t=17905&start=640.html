One thing about MS Word (and I guess all other modern WP software) is that they have tons of fonts, but they're listed alphabetically. So, when I play with fonts (because let's face it, Times New Roman is ugly, and it's so compressed it's kind of depressing when you're writing and writing and writing and it turns out you've only written a page and a half) I tend to stick to those fonts whose names begin with letters at the beginning of the alphabet. Century, Book Antiqua, sometimes as far as Footlight or Goudy Old Style are about as far as I get.

But for a sans serif font (and I think I read somewhere that sans serif fonts are best for when people will be reading on line) I think Latha is a good one.

I really think MS should figure out a way to organize its font listings some way other than alphabetically. Many of the fonts that I find clutter up the font menu probably are only useful for a handful of users, and I suspect if you're doing the kind of graphic design where you'd use something like Alfredo or Agency with any frequency you're not using MS Word but probably some kind of specialized graphic design software.
