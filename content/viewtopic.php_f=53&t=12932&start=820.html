prospects are also like very volatile stocks.

if you were rating a prospect's trade value on a scale of 1 to 10, with 10 being absolute max, Brown's was like a 9 last winter. Klaw had him ranked in his top 5 overall, BA had him very highly ranked. Had he been eligible this winter, he'd probably be in the 20s or 30s. His trade value now on a 1-10 scale is probably a 7. If he has 3 mediocre to poor months at AAA, his value is now a 4. Of course if he destroys AAA and improves his defense, his value shoots back up.

its a big gamble. Headley is already a quality MLB player with a few years of pre-FA service time and good numbers outside of his ridiculously unfriendly home park.
