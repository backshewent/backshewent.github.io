It's actually the primitive thing that I kind of like about them. Like a trace of humanity linking us to ancient ancestors. But anyway, the reasons I don't like them:

1.) They never look good. Never. Some look better than others. But they all look crappy because human skin does not serve as a suitable landscape for art. Either that or tattoo "artists" are just all terrible.

2.) I'm not even sure I believe in lifelong monogamy or unconditional love (though every year my parents' successful marriage puts a dent in my internal argument). I don't believe in absolutes. So the idea of putting something on my body forever--it'd be like carving the Constitution on the surface of the moon without an amendment process. I hate the idea of thinking I'm going to live by anything for the rest of my life, because i'd hate to let my stubbornness keep me from doing or believing the right thing. The world changes every day, and we need to change with it. My view of tattoos is just a very tangible version of that philosophy.

3.) It's possible this opinion is an extension of my crippling self-consciousness and solipsism and that I begrudge others for not sharing those issues.

4.) It's also possible I haven't come up with an idea I've deemed cool enough and that I'm jealous others believe so strongly in their own ideas of what's cool.
