Phan In Phlorida wrote:NOT ENOUGH SMALLBALLZ!!!

small ball inspired forced rant:

The Phillies had the highest SB success rate in MLB, had the 9th fewest K's, were 17th in sacrifice bunting, and 7th in runs scored. How can someone characterize a team with those statistics as one eschewing a "strategy involving bunting, sacrifice hitting and good base running to score a small, but sufficient sum of runs?" The Phillies were below average in number of sacrifice "hits," but given the considerable research showing that sacrifices decrease a team’s chance to win in more than 2/3 of situations used, it's hard to find fault when teams decide to forgo giving away outs. I think we scored too many runs to want to substitute our strategy for one of small-ball.
