My mother in law had the same thing happen in Oklahoma many years ago. Dog found the nest and started bringing the little bunnies back to the front porch. Three of them. Father in law brought them in the house. Dog figured it out and ate the rest of the brood.

Mother in law and mrs Smitty called a vet and he said that can feed the little things kittens milk out of a dropper. They did it but two died. One lived. Once he started pooping we knew he would live. Sure enough, a few days later the eyes opened up. A few more days and he looked like a mini bunny.

We named him Lucky Pooper. He was the lone survivor and we knew he would live nice he pooped. This the name.

We ended up transporting him to Washington. He was able to run free in the apartment and then the house. We got him the Taj Mahal of cages. He ran around the house, under the bed was his cave. He went out through the door to his cage to poop and pee.

He was amazing.

Lived for eight years.

Life span is normally 1-2 years.

Many years after we had him, we found out if you find a disturbed nest, you should take the baby bunnies to a rehabilitator. Because it's impossible to have a wild cottontail as a domestic pet. But we done it.
