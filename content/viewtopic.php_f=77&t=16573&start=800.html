It's a story that says he's seriously considering dropping out of the race by the end of August. Its three pieces of evidence are he might miss the 2nd debate, he lost the argument with Paul among GOP voters, and he is stuck at low numbers in early states.

The cutoff for polling to qualify for the 2nd debate is September 10th, and it includes national polls that fit CNN's criteria conducted between July 16th and September 10th. This gives Christie a leg up over Kasich, because Kasich was polling worse in the mid July polls before he entered, and gives both a leg up over Fiorina because even if she does get a debate bump, there are a number of polls from before the 1st debate included in the average. It is possible he will narrowly miss the debate, but he won't know that by the end of the month. If missing the 2nd debate is a reason for leaving the race, one would expect he will soldier on at least until past Labor Day.

In the aftermath of the GOP debate, we haven't seen much in the way of polling, and what has come out has been mixed. 538 tallied up moves in polls and Christie and Paul are both basically unchanged. Gallup found that Rand Paul's fav/unfav took the biggest hit after the debate, dropping a net of 19 points. I'm not sure how much we've learned on how public opinion has been influenced by the debate, but from what we've got, it sure doesn't look like Rand won much of anything among GOP voters.

The point that Christie is not doing well in NH or Iowa is true, although he hasn't been doing well in Iowa for a long time and doing well there has not been part of his plan for winning the nomination. Kasich and he are battling over the same sort of voter in NH, and between Kasich's announcement bump and his Super PAC dropping about $4 mil on ads in the state, he's riding a wave. I don't think there's good reason to think this is permanent or irreversible. Christie is putting in the groundwork in the state needed to win or place well, and the short term ups and downs aren't going to cause him to leave the race.

If I was writing a made up blog post with no facts or sources about Christie's impending departure from the race, I'd have come up with more believable reasons for why he was bowing out. It's a really, really stupid blog post.
