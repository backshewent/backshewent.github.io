So what have we learned in August, as far as usable info for next year?

--Kratz and Frandsen are more than serviceable backups who probably represent value at the league minimum
--Kendrick really is okay as the fifth starter and might even be usable as the fourth
--Brown has a broad skill set, still isn't a finished product, and is always likely to get hurt a lot
--Horst should have a spot in next year's bullpen; everyone else among the young 'uns still must prove themselves
--Doc and Lee are likely to remain effective, if not full-on face-breaker, through 2013

About Kendrick: his K/9 this season is now up to 6.4. Best he'd ever previously done in a full-ish season was 4.6, last year. Last year he had fluky-low BABIP of .265; this year it's .292. I still sort of want to smash his face with a brick, but it's time to face the reality that he is, or can be, a pretty effective pitcher.

Questions for September have to be around Brown and Schierholtz, the relievers, and I guess Cloyd. And whether Ruf can rise at least to a Luzinskian level in left field.
