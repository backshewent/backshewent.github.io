“We are looking at ways to counter the rise of an ideological brand of conservatism that, for lack of a better word, is more anti-establishment than it has been in the past,” said David French, the top lobbyist at the National Retail Federation. “We have come to the conclusion that sitting on the sidelines is not good enough.”

The shit that passes for news on the internet is increasingly depressing me. And the misleading headlines (obviously designed not to help the citizen but to get page views) is only the tip of the iceberg. Things really were better when you had 3 networks, a bunch of newspapers, and some magazines. Does Judis's piece on the death throes of the Republican Party (which isn't exactly terrible or wrong) offer anything new? Is anyone other than me worried that the decline of the two party system to a system where one of those parties seems uninterested in winning national elections and is instead committed to catering to a shrinking and increasingly deranged base and another party that doesn't seem to understand the game?

Also, why is no one pointing that if the Republicans do implode completely, that would be very bad for the nation? Our problem today isn't partisanship, it isn't even exactly money. It's that the incentives are all lined up incorrectly--there's too much money to be made from being a beltway insider and being perceived as influential that no one is really interested in doing the real work of politics. You make more money spouting off bullshit on Fox or Huffpo or wherever than you do reporting. And you get on those stations by flattering the prejudices of your audience. You make more money being Grover's dancing monkey than you do trying to guide the treasury department through a manufactured crisis.
