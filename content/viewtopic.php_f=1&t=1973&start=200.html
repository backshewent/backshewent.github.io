The thing with aces is that they usually don't pay off this big and in most cases will win you a small pot and can definitely lose you a big pot. (but it doesnt happen as often as you think)


The scenario from yesterday:


cash game FTP .50/1 blinds 6 handed NLHE, I was MP Villian was UTG

UTG player raises to 4.

UTG+1 folds

UTG player was mostly tight from what I saw, I have A:c: A:d: so I raise it up to 10, I don't want anyone else in the pot and I think Villian will call here or even raise, even if he folds, Im fine with winning the 5.50.

Button folds, and the blinds fold as expected.

Villian calls.


Flop: 7:c: 7:s: 6:s:

Not the best flop, but unless Villian has pocket 6's or 7's (unlikely), I'm ahead here, even if he has A:s:K:s:

Villian checks.

I think Villian is either going for a check raise or is check folding, either way I'm gold. I bet about 3/4 of the pot and bet 16.

Villian immediately goes all in. ($88 total)

The immediacy here tells me that he isnt even thinking about it and already planned the check raise. Unless he's crafty with the 6's or 7's, I put him on Kings or Queens and call.

Villian shows Kings with one being a spade.

Turn was 3:s: (YIKES!)
River was a safe 6:d:

Hero wins a nice big pot.
