by Brantt » Fri Oct 03, 2014 08:51:09

Don't let the negative Nancies get you down people, make no mistake about it, IF this goes down it will be the biggest moment in the franchise's history sans the two world championships.

The way the ownership structure is set up and has been set up for decades, no one owner can own a majority share, and a "Chairman" is appointed by the ownership group to have full authority in running the team. As we all know the Chairman for the last 15+ years or so was Dave Montgomery, and Bill Giles preceded him I believe. Both those guys had full decision making authority on baseball operations and the rest of the owners stayed completely out of the baseball side.

That all changed when Middleton sold his cigar company, became a billionaire and started buying more and more ownership interest in the team. He has badly wanted sole ownership of the team for years now and at the very least to be a majority owner and have full control of the baseball operations. Eskin's report seems to suggest that is imminent, and my belief is some recent deaths of Phillies owners has allowed Middleton to swing the majority of the group his way by convincing them him running the team is best for business. For example, I know that Jim Buck's son has zero interest in the Phillies......the family investment in baseball was 100% his dad who just passed away.

Anyway, here we sit and it appears Middleton is about to get his wish. I don't know John Middleton, but I know two people who know him very well and they say the Phillies are his passion all day every day. Once they won in 2008 and he got a taste of what that was like, he badly wanted more championships and recently has become "sickened" by the product on the field. Gillick would be his front man but that's probably only a1 or 2 year max deal as Gillick is just too old at this point for the job. Middleton will definitely be hands on though and will not tolerate what we've seen the last few years. I hesitate to compare him to George Steinbrenner because I don't think he is nuts like "Big Stein" was, but he is going to be active and do whatever it takes to bring the Phillies back to where they were in 2007-2011. Middleton was never a member of the "good ole boys" club that has run the Phillies for decades, so my guess is there will be a ton of changes coming in short order.

This is a huge, huge deal and a massive win for everyone who loves the Phillies.
