by Bucky » Fri May 08, 2009 06:58:44

Here's one I've been languishing over lately.

I've always been a staunch defender of proper use of apostrophes when assembling a plural. Actually more of a ridiculer of those who insert an apostrophe when one is not called for.

"Hundred's of Car's in Stock Every Day" !!

But now I find myself in lots of use cases where I need to pluralize an acronym or abbreviation- things where there's no proper plural when there typically would be (like a word ending in a vowel). I hate myself for using an apostrophe, but it's just not rights without one. Take "pro", for example, a typically accepted shortening of "professional".

Is it:

A) "Time to let the pros take over"!
B) "Time to let the pro's take over"!

I'm so conflicted.
