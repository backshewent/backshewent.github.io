Saturday night I was out in Manayunk, wearing a gray button-down shirt and sweating more than I've ever sweated in my entire life. And I sweat a lot, possibly more than anyone I know. Obviously, this shirt is soaked as a result and everyone can tell because it's a gray shirt. My friends are making fun of me constantly for how much I'm sweating, and I'm mortified because I have no idea why I'm sweating so much. I mean, it was hot, but I was drenched and there were no signs of it stopping. I take the ribbing in stride all while wondering to myself what I can possibly do to solve this problem.

So after the bars let out, me and my friend are waiting outside for our buddy to get out of this cheesesteak place with an insanely long line, and I see these two girls walking by. Both are dressed appropriately for the occasion and the weather, except for one notable exception: One of them was wearing a red scarf around her neck. A FUCKING SCARF.

So as she's walking by I turn to my friend and I just go "A scarf? In the middle of August?" The girls keep walking across the street and I keep going on to my friend about how ridiculous it is to wear a piece of winter clothing when it is 100 million degrees outside. As I continue with my dumbfounded rant, the girl's friend walks back up to us and says, "Were you making fun of my friend?"

So I say, "No, I wasn't making fun of her. I was just saying how FUCKING RIDICULOUS it is to wear a scarf in the middle of August."

She looks at me and says, "Well, we're both more attractive than you'll ever be. And you're the sweatiest person I've ever seen!" And she storms off and I lose it laughing and continuing to yell about this dumb girl's scarf.
