pacino wrote:we shouldn't even have electors

So that is the mess of the matter. Without Electors, and the Electoral College, then what, just straight votes? That is not mind numbing.

To review:

- There are 538 electoral votes (i.e., why 270 matters, and why FiveThirtyEight.com is named)
- Each state receives one electoral vote for the number of members it has in the House of Representatives (435)
- Each state receives one electoral vote for each of its members of the Senate (100)
- The District of Columbia has three electoral votes (3).

- It is winner take all in 48 of the 50 states and DC.
- It is screwy in Nebraska and Maine where these states potentially split the electoral votes according to the number of votes each candidate receives and how the votes were distributed.

Some have indicated that scrapping the EC, and working the simple math of winner takes-all is great (it would have only changed how badly the beating was), while others suggest that some type of state wide splits could be calculated (potentially allowing the middle of a state like PA to really change the complexion of what its vote looks like), making the calculation harder (while providing more candidate money to local concerns the working election system).

What is a good answer? There are not many choices in my opinion except getting rid of the EC while keeping the EV distribution as governed by the state which would allow the easy math to be worked out.

One quirk is that the whole Electoral College was established because the simple citizens were not really trusted to make quality decisions above influence, which wold make the argument of many that the EC should vote its concision for the good of the nation based on the election 2016 results. Like you, I don't think going to a straight populist vote is the best answer, but there's got to be a way that doesn't overvalue the voice of small states. They already receive a disproportionate voice in the Senate--they shouldn't also get upgraded in the presidential vote too.

That and the disincentive for opposition voices to cast a ballot in "safe" states is more than enough reason to want an overhaul. For example, here in Virginia, we didn't have very much on the ballot other than President and Congress. Our statewide elections are in off-off years.
