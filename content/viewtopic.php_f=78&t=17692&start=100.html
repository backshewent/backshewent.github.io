by jerseyhoya » Fri Dec 16, 2016 14:05:07



Kind of a fascinating map with a lot going on-Blue areas are where Dem Senate candidates ran ahead of Hillary, Red areas are where GOP Senate candidates ran ahead of Trump. So an area can be blue because the Dem Senate candidate did amazingly well (like Missouri) or because Trump did especially well for a Republican (like Kentucky). Red reasons would be reversed. Darker colors, bigger margins.

The metro/rural divides are consistent and interesting. Unless I'm missing one or two the only metro cores Trump outperformed the GOP senate candidate in the top tier races are St. Louis, KC, and Las Vegas. This holds in races where Trump did a lot better than the GOP candidate, like in Indiana where Trump ran 10 points ahead of Young, but Indianapolis is red. And then on the other side Ohio, where Portman ran way ahead of Trump, is dark red around Cleveland, Columbus and Cincy, while southeast Ohio was Trumpland. Florida, where Marco ran 7 points ahead of Trump building huge relative margins not only in South Florida, but around Orlando and Jacksonville, while Trump did better in less populated parts of the state. Even in some of the less competitive senate races where Trump did relatively better statewide than the senate candidate, places like Seattle and Denver are red.
