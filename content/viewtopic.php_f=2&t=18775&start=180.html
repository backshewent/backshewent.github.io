I've been waiting until i was in front of a keyboard to share this.

On Sunday, I preached a sermon that talked about the importance of instilling our kids with self respect, good values, a willingness to help people, and otherwise the need to be good people. I said that we needed to be a good model for them- often we teach them the wrong lessons unintentionally by our own actions.

I weaved in the detention of minors and separating them from their families. I believe this is a message of no compassion for people that have already been through much trying to seek out safety.

In the middle of the sermon, someone started yelling back at me about the "rules". About how the parents are at fault because they are putting their kids in that situation. I let her share her peace, then responded that we can't control what others do, but that we can control what we do. And that if we are following Jesus' example, we should show compassion to the kids. Regardless of how you feel about this, it's not the kids' fault. I think we can all agree that we are innocent.

She started to yell about the rules again. I have never been argued with during a sermon before. But I can't believe that "we should be fair to kids" is a controversial message.

Her dad, who she brought to church, fell and needed to be taken from the hospital. I was kind and stayed with them until they got him on his way. She wouldn't look at me, even as I was holding the wheelchair so that her dad could get in.

This is a human rights issue. It shouldn't be a political issue. But here we are.
