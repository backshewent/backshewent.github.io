So with this news, it appears its

1. He stays in Toronto
2. He goes to Phillies

I think the best course of action is as follows. You make 2 offers to Toronto

A.) Taylor, Carrasco, Donald, and then either Marson/D'Arnaud, + one lesser pitching prospect that doesn't include Knapp, Worley, Stutes, May or Pettibone

B.) Drabek + Donald + Marson/D'Arnaud + lesser prospect

If they want Drabek so badly, then they get less secondary pieces. If they want more pieces, then they take Taylor as the centerpiece.

If they honestly feel they will get more for Halladay this winter, then so be it. I think Roy wants to go, and he wants to go now. If they choose to drag it out this winter, he may just say "forget it, I love Toronto, I'm staying and then im walking in free agency".

Its a big gamble for JP. Id love Halladay, obviously. But Toronto only has a limited amount of leverage, because they have very fewer buyers. When the market dries up, you're holding your asset and its only worth what someone is willing to give you for it. If we can't get Halladay now, there's no point in trading for a much lesser guy. I'd call on Lee, I'd call on Haren, but you address it again this winter. This winter you might be able to get a healthy Jake Peavy on the cheap.
