If the POTUS doesn't sign the bill by Saturday (I think), but the Senate is still in session (full or pro forma) there may be a Constitutional question to whether or not a pocket veto is valid. If the POTUS doesn't return a bill by 10 days w/out objection and Congress is in session, the bill becomes law. I don't have the time to continue down the rabbit hole, but there are a few things that may work against a pocket veto:

1) Can only happen if Congress is not present to receiving the bill.
2) Senate is still in session, as of today, but I don't know if they will adjourn for recess or not.
3) Senate can keep a bare-bones session in place to combat recess appointments and potentially pocket vetoes - called pro forma session
4) The bill, IIRC, originated to the House, which is on recess, the veto clause of Article I states that the President has to return the bill to the House of Congress it originated, which is the HoR, which is on recesss
