by TenuredVulture » Wed Sep 14, 2011 14:38:25

jeff2sf wrote:What exactly were your food tastes when you were in high school? I think everybody liked bennigan's or chili's or TGI Friday's or whatever. You're in high school, you think drinking wine out of a box is high society.



And your point is? To be honest, when I was in high school, such places weren't all that common. We did have Friendly's and Denny's, but I don't recall anyone thinking those places were good. Denny's was open 24 hours, and that's why we went there. I'm not going to claim a sophisticated palate or anything. The mom and pop equivalents served food about equal to the chains of today. But the waiters and waitresses didn't wear clever pins, they weren't forced to pretend to have the time of their life bringing you onion rings, and there was no jokey decor. The funny thing is, most of those places are still in business, which tells me they're doing something right (among those things is they cater to local tastes) and that chain restaurants probably succeed where local similarly priced options are failing in some important respect.

Here's an example: http://www.barnaclebillsrumson.com/ One look at the website, and you'll see this is more or less a mom and pop equivalent of a chain. The place is packed all the time.
