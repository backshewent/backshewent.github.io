Re: the Kudlow thing--I guess everyone has me on ignore.

It's probably hopeless, but someone should try to explain to people that the word "deficit" in "trade deficit" is probably an unfortunate problem in branding. A trade deficit is really not at all like a budget deficit. For one, it's not like an annual trade deficit accumulate in a trade debt that needs to be paid back at some point. It doesn't mean that we have to pay interest or anything like that.

There's the book. (Actually it's 2 volumes, but I digress.) It came out more than 200 years ago. It's called Wealth of Nations, and explained why mercantilism is bad.

We could easily eliminate our trade deficit today by the way in a couple of ways. The easiest one would be to collapse the American economy so we could no longer afford to buy things from overseas. It would be unfortunate that we couldn't by domestically produced goods, but well, we'd have a trade surplus.

Eliminating a trade deficit with Mexico would actually be terrible. Mexico has a smaller, poorer population than the US. So of course we buy more stuff from them than they buy from us.

We could also wreck our currency--a few years ago there was talk about the dollar no longer being the reserve currency of the world, challenged by the Euro (bwahaha) and the Pound. Well, we're still #1! But if we decided to print a bunch of dollars, and doing the kind of things that lead to hyper inflation, we would buy a lot less from overseas because no one would want our worthless dollars. So we could become like Venezuela.
