phdave wrote:
Comment From Nik
Do the Phillies now have the best offense in the NL?

12:40
Dave Cameron: Not even close.


Not even close update:

Phillies are now 4th in runs scored per game with 4.5 (STL:4.7, CIN:4.7, COL: 4.6). They have scored 32 fewer runs than StL in 2 fewer games.

They are second in OPS post all start break, first in slugging. 3rd in overall runs scored. I'd like to know who Cameron thinks is the best offense in the NL now and who is close.


The rankings are still about the same with slight variations in the #s. But things are trending towards the Phillies moving up from 4th before the end of the season.

They have averaged 4.55 runs per game over the course of the season. If the teams above them all averaged their season average # of runs/game, the Phillies would have to average 5.33 runs per game to tie for the top spot.

The Phillies have averaged 4.93 since Utley came back. They have averaged 5.59 since the Pence acquisition and averaged 5.5 runs per game in August. The next closest NL team in August was Colorado with 4.93 runs per game.

So it is unlikely that the Phillies would overtake all of the teams ahead of them in runs per game before the end of the season. But it is possible and if certain trends continue, it could happen. Unfortunately the average age of their hitters is 31.5 which is dead last in the NL so not even close.
