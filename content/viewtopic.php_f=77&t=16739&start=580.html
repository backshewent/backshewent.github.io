Re: Penguins are my favorite animals and other RANDOM though

by TenuredVulture » Fri Oct 02, 2015 12:10:01

I think the first step is to identify the problem--what exactly is missing from TD11's life--is he looking for a real passion--that's a tough nut to crack. Typically, a passion sort of finds you rather than the other way--it becomes all consuming and even though many people can't make a living at it, the paying job then becomes a means to an end. Is he looking for a hobby that would provide opportunities for socializing and so forth because outside of work his life is pretty boring? I think most of the advice in the thread is in this direction--that's why people with kids usually don't have this problem as much--they simply spend their non-working hours doing things associated with their kid's activities.

I think what TD is dealing with is in fact very common. In college, you have a ready made set of friends and activities--you want some pals who are up for a game of beer pong on Wednesday afternoon? They're there! But you get to a point where everyone is working, many are dealing with their small kids, and our young urban professional has few options other watching TV after work. Maybe all he needs is a friendly neighborhood bar. Maybe a volunteer activity. Or start homebrewing--it can be very social, and the consumption of beer it involves helps deal with the inevitable awkwardness.
