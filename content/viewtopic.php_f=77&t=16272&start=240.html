by jerseyhoya » Mon Apr 13, 2015 17:36:06

Rubio deciding to run is the most interesting decision in the field. Everyone else (if Rand gets his caucus in Kentucky) gets to keep their job regardless of how the race goes. He is well liked by the party elites, broadly acceptable to pretty much anyone who identifies as Republican, and only 43. Seems like it's a bit of a long shot to win the nomination with Jeb crowding him out with Florida money people (Braman apparently excepted) and party leaders, Walker crowding him out as a conservative who has done things, Cruz stealing some of the nominate a Latino spotlight, etc. If he runs now and fails, as seems likely, he's giving up his Senate seat. But he'll be the best natural politician in the field (I think only Christie comes close if he gets in), and he's got a cool life story, and the field is wide open so who the fuck knows.

He does have a great chance at being picked for VP if someone other than Jeb wins the nomination, and not running for reelection to the Senate makes it easier for him to be picked for that. And Florida will have the open governor's seat for him to run for in 2018 if he wants. Still he'd have probably had an easy reelection campaign, and passing that up at his age is a big decision.
