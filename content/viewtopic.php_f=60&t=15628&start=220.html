The Times-Picayune article on Nola getting drafted
The Advocate article on Nola getting drafted

About a minute before the announcement on television, Nola received a text message from his draft advisor. "Congratulations, you’re a Philly."


The Tribune (San Luis Obispo) article on Imhof getting drafted

Though he had spoken with team representatives this year, Imhof said he hadn’t considered Philadelphia among the short list of teams coveting him since he hadn’t heard from the organization recently. But he chalked it all up to the cloak and dagger games of the draft. "I’m just so thankful for the opportunity to play professional baseball. I knew I was going to go somewhere. It happened to be the Phillies and I’m just glad it happened."


Los Angeles Times article on Brown

In a game of specialization, Aaron Brown is a throwback, the guy who can pick up a basketball and know how to shoot it, pick up a football and know how to throw it, and pick up a dart and know how to aim it. Someone who can run a 6.4 60-yard dash, throw a 93 mph fastball and hit a baseball more than 400 feet. A well of natural athleticism dipped with a wild amount of competitiveness, Brown is the athlete other athletes dream of becoming.
.
.
Before the draft, Brown said that he expected to sign, but scouts seemed to be split about whether to use him as a pitcher or outfielder. "I'm very aggressive at the plate and outfield, and I love playing every day. So if it were my choice, I would say, 'Yeah, give me a shot at the outfield and let me see what happens.' But you know what? I love doing both, so whatever they chose for me, that's what I'm going to do and I'm going to give it my best."


ArkansasRazorbacks.com article on Oliver getting drafted (w/ PDF of quotes)

“I knew from the mistake I made that I would slide a little bit, but I was fortunate enough to reach terms with the Phillies and after we had that locked in it was pretty emotional. I’m excited for the opportunity to be with the Phillies and keep chasing my dreams.”
.
.
“I know Nola is a heck of a pitcher and he has been consistently great. It’s an honor to be in the same organization with him and hopefully one day when we both reach the big leagues we can be a power duo in Philadelphia.”


Montgomery Advertiser article on Marrero getting drafted

"Today is a very special day for me. Thanks to God, my family, my teammates and coach (Mervyl) Melendez for believing in me. I'm a Hornet for life."


The Tennesseean article on McWilliams getting drafted

McWilliams had talked with scouts from other clubs, but admitted that the Phillies weren’t one of the ones that had shown the most interest.

"Actually, it was one of the teams that I’d had the least amount of contact with. I was kind of surprised. They called this morning, and I talked to them all day. I’m not going to complain about anything."

McWilliams said he will sign with the Phillies and leave to join their minor league camp soon.

"Now, my job is just baseball. I can’t really put it into words."
