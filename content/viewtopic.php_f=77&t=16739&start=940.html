Re: Penguins are my favorite animals and other RANDOM though

by TenuredVulture » Thu Oct 15, 2015 16:20:34

The Dude wrote:The other problem with this argument is that you're suggesting music can't be good now, since we can stream it for free. You're kind of all over he place


It's not that music can't be good now, it's that people don't care much about the music they listen to. Obviously, this does not apply to everyone--some people care a great deal. But when people tell me they've downloaded 15,000 songs, I wonder how close do they listen to any of it. When I bought Quadrophenia with my hard earned lawn mowing money, I listened to it probably dozens of times if not more before I could afford to buy another album.

The overall point is we're increasingly treating things as disposable. I think TV is an exception, because for a couple of reasons we're actually more invested (both financially and in terms of the time we spend watching it) than we used to be. TV is different in other ways too--I think the current number of channels puts things in a kind of consumer sweet spot. When there were only three channels, shows had to appeal to a wide audience, and thus ended up appealing to that old lowest common denominator. Now, with a couple of hundred channels, you can have a "hit" show that really only appeals to a niche audience but there aren't so many channels that the audience becomes so diluted there's no money to be made anymore and all there is to watch are shows about storage locker contents and you tubes about people throwing food on the floor.
