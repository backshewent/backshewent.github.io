by Barry Jive » Tue Sep 08, 2009 00:35:53

I would've liked to have spent more time in Cooperstown, but we weren't in a very patient mood today, being completely exhausted from the whole weekend. But the few hours we were there still was worth going slightly out of our way.

Also:


Yes, I'm a child. And no, this wasn't the only thing I liked or will remember about the Hall. It was a great place full of history, history I'm actually interested in--seeing the collection of year-by-year World Series rings, for example, gave me chills, and listening to the full inning broadcast of the Shot Heard Round the World call was mesmerizing. My favorite part of the whole trip may have been giving a guy decked out in full Mets gear a shit-eating grin and a nod while standing next to the 2008 WS exhibit.

The only disappointment, for me, anyway, was the relative lack of Phils gear in the store. The place is filled to the gills with Yankees stuff, with a lot of representation for the Red Sox as well. The Mets, Cardinals and Pirates seemed to get a lot of notice, too. While the Phils had more decent apparel there than most MLB teams, I'm not really interested in a t-shirt featuring every HOFer who ever played for the Phils--including guys like Kid Nichols, who pitched 21 measly games for them at the very end of his career

I did end up getting a nice mug commemorating the 1980 WS champs. I would've gotten a 2008 one, but they were completely sold out. This mug's gonna get a lot of mileage.
