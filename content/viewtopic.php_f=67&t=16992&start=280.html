Schwartz did an interview with FootballOutsiders over a decade ago discussing the role of analytics in his coaching decisions: Howie probably jerked off to that when he was getting Lurie coffee.

FTR I'm not seeing the appeal, either. You can say that Buffalo's defense got better when he succeeded Pettine, but I'd counter by noting that he had three DL have career years. If he's hired you'll see a lot of the WIDE 9. The difference is the back end: they'll play a lot of Cover 4 to involve the safeties in the run fit and make up for the deficit up front. Traditionally his teams are great against the run and suck against the pass because of this. I thought it was telling when Terryl Austin walked into Detroit and immediately produced two defenses better than anything Schwartz ever did (and this last season he lost Suh and Fairly).
