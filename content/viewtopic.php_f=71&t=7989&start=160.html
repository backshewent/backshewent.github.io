I don't know. People here get as catty about music as they do with politics. And often seem as inflexible.


It's completely subjective. I think people like to argue about it because they can't be proven wrong. It's whoever gets the most pig-headed first wins.

As for me, I have a raging hard-on for Soundgarden that is impossible to tuck away. Sorry about that.


Excusing the Steagles:Jean-Gilles::Crashburn:Cornell comment at the end there, I like argumentation as much if not more than anyone I know, and I like talking about (not necessarily arguing about) music more than anything else (aside from, you know, listening to it).

But with music, I can't accept that it's subjective. Well, it is, inherently, but so is everything in the universe worth arguing about. Music, however, has this near-impossible-to-find thread of objectivity running through it, and it's the Holy Grail of music evaluation.

It's that back-and-forth between objectivity and subjectivity that makes these arguments so great. Absolutes are silly, except when they're not. There's room for perspective everywhere, but sometimes they're stupid. There are no winners in a music argument, but everyone wins.
