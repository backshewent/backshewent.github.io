I don't know. Working hard, investing, building, etc., is not a guarantee of success. (And I'll ignore the inference that someone isn't working hard at a "normal" job.) Once a person gets to the point in life where they've got a spouse and kids, taking a huge risk like that isn't just risking your own well-being. As an attorney, I could start my own law firm and make a lot of money down the road (and in turn, employ other people), but I'm also running the significant risk of not being able to afford my daughter's day care bills or my law school loans (or my wife's for that matter) in the short term. That wouldn't be doing my family any favors.

It's very, very easy for people who are born into enormous wealth to take risks, because the fallback options are always there for them.

Not to mention that the "spend less time drinking... more time working" line is ridiculous.
