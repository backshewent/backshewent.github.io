Last Wednesday I pulled out question after question. The normal questions, "do pets go to heaven", that kind of stuff. Then we got to the last one.

It was, "Is it okay to kill a bad President?" After that question, Obama was in parenthesis.

I am still in shock about this. I said of course not, then about five kids (in a 20-kid youth group) started arguing with me vociferously. They think he should be executed. When I asked why, they couldn't really give me a stated answer. These are 5th and 6th graders. Racist ones, raised by their racist parents (although I'm one to argue that simply on the basis of being white, we are automatically racist). But these are Confederate flag loving, forgetting that we are in PENNSYLVANIA redneck kids.

They get it from their parents. I talked about how "you shall not kill" was important to understand, and that we can't just kill people we don't like, and that every president has had people that loved them and hated them. I also said that when I was their age, nobody in my classroom really cared about politics.

But the whole thing just left me sad. I don't know if I changed any minds- if if I did, they are going to go home to redneck dad and Bible thumping mom who tells them about the end of the world coming soon and what I said won't matter anyway.
