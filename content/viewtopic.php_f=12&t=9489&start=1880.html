Polar Bear Phan wrote:For those that attended these games in Denver, what are your lasting impressions of Rockies fans? No feelings, mild annoyance, lasting annoyance, undying hatred, etc.?

Personally, I developed a real hatred of Dodgers fans after NLCS Games 3 and 4 last season.


For the most part, Rockies' fans are harmless. We weren't hassled at any point during the game, even when the Rockies took the lead.

What's annoying about the Rockies is the mind-numbing entertainment program that plays the same songs (Hey Baby-crap) every single game, has the same stupid contests every single game, and basically makes the Rockies' organization look like a second-rate organization every time you go to a game.

If anything, the fans can be blamed for going along with the stupid "Tulo" chants and other routines put forth by the Rockies' marketing/game-day entertainment department. They need to rise up and demand to be treated as a major league fanbase, although I'm not sure that will happen anytime soon.

Bottom-line is they're not worth the energy to despise, even with this recycled Rocktober crap. Hopefully for CO, the Phillies put that BS to bed for good.

today was awesome. ate at this delicious place called snooze's. then checked out red rocks which was so awesome. came back and got ready for the game.

after the win we rushed over toe stand behind the visitors dugout and cheered on the guys. it was real fun being there with 70 or so other phils fans thanking our heroes.
