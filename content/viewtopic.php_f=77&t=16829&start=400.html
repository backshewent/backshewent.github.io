It's actually making a comeback in nerd circles. Podcasts like Brian Posehn's Nerd Poker, or the D&D/drinking game Crit Juice show some of the humour and remind older players why they liked it. Geek and Sundry does a weekly Twitch stream called Critcal Role with professional voice actors that's pretty damned cool just as some fantasy fiction. They really highlight the roleplaying side of things, but being voice actors they really sell the story.

Also, 5th edition D&D released fairly recently and is a pretty good system that's fairly easy for newcomers to pick up while still retaining a lot of the elements of 3.5 and AD&D that long time players missed/bitched about in 4th ed.
