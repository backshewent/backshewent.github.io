Re: ~Take a look, It's in a BOOK, Back She Goes~

by JUburton » Fri Mar 13, 2020 09:53:47

I commute by train mostly so I devour a book every week or two. This year I've read:

Ian McEwan - Machines Like Me; my first of his and it was a mistake to start there. Not great.
Atwood's The Testaments and The Blind Assassin. The former was kind of a heavy beach read. A page turner but didn't hit deeply for me. The Blind Assassin is not about a Blind Assassin really but once you get into it, it's a slow burn that was masterfully executed.
Julia Phillips - The Disappearing Earth; from the start I thought it would he a mystery but it's more of a broad look at life in rural Russia. I liked it well enough.
Ben Lerner - The Topeka School; I thought this was a pretty good quick take on how much things change but also stay the same.
Ted Chiang - Exhalation; a collection of short stories by the Arrival guy and I loved it and I typically don't really like short stories
Kazuo Ishiguro - The Remains of the Day; excellently written, fairly funny, and a bit tragic
Oscar Vuong - On Earth We're Briefly Gorgeous - a (first?) novel by a poet, so quite...poetically written. fairly poignant, obviously autobiographical and quite touching.

Currently going through Mitchell's The Thousand Autumns of Jacob de Zoet. Still love Mitchell.
