No way. Ryan was Marine and a badass. I was Army and a nice guy who got things done. Not the easiest way to go but it worked for me. My book was a text book published by the Field Artillery School. None may exist. But it was fun to do.

Having a Journalism degree when you get commissioned was very unusual in my day. Had bosses who wanted me to write a lot and do investigations like I was Bob Woodward or something.

The Army finally got smart in the eighties and decided to do away with the traditional beuraucratic writing style, which no one read and didn't understand if they tried to read it. Someone saw my Jounalism degree and put me in the section that would teach how to write more better. It was pretty valuable for me. I went to a unit in Germany and the commander found out my secret.
