by BuddyGroom » Thu Oct 09, 2008 13:59:48

dajafi wrote:My guess is that SCHIP will be reauthorized along the lines that Congress passed and Bush vetoed--definitely under Obama, probably under McCain. Otherwise I'm hopeful they'll do something to incentivize electronic record-keeping and maybe play with pilot programs to move toward a prevention model--though I don't know what that would look like.

But with the economy in the crapper, I have trouble seeing how they could pay for Obama's plan, and McCain's never would get through Congress anyway.


Well, one of the best arguments for substantial healthcare reform is it would produce savings, particularly in the long-term. For instance, it might be cheaper long-term to make sure every person has health coverage than for the desperate, uninsured and truly ill to turn to ERs in every case. Such cases cannot be turned away and must get paid for somehow. How - public money, of course, except for the rare instances where the patient can afford to pay some or all of the freight.

Likewise, another facet of true reform would be an emphasis on preventive care - spend some now to hopefully prevent or at delay more expensive and serious conditions.

A true reformer would make these and other similar arguments - we'll see if the next president is a real reformer. Regardless of the current state of the economy, long-term we stand to benefit if we fix the healthcare system and address the long-term financial health of Social Security and Medicare.

It's not as if the current health system is (a) cost-effective, (b) the best, broadest use of all our advances or (c) a good deal for employers who provide health insurance. Even though it might shake up the market, true healthcare reform eventually would benefit the economy in a number of ways.
