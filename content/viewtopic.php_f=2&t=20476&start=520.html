by traderdave » Wed Sep 30, 2020 10:25:18

If we are giving letter grades to last night's debate I think I would grade it as a C for Biden and a D- for Trump.

Biden was not great by any stretch but he had several really good moments and I thought the direct talk to the voters was a nice touch. As was mentioned above, I thought Biden watched a few pitches right down the middle. He also got flustered a few times which caused him to stammer a bit.

Trump on the other hand was a raving lunatic most of the night. His constant backtalk and interrupting screamed defense. While he landed a few hard punches he was mostly just yelling at clouds. His victim complex was on full display very early on when he started his healthcare plan response to Wallace with "First of all, I guess I'm debating you and not him. But, that's okay, I'm not surprised". Oh, everybody is out to get me. And, of course, his Proud Boys call to arms was inexplicable.

It is hard to imagine that anybody on fence would have been overly impressed by either candidate but it is impossible to imagine that anybody would have settled on Trump after watching that. If someone settled on Trump after last night, they were never really undecided. I agree with the thought above that the town hall format of the next debate will lead to a slightly more controlled event and certainly favors Uncle Joe's style (much like it did Obama). I am probably way off base with this but I think the VP debate might mean a bit more than it usually does this year. I expect that Harris will destroy Pence. Peaking a bit into the future, that may actually be the first debate of the 2024 Presidential election.
