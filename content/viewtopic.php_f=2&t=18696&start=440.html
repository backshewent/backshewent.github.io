by TenuredVulture » Fri Jun 01, 2018 11:17:33

Hilary lost for a number of reasons, many of them structural (the electoral college). But her "personality" problem I think had a lot do with the fact that deep down she's a technocrat. There's simply nothing in her political past that shows she cares about receiving input from ordinary people. She has always pursued policy in a top down fashion. I would argue (and there's a great political science book by Page and Shapiro that backs me up) that the reason Clinton's healthcare reform failed was because it was conceived by a bunch of technocrats with practically no input from ordinary people (or their representatives.)

Somewhat paradoxically, I think the other thing that hurt her was her long history of trying to appeal to moderates and even conservatives--her vote for the Iraq war is the thing that cost her the nomination in 2008, and deservedly so. All this made her appear as someone who really had few political commitments, who was more a weathervane than a political leader.

As an aside, I don't consider myself a prude when it comes to profanity at all, but I am not sure alleged reappropriation of these terms (the c one, the n one, and even the b one) is doing anyone any good.

In some respects, I think LGBTQ activists have much better political instincts regarding all this. You don't hear this (at least in public) the f word being used this way.

More to the point, the resistance could do a lot worse and look at how Act Up and other organizations responded to the HIV crisis and the rampant homophobia it unleashed. Memories are short, but a relatively small persecuted minority was able to chalk up lots of impressive political victories. Those strategies should be more widely adapted.
