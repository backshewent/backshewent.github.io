ek wrote:
Eem wrote:Halladay: Trying to strengthen his core, which Salisbury said is weird considering he's already in great shape. No real health update. He has to make 28-31 starts and pitch well.


this was interesting. he said he's doing it to keep stress off the shoulder which makes sense i guess

It's interesting how underworked/appreciated the core is for guys that "are in great shape".

Guys that are "lean and strong" may not be doing any core work, and especially for a rotational type of profession (throwing, swinging, etc) every bit of power starts with the core stabilizers. If those are underdeveloped, the rest of the kinetic chain has to make up for the lack of power, leading to overuse/strain, etc.

I always find it odd when I hear that athletes are adding core work - don't that have professional trainers guiding them?
