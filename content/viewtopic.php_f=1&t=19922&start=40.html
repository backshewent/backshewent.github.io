Yeah, I understand it's risky, but if Wheeler were less risky or had a longer track record, he'd be getting Cole/Strasburg money

This is about where I am too, despite my misgivings about the medical issue.

Wheeler's no doubt a risk, but he's one that the Phillies kind of had to take. They're now locked in with at least one significant upgrade early in FA without having to scramble on the market, which is obviously good. They have Price as the pitching coach now, who likely has as good a chance as anyone to unlock Wheeler's next level. And they made changes on the training side, which hopefully helps to eradicate at least some of the plague that befell their pitchers last season.

Probably always gonna be nervous watching Wheeler, waiting for the other shoe to drop with regard to his health, but it was almost certainly the most impactful non-Cole/Strasburg signing they could make to improve the pitching.
