I would assume most other boarders share this opinion (and probably have voiced it), but I think Byrd absolutely has to go for best offer this winter. The 5.5% BB/29% K marks from last year are like a flashing TRADE ME NOW sign. If he pulls a hammy or breaks his hamate or whatever in ST, the window for moving him is probably gone. Other teams realize that (which is probably why he stuck around last year), but I still think Byrd is a guy scouts would like a lot, so maybe some of the less SABR-driven orgs who are looking to win now could still be interested.

I could see teams preferring Melky, but that is likely going to require at least 3 years. Also teams are probably scared shitless of Cruz demands now after the year he just had. I would also guess many teams prefer him to Cuddyer because he's a better right fielder and has been healthier recently. His deal, even if it vests is 2-16, vs Cuddyer's 2-21 (backloaded with 12.5mm in 2016). If there was another team in addition to the Mets that was willing to give up the comp pick on Cuddyer, I think they'd give something of value for Byrd. Unfortunately he has that no trade veto to the Ms and KC, who I think are both logical fits. I think LF for the Giants is also a fit, especially if they lose fatty sandoval
