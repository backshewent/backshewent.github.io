by FTN » Mon Feb 13, 2012 11:10:25

There is a difference between solid money and money that screws the rest of the team.

Its easy to say "Give Cole 7/140", which I'm sure is the starting point. You hope you can get him for 6/110.

Lets say its 6/120. That means you have your top 3 starting pitchers making $65M combined. Halladay has a vesting option for 2014, Lee is signed through 2015 with a vesting option for 2016. Hamels will be locked up through 2017 or 2018.

Howard is here forever. Rollins for 3 or 4 years. But you need to figure out CF, 2B when Utley's contract expires in 2 years, 3B which is currently a black hole, RF if Pence walks, and maybe C as Ruiz nears the end of his productive years.

If the team wasn't afraid of the lux tax, then it wouldn't be an issue keeping Hamels, but so far that appears to be the internal cap.

As much as I dont want to really say it, this might have been the winter to trade Hamels. Considering how awesome he is, even with only 1 year before free agency, we might have been able to add 2-3 elite talents with 0-1 years of service time that could have helped set up our club for the next 5 years. I also think that instead of paying Hamels $20M per, it might make sense to allocate $25M total next winter on 2 SP, one to replace Hamels and 1 to replace Blanton. There will be relative bargains. The Yankees got Kuroda fairly cheaply on a 1 year deal this year. Obviously having a top 10-15 SP locked up for 6 years is nice, but we don't appear to be getting a discount from him. That could be posturing. He seems to love Philly, and it might just be his agent telling him to wear the stone face now and appear willing to walk, when in reality he just wants to get as much as he can from Philly with no intention of leaving.
