One of the more bizarre elements about Americans and their misuse of both terms is the relationship of markets to liberalism. A belief in the goodness of free markets more or less depends on the idea that people are basically good--that they won't do irrational things like use their market position to oppress people in unprofitable ways, like refuse to serve customers of a certain ethic group. Conservatives know better--people are stupid and irrational, and government, though not perfect (since perfection is found only in heaven, and is not for us in our fallen state) is necessary to limit the harm their stupidity and irrationality may do.

I actually like the emphasis on original sin--liberals think the stain can be erased in this life, conservatives do not. Everything else pretty much follows from that.
