by TenuredVulture » Wed Aug 16, 2017 15:05:40

I'm going to say dunk, with the caveat of the specifics of the rules. If you get only one pitch to hit, then it's definitely dunk. If you get 3 strikes, then it's still dunk. Heck I'd bet that would be true even among MLB players. But if you get say 1000 swings, then I'm less certain. And are we talking batting practice pitching, or real pitching? If it's real pitching, then maybe even with 1000 chances dunk still wins.

But unless you really tilt the rules to favor the baseball player (Coors field, lots of chances, easy pitching, the player dunking gets one chance and they have to dribble from half court) it's gotta be dunk.

It seems to me the key to the hypothetical is the "without preparation" which means to me they simply hand people a bat and say swing away! Even if you have the strength, and you get lucky with the timing and making clean contact, you've still got to have a ton of coordination to propel the ball over the fence. Now, it might not take much training to get to that point, but it takes some.

On the other hand, I guess if you see hitting a home run as something like hitting a hole in one, you might see a lot of people can get lucky. Pretty much anyone who has played a little golf can hit the ball far enough on a par three and get lucky. But I don't think hitting a homer is really like that.
