Part of the problem is that there are no real quick fixes in baseball anymore. You can try to sign a big time impact free agent, but that will cost you down the road. And there aren't many of those guys. In a weird way, there's a sense that big payroll teams paying aging superstars to huge long deals has in fact contributed to a league where a team like the Pirates can go from crummy to best team in baseball in one year. Indeed, of the bottom 5 teams in terms of payroll this year, 3 are competitive! (Pirates, Oakland, and Tampa.) Baltimore is decent for the second year in a row with a payroll square in the middle.

Ladies and gentlemen we seem to have achieved something like parity in MLB, which sucks now that we root for a team that has piles of money to spend. WHY DOES EVERYTHING HAPPEN TO US?
