I've seen a lot of live comedy in the last month.

I was in LA and saw Jon Glaser's Delocated Variety Show. He MC's the whole show in his ski mask with his voice modulator thing. Sarah Silverman, Tim Heidecker, Bret Gelman and Kristen Schaal were there. And Zach Galifianakis was a surprise guest. Reggie Watts closed the show. He's meh. Heidecker was probably the best. I had never seen him do stand-up.

Also saw a sketch comedy group while I was out there called Murderfist. They're based in NY. There's a dude in that group named Henry Zebrowski, this hilarious little goblin with no shame whatsoever. Imagine a shorter Chris Farley. He came out at one point and did a one-man sketch about a guy with large hands and it was the hardest I've ever laughed in my life. I seriously thought I was having an aneurysm. Feel like he's about to blow up. (Murderfist is going to be at a comedy thing at BAM tonight for any NY-based BSG'ers.)

Then Jon Glaser did his Delocated live show in NY last weekend. The guests were Toddy Barry, Eugene Mirman, Janeane Garofalo. Yo La Tengo closed the show. Pretty cool. The best part was that it was during the Michigan/Kansas game and Glaser's a huge Michigan fan. The entire Michigan comeback played out while he was on stage with people in the crowd giving him score updates and him just riffing with that ski mask on and his voice all distorted. He turned the whole thing into a 20 minute bit. Dude's a genius.
