yeah, key is finding someone you trust. Most indy mechanics fall into this category, in my experiences. One life lesson that took me way too long to figure out is DON'T EVERY USE THE DEALER SERVICE DEPARTMENT FOR SOMETHING YOU HAVE TO PAY FOR. I used to live next to a shop (the owner had the same last name as me but we're unrelated, oddly enough). That was obviously super convenient. Then when we moved, I spent a couple years back at the stealership. Big Mistake. Now I got hooked up with a mechanic who lives a couple miles from me but his shop is about 10 miles away. So I drop my car in his driveway late night/early morning, and he drives it to his shop, and I retrieve it at the end of the day. Even lets/encourages me to source my own parts, since I can usually get them somewhere online way cheaper than he can get them from his suppliers.
