I was joking about getting back Braun and A-Ram, but I wonder how enticing that would be to the Brewers, who are kind of in NL Central purgatory. You're taking $26M off their books in 2014, plus a $4M buyout for A-Ram thereafter and the gross domestic product of France for the fan- and teammate-reviled Braun.

What if we essentially offered them Howard with us absorbing most or all of his money due after 2014, plus a young player or prospect(s)? I'm obviously skeptical of any team being interested in Howard especially before the start of the season, but it's somewhat of a unique situation where they'll need some marketability and he could maybe possibly excite the Fielder fans a little bit.

It's a salary wash for us (and maybe even a savings depending on who goes to Milwaukee) and Ramirez holds 1B down for a half of a year or the full year until Franco is ready. It's a risk that Franco won't hit like a 1B any time soon, of course, but if he does and Brown plays well the sunk Howard money won't matter much. Maybe get really weird with it and take Lohse's contract, too, for a pitcher like Kendrick or Boner.
