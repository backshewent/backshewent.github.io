My own feelings, basically worth nothing since it's just me wetting my finger and holding it in the air to test the zeitgeist, is that it could go either way. Back in July I thought Obama was totally dead in the water, since his record was as easily defended then as now, and yet his approval ratings were terrible.Romney's failings as a candidate tightened things up a bit and perhaps put Obama slightly in the driver's seat, followed by Obama's failings as a candidate squandering things and getting things back to square one.

For the non-politically interested millions, and I think there are still many,may of those, my theory is that who they vote for comes down to who makes them feel better about themselves on election day. this can often boil down to just siding with the winner, but I think in 2008 also included many who liked to view themselves as able to overcome their own prejudices and vote for the black guy, and this accounted for much of his slightly larger than expected victory. Having done this once, though, I don't think Obama can count on these guys a second time around, while the people who would never vote for "the black guy" will still be out in force.

I don't really know who these guys will go for, or rather it will be divided among those voting for Romney because they accept the Obama as Great Apologizer line (and dammit, they want to feel great about their country!), or those voting for Obama because because they accept the Romney as the spokesman for the rich line (and dammit, I know when I'm being played for a fool!).

I think it will be close as hell. I think Obama has gained in support of the last couple weeks and will probably will the popular vote, but I don't think it at all unlikely that Romney will win the electoral vote.

Although I trust Nate Silver's judgment more than my own. I'm hoping for the best, but prepared for the worst. And at least glad the republicans ended up with "least objectionable man" as their nominee.
