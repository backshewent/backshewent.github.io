by Houshphandzadeh » Fri Mar 06, 2009 10:41:09

Well, there's a bar on the bottom floor that has great wings and this weird burrito thing thats like a cheeseburger and fries all wrapped up right there in the burrito, so that's a plus. The neighborhood is fine, it's kind of right between the art museum area and center city and he'll be able to walk to lots of cool joints and if he has a balcony on the southwest (I think) side, he'll have a great view of the art museum. So I can't speak really on the actual apartment or management because I haven't known anyone who's lived in that specific building, but the area is fine.

Then again, everyone here pretty much laughs off living in any of those complexes on Pennsylvania Ave. The general feeling is that you can usually find a really nice door to the street (or door-to-small-stairwell to street, whatever) apartment for about the same price, so why bother? Although I guess they probably will provide him a washer and dryer and whatnot. There are a lot of people who tried those kind of apartments when they first moved to the city and generally regard it as a bad experience.

Yeah, if he makes six figures, it sounds like a bad idea to me.
