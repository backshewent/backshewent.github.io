by Shore » Tue Feb 05, 2013 11:52:51

Obviously, I'm on the other side.

It's not even the individual moves that he's made, though I will (and have) debated those. It's the simple idea that he took over the best team in baseball, doubled the payroll, and has the 12th best team in baseball. He gambled by making an old team older, and the gamble failed, and now he's left with an expensive but not elite roster.

The arguments about who was available via free agency, who else could he have signed or traded for, etc., are all components of his job. Projecting that. Feeling that out. Pre-emptively making decisions if the future market appears to be light at a certain position.

His talent evaluation skills are, I believe, questionable. 99% of the world could identify Roy Halladay and Cliff Lee as "elite" guys. It's the middle tier... he doesn't seem to see a difference between the 30th percentile and 70th percentile of players. Sometimes I think he internally confuses league average with replacement level. Not in those terms, 'cause those are for nerds, but conceptually.

Whatever. I hope our 3 pitchers are happy and healthy, and pitch us to a remarkable number of 1-run wins that our bullpen finishes efficiently. But my belief is that we'll be closer to the Mets in record than to either the Nationals or Braves, and my fear is that Amaro will be given the chance to rebuild. I don't think he's the right guy.
