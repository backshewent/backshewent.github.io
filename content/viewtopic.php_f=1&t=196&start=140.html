by Stripes » Fri Mar 12, 2010 00:29:13

Back in my younger days(I'm 49 now), when I was still playing softball every summer, I usually gave up bowling in the summer. If I didn't, I felt burned out early in the fall season.

The last 2 years I've only bowled one night a week, due to work changes and then ultimately no work. It was a big adjustment at first. I wasn't any where near sharp for tournaments, missed cashing in 5 regionals, and finally backed away from the tournament scene.

In December, a friend backed me in a tournament at Blue Ball, and I did well enough (10th out of 87), that it got my competitive juices flowing again. Since then, I've broken a house record with an 865, and won a scratch tournament this past weekend in Danville. While I'm looking forward to hitting a few tournaments this year, I'm really aiming for next year when I turn 50 and can start taking the old guys' money!

I guess what I'm saying is, sometimes a break can do you good.
