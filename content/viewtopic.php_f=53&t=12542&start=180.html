Re: Oh FFS, the EPL kicks off and other random soccer stuff

by BoonePhillie » Sun Aug 28, 2011 16:49:39

The difference between Ferguson and Wenger is that Ferguson has figured out that you can compete for the title in the current season as well as bring in players for the future. Since the Invincibles season Wenger has been determined to build a second season like that but hasn't kept his eyes on the current as much as he should. Then when young players like Flamini and Adebayor and now Nasri and Fabregas leave, it sets the whole process back.

And that brings us to another problem: the current Arsenal wage scale. It's rumored that Fabregas was on about 90,000 pounds a week which is great until you look at Rooney pushing 200,000 a week and others up in that area. How can you ask world class players to play for half the wages of another world class player? They were in negotiations with Nasri until he became aware that Man City would pay him 50% more than Arsenal were offering. Who can blame Nasri for his decision? And the same thing will happen next summer when RVP has one year left on his contract. How will he react when he is offered 100,000 a week. He'll say 'thanks but I'll wait til next year and move on'. Will Arsene sell him so they get something out of him? And Vermaelen will eventually be in that position also. The bottom line is that Arsenal are now a selling club, not much different than clubs like Villa and Everton.

There is now a current top tier in the EPL and it includes the Manchester clubs and Chelsea at the top followed by Arsenal, Liverpool and Spurs. This comes down to the willingness and ability to spend massive amounts of cash to bring in players. I'll be interested to see if the Man U structure is a house of cards waiting to collapse in on itself. When Ferguson leaves will they find an adequate replacement?

So where will Arsenal finish this year? Certainly out of the top 4 but they'll still be in a position to qualify for the Europa Cup or what ever they call it. Without a change in philosophy from Wenger, Gazidis and the board that is where they will stay for the forseeable future.

Its not just the amount spent on wages, its how its spent.

We overpay nothing players like Almunia, Denilson, Diaby, Bendtner, and others who haven't done shit. They make £55-60K per week, and our top end earners are making only £90K per week. I bet Danny Wellbeck isn't making £60K per week already. We need to pay elite wages to our elite talent.

Everyone is blaming the board. The problem is Wenger. He wants to win a title with a "team of kids", and he feels like spending big sums of money would invalidate his project. The problem is, the board has given him unchecked control and is allowing him to turn the club in to his own experiment. We're sitting on over £100M in cash reserves, we've turned a profit on transfers since moving to the Emirates. We can spend £12M on Chamberlain, a prospect who played in League 1 last year, but we can't spend £10M more on a world class talent like Mata, who is only a few years older. Wenger only wants to buy players that have big re-sale values, which is why he put in a lousy loan offer for Lucho Gonzalez.

The club is in shambles. Top 4 appears to be gone. Not only has Wenger lost the plot in terms of balancing the squad with youth/experienced players, but he can't coach defensive tactics. Our team is so lazy off the ball and so tactically naive it hurts to watch.
