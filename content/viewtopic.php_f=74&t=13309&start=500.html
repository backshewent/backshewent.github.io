At first I had the same thigh-slapping reaction as the legions of responders to this article.

Upon further review, however, this doesn't hold up, and we must recognize this article for what it is: An opportunist, almost propagandic, divisive article intended to incite class warfare.

Anyone who doesn't make as much money as they thought they were going to is likely to complain. That goes for Warren Buffet and Bill Gates all the way down to Jim the Janitor. Ask enough "1 percenters" about their situations, and you'll find enough absurd examples to write this article. I didn't see anywhere where the people interviewed demanded less taxation on them and more on the 99%, or assertions that their plight is worse than anyone else's. Nor did they contact the writer to complain. The writer had to actively seek out these people and portray their situations and complaints in the worse light possible. Can some of these people be narcissistic and lack self awareness? Sure, it's possible. But that's not really the point.

The end result is a writer who decided to make a name for himself by highlighting absurdities to become a "champion of the 99%" and further drive the wedge of class division.

I'm sure this article has been written many times featuring welfare recipients complaining that what they get for free isn't enough in an attempt to elicit scorn from the taxpayers who support them.

Both types of article are nothing but vile.
