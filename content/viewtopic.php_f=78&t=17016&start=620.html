Nah. Don't wanna sound like THAT GUY but I'm confident in my abilities here. In fact, an inspection was required for my COO when I added the conservatory and new sub-panel. The inspector came out and questioned why I had the grounds all on the neutral bar on one side and the neutrals all on the other side. I had to point out to him that I had also removed the bonding bar between the two sides because that's what you have to do on sub-panels as per NEC. He was like "oh, yeah, I never knew that".

Wheels Tupay wrote:Bucky is going to electrocute himself.


Would be the first time! I do admit that I take extra time working in the panel because of the exposed hot lugs.



you see those two large silver screws in the circle? They're where the wire from the street attaches to (thru the meter box). So they're exposed metal and charged 120v each with a LOT of amperage- i.e. no fuse or breaker between them and the transformer out on the pole. So when you're feeding long wires through there (especially a bare ground wire) you've got to be very careful to control the wire and not let it (or your fingers) touch there or you can have a new hairstyle, along with a world of hurt
