by jerseyhoya » Mon Jan 24, 2011 02:58:00

The reason I stopped ranking going back is I was embarrassed to admit I rooted for the Cowboys against the Steelers and Bills. In my grade school days I took a lot of pride in the NFC's streak, partially I guess because of the Giants role in it winning as an underdog in 1990, but also just because it was a cool streak and fun to root for the winning team every year. I legitimately don't recall how I felt about Falcons-Broncos, but I was pretty heartbroken when the Packers lost to the Broncos. And was pleased when the Niners, Cowboys, Redskins and Giants won.

The first Super Bowl I remember is the 20-19 Norwood game. In retrospect that was a crazy weekend. My dad must have been absolutely obliterated driving me into school that Monday. We watched the game up in North Jersey (at a party that roasted a buffalo; my mom doesn't eat red meat so that was the first time I had non chicken/turkey/fish meat). After the game my dad and his friends took a limo into Manhattan and partied. The next morning we woke up pretty early and went to a hospital in (or near) Montclair, to visit my dad's sister who gave birth on 1/27/91. My uncle seriously wanted to name the kid Victoria in honor of being born the day we won the Super Bowl, but my aunt had the good sense to say no. After that we headed south and he got me to school in Medford before lunch. I was disappointed that no one else in my 1st grade class was as pumped as I was.
