by TenuredVulture » Thu Jul 12, 2012 10:48:20

The key for Romney, and for VP picks in general, is to do no harm. Thus, I don't think Romney will pick Christie, as he's apt to say something that will alienate lots of voters. It's sort of a Palin pick--initial excitement and enthusiasm, followed by a long period of regret. Jindal makes a lot of sense, but I'm not sure a governor of Louisiana, even one who's a reformer and has nothing to do with the corruption of his state is a great asset. Also, I wonder what the impact of a Mormon/Catholic ticket will be on evangelicals. (And I think for Romney to win Florida and Ohio, he's going to need some enthusiasm them.) Huckabee intrigues, because he can bring home evangelicals without alienating moderate swing voters. But lots of other people in the R coalition hate him, and he probably doesn't want to run for Veep anyway and he's sort of a lightweight version of Christie both literally and in terms of tending to say things he shouldn't with the microphone on.

Pawlenty is a nice safe choice who is an evangelical. So that's who I'd pick. But I'm probably wrong about all of this. So it probably will be Christie.
