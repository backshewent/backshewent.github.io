Everyone starts at Cat 4 once they get a racing licence, and if you accrue sufficient points in a season you'll be bumped up to a Cat 3 and you'll never be cat 4 ever again. But after that, you need to maintain a points tally once you've got up to a new category. Despite being a qualified assistant commissaire, I am hazy as to how Elite category relates to amateur status. Maybe I should read my British Cycling handbook more often.

It would be nice to have everything in place for next year, but I'll need to do some research first. Since it would be primarily an amateur undertaking, there's a lot more emphasis on coordination and checking, especially if you're running it across 3 stages (although in my head the possibility of four is tempting, it's probably a stretch too far). An event is only as good as its staff, and all that.

Still though, I'm definitely going to try and do it. Scotland has a nice variety of terrain so we could get some good routes out of it. Will have to be may/june which is generally when scottish weather is best. Accommodation would be an issue, as would trying to organise a big finish in a city. That costs a fair bit, because you have to organise and pay for road closures and police help.
