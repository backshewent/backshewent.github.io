Grotewold wrote:Is it about making sure the signature looks like the one they have on file, or the signature just confirming that you voted?

I signed mine real sloppily


In my area (Jersey), you're presented with a landscape style book that has three relevant columns - one with your name typed out and maybe some other info (voter ID # and whatnot). The second is a column that has what your signature should look like, presumably scanned and printed from my voter registration. The third column is the blank that I sign today. It'd actually be difficult to cover without a lot of fuss, the way the book is laid out.

There's also another perforated card that I sign and hand to the lady running the voting machine, but that doesn't include a handy "how to forge my signature" guide.

With all of this said, I'm not a conspiracy theorist who expects my vote to be taken from me. I'm just saying the system seems bizarrely loose.
