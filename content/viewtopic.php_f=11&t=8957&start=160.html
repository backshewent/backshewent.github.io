Why are people convinced that Toronto will get a better deal?

Dodgers/Angels/Yankees - Will probably have to give up significant contributors from their 25 man rosters, assuming they're even interested?
Boston - Buchholz is the key, very unlikely they'd be willing to do this, not to mention they're in the same division as Toronto and would be expected to pay a premium
Texas - more prospects than the the Phillies, but team has significant financial problems. Seth Everett mentioned this morning on WIP that there was a rumor going around last week that they had trouble meeting payroll.
White Sox/Brewers - maybe, but is Halladay willing to go there?
Phillies - might have to give up one player on their 25 man roster - Happ, who many believe is pitching over his head, and thus may be at the high point of his value. Besides the Rangers (and possibly Boston) have more attractive high level minor league prospects available to trade.
