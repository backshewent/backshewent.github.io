I know the conventional wisdom is that Hillary lost because of sexism. But I think that misses several important problems with the HRC campaign. The fact is that through the 90s and 00s, she was part of the DLC, which had embraced a strategy of moving to the right. Sure, it was Bill, not Hillary that signed welfare reform and the crime bill and so forth, but Hillary wasn't exactly critical of that either. She voted for the Iraq war, she made those speeches to Goldman Sacks, and so forth.

It's not just that the Dems have moved to the left, there are important strategic assumptions that a successful Dem President needs to embrace. Biden seems to think that Dems can count on Republicans to work in good faith towards solving problems. He doesn't recognize that Mitch McConnell and the Newt Gingrich legacy, not Donald Trump, is the real problem with the Republican Party. Blocking Merrick Garland is the new norm. Undoing the voting rights act (because the only way Republicans can win elections is to make it more difficult for young and non-whites to vote) and so forth is the basic Republican strategy.

I think Biden's appeal is that he represents the idea that once Trump is gone, we can get back to normal. Alas, it doesn't seem to be the case.

Trump isn't the cause, he's the symptom.
