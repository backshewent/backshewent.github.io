The style is purposely edgy of course, so they might have an alterative that's toned down, but the most radical styling cue, the sloping sides from the back of the cab to the rear of the bed, is probably driven by necessity. As this truck's bed is not decoupled from the cab as in a traditional pickup, that is needed to provide torsional stiffness to the whole structure. See also Chevy Avalanche and Honda Ridgeline for other (though less extreme) examples.
