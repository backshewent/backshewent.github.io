I shouldn't have to explain this to you, as you are an intelligent guy and have access to Google as well.

Tatum fundamentally misunderstands (or perhaps disingenuously misinterprets) what white privilege means. It might be better re-framed as "black [or BIPOC] disprivilege." It does not mean that all white people automatically get things that black people do not, just that their skin color is not something that holds them back. You, as a white person, might not feel this in your bones, but you should already be aware of it because of your many friends who have been lighting up Facebook the last couple of weeks.

"But if I was a young dude with my pants hanging down to my butt, I could understand why a store owner would be concerned. I used to be a cop. Believe me, I understand. If I owned the store, I’d be tracking that kid, too—whether he was black, white, or anything else."

"If two people are equally qualified for a job, the black person will usually get it. Big companies and prestigious universities fall all over one another trying to sign up talented black people."
This is so amazingly wrong I don't even know where to start. I see you went to BYU... were there lot of black students there? Do you think if BYU or OSU was clamoring for more talented black people you might have seen more? Then we get to the corporate world. I don't know your employment history but I personally have worked for three different companies where black people were either entirely absent from the workforce, underrepresented in the extreme, and/or underrepresented in management. Is your experience different?
Shall I continue?


I accept the criticism that I was too dismissive of Tatum's views. No black person's voice is any more or less valid than another's. And as a white person I certainly am in no position to deny his experience.
That said, let me clarify a couple of things.
While his own experiences are certainly valid, he is arguing against a straw man using made-up facts, and he can and should be called out as "wrong" for this. White privilege does not mean what he thinks it means (or what he's pretending it means), and just because he says "If you deny this, you are denying reality" doesn't make it so.
I did not say that all cops are fascists and racists, I said that the police *as an institution* is fascist and racist.
