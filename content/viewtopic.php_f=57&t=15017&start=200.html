sydnor wrote:I don't like the idea of Franco being the short side (Right handed batters get max 25% of the ab in a strict platoon, and him serving at two different positions doesn't really increase that.) We can't be bringing up a 21 year old, next year, with this guy's future to play 25% of the time.


there is no rule saying platoons need to be strict. given howard's durability, defense, and lefty issues and franco's more than respectable same-side splits this season, even the phillies might well choose to limit howard to no more than 4 starts per week regardless of the opposing starter, and put franco in for defense at 1b in the late innings even when the opposition can't easily line up howard to face a lefty reliever. when you want both asche and howard sitting against a tough lefty starter, or need to ph for asche and upgrade defense at 1b, you go with franco at 3b, ruf at 1b, and mayberry in the outfield corner.

at least 3 starts per week plus being the first bat off the bench gets franco to something like 50% of the pa's of a full-time starter.
my favorite examples are the chamberlain-eisy and thompson-inky platoons of the 93 phils. the latter may be most relevant because the lefty eisy had health issues and needed extra rest. another one off the top of my head is that the senior citizen version of julio franco got a bunch of pa's in 1b platoons on several occasions. some of those happened because the platoon partner got injured or arrived late... but injury is a big part of the howard situation, and franco wasn't really an option at 3b to get him extra pa's.
