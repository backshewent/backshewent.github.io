by swishnicholson » Mon Sep 14, 2009 23:15:58

CrashburnAlley wrote:I'm trying to think of a word and Googling hasn't helped me. It's sort of like a portmanteau (a blend of two or more words). But this one has to do with the mispronunciation of a word eventually taken to be the correct pronunciation or one of several correct pronunciations. I think a good example of this is "vice versa" being pronounced "vice-a versa". What's the word for this? Or am I crazy? (Not mutually-exclusive.)


I don't believe I've ever come across a single term for this. A spelling pronunciation seems to be the closest to it, but I don't think that's as catchy as you are looking for.

I'm not sure vice versa is such a good example of this. Both pronunciations (with one or two syllables in the first word) are accepted. It's really just a straight Latin term that has been accepted into English, and in Latin vice (used in this sense)would receive two syllables. The pronunciation of vice with one syllable is actually probably more recent, and has been adapted because that is how the word vice is pronounced in English. it has been accepted even though the word vice meaning evil is from the Latin vitium meaning offense or blemish, while the vice in vice versa is from vicis meaning position or office (vice versa meaning literally "the position being changed".
