Who the party chairperson is is generally overrated. I loved to harp on DWS because I thought she was bad at every aspect of her job - she was bad on TV, wasn't a great fundraiser, didn't have any longterm strategic vision, had a habit of getting in nonsense controversies. But in the grand scheme of things she didn't fuck anything up or cost the Dems the election.

Would think a major lesson the institutional Democratic Party should take away from Tuesday is the party reallllly needs to work on party building efforts down ballot and in parts of the country away from major cities and the coasts. Picking a super progressive congressman who represents an extremely liberal city in Congress to become the new head of the party seems like an odd way to go about doing that, but maybe he understands grassroots building and the focus you all need on downballot stuff to rebuild the base of the party. I dunno, really don't know a ton about his background. And thank god BERNIE has endorsed him. Is Bernie still a Democrat?

I know Dean gets all that because he's done that before. His 50 state strategy was extremely useful in the Dem waves of 2006 and 2008. A lot of it was riding GWB's unpopularity and later Obama's star power, but the Dem run during his chairmanship was unprecedented and mindboggling. Him at the DNC, Rahm running the DCCC, Schumer running the DSCC, ridiculous lineup.
