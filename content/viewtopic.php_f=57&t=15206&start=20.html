Grotewold wrote:Cameron on Ellsbury and encouraging comparisons


I don't really find those results encouraging. 17 WAR per 7 year period (2.4 per year for ages 30-36) screams "average" to me. 90 out of the 140 players (64%) that qualified for the batting title this year had 2.4 or more WAR. Martin Prado (.282 / .333 / .417, 14 HR, 70 RBI, 3 SB in 664 PA while playing slightly above average defense at 3B (and LF, and slightly below at 2B) was a 2.4 WAR player this year. It's like the definition of average.

The same guys averaged 4.3 WAR per year from ages 27-29, so they lost 44% of their annual value from their peak years to their 30s. That sounds like a disaster, to me.


Some day, I'll have the time and energy to fight the WAR * $6M = VALUE!!! thing that seems to be going around to justify contracts.
