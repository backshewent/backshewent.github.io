by kruker » Sun Feb 19, 2012 01:50:33

Beede was 86-88 all day. Touched 91 a few times, but you could see he was reaching back for it. Nice arm action on his changeup. First inning he got ahead of all the hitters. Defense let him down (Vandy's infield D was putrid all day--3 errors). I really doubt the scouting reports that said he was hitting 95. I understand it's his first game of the year, but no way he's hit 95 in a game. Made a really athletic play on a bunt. Small sample size and all...but going to college might have been a bad decision. His fastball is on a single plane, can't teach movement.

Mooneyham from Stanford is an intersting lefty. Missed last year because of a laceration on his hand. Was 88 most of the day, but could run it up to 94. Mechanics were all over the place. Decent curve every now and then. Big kid. He'll go in the top 5 rounds on lefty arm strength. Was surprised he didnt' give the Vandy lefties more trouble.

Chris Harvey from Norristown DH'ed for Vandy. True freshman from Germantown Academy. Definitely looks the part of a middle of the order masher and, from what I've read, is apparently sturdy behind the plate. Looked completely lost at the plate though. Didn't take the bat off his shoulder for the first two at bats, then took timid swings in his other appearances.

Appel was listed as the DH for Stanford but never saw the plate.

David Schmidt, a true freshman for Stanford, came in and was hitting 92-94 consistently. Granted it was in relief, but very impressive. Compact, efficient delivery. He'll get knocked for being undersized.

Mayberry--->Michael Taylor--->Austin Wilson. No idea why he was batting 8th. Stanford swing personified. Haven't seen a back elbow that high since little league.

Connor Harrell from Vandy went yard twice. Built like a running back.
