by Wolfgang622 » Thu Feb 28, 2019 12:25:40

Bossss wrote:
Wolfgang622 wrote:I think it is time for the Phillies to admit they have lost this negotiation. They have lost for three reasons:

1. For years, they have been, to put it mildly, unsubtle about their strategy for winning: clear payroll, develop what talent they have, and wait for the bonanza of 2018-19 offseason to arrive. Anyone paying the slightest bit of attention knew that the Phillies were putting all of their eggs in this free agent basket, and were ready to spend some money. (Compare to the Padres, who basically did the same thing, only better, because of better drafts (which has a luck element tbf), and because they never spend money, and because they kept their mouths shut, nobody saw them coming).

2. If somehow one was stupid enough to miss the Phillies' strategy, Mr. Middleton made his "stupid money" comment, further backing the team into a "do or die corner" with respect to Harper/Macahdo.

3. They allowed the only leverage they had to evaporate by not sticking to their stated "stupid money" intent and making a single 10/$326M record-breaking offer immediately to both parties and telling them whichever of them took it first got it, either motivated by the self-contradictory notion they perhaps they could save money and not bid against themselves (even though they were going to spend "stupid money" - which, bidding against oneself is in fact almost the embodiment of the definition of "stupid money"), or by the foolhardy thought that if they could keep the price below $300M for each player they could land both. That's nice but you HAVE to get one. Instead, they allowed one to go off the board, painting them into the narrowest corner of all against the best and most ruthless agent of all.

So now, it is time for a combo strategy move of JFLNYC's "pull the offer" concept, and the ultimatum concept, and the Godfather offer concept. Here's what they have to do:

1. Klentak must text both the GM of the Dodgers and Giants directly, ASAP, and tell them exactly what they are offering to Harper now, and what they are about to offer and how they are going to go about it. This way the Dodgers and Giants stop negotiating long enough to see if the Phillies are out (giving them more leverage), or they just go higher than you are TRULY willing to go.

2. You call up Boras. You congratulate him on the great job he has done representing his client, and that while you are still very interested, you also need to be able to move on to the next thing. Therefore, you are making a final offer that is a take it or leave it offer, and they have an hour to accept. If you are currently at a record deal ($326M plus), offer another $20M over top of it - $2M more a year. If you are not at the record deal, you still offer however high you are willing to go in excess of the record, but it had damn well better be in excess of the record (so if that means you are adding another $50M instead of $20M to your original offer, or whatever, so be it). You explain this extra money is simply a "let's sign it now bonus" and if it is not taken within an hour, you are moving on. Without saying so, you're admitting: he has won. But now it is time for him to know when he has won and take his winnings and leave the table.

3. Alert the media.



So to sum it up: You collude with the other GM's, give into all of BORAS's ridiculous demands, and alert the media to how terrible negotiator you are? Sounds like you could make a great GM one day. If only our team was run so well.


Not my first option but they have created the situation they find themselves in now. They have lost in terms of negotiating anything other than a record-breaking price. They have to salvage this. Have to, or die trying.
