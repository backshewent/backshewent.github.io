I'm sure a lot of people know this but... I was always taught the primary colors are yellow, red and blue. It turns out that is completely wrong and they're actually green, red and blue. That part I figured out years ago. But I still thought yellow red and blue were the primary colors when you mix paints and rgb was for light. But it turns out that's wrong too. It's actually:
red, green, blue = additive primary colors
and
cyan, magenta, yellow = subtractive primary colors.

For painting you want cyan, magenta and yellow, since those 3 colors let you mix any color you want. If you just use red, yellow, blue, you can never make magenta or cyan. This explains how when you mix red yellow and blue you get an ugly brown purple color, since the magenta and cyan are missing. If you mix magenta, cyan and yellow you get pure black. And printers use magenta, cyan, yellow and black ink.

This was taught wrong to me in every school level from kindergarten to high school. It's kind of infuriating.
