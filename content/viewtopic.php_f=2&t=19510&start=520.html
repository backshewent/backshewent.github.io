by Bill McNeal » Fri Jun 28, 2019 09:46:34

Last weekend I was trying to get the kids to go outside and do something. So I filled up a kiddie pool we had and tried to get them to come out and play in it. They're 7 and almost 12 and took one look at it and were like yeah, no. So I was looking for something fun water based thing they could do.

I found an inflatable pool that is something between a kiddie pool and an above ground pool for $50 and they were in to that. One problem though is that our yard has a steady decline away from the house and it would need to be level. NBD I thought, I'll get a shovel and level some yard. Spent the rest of the day digging up a 16' circle in my backyard trying to level it. After hours of doing this, it was coming along ok, but not really getting there. So I spent a couple nights after work at it trying to level the ground, and as it turns out, the decline in my yard is a lot steeper than I initially thought. By Wednesday night I was pot committed to this idea but not getting anywhere, so I started looking into how to level this fucking ground. Sand. Ordered 3 tons of sand yesterday morning and it was delivered by the time I got home from work. Spent the evening wheelbarrowing sand from my driveway to the other side of the yard and smoothing and leveling it. So now I have a perfectly level 16 foot circle of compacted sand in my backyard and ~ a ton of sand in my driveway. Isn't it funny how a fleeting idea can turn into a fairly large and involved project without you intending to do it.
