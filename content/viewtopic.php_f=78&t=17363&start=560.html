Look, let's be clear about what this story is and is not. It's not about borrowing some language from someone you admire and using it yourself even when, as here, it was done without attribution. Sure that's wrong but, in the scheme of things, not such a big deal.

What the story IS about is yet another instance of Trump and his campaign showing that, when it comes to politics and the administration of his campaign, it's amateur hour. Being an outsider cuts both ways: It's great if you're not beholden to the establishment, but not so great if you don't know how to captain the ship -- and this is a really, really important ship with lots of people's lives, liberties and pursuits of happiness involved.

Worse yet, this would-be captain is allergic to expert advice on how to run his ship. Maps? Radar? Sonar? Charts? Navigation equipment? Not for this guy. He trusts his gut! As I've said before, even with all his other problems, maybe the single biggest reason he should never be President is he doesn't know what he doesn't know and, worse yet, doesn't WANT to know.
