his groundball percentage has been above 50% every season. having butchers at every infield position isn't helping him. he's had BABIPs of .277, .307, .316, and .344 the last 4 seasons, its gotten progressively higher as the tigers have gotten progressively worse in the infield.

our current infield defense doesn't project to be great, so maybe its not an ideal fit. but he hasn't even come close to his physical peak. the tigers screwed him up after they drafted him, put him on a very restrictive pitch count and told him he had to pitch to contact and use a sinker. in high school, his fastball was 93-98 and he had a hard 12-6 curveball. he was a strikeout pitcher and they essentially neutered him. his xFIP has gone down each season: 4.27, 4.24, 4.02, 3.89 in 2012. his K per 9 has gone up slightly the last 2 seasons, but its still really low, because again, hes been told he has to pitch to contact and use a sinking 2 seamer instead of his 4 seam. hes only thrown his curveball 4% of the time in his major league career and it was his best weapon in high school behind his fastball.

his fastball velocity ticked up 2 full mph last year on both the 2 seam and 4 seam. he still throws his changeup too hard, but expose him to halladay, lee and hamels and see what happens.

he'd be an awesome #4 starter, and would benefit from the league switch. let lannan and kendrick battle it out for the 5th spot, with the odd man out moving to the bullpen
