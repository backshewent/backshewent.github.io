I was a scout, but never made Eagle. Topped out at Life. Went to Philmont. There were girls there. It wasn't a problem.

I found scouting valuable. I think there are lots of reasons boys participate in scouting--some, like me, were most interesting in the backpacking and outdoor activities, and not really into merit badges and awards. Others find other things valuable. I do think the Eagle Scout is one of the few real achievements available to boys who may not have exceptional athletic or academic prowess but have well above average persistence and initiative. Many scouts who may not have a father involved in his life found scouting to provide opportunities to do father/son things they wouldn't otherwise get to do. The strength of scouting in my opinion really depends almost entirely on the scoutmaster.

While I'm not really upset by it, I think having girls in scouting will make a big difference, and I'm not sure it's entirely positive.

I really do think the adult leadership of BSA has been wrong about almost everything, probably in part because of the huge Mormon influence on scouting. BSA took positions on divisive social issues when it really did not need to.
