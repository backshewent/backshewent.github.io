there were 3 supervisors seats available in the township. All D's currently. I D running to re-up with 2 new D's.

A group of two former Ds who switched to R, along with a native R, ran on the R ticket, but also "Adopted" (for lack of a better term) a community activist who ran as an I. So 4 people on their "team" for 3 seats.

Well, the Ds complained that the 4th guy was running simply as a 'spoiler' to take votes from the Ds. It wasn't true at all, but I didn't realize how ridiculous the statement was until I saw all the results. He was a spoiler, all right- but possibly cost one of his team a seat.

There was a 468 vote spread between the highest R and the lowest D. The I guy was well in the cellar, but he had almost 1500 votes. There's no way of telling the matrix of different voters, but I foresee a scenario where lots of people picked 3 of the 4 of that "team". So maybe enough people left off the highest R vote getter in favor of the I. I myself did that- the D's have done a lot of shady things, and are aligned with the crappy montco democrats, a centrist org who have more in common with the conservatives than progressives . The top R vote getter was a female, and I'm sure there were some misogynists who voted for the three males on the "team".

So in the end, the strategy backfired in a big way. I really wish there was a way to find the permutations of the votes.
