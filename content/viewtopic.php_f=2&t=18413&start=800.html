On the beltway, 95 south, there are parallel non-toll lanes, and 66 outside the beltway has not gotten its High Occupancy Toll lanes set up yet. You opt in on 66 inside the beltway (during rush hours if less then 3 people in the car), then the extension to the airport (set cost), then the next extension to Leesburg (set cost with rungs for time of day) are toll lanes. So there are 3 separate transactions to go from Leesburg to Arlington at rush hour. You can take that route on route 7, but itis mostly a 2 lane secondary highway full of traffic lights, stores, and housing communities. Basically it is between very overwhelmed to impassable certain times of day. There is a way to take a super windy, really congested road parallel to the river, where the uber rich live, to get from Reston to the MD/DC/VA line, and then onto the equally jammed 2 lane GW or Clara Barton Parkways (each side of the Potomac). Basically the whole region is a case study in the failure of urban and infrastructure planning.

Look at the DC Metro and how it poorly server the suburbs (ill place stations, limited parking, short routes, and pretty expensive). Virginia especially is a small set of spoke, which are mostly secondnary highways through population zones augmented by 3 highways that get overwhelmed easily, and often, if not daily. Its very fragile. The commuter rail south down 95 sharing Amtrak seems to be an OK solution (no where near me, so it is observations). I took a bus 9 miles to work once and it took me 95 minute. Its just a pathetic to see how poorly this whole area was allowed to grow with very little though about what it actually means. Its just sad when it could have been though through. There are people who actually know how to do this, and could have easily been employed but that thinking likely came decades too late.
