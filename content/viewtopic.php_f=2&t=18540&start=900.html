by Monkeyboy » Tue Apr 10, 2018 07:08:09

Some don't think the Cohen raid was about Miss Daniels. I think this may be behind a firewall, but here is the gist.

https://talkingpointsmemo.com/prime-beta/i-dont-buy-it

The biggest hurdle is that Cohen is the President’s lawyer. Their communications are protected by lawyer-client privilege. The privilege is not absolute. There’s a crime/fraud exception to it. That seems clearly to be the basis of these raids. But DOJ guidelines create a very high bar to piercing those confidences. Whoever made the decision almost certainly needed the consent of the top leadership of the DOJ. The judge who signed these warrants would also require a strong state interest to act on such an exception, all the more so when the client is the President of the United States!

Prosecutors routinely make prudential decisions, separating what might be technically possible under the law and what is proper and justified under the circumstances. Out of control prosecutors who either abuse their office exist of course, as do ones who make convictions their only guide. But even if you assume that Mueller or his staff attorneys suffer the kind of obsessive myopia that has come to afflict other Independent Counsels and Special Prosecutors, he could not and did not do this on his own. Rosenstein needed to sign off on this at multiple stages. Berman – Trump’s recent personal choice – had to make the key decisions.

It simply doesn’t add up. This is something you do when there is major criminal activity and delay or warning will lead to destruction of evidence. That’s not the NDA with Stormy Daniels.

So what is this about? I don’t know. I will say however that a number of knowledgable lawyers (knowledgable in the broad sense, not with inside information about this case) have suggested to me that we shouldn’t take at face value the idea that Mueller decided that this was unrelated to his case or outside his purview. This may be an effort to insulate himself, or attempt to insulate himself, from Trump’s retaliation. Mueller knows (likely with more detail than we do) that Trump has already tried to fire him.

That whole line of thinking is highly speculative. It could be totally wrong. But on the big point: Is this about the $130k Stormy Daniels transaction? That doesn’t add up. It’s simply not a crime of the gravity to merit this kind of action. A lot of good reporters are reporting tonight that that is what this is about. It just doesn’t add up.
