I'm not really sure what triggered it. When I saw it I assumed it was in response to something in particular but I didn't find anything that stood out.

Guessing from twitter timelines I think it might have been a result of a twitter argument he was having after Werth said that the Phillies were the Nationals' main competition. He mocked Werth as a troll and had some back and forth with someone who was saying the Braves aren't relevant because they often choke.

For example...

Giancarlo Baumann ‏@MJ_Baumann
Though if you think one game in 2011 has anything whatsoever to do with 2013, I'm not sure *YOU* know what relevant means.


After arguing a bit he tweeted

Giancarlo Baumann ‏@MJ_Baumann
I will not feed the trolls. I will not feed the trolls. I will not feed the trolls. I will not feed the trolls. I will not feed the trolls.


Then a few hours later tweeted a link to the satire thing.

Giancarlo Baumann ‏@MJ_Baumann
I tried to make this as long as a real newspaper column but blood started pouring from my eye sockets.


Then he wrote this:

Giancarlo Baumann ‏@MJ_Baumann
I wrote that 1) to see if I could and 2) for people who won't realize it's really mean-spirited satire.


So the best I could tell is that, instead of fighting with someone he considered to be a troll, he felt it would be better to be troll himself. One thing may not have had anything to do with the other but I'm guessing they are related because of the timing.
