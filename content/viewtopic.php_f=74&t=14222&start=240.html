by dajafi » Thu Oct 25, 2012 20:15:53

I'm a believer in social insurance and the social contract. They overlap in conditions placed upon receipt of benefits. If birth control is available and you're receiving public subsidy, there's no reason to have additional kids--or rather, have at it, but don't expect the community to support you rather than putting that moneu toward hiring one more teacher or subsidizing a low-income college student or fixing a road or weatherizing buildings. Every dollar expended carries an opportunity cost.

This is the same idea as corporations receiving subsidies and dumping waste or cheating on their taxes; the scale makes it mich less egregious, but the ethic of "fuck you, I'm getting mine" is analogous. We liberals started losing ground when we lost sight of what happens when public policy diverges too far from shared secular values. This helped open the door for the right to go full Randian, and now we're at the point where something even as clearly cost-efficient and morally unarguable as universal healthcare coverage can be assaulted, spuriously but effectively, as a raid of the takers upon the makers.
