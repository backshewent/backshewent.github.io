1. This has been touted as a way to get kids off of sugar drinks and into drinking healthier options(water). So what do they do? They made the tax on Liquid Volume and not Grams of Sugar. So a drink with MORE sugar in it, but at a lower volume of liquid, will have less tax applied to it than a larger drink. They have in essence put an incentive to drink high sugar/low volume drinks than something that might be more healthier but in a larger volume.

2. The tax is levied against the distributors and not against the consumer. I'm assuming they did this so its easier to collect taxes from a few places than chasing down their tax revenue every year from every single store in the city. But all they did was punish ALL consumers in the city. The Store/Market/Bodega is free to raise prices as they see fit. So a small corner store will be able to either raise only prices on all items and push their tax burden that way. Or they can stick to their morals and only raise the prices on sugar beverages the proper amount. What corner store is going to stay in business? the one who has an imperceptible increase across all products or the one who only raises the prices on their cash cow?


soo, it should have been a tax on gram per sugar. and it should be on the sole consumer and not on the distribution.
