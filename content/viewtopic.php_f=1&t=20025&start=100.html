Uncle Milty wrote:I didn't get to check it out fully but this looks good:

Last blasts: The final home run in a Phillies uniform of 20 team legends


It's an interesting article to read, but it's basically a box score recap for most of the HRs, absent any real quotes or insight other that opinions about the players, some of them egregiously wrong:

"[Bowa] was also a five-time NL All-Star and won two Gold Gloves, a travesty since his fielding and overall defense were clearly the best in the NL during many seasons in which the award was handed to someone due to offensive contributions at the position."

Those weak-fielding SSs were Ozzie Smith, Dave Concepcion, Don Kessinger, Bud Harrelson, and Roger Metzger. All had substantially more range than Bowa, stronger arms, and very good consistency as well, just not quite at Bowa's later-career level. Hard to argue that 5 less errors by Bowa would out-weigh 50 more plays by Concepcion. Bill James treats this exact contrast in his first article on range factor. No excuse for being a 2020 baseball writer and not understanding that there is more to good fielding than minimizing errors.
