This sort of points to what I tried to say a few days ago, but that I don't think I said as well as I should have. The problem with percentage-based forecasting is that, ultimately, there is no way to falsify it. How will we ever know whether the President having a 78% chance of winning re-election was close to the right percentage? We can't run it 1,000 times and find out... it will either come out one way, or the other. If Romney wins and Nate says he only had a 22% chance of winning, does that make Nate wrong? Or does it just mean he had a 1 in 5 chance and this was that 1-5 time? Nate can always claim the latter, and how would we ever be able to contradict it?
