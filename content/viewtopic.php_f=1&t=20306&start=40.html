Every team page on the Athletic has a "bio" and the Phillies' really sums it up well. It's been fun!

The Philadelphia Phillies have lost more games than any professional sports franchise in North America. They were born in 1883 as the Quakers and didn't capture a World Series until their 97th year of existence, in 1980. The Whiz Kids won the National League pennant in 1950, but they were the last NL team to integrate and didn't finish above third place again until 1964. That year, they held a 6.5-game lead with 12 to play and authored one of the most epic collapses in baseball history. It haunted a generation of Phillies fans even after the glory years of the late '70s and early '80s. The boisterous Macho Row squad captured a pennant in 1993 only to suffer one of the most backbreaking defeats on a Series-winning Joe Carter home run. They experienced a renaissance under the deft guidance of Hall of Fame executive Pat Gillick and won five consecutive division titles from 2007-2011. The 2008 world champions enthralled an entire city, but the Phillies have failed to return to postseason baseball since a 102-win season in 2011, which ended in disappointment.
