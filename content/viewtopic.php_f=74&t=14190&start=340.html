by Wolfgang622 » Fri Oct 12, 2012 10:10:41

This should be Obama's "closing statement" for the next two debates, and for his campaign:

"Since the beginning of October, the articulated Romney/Ryan plan has become this:

1. Cut taxes, but in a revenue neutral way, by eliminating loopholes, although they won't say which ones, and they won't eliminate loopholes for those who need them, only they won't say who they think need them.

2. Increase military spending, but get out of Afghanistan exactly on the schedule already set.

3. Keep Medicare exactly the way it is, except for people under 55 who want to use a voucher system.

4. While doing all this, reduce the deficit.

So let's review:

1. They're pledging to collect no more revenue, so there won't be any more money to pay down the debt.
2. They're pledging to increase military spending, so that will be more money spent.
3. They're pledging to keep Medicare the same for anyone who wants it to be the same, which means there will be no savings.
4. And somehow collecting the same amount of money, spending the same on Medicare, and spending MORE on the military is going to lead to deficit reduction.

That's not voodoo economics. That's voodoo math.

I don't think Governor Romney has arrived at this message because he is a bad guy or because he doesn't know what he really wants to do, but because he found himself losing at the beginning of October and decided that the only way to catch up was to tell everybody everything they want to hear. And that's exactly what his message has become:

I'll cut taxes! (Don't worry, it won't cut revenues!)
I'll reduce Medicare spending! (Don't worry, the program you like will stay exactly the same!)
I'll increase military spending! (Don't worry, I'll end the Afghan war and won't start any others!)
I'll get rid of growth-choking regulations! (Don't worry, I'll still make sure Wall Street follows the rules!)
I'll repeal Obamacare! (Don't worry, only the bad parts!)

This is what you have to do when you find yourself losing in October. Tell every group of voters what they want to hear. And it works - for a little while. Governor Romney hopes it works long enough for him to get elected.

It's up to the American people to see through this, and make him answer: what are you really going to do? If he won't answer, do you really want to vote for him?

My record is clear: After inheriting an economy in free fall, first we stopped the bleeding, then passed programs designed to get us growing again. We've had 31 consecutive months of job growth. We successfully saved the American auto industry. We passed health insurance reform, so that more Americans will be covered than ever before, and those with pre-existing conditions will no longer be excluded from getting good health insurance. We've preserved the middle-class Bush tax cut, and we are committed to making it permanent if we're re-elected. If we're re-elected, we'll continue to pursue policies designed to promote a healthy middle class, add jobs, push us toward energy independence, and keep us safe at home and abroad. And we're more than prepared to discuss, in detail, how we're going to do it. We can't be everything to everybody, but we can do what we think is in the best interests of the greatest number.

If that's a path you'd like to pursue, please give us your vote on November 6th."
