by allentown » Sat Jan 05, 2013 21:46:17

ReadingPhilly wrote:that deal is such a no brainer and would make me feel real good.

Is it really that much of a no-brainer. Upton's numbers 1) weren't all that good last season and 2) were inflated by playing in AZ. On the road he's got an OPS of .670 in about 300 AB. So, statistically significant and pretty craptastic. His trade value is increased by the bet that he will get back to the near-.900 overall OPS of 2011. He seems to alternate good/poor seasons, so 2013 might well be good. But, he is expensive, so it's a gamble for a corner OF who may well turn in another .670 road OPS. His road OPS is pretty much equal to Mayberry for last season. Yes, Upton steals some bases, but at a success rate where the steals are just about dead neutral in value. Someone will pay a lot for him, betting he'll improve significantly over 2012. The Diamondbacks, who know him best, will trade him betting that he won't significantly improve and won't be worth his contract going forward. Beware of hitters or pitchers whose success is largely a function of their home ballpark. They are always over-priced. Better to go the opposite direction and take the guy whose road stats are better. People tend not to correct stats. I think even GMs are guilty of that.
