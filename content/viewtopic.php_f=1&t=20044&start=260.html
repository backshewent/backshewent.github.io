by mcare89 » Fri Jun 05, 2020 12:37:37

Baseball's problem is that they are uniquely positioned for failure. They already had a massive labor relations issue that was probably going to lead to a work stoppage the next time around anyway, and now they have to essentially re-write the CBA within a few weeks when each side distrusts the other and nobody wants to give an inch that might be used against them later.

I think the players position is that it might be worth just throwing in the towel on the season rather than accept such a comically low piece of the pie and then have that be used later against you when it comes to the big CBA negotiation. If the owners perspective is "we can't make money playing baseball," I don't know how you respond to that.

I don't know if either side has a particularly good PR strategy in this, but ultimately, I don't think there is a good PR strategy because I think no matter what, both sides are going to take the brunt of it. The players probably take the bigger hit in terms of individual Q rating, but the bigger issue is that if people don't like baseball players, they don't watch baseball games and buy baseball tickets and merchandise, which ultimately hurts the owners as well.
