Rev_Beezer wrote:I like the mangrate for grilling. Works pretty well.

http://www.mangrate.com

That site is just loaded with bad information, plus grammatical and spelling errors.

* they utilize convection, conduction, and radiant heat, not just radiant heat. This is true of a normal grill as well, though I expect that this system, having more surface area than normal grill grates, would radiate more heat.
* there is no such phenomenon as "searing in the juices"
* oils and fats released during cooking do not "aid in the natural smoking process" - in normal grilling, very little smoking occurs at all, and if these fats are burning, the smoke is something you do NOT want. Smoking meats and other foods is done with various kinds of wood; animal fats produce carcinogens when burned.
* "flare-ups", not "flair ups"

Plus, it's called ManGrate.

That said, I think it would work well, though you would have to preheat your grill longer than you would with normal grates.
