by Werthless » Mon Oct 29, 2018 23:07:21

Stripes wrote:You're so worried about the machines, but y'all don't want voter ID. Weird. And confusing.

As a Republican, perhaps I have some credibility to answer your question.

The basic principle is a desire to make voting easy, accessible, and accurate for people legally able to do so. If something impedes on these 3 factors, there needs to be a compelling reason to do so. Given the lack of evident voter fraud, voter ID laws make voting less accessible with no corresponding benefit. It doesn't improve accuracy. It doesn't make voting easier, but harder.

Ensuring the accuracy of voting machines, where the only corresponding trade-off is cost, is consistent with those principles. And when you think about it, accurate vote tallying is an essential characteristic of a functioning democracy.
