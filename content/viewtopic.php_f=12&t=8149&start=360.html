by FTN » Mon Apr 13, 2009 13:36:09

what makes his article so funny is that he doesn't seem to understand what a baseball does coming out of a pitcher's hand. Lidge's release point on his fastball and slider are identical. When you maintain a consistent release point, the batter has to wait to see the spin of the ball before picking up the pitch.



Thats from yesterday's game. Those are all the pitches he threw. His release point is extremely consistent. Because of this, the batter can't tell what is coming based on where he releases the ball. For a pitcher throwing a 90 mph fastball and an 80 mph slider, you have a split second longer to react. For a guy throwing a 94 mph fastball and an 84 mph slider, you don't have that luxury.

machinegundump does bring up a good point though. Lidge could stand to throw a few more fastballs, and his command of the pitch isn't as good as the command on the slider. But the overall premise of his article is dumb.

Hitters "sit" on Trevor Hoffman's changeup and they can't hit it. They sit on Jamie Moyer's batting practice fastball/changeup and can't hit it.
