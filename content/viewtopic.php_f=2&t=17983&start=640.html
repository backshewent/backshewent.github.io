The suit cites several examples of egregiously gerrymandered districts, including the infamous 7th District that has been called "Goofy kicking Donald Duck" for its odd shape. At one point near King of Prussia, the district is held together only by a restaurant, Creed's Seafood and Steaks.

Reading was then added to the staunchly Republican 16th District where "the votes of Democratic voters are virtually certain never to matter," the suit says.
The lawsuit aims to change this by forcing the state to redraw the maps. It follows a similar suit in Wisconsin, where a federal court ruled the state's House plan was unconstitutional. That case is before the U.S. Supreme Court.
Similar suits have also been filed in Maryland, where the Congressional map unfairly favors Democrats, and in North Carolina, where the map favors Republicans.
