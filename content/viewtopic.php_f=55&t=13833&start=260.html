It would be interesting to see what Lee's trade value is. Texas would probably give up a couple real good prospects for the guarantee of signing Cole at like 7-160 (hypothetical trade with signing window). Cole is significantly younger, but I don't know that he's any better than Lee right now. If they get Lee they're on the hook for 3 guaranteed years at 87.5mm, plus a vesting option for a net price of 15mm. I would think Lee's contract would be viewed as very valuable by a big market team just because of the (reasonable) amount of years involved

Would they give up Cruz and a good prospect, say Olt, for Lee? That would clear about 14mm of "cap" space from lux tax threshold next year (Cruz makes 10.5mm), and would make Texas better on the whole this year. We may have the leverage to demand Cruz since there is no urgency to move Lee. Haven't heard anything about extension talks with Cruz either, and they need to take care of Hamilton. Then we could either keep Cruz and Pence in the corners next year, with money to sign Chole, or flip Pence for more useful cheap parts
