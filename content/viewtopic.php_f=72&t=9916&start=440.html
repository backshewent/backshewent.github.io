by lethal » Fri Jan 08, 2010 20:27:37

You can either deduct per mile or actual expenses, not both.

So what you can do is calculate the rest of your actual car expenses and deduct those as unreimbursed business expenses or you can log your miles (you need to keep an accurate logbook of miles traveled and what day), multiply that by the federal per mile rate, then reduce by the amount that you've been reimbursed (for gas) and deduct that total.

Previously I was referring to the job seeking expenses deduction for Crashburn. That's a different one.

I've come to realize over the past few weeks that individual income tax is the area that I really enjoy even though my practice involves approximately 1% individual income tax and 99% corporate and international tax.

I have no idea how to make that switch and not take a salary decrease though.

***The above should not constitute tax or legal advice. Please consult with your tax consultant to prepare your tax filing.
