I know Pujols was off to a cold start last year, so his stats at this point are meaningless, but still:

.231/.317/.397/.714

Oh yeah, he's 33. And they're "only" paying him $16M this year... he gets wildly more expensive starting next year, to the tune of $26.5M/season AAV... for eight more seasons.

Howard is having a better season in every respect at this point, is the same age, and is only signed for 4 more years at an AAV $24.5M/season:

.282/.320/.513/.833

We'll see how it comes out when the season is all said and done, of course (I am sure Pujols will end up with better numbers than Howard, but maybe not hugely so), but the thing I think about when I look at all of these contracts is why the "common wisdom" on long-term contracts handed out to veterans continues to be to back-load them. You kill yourself that way - you pay more and more for less and less. At least Howard is $25M steadily until the last season, when he drops to $23M. Pujols' number keeps rising and rising, until they are paying him $30M a season in his forties.
