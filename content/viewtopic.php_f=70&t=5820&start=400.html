by Bucky » Tue Jul 01, 2008 21:10:35

RE: Text vs. email

Not only is the computer protocol different, the cultural protocol is also different. Most emails are meant to be replied to at your convenience. Text messaging is more of an immediate need type thing- yo, I need an answer right now- are you coming to this meeting or not??? Or, MEATBALL IT'S BUCKY WHERE THE HELL ARE YOU GUYS PARKED??? That sort of stuff.

I have different alerts for emails v. text messages. So I know if someone is trying to get me with some urgency. Why not just call then?? Because that's even a little different protocol. People can't always answer their phone, but they can usually find somehow to answer a text within a couple minutes.

Phone:Synchronous
Text:Semi-synchronous
email:asynchronous

Each has their place. Just demonstrated this today during my trip to DC. One of my customers sent me an email about a meeting he wants me to attend in NYC tomorrow. Saw it on my blueberry, and was about to leave the house...so I called him on the drive and we discussed. Agreed I would send him my Amex # and he would book our train tickets. Got to DC, emailed him that stuff. He emailed me back a few other backgound docs about tomorrow's meeting which I read at my leisure at lunch. Later I was walking down around the monument, and heard my text chirp-looked at it right away. "CHANGE OF VENUE- JERSEY CITY NOW. STILL ACELA? So I just sent one word back "Drive". Had my phone rang, I would not have even looked at it to see who it was. Wouldn't divert that much attention from my kid during family time.

We effectively used all three forms of communication in the course of a day.
