took my family fishing yesterday on the Chesapeake. Beautiful evening, warm but a nice breeze out of the southwest.

Ended up with a mess of white perch, lots of undersized striped bass, a few spot, and I caught a 40 pound cownose ray. My kids were dumbfounded when I got up next to the boat and they saw it flapping its wings. It actually straightened out my hook so release was easy.

On a sadder nose, we also caught two 28 inch stripers that were badly infected with mycobacteriosis, a terrible infection that can be passed to humans and is lethal if not treated promptly. The Maryland DNR believes up to 70% of stripers have the disease, which ends up being fatal to fish. Brutal stuff, the water quality in the Bay, while the best its been in years, is still in need of major improvement.
