by Luzinski's Gut » Fri Sep 30, 2016 08:18:37

The Congressional override on the 9/11 legal foundations may have a very interesting side effect, one that has the possibility of fundamentally changing our foreign policy for the better.

If the Executive Branch is this concerned about retribution lawsuits from people in the Middle East, then it may force two very substantial discussions. The first is that it probably will limit the Executive Branch from being the sole determiner of whether we commit military force to a region as the President, the VP, the National Security Advisor and other foreign policy creators will be the one being sued. This could force Congress to actually do what the hell they are charged to do, which is declare war for the nation. Once that decision point has been reached, you now have a much broader base of support and the financial resources to stall out any kind of lawsuit.

The second discussion, I think, will be around how these potential lawsuits constrain our foreign policy. We have had an interventionist foreign policy - meaning we are going to get up in other countries via diplomatic, economic, intelligence and military matters as we see fit - since the end of the Cold War. I can easily see this changing now. A side effect would also concern the moral dimensions of these interventionist actions. There's a good possibility that the constructs of the just war theory will come back into play, and that is going to greatly restrain our interventionist foreign policy aims.

Some info on just war theory here:
http://www.iep.utm.edu/justwar/

I actually applaud Congress for doing this, although I suspect very few of them realize the scope and nature of their decision. I suspect this is why we are seeing such strenuous resistance from the highest ranking officials in the Executive Branch.
