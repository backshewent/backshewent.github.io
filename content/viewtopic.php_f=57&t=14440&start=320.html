by Trent Steele » Fri Mar 01, 2013 10:13:50

Ticket sales are down by about 500,000. 2.6m sold compared to 3.1m sold last year at this time.

That's a significant number. Obviously, they'll make some of it back on walk up, but even 200,000 is a big deal and would likely equate to around $15-20m in revenue decline (which is almost pure profit other than cost of goods sold for concessions).

This worries me more than anything else and I hope the failure to truly spend this year isn't reflective of the fact that the Phillies are worried about it. Grote's assumptions are premised on maintaining a luxury-tax level payroll. All bets are off if that doesn't happen.

Maybe the local TV contract means this won't matter, but I don't believe that. We're committed on this board, but most of the Phillies' revenue growth has been driven by people who are not. I firmly believe they will bail if things go badly.

by Grotewold » Fri Mar 01, 2013 10:19:45

It's a legitimate concern, but I think the automatic sellouts in 2010-2011 were the anomaly. This year's sales are actually above those of this time in 2009, coming off of a WFC. I think the key factor is season-ticket holders who used to scoop up stacks of extra tickets to sell on the secondary market not doing so after having trouble selling those last summer.

I definitely think there are fairweathers who will bail with another down year, but hopefully they'd be replaced by people who just love going to CBP and couldn't easily do so the past few years. And the new tiered pricing system should help.

But I really can't see the Phillies significantly cutting payroll anytime soon, regardless of on-field results. It would be incredibly short-sighted, imo, from a business perspective, in terms of the TV-deal renewal and their current profile locally and nationally. If they let go of this thing it's gonna be really hard to get it back.

My view is that this year is critical. To drop season ticket sales by 15% in one year is big. If they throw up another 80 win season, I think you will see an even larger drop next year, especially if pieces start to get sold off. Things could spiral very very quickly. If fans don't think this is a playoff team in 2014, a large number will just buy tickets on the secondary market (the prices of which will significantly drop) instead of buying packages.

I don't know what ownership will do if that happens, but I'm not optimistic. In my view, Amaro blows with a $180m payroll. I can't imagine what he would do with a $140m payroll.
