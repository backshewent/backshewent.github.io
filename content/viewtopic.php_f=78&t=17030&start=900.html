I'll admit I am rooting for Trump, and, perhaps for the reasons jh I outlined above, I shouldn't be. But, I'll say two things:

1. I am reasonably certain that even if Trump wins, he's actually full of it. The truth, I suppose, is that Trump is in that "Tyson Zone" that one of our posters named and which Bill Simmons gave fame. I'd believe almost anything at this point about from where Trump might attempt to govern. Will he govern from a hard, autocratic, populist (even fascist) right, as much of his campaign has implied? He might. I equally believe it is possible he'd do a 180 and be the relative leftist he's always been in terms of the populism, but drop the racist bravado entirely. Or just about anything in between. I believe any of this is possible because the final, irreduceable (sp?) truth is obvious: he has no actual ideas. He is a cipher, an empty suit. Thus, he is very likely to blow with the wind, in particular the one at his back, forever finding the path of least resistance - the one he has always tread.

2. Ted Cruz is genuinely more frightening to me than Donald Trump, because he appears to believe what he says. And Ben Carson just doesn't seem capable on any level of the office, even the performative aspect of it (that Trump has at least attempted to "master" - OK, I just died a little writing that). That leaves Kasich and Rubio. If Trump is everything he says he is, yes, I suppose they are "better" in that they are not monsters. But the truth is none of these guys - not Trump, not Cruz, not Rubio, not even Sanders or Clinton - are running for dictator: they are running for President. The most impactful thing any of them will do is make a SCOTUS appointment or three. Who frightens me the most in terms of picking SCOTUS justices? Cruz, Rubio, Kasich, and Trump - in that order. So I root for Trump.

Still don't think he'll win.
