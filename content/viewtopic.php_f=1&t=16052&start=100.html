Eem wrote:Here's the thing that's soured me on Foles. You can live with INTs and even inaccurate throws. Young QBs make them ALL the time, and it isn't necessarily fair to expect Foles to live up to what he did last year. Those things are mechanical -- on both picks, he made the right read, just bad throws. They're backbreakers, but at least you know he made the right throw.

But all of a sudden, he is cowering in the face of pressure. I don't know if that's something I've ever seen a QB recover from. He overreacts to teams just showing blitz. Bowles knew it -- look at that play to end the game last night. Bowles knew Foles was going to back up as soon as the ball was snapped, and he'd have to make an extremely low-percentage throw to win the game. How many throws did he make yesterday that had just no chance at all of being completed?


A silver lining could be that it's out of character, from everything I've read and seen. Maybe he'll get a psychological boost from Kelce (especially) and Mathis returning
