TenuredVulture wrote:Has infighting really damaged the Republicans? I don't really see how.

I'm basically getting to the point where almost all political commentary about this or that being a problem for this or that other thing is pretty much nonsense.

The GOP controlled the House for 8 years and consistently could not do the most basic things like pass a budget or pass appropriations because of defections from the perfect is the enemy of the good folks of the Freedom Caucus (who have mostly, and hilariously, become the least principled/most reflexive Trump defenders when he departs from True Conservatism). This meant they always had to reach out for Dem votes which resulted in bills that had to giveaway items on the Dem priority list. It was a self fulfilling prophecy - fail to pass a budget with GOP votes, add spending that Pelosi and her caucus wanted to get the Dem votes needed to keep government running, GOP leadership are big spending RINO sellouts - that repeated constantly. Beyond what that has meant for governance, I think this also led to party leadership being increasingly unpopular with the base and becoming the whipping boys of talk radio and Fox, in part paving the way for Trump to win the nomination if by nothing else harming the ability of people who may have otherwise had the standing with party voters to affect anything.

A variety of other policy specific examples come to mind, led by Obamacare things. There was the divide that led to the Cruz/HFC gov shutdown over Obamacare in 2013, which was a trainwreck, and then later divisions in 2017 and 2018 on how to approach it, which somehow led to an outcome where limited policy ends were achieved (mandate repeal happened in tax reform not a health care bill), the central goal was completely whiffed on, but the unpopular bills that failed were still voted on and used effectively by Dems in messaging in the 2018 wave.

GOP infighting on the wall led Trump to fail to achieve maybe his most notable policy promise, and also resulted in a completely moronic 5 week shutdown.
