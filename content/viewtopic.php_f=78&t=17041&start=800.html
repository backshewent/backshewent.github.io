Regardless of the merits of raw milk, often food "safety" regulations are not as much about consumer safety as they are about protecting incumbent businesses. It's not as bad as the liquor industry in that regard but there are lots of examples. Of course, the "let the market decide" won't work either, as there are vast information asymmetries between the business and consumer so we do have to look at this on a case by case basis. For instance, it is possible that backyard, unregulated eggs are probably safer than the ones you might buy in the grocery store.
