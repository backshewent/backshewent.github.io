Saw something today that pretty much perfectly captured what it is like living where I am currently living. I should have videotaped it and played it any time someone asked me what it's like....

I was coming to class this morning and it was snowing outside. There was a light dusting on the ground, maybe 1/4 inch, so they had people out to clear the sidewalks. But they don't have 2 or 3 people, there was like 30 people to clear maybe 200 meters of sidewalks total and that's if you count the walkways around all the buildings that are never used in the morning. Rather than being spread out, they were in two groups of 15 or so. There was one guy with a wheelbarrow filled with salt. He started in one group for about 2-3 minutes and then he went to the other big group. He went along the sidewalk throwing handfuls of salt onto the snowy sidewalk. The 15 or so others, each armed with a shovel or broom, followed after him, shoveling the snow AND THE SALT into the grass and leaving the slippery sidewalk with no salt. I pointed at the guy with the salt to try to tell them they were shoveling the salt into the grass, but they just smiled and the guy with the wheelbarrow gave me a thumbs up.

Each year they have to replace the grass in all the grassy areas because they can't get it to grow properly. Now I know why.
