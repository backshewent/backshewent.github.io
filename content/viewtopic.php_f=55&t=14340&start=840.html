That's what I feel like they are doing which is why I'm not getting too excited about any of the moves (while not enjoying any of them either). They are basically adding no significant salaries beyond this year and not giving up any top prospects or draft picks while staying below the lux tax this year. If the aging core can get it together in the beginning of the season and keep them in the race, maybe they rethink and add someone in the middle of the year and take the lux hit or give up prospects. If not, they sell off what they can mid season and get what they can.

Previous risks put them in this position now and they can't really move any of their big salaries and retool. So they have to wait until after this season for a couple of the big salaries to expire and for some of the prospects to either become major leaguers or more valuable trading chips before they can do any revamping. Any of the big moves would have put them at risk for future seasons and may not have helped enough this year anyway. Not fun but it might be a realistic approach given the circumstances. I'll just keep reminding myself how crappy I thought the previous WS winners were and how great the Phillies were when not winning the WS.
