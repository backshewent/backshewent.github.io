regarding Chip. His has three choices:

-- retire - he's already a wealthy man
-- go back to college forever
-- go back to college, dominate again and then pull a Pete Carrol

Some day Belichick is going to retire and Brad won't be there anymore. I could see Chip doing a 5 year thing at a brand name college and then coming to NE with a a "new perspective" and Belichick as a kind of advisor.

Chip has a great offensive football mind. And he will be a good recruiter and commander in chief in college. He will succeed there. I suspect he would like to prove himself in the NFL again but that's not happening now.

What college is the right one for him? Not sure...

Nick Foles 27-3-2, Shady McCoy 1600yards, lead NFL in plays over 20, 40 and 50 yards - (2013) that actually happened. He did something right. He knew/knows something... he also probably saved Colin Kaepernick's career.

Chip has to do some soul searching and growing, being a leader of men, it's a challenge. But I think we'll see Chip succeed again - at least in college.

Is he right for Notre Dame? No idea. I don't follow that school or college football politics that well. But he'll succeed again.

His mistake, was not taking a year off between Eagles and whatever came next, and then going to a team with bad owner and GM. He needs to learn how to operate in the NFL. He's way too defensive in pressers and snarky.
