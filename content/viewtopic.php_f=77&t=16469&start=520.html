Yes.

Completely underrated stuff. It's too mild to be much on its own, but it has the ideal creamy/salty flavor and texture to complement a burger, a cheesesteak, a ham sammich, an omelet.

Cheese and crackers, you want the more interesting stuff, of course.

Agreed. I think there's a comfort food quality to putting it on burgers, especially thin patty, diner/fast food types of burgers.

I don't disagree with some of the stuff in that burger article, but to act like there is one right way to make a burger is silly and clickbait-y. I've had thin little flat-top diner burgers that are great and giant, fat pub style grilled ones with fancy cheeses that are awesome as well. That's why burgers rule.
