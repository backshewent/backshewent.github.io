Youseff wrote:gotta admit, I love to eat food more than smell it. heck, eating is smelling! unless your olfactory skills are damaged, it's impossible to separate the two. and heck, when you think about it, eating food helps you survive!

eating is awesome, one of my favorite daily habits. All of the senses are at play when we're eating, separating those is interacting just as an aside. In context of consumption though, I do like to eat to live, and not live to eat, and make attempts to always leave room for air and water. So important. Food water air and the breath is sustenance of life. Sometimes we fill ourselves to our hearts content instead of our stomachs, submit to gluttony and then pay the price afterwards. Triggering that sense of smell can bring me euphoria, which allows me to breathe the moment and appreciate the food, filling part of me up. Then maybe my minds like, "mm, i wanna taste a little bit too.", but most times it's like "ah that was good" and I notice i'm already full of the other stuff or that i'm hungry for the other stuff, and i'll top it off with the taste of food later. Think of our circumstance in time and place right now, everything we want ever is always forever there easily. Filling ourselves probably won't ever be a real problem, but maintaining that hunger metaphorically will always be a battle. I'm always reminded of this Socreates anecdote:

"Even Socrates, who lived a very frugal and simple life, loved to go to the market. When his students asked about this, he replied, "I love to go and see all the things I am happy without.”

I wouldn't like it very much if it seemed i was pointing my snout up at anybody, i select moments to indulge too. Food is fuel is energy so if my inside is asking for seconds, i'll comply. But maybe it just needs some air and water. I guess you gotta understand your pangs.
