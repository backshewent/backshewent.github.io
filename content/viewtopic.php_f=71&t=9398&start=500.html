Were you at his show last night? Oh my god. Unprecedented hilarity.

There's a story I like to recount, one I read in a Jimi Hendrix biography. A big shot blues guitarist went to see him at a small club early in his career, and afterward he said that some guitarists were so good they made you want to play guitar yourself. Hendrix was so good he made you want to quit playing because you'd never be in the same universe.

Anyway, that's how Oswalt made me feel last night. Things that would ordinarily have been funny just weren't good enough to make me laugh; any jokes that crossed my mind just weren't even worth making. He killed it, and then when I was satisfied, he came out and did arguably his funniest bits in the encore. He did a set almost entirely made up of new material just a month after releasing a great CD. I was stunned.

His CD had some stuff: b-word fat, treadmill, the Ratatouille stuff, Sky Cake, Obama making time travel cheaper, the orgy story and the rat story. But a lot of stuff was brand new.
