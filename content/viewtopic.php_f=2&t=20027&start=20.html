Got busier this week at work and was annoyed at the seeming inconsistencies of the day to day fluctuations, but week to week seems like the data should be real. Or as real as it can be with the testing issues.

Things are bad and I don't think they're getting better, but I think on the whole the rate at which they're getting worse seems to have plateaued. Still almost half of the deaths in the state happened in the past seven days, averaging 270 per, and I'd guess we hit something close to that number again next week. Unless this week was inflated by adding in nursing home deaths that have occurred all along (hopefully?) or maybe nursing homes will do a better job moving forward (or so many people have died in them that we're running out of people who can get it). I saw on the news last night that 40% of deaths in Jersey have occurred in nursing homes, which is staggering.

Camden opening up some space in the 'lead' among counties in South Jersey. The city of Camden, looking at the municipality data, is worse than the county as a whole, which makes sense given what we're seeing around the country in cities vs. more suburban and rural places. Seems very much though like social distancing went into place early enough in SJ that it won't get truly awful like the northern part of the state. Just quite bad. Rate of growth of new cases isn't that steep, which is positive, if it's not entirely an artifact of testing capacity. Everything still looks like a ways away from being OK though.
