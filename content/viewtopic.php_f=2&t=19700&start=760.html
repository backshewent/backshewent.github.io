Growing up, I took the bus from first grade (I didn't go to public kindergarten) through fifth. Don't remember my parents ever being at the stop; maybe the first day of first grade, but I don't recall.

At our place in Lansdale, the elementary school my kids went to was right in the center of the neighborhood; I think everyone who went to the school lived within 3/4 mile. There was no busing by the district at all. My kids went to the Y for daycare and they were bused but that was later in the morning so we never had the bus stop thing to deal with.

At our new place we're about 2.5 miles from the elementary school (and the middle school which my eldest now goes to). So the kids take the bus. There are always parents at the bus stop even for the middle/high school bus, but my wife and I only went out with the kids for the first day of school. What I find weird is that in our little neighborhood (52 houses total) there are three stops. If there were just a single stop down where the neighborhood meets the main road, nobody would have to walk as much as half a mile.
