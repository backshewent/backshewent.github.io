its like we went from the being the yankees of the NL to being the pirates in like 3 minutes. i expected there would be a gradual comedown. i figured after 2011, with howard injured, that we'd see a slight regression. i didnt expect a catastrophic season like last year (relatively speaking)

and heading in to this year, my optimism was at pre-2007 levels. it all happened so quickly. it kind of sucks

If you're right and it's over, I'm not sure the sudden collapse is worse than a gradual decline. It's sort of a sudden death of say a major stroke v. the slow decline of alzheimers. Neither are good. But the grieving is longer in the latter case. And you can spend a long time in denial about the whole situation. Given that the Phillies aren't literally going to die, the quick death really does have advantages. A losing season will make it very difficult to deny that the team needs to enter a rebuilding phase. I'm sure they would have preferred to one by one replace the core as they faded into retirement, and I suspect that was the plan. But more or less it's pretty clear that's not going to work. The core aged rapidly and all at once.

In fact, it really looks like the Phils started to accept this reality in the off season--the lack of any big moves seems consistent with a "wait and see" approach to 2013. The stroke hit last season. The patient has been on life support ever since. The plans to take that one last luxury trip to Europe were cancelled. Soon, it will be time to acknowledge that he's not going to recover and it's time to pull the plug.
