Bakestar wrote:I got stiffed on gifts by two wedding guests, including a guy who invited himself to the wedding (and brought his girlfriend), and a guy who lived on my couch for four months.


I got stiffed at my wedding by two couples (and one really cheap one)- all on my wife's side including her closest cousin. He just re-upped with the marines and is headed for Afghanistan but he had a few extra days to waste in NJ before he had to report to North Carolina I think so we let him crash here. Before he left he gave us a wedding card with a $100 gift card to Walmart inside. Yeah our wedding was almost 6 years ago. I went out a few days later and put it towards a new Xbox that had HDMI for my gameroom (which I'm now selling on ebay along with a few games so I can upgrade to the Elite for basically the same price).

One of her best friend's didn't give us a fucking card let alone a gift (for which she had promised to make up for but never even made an attempt). Her parents were invited as well and broke out a crisp $50 bill. So yeah $50 from 6 people- so we only lost $1000 on the deal (well $900 if you count the gift card 6 years later).

Before our wedding our parents sat us down and said we will either:

A) Throw a small reception for immediate family and friends- maybe 30 people tops and then give us the rest in cash for the wedding that my wife wanted.
B) Have the wedding but get no cash since it ended up being ~220 people @ ~$175/head, plus flowers, a dress, tuxes, limos, a vintage Rolls Royce, a photographer, and all of that other stupid BS.

I immediately said cash. She immediately said wedding. Her eyes welled up and I gave in despite the opportunity to be able to pay off all of our debt and put a decent size down on a house. I thought about it and concluded that the wedding is the woman's equivalent of her team winning the Super Bowl or World Series. They dream of that day since they are like 10. But guess what? I dreamed of playing DE for USC since I was 12, too. Sometimes you have to roll with the punches. You would think at some point you have to use some logic in the equation but I digress. I ended up spending 75% of the night doing blow in the bathroom with my scumbag friends. I don't even remember it really. I know I didn't eat anything.

Anyway if this opportunity ever presents itself to you... take the cash and do whatever it takes to convince her that's the smart move. We probably haven't even talked to 75% of the wedding guests more than 5 or 6 times since the wedding. Ridiculous.
