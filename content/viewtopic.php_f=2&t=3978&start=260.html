What's the better brand? Kodak, Canon, Lumix, Nikon?

Not Kodak - they're bankrupt and haven't made good cameras in ages.

You can't really go wrong with Canon, Nikon, Sony, Panasonic, or Olympus (the latter is troubled as a company due to some shady financial dealings, but their Micro Four Thirds equipment is fully compatible with the standard and thus, even if the company goes belly-up, will continue to be usable into the future). The former two are the most established brands and have the most robust lens lineups, which may or may not interest you. Sony bought Minolta some years ago so it also has a well-established stable of lenses. Panasonic and Olympus jointly created the Micro Four Thirds standard a few years ago, which is a standard for interchangeable-lens cameras that utilize full-time through-the-sensor image viewing, rather than the previous standard SLR format. This permits more compact design but has some disadvantages, primarily poorer auto-focus performance (though still very good compared to point-and-shoots).

I own the very first Micro Four Thirds camera, the Panasonic Lumix G1. Though the format was designed from the ground up to enable superior video capture vs. any existing digital still camera, mine has no video mode at all. The story goes that Panasonic didn't have their video mode fully refined so they opted to omit it entirely rather than put some half-baked video mode into the camera. Since I personally don't care at all about video that's not a drawback for me. But all subsequent MFT cameras have very good video.

What are your requirements for a camera? Can you rank the parameters below?

Compactness
Large lens lineup
Low-light image quality
Direct manual controls (as in, lots of knobs and buttons, vs. going through menus to change settings)
Depth-of-field control (the ability to focus on the eyelashes of your portrait subject while having the rest progressively in less focus)
Video quality
Ability to exercise manual control over photographic settings during video shooting
Auto-focus speed
