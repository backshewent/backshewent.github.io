The Congressional Commission on Military Compensation just released their report and recommendations on how to reform how we are paid and how to reform the retirement system.

http://mldc.whs.mil/public/docs/report/ ... N15-HI.pdf

What I like:

- Giving the opportunity for personnel to leave the service with a retirement plan/401K before serving a full 20 years. I've always hated the all or nothing aspect of the military retirement program.
- Grandfathering current service members into the current system. Only right after 14 years of persistent combat.
- Ensuring the best medical care to wounded servicepeople
- Adding flexibility and expanded choices of doctors under TRICARE. This caused our family serious problems when my wife was diagnosed with her rare genetic disease and forced her to leave the TRICARE system so she could be seen at Hopkins within a month instead of the usual 4-6 month wait under TRICARE.
- Ensure better transition from military service to civilian careers. Honestly, a dead man could do a better job because at least he was cause no harm.
- Better Survivor Benefits. The progam is weak right now.

What I don't like:
- Creating yet another 4-Star headquarters. More bureaucracy and a massive 4-Star staff is going to suck personnel out of already critical jobs and create multiple layers of frustration.


All in all, a good report becuase it gives more options, potentially better service and better compensation.

Pennsylvania state treasurer quits, will plead guilty to campaign finance charges

Pennsylvania state Treasurer Rob McCord, a Democrat who ran unsuccessfully for governor last year, resigned abruptly on Friday and said he would plead guilty to federal campaign finance charges.

Federal investigators were looking into allegations that McCord solicited campaign contributions in exchange for official acts, according to the Philadelphia Inquirer. The probe has been going on for several months.

McCord’s attorney, Robert Welsh, said in a statement that McCord would admit to campaign finance violations.

“Mr. McCord will be pleading guilty to certain federal charges to be filed arising out of his attempts in the spring of 2014 to raise campaign contributions from two potential contributors by communicating that if they failed to make campaign contributions, he could make it difficult for them to do business with the Commonwealth,” Welsh said in a statement. “Immediately upon being contacted by federal agents about these matters, Mr. McCord acknowledged that he had overstepped the line of legitimate political fundraising.”
