by TomatoPie » Wed Sep 30, 2015 09:00:08



Via 313 serves bar pies and Detroit-style pizza in Austin. I still haven't been to Detroit for this stuff, but this one is superb. Kind of like the Best Sicilian Ever. Baked without sauce, which is ladled on post-bake. Cheese deliberately wraps around the edge for a caramelized crunch on every slice. The bar pie was also excellent, but this one was the star.

If you can get to Manheim on a Tuesday, Norma makes an even better Detroit pie at Roots Country Market.

Bazbeaux, the iconic pizza of Indianapolis. Crust was medium-thin and sturdy, but not distinctive like those Austin pies. Tasty toppings include house made Italian sausage and chorizo (both on this pie) and Andouille. Too much cheese. This would have been Grade A 15 years ago, but is just a B or B+ today. Lovely people here in Indy, but not a great pizza town. Stick with steaks at St Elmo or Harry&Izzy.

Breakfast pizza @ Wolfgang Puck Express - Indy Airport.

Fairly authentic Neapolitan pie from a wood-fired oven. Great flavor and balance - slightly undercooked but otherwise spot-on. Pretty equivalent to Rapidough's.

The good news is - there is going to be a LOT of these "pretty damned good" Neapolitan chains.

The bad news is that they will drive out a lot of the fabulous pies that cost a little bit more.

Enjoy this pizza renaissance while it lasts.
