PrattRules wrote:Make sure to keep it turned off.

To avoid roaming, I assume?



Yeah. Who's your carrier? I have AT&T, I went to the Domican a few years back, they don't charge you to recieve text messages, but they charge you an arm and a leg to send them, but if someone calls you, and you don't pick up, they charge you for 1 min of international roaming. Really, it's not worth using you cell phone out of the country.

What kind of phone do you have? This won't work if you have an iPhone, but if you have pretty much anything else, you can call your carrier and tell them you are going to be traveling overseas and you'd like the unlock code for your phone, you might have to try a couple of times before they do it, but I got my Treo unlocked back in the day doing that. Then when you are wherever you are going, you can pick up a prepaid sim and drop it in your phone. They'll still kill you for calling back to the states, but since it's prepaid, you can limit the damage, and then if you need to make any local calls you can do that and it'll just use the min. you bought on the prepaid sim.
