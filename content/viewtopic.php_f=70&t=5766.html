by Laexile » Fri Jun 20, 2008 03:01:18

John McCain has been accused of flip-flopping. While I think most of it is a brilliant Democratic marketing campaign to simplify a candidate with a complicated record, Senator McCain needs to explain how he could consider a windfall profits tax one month and then criticize Senator Obama for doing so the next month. A candidate must be challenged especially when he runs on authenticity. People should keep pressing him to clarify where he stands and why his position may have changed.

Unfortunately, there won't be the same call for Senator Obama. What he did today was so distrubing that he irked Russ Feingold, a fervent supporter. I have no problem with him opting out of Federal financing if he hadn't been calling for Senator McCain to stay in Federal financing with him once a month. That wouldn't be that big a deal if Obama were just honest about why he was opting out. If he just said, "I'll make the best President and this gives me the best chance to win" he might be going back on his reformer image but at least he'd get kudos for honesty.

Instead he says, McCain is "not going to stop the smears and attacks from his allies running so-called 527 groups, who will spend millions and millions of dollars in unlimited donation." There have been no Republican groups running smears and attacks on him and there doesn't appear to be any on the horizon. The 527s hate McCain and would like nothing better than to see him lose. They know that he'll try to pass legislation to put them out of business. The only 527 out there, Moveon.org, is making "smears and attacks" on McCain. I'm sure Obama figures that he can make McCain look bad for being behind non-existent smears while he bears no responsibility for Democratic 527s. I think Republican 527s would rather run ads in favor of Obama. At least then they could stay in business and make smears in favor of candidates they actually like.
