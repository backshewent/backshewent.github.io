I own a Cutco vegetable knife (looks kind of like a long but light cleaver - an 8+ inch blade, but not tapered like a chef's knife) and a paring knife. The steel sucks - they were razor sharp when I got them but they're impossible to keep sharp for more than a few cuts, it seems. And the handles don't suit my hands at all (well, it's fine on the paring knife but too small for the veggie knife).

If you want a good knife, far cheaper than Cutco but a better, easier to maintain edge, look for a budget Japanese Gyuto. I got mine for about $50 a few years ago. It's a thinner blade than a standard European-style chef's knife, made from a harder steel that holds an edge much better than the crappy steel Cutco knifes are made from.

I have found them really easy to use and I can cut the hell out of everything I've tried so far. It's made cooking tons easier than ever before.
