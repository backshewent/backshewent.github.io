by dajafi » Thu Sep 04, 2014 10:23:23

Brown's going to be a great case for or against the efficacy of Phillies coaching and development at the major league level.

This team sucks, and they're going to suck for awhile. Getting the absolute most out of whatever they have should be the top priority. We've already seen Dom flounder through a couple partial seasons, absolutely explode for a couple months, fail to sustain it and flounder again. His defense actually has gotten worse, and there's a strong case to be made that the one guy who got through to him, Joyner, was the one they parted ways with.

I think he's gone this offseason. If he goes somewhere else and re-emerges as a solid contributor, or better, that's really damning.

An overlooked factor to the great run was that pretty much every homegrown guy other than Hamels exceeded expectations after reaching the big leagues, as did some of the cheap acquisitions. I don't know if it was Manuel's genius as a hitting coach or what, but Utley and Howard especially were much better than most thought they'd be. Same with Victorino, who had been a multiple Rule 5 guy, and Werth. They didn't arrive as stars. Given the known holes in Franco's game and the likelihood that whoever they add this winter will be flawed too, this matters a lot.
