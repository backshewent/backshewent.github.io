Adding to that, when there are not outsized rewards for innovation as there are in the private sector, then you are probably going to get less innovation overall. Anyone that has worked for a large employer certainly can attest to the inefficiency that is baked into any large organization. Well, the US defense department is the largest employer in the world, 50% larger than the largest private entity (Walmart).

It's actually an interesting question--there are obvious economies of scale, and there are also diseconomies of scale as well. Where's the sweet spot of efficiency? It most likely depends on the activity in question--I'd suspect, for instance, for a university, that sweet spot is around 4000-5000 students, which would mean a faculty/staff of 300-500. Much beyond that the institution becomes administratively top heavy.
