by kruker » Sat Feb 26, 2011 03:57:19

Hitters decline as they get older?

Like I said, I don't think the difference between Utley and Zim is going to be that dramatic over the lifetimes of their current contracts. Unless you could guarantee Zimmerman signing a favorable extension at the time of the trade, I don't think there's a big enough benefit to shake up the organization. For as good as Zimmerman was last year, his WAR was only 1.2 higher than Chase in 27 more games. He hasn't posted a WAR that would be in Chase's top 5. That's not a knock on Zimmerman, but just going to show you that he's not a lock to outproduce Chase over the next three years. Chase isn't going to regress from being a consistent 6+ WAR player overnight to a dead weight. So where's the benefit to trading him now?

You've got to take the analysis a step further here. If you keep Utley, you get comparable production over the next three years, then are free to either sign Zimmerman as a free agent or that third sacker who plays in NY who also happens to be a free agent after 2013. Or someone else...whatever. The point is, when making this comparison, its wrong to consider anything achieved by Zimmerman at the end of his contract without factoring in what can be done with Chase's 15 million.

I don't see any reason to expect Chase's production to fall off a cliff. He's been as reliable as anyone in the game since he became a starter and his injuries haven't been of the chronic variety. I understand the argument that you minimize production volatility by going younger, but that has to be weighed against the likelihood of Utley severely regressing and the effects to the team and organization. Part of the reason we've been able to attract Halladay, Lee, Oswalt here is that we're an organization with continuity and an established nucleus. Trading Utley, while still producing at an MVP level, shatters that image. If you have a good reason to believe that Chase is going downhill, ok, but a tenuous comparative analysis based on position and statistical comparability isn't convincing.
"Everybody's a critic. This wasn't an aesthetic endeavor."
