kruker wrote:I get that was the easy move, but....



I love how Haseley has looked, but i don't see how you justify any other move. Obviously, you have Harper in right. Corey Dickerson has done everything he needs to justify getting most of the starts in left field. Quinn has been hitting great and you don't want to eliminate his speed and defense from the roster. Bruce can finally slot in as a pinch hitter and occasional sub where he belongs. You want Haseley to play, and there simply aren't enough openings on the major league squad for him to get lots of time.

At least until Quinn gets hurt.

I guess I get the hate toward Rodriguez, though he's done pretty well in what he's been asked to do. I always prefer guys on the bench who can do at least one thing well, rather than Bruntletts who are able to do a number of things okay. But I've resigned myself to the fact that managers love multi-purpose players-fun as it is for us to see players out of position, it causes them agita. And it's certainly not a role I want Haseley to be playing at this stage of his career.
