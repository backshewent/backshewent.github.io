He is a perfect example of an attitude towards baseball analysis that I abhor. He happens to write about the team I follow. He is not just some local guy running a blog. He writes for ESPN.com and is interviewed on the radio to provide expert analysis. Obsessively documenting bad/annoying writing on the internet about the Phillies is one of the main things we do on this board. There are probably dozens of threads like this. You just happen to know someone personally who is providing the content.

The response from criticism of his over the top negativity in his awful spring training article was that we didn't understand that he was a gifted storyteller and fans who didn't like the negativity were being elitists by telling everyone else that they should not be negative (when he was the one telling everyone that they should not be optimistic). That made me really irritated. The over the top poor me I love Dom Brown ESPN.com post made it worse. I started paying more attention to what he wrote and saw lots of examples of bad writing, elitism, obsessive over-the-top negativity, etc. Rather than attacking someone personally or calling them names, etc., when I criticize/mock those who write about the Phillies I prefer to use their own words against them. That requires a lot of examples and he keeps providing them.
