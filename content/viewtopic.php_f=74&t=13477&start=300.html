by TenuredVulture » Fri Apr 13, 2012 12:42:16

I think some of this is governed by state FOIA statutes. There is a context here as well. Local boards and governments have long operated under a veil of secrecy and have sought to obstruct compliance with FOIA requests at every turn. In response, a number of journalists and activists have gotten very aggressive in filing FOIA requests. U of A probably just figured it was a lot easier to comply (in part because they knew information they released would add to the justification of firing Petrino) than dragging this out with an FOIA fight. So, they're not dragging Petrino's name through the mud, they're complying with state law.
