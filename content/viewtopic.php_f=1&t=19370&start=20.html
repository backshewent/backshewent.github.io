When a team come off of a disappointing season, things usually look bleak for a while. As for the Flyers, they are set in goal for the next decade and a half with Hart and the other goalies that are in the system. Its pretty much the same with the Flyers on defense. The forwards need some more speed and finishing skill. The greatest organizational need is a coach and staff that can scheme better with the talent that is here. This is a PO team roster for next season providing injuries don't raise havoc with it again.
As for the draft, so what? Hughes may be a player and then again maybe, not. There is just as much of a chance that the player that the Flyers take at eleven will have as much of an impactive career as Hughes or KK. These are eighteen year olds. What they will become by the time they hit twenty-five is anyone' guess.
Regarding the Devils getting the top pick in two of the last three seasons, the lack of transparency with the draft, it smacks of Bettmann having his hands on the scale to help save a dying franchise. It is destined to be transferred from an area that is never going to support that club. The Jersey situation is similar to the situation that existed in Hartford. That area was comprised of established Ranger and Bruin fans; the Whalers never had a chance. The vast majority of the Jersey suburbs of NYC are, have been, and will be Rangers fans. Inflating the Devils' roster isn't going to change that and make it into a regional draw. Just transfer the Devils to Quebec city and be done with it.
