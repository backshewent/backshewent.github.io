by TenuredVulture » Fri Aug 09, 2019 14:44:11

On the generational issue of student debt--keep in mind that the older you are, the more heavily subsidized your tuition was. Even if you attended a private college, tuition was still substantially less in constant dollars than it is today. Also, there were numerous federal programs to help make college affordable through the seventies, until they were systematically gutted by the Reagan administration.

Still, though I don't think making public colleges entirely free is necessarily the best answer here. Warren's plan could reproduce something like the European system of higher ed--a handful of elite public universities with fiercely competitive admissions with everyone else attending massive non-selective publics that churn out credit hours with substantially less student engagement than our current system. It seems likely that only the most elite private colleges and universities would survive if they had to compete with free public universities.
