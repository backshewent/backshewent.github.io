I thought it was a good trade at the time, but that was because Glasnow was slow and inaccurate to the plate. His 2017 xFIP was 5.64, with a worse ERA and FIP. He was a bad pitcher with potential, and the Rays have done well in turning him around. Austin Meadows had over 600 ABs of average offensive production spread across 3 seasons. I think we forget how much those 2 guys were shaded toward potential, versus production.

Archer has been prone to HRs, but otherwise been valuable. His extended time in the AL East didnt help his numbers, so I've been genuinely surprised at how poorly he's done in Pittsburgh. Good trade for the Rays, obviously, but a lot of credit goes to their scouting/analytics groups for identifying players who could be improved.

This fangraphs piece written at the time came down pretty neutral, which is where I was. https://blogs.fangraphs.com/the-pirates ... gest-move/
