Re: Shove me in the RANDOM water, before I get too deep

by TenuredVulture » Tue Jul 28, 2020 12:06:10

Though I grew up in central Jersey (Monmouth county), I always considered it part of north Jersey. The two obvious ways to divide the state, especially back before area codes exploded were whether you had a 201 or a 609 area code, or if your TV stations came from NY or Philly. I never thought for instance that Mercer county was in the same part of NJ that I was in. The addition of 908 may have given central NJ more of an identity.

On the other hand, the Raritan River seemed to be a dividing line. Going north over the Driscoll bridge on the Parkway, things seemed more urban. Of course much of North Jersey is suburbarn and even rural--especially in those days. Morrris county for example. So, the other thing I think sort of defines my region of NJ is that it's the Shore. This again would explain why as far as we were concerned Mercer county, and even Middlesex county were in another part of the state. Monmouth and Ocean counties have always been connected in this way.
