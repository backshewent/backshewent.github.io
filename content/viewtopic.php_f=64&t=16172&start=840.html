by laf837 » Mon Sep 21, 2015 09:23:13

slugsrbad wrote:My nephew is 11 and wants to be a pitcher. I don't think he'll be particularly good, sorry there champ, but does anyone have any suggestions on how to help him chase this new dream? Any good books/videos/instructions?


Not that I think they are totally worth the money but he should seek out some instruction from his local club team. Generally these are teams that have paid instructors (usually washed out college and pro ball pitchers) and will offer instruction for a price. It's important to learn the nuances and tricks of the trade from guys that have pitched at a higher level. Most of them are not good instructors, however, so it's hit and miss on the quality. Also helps if your nephew is good at taking instruction and working on it after he leaves. 30 minutes a week with an instructor will not do much unless he continues to work on it at home and at the ball field. As simple as looking at form in the mirror, doing drills, long tossing...etc.
I also recommend long tossing as much as possible to build arm strength.
