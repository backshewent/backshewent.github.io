Everything's fine here in NJ:

I’m in Audubon. My 6 year old daughter has developed a fever of 101.7 and a slight cough. She says she’s fine, although a bit cold.

As a responsible citizen, it’s my belief that she should be tested for COVID-19. She’s been at school all week, and my wife has been at work in Camden with a high-risk population. Both the public schools and the non-profit my wife works at should know ASAP if V has this virus.

I called the doctors office. They referred me to the emergency room because they do not have the ability to test, and then admitted that they weren’t sure that the ER even has the ability. I then called the office of our state reps, who also referred me to the ER. I expressed surprise that anyone with a fever is advised to go to the ER, as it seems likely that that would quickly overwhelm the ER, thus potentially depriving quick care to actual medical emergencies. Do I really want to go sit in the ER all day with a feverish child? Do I want to risk the economic shock of a trip to the ER for a non medical emergency? Hard no.

I got a call back from the state reps office. I was told that the only tests that are available now are for people that are in a bad state; patients that are having respiratory failure. I was told that this is what has been directed from the CDC. There’s simply not enough tests for everyone.

So that’s where we are at. We have no idea what’s going on, truly. I’m so frustrated.
