Werthless,
I'm not sure how you think you're disagreeing with me, but I found your examples not very helpful. I work in Analytics and while we're not as smart as statisticians, we're often asked to provide error bars and projections. I'm going to leave the customer example out but just kind of assume something like what you described is happening:

"Josh, here's what we're doing: we've drafted Embiid, we'll get another high lottery pick in 2015, Saric comes over in 2016, etc.

We think this will result in a very rough year this year (25 wins) a less rough year next year (30 wins) and we should be on the upswing in 2016 (40 wins). Now, this projection involves any number of things wrong, the players we draft may be worse than we thought, or we drafted too injury prone people, or somebody dies or what have you, so our confidence in each of these win totals is less each year. I'd say there's a plus 15 on year 4."

First, if I was Josh Harris, I would ratify that plan - that is a GOOD strategy. Now, fast forward to year 4, and we win 30 games. Josh Harris is going to pull Sam Hinkie into office (and barring major fluke injuries that do not happen to Embiid because you can't call Embiid injuries flukes) and fire his ass. He's not going to care that the projection was right or more specifically "fell within reasonable error bars". He's not going to care that the strategy was right. The execution was wrong.

Jersey was right with respect to the notion of politics thread in that what I feel is happening is that the goalposts are being moved. First it was a year long tank to get Wiggins or Parker. Then Embiid came in. Now it's a two year tank. And let me stop here and say that's a very very viable strategy. But now mcare suggested that it might have to be a four year tank and we have to be okay with it. No one asked mcare if he wants it to be a five year tank because the board would have ended, but conceivably there are situations where he'd be jumping up for joy with that too. At which point we've just become the Phillies. What exactly is the difference if the Phillies try to win, don't win, and look like they have no hope or the Sixers not trying to win, don't win, but always suggestthey just need another year? Two's fine. Four? No. You must be able to define what good looks like and what success looks like so that you can move towards that. The thing is this all started in the most innocuous way, the Nightman threw a softball to mcare and he totally whiffed on it.

SH: "Exactly. Why fret about tomorrow? Enjoy today. By the way, did I tell you Hollis Thompson has a hangnail?

JH: "Hmmm. Well, you know, five years of famine is a lot to ask of the fans, you know, the ones who pay your salary. What should we say to them to get them to buy tickets for the next few years?"

After all, we're smarter than they are and none of them could run this team as well as I can. Do you think just anyone could lose this much while making it look like we actually have a plan? Let me tell you, 26 losses in a row is no easy accomplishment."

JH: "So because discussing years 4 and 5 of the 5-year plan is all so speculative and arbitrary that it's meaningless, I guess there's no sense discussing goals like where plan to be in another 4 years."
