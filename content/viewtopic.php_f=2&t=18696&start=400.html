So Trump is pushing ahead with the steel and aluminum tariffs. Threatening tariffs on cars. Between that and the exploding deficit, is high inflation far behind? I wonder how the American public will deal with that--employment remains strong (though that will put pressure on inflation) but for how long, and will wages keep up with prices?

Well, it should help out people with fat student loans. Sucks to be in academia though, as it's unlikely our salaries will keep up with prices, thanks to Baumol's cost disease and all that.

Anyway, it's been so long since we've really had inflation. If we find ourselves in a situation where inflation is high enough that purchasing power is reduced, how does that affect politics? Unlike increasing unemployment, which really is only a serious problem for the portion of the electorate that is unemployed (though it does put downward pressure on wages and does increase insecurity) inflation affects everyone, though the pain is not as severe.

Of course, there is always the threat of stagflation--especially if Trump continues to pursue trade wars.
