So movement on the discussion from last week - Three Booted From GOP Whip Team as Leaders Crack Down. Taking people off the whip team when they oppose the whip team on whipped votes seems pretty obvious. The more interesting threat is at the bottom of the article to start taking away subcommittee chairmanships from members who stray because that would be a real punishment. Boehner apparently went on a rant during the caucus meeting today, in addition to that quote in the article.

Also it's worth highlighting the bit from Rep. Labrador (R-ID) on why so many voted against the rule - "I think many of the people who voted against the rule felt it was inappropriate for our leadership to continue to negotiate with Democrats without negotiating with conservatives in the House. That's the failure of this leadership. … They're not even aware of what's happening around them and they need to be very careful about that." The Republicans who voted against the rule and the Democrats who voted against it did so for different reasons...the GOP reason being that leadership was too accommodating to Democrats! There isn't some hidden majority of 218 members who would start passing legislation if only the party structure would get out of the way. Nothing would get accomplished.
