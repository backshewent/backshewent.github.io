This morning I started listing topics that weren't covered at all in the four debates. In no particular order:

--drug war
--role of money in politics
--marriage equality
--climate change
--role and composition of supreme court
--corrections
--torture
--agriculture

I'd say that Wall Street regulation and education were very lightly covered.

Admittedly, these are issues that as a liberal I probably care about more than the median voter (though I think anyone who's interested in fiscal responsibility should be looking at the drug war and the prison-industrial complex). I guess I'm asking Werthless and maybe cshort if there are issues of more interest to the conservative-leaners that weren't covered.

And to be clear, I can't think of much that was covered which I would have tossed. Maybe a bit more on abortion and terrorism than I think was necessary, but they're important issues.

The debates are such a great opportunity for informing the public, particularly when you have two talented speakers going at it. These were decent, but could have been even better IMO.
