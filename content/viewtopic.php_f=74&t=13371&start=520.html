I met two of my brother's roommates tonight, and one of them had the cold fish handshake working. I was thinking about how one develops that kind of a hand shaking technique, and then wondered how one knows if they suck at shaking hands. I judge people if they do the limp handshake, but I mean, you don't shake your own hand so maybe all along I've been shaking hands like a wuss. Pretty sure this isn't the case...almost certain, but how can you really be sure? I've never told someone else they need to man up when they demonstrated poor form in a handshake. It's probably something people should be more open about, like telling someone if they have a piece of broccoli stuck in their teeth. It's less embarrassing to be told you have broccoli stuck in your teeth by a friend discreetly than to notice at the end of the night you went the whole gathering with something stuck in your teeth. But really how does one learn to shake hands like that?

This wasn't meant to be a drawn out sexual metaphor, but I realize portions of that post could end up in the That's What She Said thread
