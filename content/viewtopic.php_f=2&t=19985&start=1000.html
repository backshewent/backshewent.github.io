by thephan » Fri Feb 28, 2020 09:38:27

This is a hard item. I benefited both from stock programs and ESOPs. The general plan was make a little less, get it backed with some form of stock option, and win. I won some, and lost some, but it was a risk reward.

On the losing side I lost a job because my "business unit" did not perform for two quarters due the disruption in the general market. For the share holders, and how I found out I was going down with the ship, it was announced that the focus was shifting away from my area of work. Since Since my job was converting R&D into product/operations the claw back was just me. I leveraged the rest of the organization, but the shuttering of the business unit was one FTE and generally 1 FTE annually of matrix staff. To the market this was presented as a major realignment. The end result was that this fledgling or boutique business area accounted for between 8-18% of top line revenue while I was there which was then fortified (also, that equaled a revenue of 9 - 20 loaded rate resources over time which made it obscenely profitable). It was a good message for the market to cover lack of general growth, but ultimately that companies stock price has remained below what it averaged over the time I was there.

I completely agree with the 3Ps model as driving to success with the caveat that some type of cash infusion is generally what is necessary to move the needle to get a product to market, hire key/expert staff, expand infrastructure, etc. How you keep a lid on playing to stock holder value as the mantra is something that has to be managed, but it is a necessary evil to some extent.

There is no easy path to this.
