Re: Trump and the Christmas Hater caught the 'Rona

by TenuredVulture » Mon Oct 12, 2020 15:33:26

In some areas, particularly LGBTQ+ issues, I've moved to the left over the last couple of decades, but I suspect lots of others have as well. Same goes for my views on the environment--but mostly because I think it's a lot more urgent now than in the 90s.

The biggest (and most destructive) trend I've seen take hold in the past few decades has been a rise of narcissism in our political discourse. We simply do not hear anyone at any point on the political spectrum talking about the necessity for shared sacrifice. No one wants to put their phone down while they drive, and the mask "debate" simply puts this into stark relief--far too many people do not care who they infect and are unwilling to take even simple and painless steps to mitigate the spread of disease. There's a claim of "freedom" no matter who is harmed.

I've lost a lot of faith in the so-called free market, because the models that supposedly show free markets to be the most efficient way to allocate resources don't seem to hold up well to empirical scrutiny. Even if they did, I believe that our economy is productive enough that we could easily sacrifice some efficiency in exchange for more equality in distribution. And free-market fundamentalists never seem to acknowledge that government actions are needed to solve both collective action problems and address externalities.

I've never been a purist in terms of politics--I am far more interested in getting things done, in making positive change in people's lives than in philosophical or ideological purity. If anything, over the years, I've become even more skeptical about the usefulness of ideology or philosophical foundations.

Finally, in the 90s I had a lot of faith that mostly smart people who knew what they were doing were running things and would continue to do so. That was obviously wrong. I find myself deeply conflicted about the tension between democratic input and bureaucratic expertise.
