If you are missing baseball this Memorial Day weekend, as we (yes, even my wife!) are here, the old-time radio broadcasts available on YouTube are, like, astounding.

For example, the final broadcast of a game featuring the "Brooklyn" Dodgers - albeit on the road - at the Phillies at Connie Mack Stadium is available. Vin Scully sounds as Vin Scully as ever:



I was actually hoping to find a home broadcast of the Philadelphia Athletics (like the Phillies, By Saam called the games), but I couldn't find one. However, here is a Mutual Broadcasting Station national broadcast of a game from Shibe Park between the Indians and Athletics:



And here is a rando game between the Pirates and Phillies from 1975 called by Harry the K:



Much of the 1950 World Series is available (Mel Allen call), as is Jim Bunning's perfect game (Mets broadcast). Haven't found any By Saam yet.

Awesome stuff to throw on in the background using YouTube. I have one of those little adapters so it can come through my wife's "Crosley" radio.

As a Phillies fan I know it is sacrilege but if there is a better sound than Vin Scully calling a ball game in the spring or summer I'd like to hear it.
