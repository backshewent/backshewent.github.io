Hayek wrote:“Our hopes of avoiding the fate which threatens must...[be to make]adjustments that will be needed if we are to recover and surpass our former standards...and only if every one of us is ready to individually obey the necessities of readjustment shall we be able to get through a difficult period as free men who can choose their own way of life. Let a uniform minimum be secured to everybody by all means; but let us admit at the same time that with this assurance of a basic minimum all claims for a privileged security for particular classes must lapse....”

Interesting thoughts from the 1940s from a libertarian. Makes clear the contrast between social safety nets (good things) and maintaining "privileged security" in the form of corporate safety nets (not so good). I hadn't remembered Hayek saying favorable/neutral things about safety nets before.
