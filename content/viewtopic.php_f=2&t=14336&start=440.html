In most places, school districts are not part of the county or the city.

Anyway, I was on a jury once and the case was where a bank customer sued the bank because he was shot during a robbery, claiming the bank had not provided adequate security. After two weeks of testimony, our verdict would probably have hinged on the judge's definition of liability. We never got to that point though, because the parties settled before the judge delivered his instructions to the jury. I think the main purpose of the trial was to determine the degree of liability between the bank and the security company that provided the security guard to the bank. It was pretty clear that the security company was pretty much off the hook after two weeks of testimony.

In short, such a lawsuit isn't all that uncommon.
