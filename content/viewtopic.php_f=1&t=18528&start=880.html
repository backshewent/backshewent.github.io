slugsrbad wrote:
Titlehungry wrote:I don't know if Hakstol is a good coach or not


I'm leaning towards he is not. He seems to put way too much stock in veteran #$!&@ players. I understand that our roster has had some black holes in the bottom six and d-men, but he still has control over how much ice time players get.

I believe that Hextall brought in Hakstol to build a young team and turn it into a SC contender for a while. It's what he did at ND; take raw kids and prep them to become competent NHLers. To that end he's not going to put young players in positions where they will mess up and set back their careers. So he'll put the vets out there in crunch time. Next year and beyond should be a different situation. Right now, I don't want to see a Sanheim get blown by for a goal by, say, Hornquist on an outside move. Let Manning get schooled instead. So I give credit to Hakstol for nurturing along the under 25 year olds.
Right now I'm more let down by the play of the vets. These guys should be able to compete better than they did today and last Wednesday. There really isn't much that Hakstol can do with that. If a vet doesn't get it that during the POs, it's time to elevate their game, there isn't much that a coach or GM can do for or with them.
RP, I'm with you about Hagg. There's no reason to have Manning out there instead of him. He has to be injured and the Flyers are keeping quiet about it. Actually the rest of the D has played reasonably well. Ghost and Provorov were good as a top pair in their first PO series. We even saw AMac being physical today. Gudas has been under control and Sanheim has been a revelation. He is going to be a star. Manning is terrible and drags down whomever he is paired with.
