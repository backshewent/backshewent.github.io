milton bernard thompson wrote:
Monkeyboy wrote:
somebody doesn't know what small sample size means


I actually taught grad level stats, so I know what it means. I used it as a board shorthand for saying that Halladay's playoff winning % is less likely to reach it's expected winning % in a small number of games than if it were a whole season. That's why superior teams are more likely to lose a short series to an inferior team than to lose a division crown to that same team. As N goes up, you approach the predicted/real results. That's why a larger numbers of subjects in a study leads to a lower P value, assuming the same results.

your logic was somewhat along the lines of "one game in a playoff series is a small sample size, so who cares who we put on the field for it," which is silly.



That's a gross oversimplification, which is what Woody was doing to my opinion. I would much rather put Halladay on the hill for that game. But the reality is that the chance that Halladay would make the difference in winning a WS and losing it is pretty small. It's not zero, which is why I would rather have Halladay, but it's small. I think it's less likely than one or more of those top prospects will help us get to a playoff and a chance to win a WS, which is why I'd rather hold onto the very top prospects. I would, however, deal Drabek because pitching prospects are so much more likely to break down.

I hope that clears it up. I really went about this wrong. I should have just said that I want Halladay, want to make the deal for him, but wouldn't want the price to be too steep. Others have said that without the backlash.
