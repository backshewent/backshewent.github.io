Here's an interesting story about Brad Ellsworth, a Democratic congressman from Indiana, who pledged to vote for Clinton at the convention--but admitted that he cast his primary vote for Obama.

I've followed Ellsworth a little since 2006, when he knocked off a Republican incumbent in (I'm pretty sure) a Bush-voting district. Tom Cole probably would call him a Republican in Democratic clothing (well, he'd leave off the "ic," but you know what I mean): he's a former sheriff, a Blue Dog, anti-abortion rights and gun control, voted against funding stem cell research, et cetera. He's more traditionally liberal on pocketbook issues.

My understanding is that his pledge to vote for Clinton at the convention--which he's evidently at pains not to describe as an "endorsement"--is meant to honor the primary votes of his constituents. But that he personally chose Obama is interesting to me, if only because he seems like the sort of surrogate Obama's going to need to bring back some of those "working-class whites" in November.
