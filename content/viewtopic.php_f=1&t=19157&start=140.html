Re: Phillies Sign Andrew McCutchen: 3 yrs/$50 mil

by LastTrain » Tue Dec 18, 2018 08:44:26

Before analytics were as mainstream in assessing a player's worth, I think players also were OK with a backloaded deal because it helped them negotiate the "next" deal. Coming off a season in which he made $20M instead of $10M (if payouts were reversed in years 1 and 3) would have helped McCutchen in his 2023 deal. I'm not so sure that matters much in today's free agent market, but maybe there's still some perception factor. McCutchen's buyout year salary is "only" $15M, so the perception-based backloading benefit is a bit muted in this specific case.

The buyouts never seem team friendly unless they aren't utilized. Then, the benefit comes from the buyout already having been counted against the cap. If McCutchen is still producing and is wanted at $15M for 2023, he'll only count $12M against the tax. Hopefully that matters.

While I understand that the time value of money plays a big factor, I have always thought backloading deals for players was a mistake. From the tram’s point of view, the player gets paid more and chews up more of your budget as his effectiveness dwindles, and from the player’s point of view, they have to wait for their money (cash up front is better, right?), and the pressure to perform going up as their salary goes up might have a deleterious effect, especially when coupled with the likely diminishment of skill that accompanies aging. More need for the player to “press.”
