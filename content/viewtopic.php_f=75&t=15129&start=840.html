by TenuredVulture » Tue Nov 05, 2013 22:23:46

I don't really consider restaurants part of the food budget--it's part of the entertainment budget. I figure we're at about $600 at the grocery store for the 3 of us, but that includes cat food, and cleaning products and toiletries and stuff. Also, I really think that for three people, preparing your meals is cheaper than prepared food.

It really makes a difference what you eat--a dozen eggs are like 1.30, a pretty cheap protein. Buying meat on special can save a ton--sometime this weekend, Mrs. Vulture and I will fix some NY strip steaks I bought for 4.99 a pound. Boston Butt is a very economical--slower cooker for awhile and you've got a mess of pulled pork (enough for maybe two dinners for everyone) for about 8 bucks. Roast a chicken--dinner and lunch for about the same depending on its size.

Imagine a meal of NY strip steak, some frozen vegetables, and perhaps a sweet potato--that's a pretty nice meal for under 10 bucks. Now, obviously, you can't eat like that every night, but if you did, you'd be at $300 a month for dinner.
