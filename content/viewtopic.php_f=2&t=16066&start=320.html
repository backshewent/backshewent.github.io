Yesterday, America's best pizzamakers convened in NYC for the Caputo Cup competition.

It was considered a big upset that she beat the big name pros, but if you go have that pie, you'd know it's as good as any.

went to Brigantessa for dinner on saturday. The girl and I got a great pizza. They do neapolitan style pizza. Buffalo Mozz, Prosciutto di Parma, dressed arugula salad and shaved parm on top. One of the better pizzas i've had. Wasn't a wet soggy mess in the middle like most neapolitans can get. This stayed firm throughout. They offer to cut the pizza for you but serve it as a whole pie and you just rip and tear at it.

We've gotten other pizza there before and it maxed out as 'good.' This was a legit great pizza. We also got some raviolis in a pecorino broth that was pretty good, too.

Got up to Collegeville Bakery yesterday. My God, what a brutal trek up Ridge Pike from KoP to get there.

But the pies - EXCELLENT. A nice rendition of Detroit style, and an authentic NY pie. Lots of other kinds I didn't try, whole, half, or by the slice. Somebody knows dough.



The NY pie was thin, crisp, rigid, and topped with spicy cup pepperoni. Perfect cornicione. My only criticism is that the round pie needed more top heat to brown up the cheese.

Drive up to East Harlem, go eat a $12 pizza at Patsy's, where pies have come from the coal oven for 80 years. And street parking, no meters, available at all destinations.
