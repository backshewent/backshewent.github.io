Arm chair analysis of why Romney fell short would be that he lacked credibility in the end. His bending to the base, in my opinion, was his undoing. Without the pandering he likely did not get the nomination, which is sad in itself. The real Romney, although famously rich, is more moderate then radical. Let's face it, he authored the core of Obamacare which has fulfilled it's mission in MA, but he had to hate it for the base. TThat sounds like someone who either will do anything to get elected, or has not real faith in his accomplishments. Further, the mixed message about the government not creating jobs versus he would do something to crate jobs via the white house has to be a head scratcher, if you consider the first statement. I realize the message is holding the line on the growth of government and direct government jobs, but that is an obtuse message. It also neglects the ravages put on the employment in the government sector, which has reduced under Obama.

So my view is that contrary to some thinking that Romney was not far enough to the right, I think his entertaining the far right agenda actually hurt him. I think if he was a moderate candidate that allowed belief in his policies and previous actions, then it might have been closer. I also think that his VP candidate scared people. So more center in a believable way would have made it much tighter in my opinion.
