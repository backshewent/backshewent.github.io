I don't mind listening to Stairs. He's a friendly voice and often discusses hitting intelligently, though I wish he'd expand his repertoire.

In fact, I might wish the rest of the crew were better, but there isn't anyone who really annoys me, with the big exception being McCarthy. I hate the screaming homer announcers you encounter sometimes with other teams, but there's something to be said for being on a similar emotional plane to most of your listeners. Sure, he manages to crank himself up to a simulacrum of excitement when there's a home run, but there's not even a hint of disappointment when a double play ends a Phillies rally or a ball splits the gap and erases a Phillies lead. Franzke is a living example that you don't have to be a hometown guy to tell the story of a game in a way that meshes with the rise and fall those who are following along would like to hear.
