What I think most commentators critical of Heckman miss (and I don't think Heckman considers this either) but the kind of political change that would encourage a more egalitarian economic system requires the people at the bottom to be able to recognize their circumstances. That is, if the interventions Heckman discusses really work, they will do more than simply create people more able to cope with "things as they stand" they will move us in a direction where there will be political pressure to change how things stand.
