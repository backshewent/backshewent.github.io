thephan wrote:Here is the reason behind DJT saying that California can't get water to fight forest fires:

Hundreds of California farmers rallied at the Capitol on Monday to protest state water officials' proposal to increase water flows in a major California river, a move state and federal politicians called an overreach of power that would mean less water for farms in the Central Valley.


There is a bit of a 'Big Ag' angle looking to secure water rights. It is honestly a sizable problem since a good big of your veg comes from CA. While it is also true that CA has led the way in water redirection making their desert green. One would think that there could be something done, that will comes at some expense, that could put the agricultural concerns at ease while also protecting another food source, if we want to reduce a (tasty) species to that.


One suggestion I've read about, and I don't know how feasible it really is, is to redirect growing fruits and vegetables to places that get more rain than CA's central valley. Places like Arkansas for instance, which used to grow lots of strawberries for instance. But farmers are especially reluctant to make such huge changes. It's not like it's easy to take a farm that grows soybeans and convert it to growing strawberries, and the skills and knowledge involved aren't transferable. And, of course, if it doesn't work out, the farmer in question is ruined. To get someone growing soybeans today to start growing strawberries next year would require substantial incentives, and I just don't see the political demand.

On the other hand, growing rice in CA does seem quite silly. But they do.
