The point JFL was making at the end of the last thread, about the team having foregone a chance to find out whether Cesar Hernandez can be an everyday player, is a really important one. You probably can play out the argument at three or four other roster spots, maybe more. There's no reason Ruf shouldn't be playing every single day. Likewise Brown. They should at least give Giles a couple shots to close. (For one thing, I love the idea of Papelbon threatening to veto any trade to a team that wouldn't have him close; how about he not close on this suppurating wound of a team?) I'd call up Galvis and let him play rather than Blanco. Maybe MAG gets some innings out of the bullpen.

The regular lineup come September should be something like Rupp/Franco/Cesar/Asche/Galvis/Brown/Revere/Ruf. They're "trying to win games"; that's not going so well, of course, and I'd be much more interested in watching that team of twenty-somethings, much as I love Chase, Jimmy. Chooch, et al. But they seem to think it's a big positive deal to win 72 games rather than 66.
