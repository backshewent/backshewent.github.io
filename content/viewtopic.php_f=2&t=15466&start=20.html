To TV's point about 80's vs. now -- I think it may partly be due to the fact that a lot of new stuff was coming out then. More and higher quality and so that today there is a lot more derivative. As well, what were foreign and alternative, has become itself a middle-ground of "arty-ish" but still distributed by the big boys. Sundance didn't operate as it does today back then.

I feel like I've seen amazing films in ever decade of my life. I'm not sure if there is a dip in quality, but I don't favor Hollywood or blockbusters. My list had plenty of hollywood flics but I saw a ton of foreign films in the 80's. A couple of those stand the test of time...

If you've never seen "Das Boot", or "My Life as a Dog", they are worth renting. The early Jarmush was good. Lots of good stuff. I didn't include the cult hit "Liquid Sky" which apparently a sequel is coming out this summer.
