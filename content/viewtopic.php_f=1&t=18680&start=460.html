Doesn't him not taking the max prove that it doesn't matter?

If guys want to play together they will. The max stops the Wizards from paying John wall $70 million a year.

Sure, there's nothing you can do to totally legislate it out. But I think it becomes far more unlikely that four guys pass up that amount to play together. The Warriors are an outlier case because they were a situation that involved a once in a lifetime confluence of stars on below-market deals and an unprecedented cap spike, but if Steph and Durant are both allowed to make $60 million instead of $30 million, does that change the calculus going forward? Maybe it does, maybe it doesn't.

Ultimately, my problems with the max aren't really based on super teams, I think it's a better answer to the tanking debate. A team like the Pacers is never going to be a player for a major superstar with finances being equal, but if they can offer $60 million when nobody else has that kinda space, it might put them in play. It might not change the league for this generation, as guys are used to forming super teams now, but it might set the tone for the next generation.
