i am reasonably optimistic about this season, and i agree with you that the phils can succeed going forward without firesale trades. i even think that a good plan can include resigning utley and halladay while keeping rollins, lee, hamels, howard, and papelbon.

much depends on how well the young(ish) talent develops. brown, revere, asche, biddle, morgan, martin, joseph, aumont, franco, etc. i know that the prospect rankers don't value the guys in our system too highly, i don't have a lot of faith in prospect rankings. that dates back to my arguments with bp over their refusal to rank utley highly. late-blooming prospects get no respect.

all that said, if things go south again this season, it would be a really good idea to trade off expensive pieces for prospects, eating some salary if possible. if someone wants halladay or papelbon for the stretch run badly enough to offer a good deal, do it... especially if they don't reverse their velocity declines. do teams give up good prospect for a big-name pitcher, overlooking the likelihood of injury? i still remember the trade of gio and floyd for freddy garcia, and am pretty sure there are some teams out there dumb enough to restore the karmic balance of that one. i can see aumont turning into a top closer and biddle and martin upgrading the back of the rotation enough to offset the loss of halladay in 2014 and beyond.
best case scenario in that case is landing a replacement for utley (assume he doesn't provide convincing evidence that the phils should gamble on his knees going forward).

if the phils have a good season and justifiably hang on to everyone, then there is still good hope enough that enough prospects step forward that we can use them to fill any and all 2014 gaps via promotion (joseph and asche for ruiz and young, martin/morgan/biddle for the rotation) and trade (excess pitchers for 2b or rf holes).
