It's not so much the legalese aspect, but rather weighing up what the publisher's record on dealing with different kinds of rights are. You might be inclined to think you should hold on to UK publishing rights (say), because it makes good sense. But the publisher might have a perfectly decent connection with a good UK publisher so, you'd get a good percentage out of the sale, maybe for more money than you could have arranged yourself. Plus, obviously, you get plugged into decent distribution in a market you don't know that well.

It's hard to judge from the point of view of the author, which is why an agent can help, obviously. I know from experience on the other side of things that nothing makes a publisher happier than having extensive control over a variety of rights - it makes it easier to look at the bottom line when you can cover an advance by selling on film rights (not that that's likely to happen in this case, I'm guessing).
