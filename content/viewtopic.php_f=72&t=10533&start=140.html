by swishnicholson » Sat May 29, 2010 18:06:44

TenuredVulture wrote:My understanding is that if you are using a proportional font, it's one. If you're using a non-proportional font (like you would find on a typewriter) then it's two.


This really. Two was almost correct during the typewriter era, because , well, you want to make it clear to the reader where a sentence ends, same with a colon where it's a full stop. The only correct thing is that you want a thicker space after a period than the spaces in the rest of the text, and you don't want an extended gap that brings attention to itself. Whatever way your printing technology does this is fine.

I tend to use two since that's the habit I have, but if I use this in descriptions at work than it screws up the format when it's printed, so then i go with one.
