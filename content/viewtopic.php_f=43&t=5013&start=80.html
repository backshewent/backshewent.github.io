there's this funny little concept called "inflation" you might have heard of...
oh, and "replacement" means league minimum salary, which is about a half million, so the total "fair" salary for feliz is over 5 million even without inflation.

to make your argument that feliz is overpaid you would be better off consulting shore, who can point out that the estimates of defensive value that go into "morp" are highly uncertain and may well be overestimating that value. i believe the metric used is uzr, which comes from one of the leading sabremetric analysts... but he's not perfect, and there are other tools out there that give different results. i'm hoping for the phillies sake that uzr is right.

of course, the easiest argument against feliz is that he is old enough that even his defensive production is likely to go off a cliff sometime soon.

my own position? there is at least a chance that feliz will be worth his salary in 08-09 because of his defense (and i object to you dismissing this possibility out of hand), and he might have been correctly scouted as the type of player whose offense can improve significantly in the phillies system, but his age could all too easily negate both of those possibilities.
