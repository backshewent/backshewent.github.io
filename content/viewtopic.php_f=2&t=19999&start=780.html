by jerseyhoya » Fri Mar 06, 2020 23:37:59

I strongly dislike Elizabeth Warren and am glad she is not going to be the next president of the United States. She would have been a really bad general election candidate for non-sexism or maybe sexism adjacent reasons (I continue to think Klobuchar was the most electable Dem in the race, maybe Booker). In that respect, I think the Dems are dodging a bullet, whether the nominee is Biden or Bernie (if winning is sole concern). Taking electoral stuff out of the mix, a large part of why I dislike her is I think she would have been a pretty effective president at accomplishing her agenda (especially compared to Bernie, who I dislike for other reasons), and I very much dislike her agenda. I think if I was me, but left of center, she would have been my candidate.

I will say it gives me tremendous satisfaction that she flopped so badly in some part to not hiring a pollster. You read the Politico post-mortem on her campaign where her people complain about not being able to talk to non-white voters and the messaging failures around their healthcare plan. You know what could have helped that? Fucking testing messaging among Hispanics in Nevada and black voters in South Carolina, and testing different frames of your healthcare plan to hone what to emphasize prior to releasing it so you didn't have to do it twice in reaction to blowback.

I think Biden just getting one break and then just dominating everyone shows how fucking disconnected the average young Dem political staffer and progressive Twitter is from the Democratic electorate. Maybe it would have been useful for someone with gobs of money and a real chance of being the next president of the United States to have regularly and systematically asked the people whom she wanted to vote for her what they thought and how they reacted to her proposals. You don't reinvent yourself based on the polling, but maybe learn better words to use (or sometimes more importantly, not use) when talking about your agenda. And you can find things to emphasize to break through with different audiences. It's not rocket science.

She was authentic and had a plan for everything. And finished in 3rd place in her own home state and worse most other places despite adoration from the press and every other group overrepresented in political commentary (including here). Congrats, and good riddance.
