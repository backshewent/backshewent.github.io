by FTN » Mon Jul 13, 2009 19:36:13

Grades are given on a base-5 system (40, 45, 50, 55, 60, etc.)...A score of 50 is major-league average, 60 is above-average (also referred to as “plus”), and 70 is among the best (“plus-plus”). 80 is top of the charts, and not a score that gets thrown around liberally. 80s in any category are rare, and the scoring system is definitely a strong curve that regresses to around 50 at the major league level, but lower as you move down. Very few players have a 50 score or higher for every tool. Just being average across the board is quite an accomplishment.


So a 60 (or 6, if you like 2-8), is an above average pitching prospect, a guy who will be above average in terms of velocity, and projected future performance.

Drabek is probably a 65, Knapp might be a 60, and I'd guess Carrasco is now a 50 or 55.

by FTN » Mon Jul 13, 2009 19:52:23

ek wrote:is it just a pitching grade?


The 20-80 scale applies to both hitters and pitchers, but the tools rated are obviously different. For hitters its; hit for power, hit for average, running speed/aptitude, arm strength, and fielding ability. For pitchers they measure mainly arm strength, control/command, and delivery. For pitchers especially, scouts sometimes give a present grade and future grade, which is obviously based on projection

If you surveyed 20 scouts, I think the Phillies would have four prospects with 60's or higher; Drabek, Brown, Knapp and Taylor
