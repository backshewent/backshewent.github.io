Re: 2011 Random Phillies Stuff VI: Return Of The Nice Guy

by Barry Jive » Mon Jul 25, 2011 02:41:32

I'm sure someone's had this hunch before but it hit me today that because of Ryan Howard's boom and bust style at the plate he's more aggressive in hitters' counts than he should be. Baseball-Reference's splits page gives sOPS+ for each ball-strike count, meaning the adjusted OPS relative to the rest of the league in that situation. After 3-1 counts, Howard's sOPS+ this year is 43, 30 points lower than his next worst situation. He's only had 43 PAs where that's happened, so it could be a sample size issue, but basically pitchers can pitch around him hoping to get ahead of him, and if they get down 3-1 they still have hope to get him to swing at junk looking for a fastball. His OBP is decent (.442) in those situations because he's already got a 3-ball count, but he hits .172 and slugs only .310. He's struck out 16 times in those 43 PAs. So more than 1/3 of the time a pitcher gets behind him 3-1 they come back to strike him out anyway.

He's really good when he hits the first pitch into play, monstrous on 1-0 counts, and basically league-average when he puts 2-0 and 3-0 pitches in play. I suppose that's what happens when you guess a lot. But somebody needs to tell him to be a little more patient when he's got such a huge advantage at the plate.

It appears this isn't consistent with his career numbers, that he's generally been very good after hitters' counts. So maybe it's just the sample size thing. It seems a little weird. Maybe this means he's just due for regression and he'll go on a tear over the next couple months.
