by snakster » Tue Mar 06, 2018 14:35:18

15 teams total in Suburban One League between the two Divisions. Philly public has some, Philly catholic league has some. But yeah it's sporadic around the state. Lehigh valley has zero even though people have been trying for years. School boards are resisting for some reason. Kind of a shame especially for girls since women's bowling is an NCAA sport with bunches of title 9 money.

40 Boys Teams and 31 girls teams were in the east regional tournament. Mind you, some boys teams had one or two girls on the team. Some schools don't have enough girl bowlers to field a team. In those cases girls allowed to bowl on the boys team (in many of these circumstances the girl or girls are among the best on the team). Five of the 15 SOL schools went (both boys and girls). Top 2 in each division and a wildcard based on high team average of the rest.

Update: According to PIAA,org there are 149 Boys Bowling programs and 139 girls.

Update 2: All but 15 of those Boys programs come from 4 districts. Four districts have none, Three have two teams each. One has 9. Looks like only 39 of the 149 Boys programs are in the western region.
