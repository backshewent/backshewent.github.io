You know I just had an evil thought.

If The Donald wants to undermine Hillary's win, the move isn't, as he himself has suggested, to directly question the validity of the result by broadly accusing her of cheating in some way, or as others have suggested, by pursuing some legal challenge, or by refusing to make a concession call.

No, the move is to do almost the exact opposite: when he's lost, to make a speech congratulating Hillary, winking at the camera, and then imply - well, who are we kidding here, this is The Donald - directly state something to the effect of "I told you we could do it." In other words, appear to confirm the old, forgotten conspiracy theory that Donald is now what he always was - a Democrat - and that he was working in secret cahoots with Hillary to guarantee her path to the White House.

This accomplishes two things, from his perspective: it absolves him for "losing," because now, his loss was his all-along intended outcome (and thus preserves his ego), and it gets revenge on Hillary because this will surely set Republican conspiracy theorists through the roof searching for "proof" that Hillary and her camp knew and thus "rigged" the election.

It's a long shot that such a thing would happen, but man, that would be the devastating move from him, if he just wanted to watch the world burn in a rage of revenge for being rejected.
