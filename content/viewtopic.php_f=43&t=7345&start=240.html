Rotation

The top two left-handers in the National League reside atop these rotations, Johan Santana with the Mets and World Series MVP Cole Hamels with the Phillies. From that point down, there are questions, though.

For the Mets, Mike Pelfrey is a rising star and John Maine is solid but missed the final five weeks of the season with a shoulder injury. Newly signed southpaw Oliver Perez can be wildly inconsistent, but he's nails against the Phillies and their cavalcade of left-handed sluggers — he had a 0.35 ERA in four starts against Philadelphia last season.

The Mets, like the Phillies, will have a competition for the fifth starter's job. Behind Hamels, the Phillies throw out Brett Myers, who, like Perez, can be hit-or-miss from start to start. Jamie Moyer is 46, but the crafty left-hander won 16 games in 2008. Joe Blanton is a dependable No. 4 starter, but his 4.24 career ERA isn't anything to write home about.

Edge: slightly to the Mets
