by TenuredVulture » Tue Mar 15, 2016 18:00:48

The article is a form of bullying and victim blaming. But it's a trope that the left engages in as well--consider Obama's infamous "clinging to their God and guns" comment. There's a contempt many elites have for low income rural whites and it privileges no particular ideology.

But even so there are huge problems here. All the discussion has been around which particular prejudice we want to indulge rather than asking some questions of fact. First of all, of low income rural whites, how many of them voted at all, and of those who do vote how many vote Republican? Are they really the base the Republican party has depended on all these years? Even controlling for race, lower income has for a long time been correlated with voting Democratic. Now maybe for rural counties the relationship is different, but I'd like to see evidence. The typical Republican voter is a middle/upper-income suburban white.

Then of course we have the question of the extent to which low income rural whites are dependent on government--and keep in mind, it's likely that some people on disability might choose to live in a rural area because the cost of living is lower. What's the rate of drug abuse addiction and so forth. (And believe me I'm well aware that these social pathologies do exist in rural areas, and it's also the case that treatment for them is likely to be far less available and of lower quality that what you are going to find in urban and suburban areas. And recall, measuring dysfunction is a rather dodgy prospect, as there are plenty of affluent people who can literally afford various dysfunctional behaviors--they can buy their way out of consequences in the way low income people can't. So, yeah, I have a hard time taking any of this seriously without at least some attempt to look at data.

Finally, people are leaving these areas--the U-Haul solution is happening. Maybe not as quickly as Williams or TP or Doc Smooth would like, but rural population is declining everywhere.
