by Laexile » Mon Apr 30, 2007 20:06:49

stevelxa476 wrote:
Laexile wrote:
BDawk wrote:why can't they release it on DVD?

The music was cleared for TV, but not for DVD. Clearing the music for DVD would cost more than they could make. The production company wasn't thinking ahead.


I hope that is sarcasm at the end because otherwise, how could they see 15 years in the future?

When you get the music rights a smart producer should get "rights for all media existing in the present or future in perpetuity." When the show was on from 1988 to 1993 people knew that new uses would be invented. In the previous 15 years cable and VHS had been invented, providing new revenue sources. You just don't know. As a result, contracts these days include something similar to above.

Either the producers didn't have the foresight or the price the catalog companies wanted to charge them was more than they could afford. If they didn't have the money for "all media," then they shouldn't buy it. Other shows from the 60's, 70's, and 80's must have the music cleared, because they go out on DVDs.

There is a lot of tension in the entertainment industry about writers, musicians, et al being paid for new media.

When I made my movie, the music rights were for all media. What I didn't negotiate was the rights to use the songs on an album. I didn't think there would be one, so those rights could be negotiated later.
