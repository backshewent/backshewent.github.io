If you look at historical Presidential approval polls, you'll see patterns in the first year--namely, a honeymoon period of relatively high approval ratings followed by a steady decline and then often some recovery following election day. Trump's approvals have paralleled this trend, though of course he's about 10-15 points lower than most. But if the parallel continues, you'd expect at least some bounce over the next 2 months or so.

However, there's something else really important going on that not many have noticed--currently, the economy is doing rather well by most measures--DJIA, employment (though wages remain stubbornly stuck) and inflation are all positive indicators. Yet Trump does not benefit from any of this, despite his attempts to take credit for it (and really, any President would do the same, it's just that Trump really sucks at it).

So, you really can't draw any firm conclusions. It seems that in many ways Trump simply defies most patterns of Presidential approval, and he's going to remain unpopular no matter what happens.
