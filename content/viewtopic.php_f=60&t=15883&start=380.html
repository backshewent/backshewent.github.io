b-r war has the phillies at 14.5(pitching)+9.1(offense and fielding)= 24.2. with the .292 replacement level winning percentage used by both b-r and fg, that gives exactly 24.2+43.8=68 wins in 150 games. they also have the phils with 68 pythagorean wins. looks like the phils are about as close to luck-neutral (outside injuries) as possible.

interestingly, the fwar numbers are 11.4+9.8+43.8=65 wins. when i have a chance, i want to check this out for all teams. last winter i decided that bwar is more accurate than fwar, and i wonder if this will show up in the size of the war-vs-pythwin-vs-actualwins inconsistencies for the two systems. on a per-player basis, the bwar numbers for the phillies i frequently check on have seemed to make more sense than the fwar values for almost all players for almost the whole season... but that might just be comfirmation bias.
