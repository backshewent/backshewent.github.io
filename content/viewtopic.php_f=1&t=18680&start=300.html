by phillychuck » Mon May 21, 2018 14:07:27

You're glad to add those guys, but sometimes elite players don't make the team product better. If Hayward and Irving play instead of, say, Smart and Tatum, they lose a whole lot on defense...and losing just a little bit on defense opens up a whole lot for *everyone* on the other team, not just the guys Irving and Hayward are playing, because of the rotations that are forced. And a ball-dominant offensive player like Irving changes the entire flow of the offense. It could be better, but it's not guaranteed to be. Watching last night, it showed how bad the Celtics can be on offense even against one of the worst defensive teams in the NBA. I don't think we have that much ground to make up.

Honestly, the biggest effect of big-name players may be to affect the way the game is called, not so much to change the performance level of the players.
