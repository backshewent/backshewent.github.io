As far as the "bad athlete" tag is concerned, he's certainly no gazelle. But in his limited games in the OF so far, he hasn't committed an error and had 3 OF assists in Reading in only 29 games. When combined with the fact that he's pitched 3.1 innings in the minors, I think we can conclude he's got a decent arm. There have been many, many "bad athletes" who've enjoyed very successful MLB careers (think Greg Luzinski, John Kruk, etc.) One thing is for certain: You don't have to be a great athlete to be a great hitter. If you combine the hitting with steady, if unspectacular, defensive play, you can play a long time.
