by Bucky » Thu Oct 22, 2009 22:56:28

swishnicholson wrote:That was another interesting instance of the uncaught third strike. The runner was able to try for first, but it is a force out so the fact that the runner on third crossed the plate before the putout was made is irrelevant.

(I hope I'm right, Bucky)


Correct. I meant to jump in that other threat but too much hoopla elsewhere. One thing that will take you a long way in these type situations is understanding the exact definition of a "force out"- a runner forced to advance as a result of the batter becoming a runner.

Then know that no run can score on a play if the third out of an inning is the result of a force out, and you're golden.

Where this always gets people (and way too many amateur umpires) confused is on appeal plays where a runner is returning to a base after a caught fly- just look at the defintion and you know that's it's not a force out.
