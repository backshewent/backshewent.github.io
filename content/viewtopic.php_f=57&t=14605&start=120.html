by Werthless » Tue Apr 16, 2013 16:49:56

td11 wrote:
Werthless wrote:But that's a worse statistic than groundball percentage for understanding the pitchers that are good are inducing groundballs.



worse than what, gb/PA? not trying to be a dick, this is the first time i've seen someone complain about gb/fb not being a good indicator of a pitcher's groundball tendencies

Yeah, gb/PA. It was more of an aside. Think of the progression for evaluating how effective a pitcher is at striking out batters. First level, look at total K's. But we can do better, so we look at K/9. But even better, we look at K's as a percentage of PAs. A guy that K's 10/9IP is less impressive of a strikeout pitcher if he allows a ton of baserunners per inning.

Take Matt Harvey. This year, he has 0.74 GB/FB ratio, 4th worst in baseball. So if we're using gb/fo to evaluate who the groundball pitchers, he's doing poorly. However, he's middle of the pack in groundballs per plate appearance. He's dominating because he's giving up very few line drives (3 total, in 22 innings), striking a ton of guys out, and walking few. His groundball percentage would look "better" if it included the other outcomes.
