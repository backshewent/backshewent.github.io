FFS, read the post again:

JFLNYC wrote:It's not so much what Sandberg said about Jimmy, it's that anyone could reasonably believe it will makes a difference. I don't want to say it's impossible for a mid-30's hitter to change his approach significantly, but it's rare. And on the list of good candidates, Jimmy is near the bottom. Jimmy is Jimmy and he's very set in his ways.

When he had his power and speed, it was easy to overlook a so-so BA and OBP. It was easier to overlook the first-pitch pop outs. But now that his plus skills have diminished, his weaknesses are more noticeable. Low BA, low OBP, no power, average speed, average range. His only remaining plus quality is his sure-handedness.

I take nothing away from his many accomplishments. He's the greatest SS in Phillies history. But, in a meritocracy, he'd be a utility player at this point in his career. To my eyes it looks like his primary concern is to stay healthy so he can get his well-deserved Phillies records and collect his paychecks. We may yet see flashes of vintage Jimmy, but his skills have eroded and they're not coming back.


If anyone wants to believe that Jimmy's just having a bad year (irrefutable) and will bounce back (dubious), more power to you. But the facts are: His K rate is his highest in 10 years. His OBP is the second lowest of his career. His SLG, wOBA and wRC+ are all career lows. His fielding and base running WAR's are his lowest in 10 years. His overall WAR is a career low. His UZR/150 is his lowest since his rookie year. After stealing 77 bases in 91 attempts over the previous 3 years (84.6%), this year he's 15 out of 21 (71.4%). When you consider that his BABIP is .280 -- his highest since 2008 and just a bit below his career mark -- the notion that he's going to transform into a high OBP stolen base machine is laughable.

I wish the whole discussion were a dead horse, but it's more like a dead cat that keeps getting dragged out in some delusional belief that Jimmy's just having a rough year and is going to rebound to become a productive hitter again in his mid-30's. The steroid era is over. Jimmy's not suddenly going to become a better hitter as he jogs towards 40.

Facing the facts is not pretty or easy. If anyone wants to click his heels together three times and pretend Jimmy's decline is a bad dream, be my guest. I'd prefer not to kid myself.
