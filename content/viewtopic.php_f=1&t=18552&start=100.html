Re: Phillies Sign Kingery to Six Year Deal

by mcare89 » Sun Mar 25, 2018 17:15:21

From a Phillies standpoint, this is a phenomenal deal. The only way it doesn't work is if he flames out so hard that he wouldn't even be in the league in three years Singleton-style (for which there were other off-the-field issues) or suffers a catastrophic injury, and if the latter happened, the money would be the least of my concerns.

Any year where makes less than 4 million, which is the first four years, is pocket change. It's less than 2 percent of the payroll on a tax-level team, which is what I'd expect the team to be going forward. The only years that are "risky" in any way are years five and six, in which he'll make less money than the Phillies 7th inning middle reliever makes today, and I'll gladly trade that for the insanely team-friendly team options that they now have in the event that he becomes an above-average regular or better.

I'm not really worried about precedent either, because this deal is so team friendly that I'd imagine high end prospects in the future might prefer to roll the dice, but hell, if this is the new precedent, sign me up.
