by JFLNYC » Thu Jul 31, 2008 17:44:25

I just saw a report that the Dodgers agreed not to pick up Manny's option and the Manny agreed to decline arbitration. So the Dodgers will get their picks.

The Red Sox insisting on getting Bay suggests to me that the Phils had no chance unless they were willing to give up Burrell. Werth is just not a close enough substitute for Manny and it seems the Sox were (rightly, IMO) concerned about making sure their lineup didn't take too much of a hit this year. Bay's production is close enough to Manny's to make it palatable to the Sox.

The Dodgers badly need offense and they'll get the picks if they don't re-sign Manny. LaRoche's has yet to show he can hit big-league pitching, so trading him plus Morris doesn't seem to me to be an unreasonable price to pay.

The Pirates get two guys in LaRoche and Hansen who haven't yet shown they're going to make it in the majors, but either could yet turn it around. Morris has a great arm, but is very young. Moss doesn't project as anything special. Time will tell whether it works out for the Pirates.

All in all, I think all 3 teams got what they were looking for.
