I want a Trump impeachment. Obviously, it would be best for the country. But also, from a partisan perspective, it would tear the Republican Party apart. (Which is why you're going to need a lot, lot more stuff to come out before this even gets to a vote in the House--it is almost impossible to imagine a scenario where Ryan lets this happen.) Many of those Trump diehards do not identify with the Republican Party, they identify with Trump. An impeachment would alienate those voters. I really think if we get to a Trump impeachment (even if it comes on the heals of a Democratic House victory in 2018) you'll end up with a Trump led populist party and an establishment oriented one.

You might argue that the Republicans want Trump out of the picture for the long-term health of the party. But politicians have no real incentive to think about that. A vulnerable Republican House member easily distance himself from Trump without alienating those voters.
