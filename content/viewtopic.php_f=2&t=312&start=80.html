I used to work at NBC, down the hall from the "Saturday Night Live" offices, so I'd frequently find myself at the urinal next to Tim Meadows, Norm McDonald, etc. One night in 1995 or so, a middle-aged balding guy walked into our cube farm with a confused look on his face. I thought, in order: "That guy looks like my dad; that's not my dad; that's Chevy Chase."

Also at NBC, while waiting for an elevator probably that same year, the doors opened up and I saw Mike Ditka and Joe Gibbs. I stared at them for a couple seconds--then Ditka ROARED, "Come on, are you getting on or not!" I got on.

Since then, not so much with the celebs. My wife and I saw Matt Dillon at a concert in Central Park last summer.

My all-time favorite of these is that my uncle was a frat brother of Jonathan Frakes, who played Commander Riker on the second Star Trek series, at Penn State in the early '70s. In the afternoons, they'd get stoned and watch reruns of... Star Trek.
