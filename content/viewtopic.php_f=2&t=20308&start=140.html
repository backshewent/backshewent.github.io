Dunno if anyone else has ever made orange vinegar, but basically it's just soaking orange peels in white vinegar for a couple weeks. The resulting liquid smells nicer than regular vinegar and supposedly the orange oils or whatever that are extracted have useful properties for cleaning.

Anyway, I had been saving orange peels in the freezer for a while and a couple months ago I pulled them out. I also had a bunch of clementines that were past their prime so I peeled those too, put all the peels into Mason jars, covered with vinegar, sealed the jars, and put in a cupboard. On a whim I also put the aging edible part of the clementines in a separate jar and did the same.

I then forgot all about them until tonight.

The vinegar with the peels turned out as expected, only more so. A deep orange-brown color, smelling more of orange than of vinegar.

The vinegar with the orange fruit though was interesting. When I opened the Mason jar it started fizzing a lot, indicating fermentation had been happening. The smell was surprising; faint orange smell, a little tang, a little yeasty/boozy. I decided to taste it... it was a bit tart but didn't taste like much of anything otherwise. Hope I don't die
