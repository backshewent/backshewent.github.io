They have offices, benefits, and all the other perks that full time faculty have, and sometimes even more (because the department, as long as it isn't horribly dysfunctional, or an Ivy league, wants its assistants to merit tenure. Jindal was first elected to LA's governor on the basis of being an effective administrator, a pragmatic problem solver, not so much as an ideologue. His career started to go downhill when he agreed to participate in the most self-destructive of all American political rituals, the opposition party's response to the State of the Union Address.
