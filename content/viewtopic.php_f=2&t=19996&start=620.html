by Monkeyboy » Thu Mar 12, 2020 02:29:55

I've been teaching online for 6 weeks now. I like it better in terms of being at home and not dealing with as much stupid shit, but I don't like the loss of student interaction and I am just now starting to learn the tools that will make it better. We were thrown into it. It doesn't help that we're using Teams, which works horribly for something like this.

I'm also more of a project teacher. I think it presses the most buttons for the most kids and it fits my style much better. I can walk around and help students individually rather than doing a lecture. The online stuff feels more like a lecture. Even with the extra gadgets, you're basically passing information.

We're also not allowed to give homework right now, so we have to design the lessons well to get over everything and allow time for practice. We had to also cut out some things. The kids can't keep up because they do not use their time wisely when not in a class. At school, they would be forced to do something, but at home there's lots of distractions. Many of our students are really struggling with it. I suspect that will be the case most places.

by Monkeyboy » Thu Mar 12, 2020 02:42:18

On another note, I think this virus has the potential to have long term effects on education.

I know some people think this might change how we do business because companies may find that all the business trips are not necessary. They may be forced to use the new telecomputing technologies and decide that it's much cheaper and worth it once they make it through the learning curve. The initial investment will be forced by the virus, but once that investment is sunk cost, you might as well cut down business travel and save the money. It could cripple the hotel and travel industry.

I could see the same thing happening with education. MANY schools around the world will move to online learning for the next few months, possibly even going into next year. This will force many schools to learn online platforms and many students will do quite well in the format, even if most do not prefer it. The students who do well in this format will want to continue in it. I suspect we'll see a big jump in the number of online high schools around the world. The current situation will also probably drive innovation and new programs and technologies will come out to match the needs of the growing market. Yes, some companies are already working on these technologies, but I think the demand will drive more investment in the area. If I knew I was doing it from the start and could develop better units, I would definitely enjoy teaching from home.
