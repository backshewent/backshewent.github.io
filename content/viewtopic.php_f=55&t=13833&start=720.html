the phillies are at 40 on the 40 man roster. subtract two for the 60 day dl guys (contreras and herndon) who won't be back this year. so thats 38. galvis is on the restricted list, when his suspension is over, i expect he'll be placed on the 60 day dl.

however, as soon as the season ends, the 60 day dl goes away. and the team will the have to set the roster before the rule 5 draft.

here are the guys who will be on the 40 man after the season

P (19) - aumont, bastardo, de fratus, diekman, halladay, hamels, herndon, horst, kendrick, lee, lindblom, papelbon, jc ramirez, rosenberg, savery, schwimer, stutes, valdes, worley
C (3) - ruiz, kratz, valle
INF (8) - howard, utley, rollins, cesar hernandez, mini, luna, frandsen, galvis
OF (5) - brown, gillies, mayberry, nix, schierholtz

thats 35. the following guys are on the 40 man now and will be free agents

polanco, wigginton, contreras, schneider, pierre

you have a few DFA candidates, obviously. luna, frandsen, jc ramirez, mini mart

but you also need to replace polanco, wigginton and pierre on the active roster. additionally, you have to protect guys for the rule 5 draft.

if my calculations are correct, that is all prep guys 18 and under when drafted in 2008 and all college guys 19 and older drafted in 2009, as well as latam signings who were 18 and under in 2008. i think this is the list

hewitt
collier
pettibone
may
j-rod
overbeck (was eligible last year i think, wasn't picked)
cloyd (was eligible last year, wasn't taken)
leandro castro (was eligible last year, wasn't taken)
jiwan james (was eligible last year, wasn't taken)
hyatt
ruf

so they have to protect ruf anyway, which means if they don't think he can hack it, they give him a shot and he fails, they can just DFA him anyway. pettibone and may will obviously be protected, and julio rodriguez seems likely as well. we're quickly running out of 40 man spots.

This is another aspect of trading vs. hoarding prospects. You can't hang onto all of them as long as you want. Converting several minor leaguers into a smaller number of major leaguers allows the team to protect more of the remaining prospects. Obviously they would be giving up the ones that are likely to be no-brainers for protection but there are probably a few guys they would like to protect but might not be able to if they don't trade anyone.
