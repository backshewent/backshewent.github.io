NAIROBI, Kenya (AP) — Kenya's attorney general warned Thursday that the opposition's move to "inaugurate" its leader Raila Odinga next week to protest the recent presidential election would amount to treason.

Attorney General Githu Muigai's comments came a day after the United States urged Odinga's supporters to call off the mock "swearing-in" on Dec. 12, the country's independence day.

The opposition objects to President Uhuru Kenyatta's re-election after months of political turmoil that included a nullified August election and a repeat vote in October, saying that electoral reforms have not been made.

The attorney general warned that institutions created by Odinga's group such as "the People's Assembly" are unconstitutional and illegal and "may be visited by the full force of the law."

Odinga on Thursday rejected the U.S. advice to cancel next week's event, saying he doesn't recognize the legitimacy of Kenyatta as president.

Odinga also repeated his accusations against Kenya security forces, saying dozens of people have been killed in the months of election unrest and "nobody is talking about it. Yet they have the audacity to come and advise us to forget and move on."

My favorite topic that might only effect me is tolling <formerly> public roads. The latest opened this week. Route 66 from the beltway to D.C. is 9 miles. The minimum toll is $8, but on its first day the algorithm made it $40! If you take a look at a map of the area, this is the only highway into the city at this part of the state. All roads need to cross a bridge, and there are few of those. 66 is literally the only direct route from North Fairfax county, Loudoun and points west (Manassas and the masses of people who live way-way out). The road previously was HOV, but now it is pay to play.

Anyone else seeing single, bottle neck highways becoming profit engines? Part of this is simply a shifting of taxation methods so that the status quo can be maintained, and in our very special scenario Northern Virginia is not permitted to have its own taxes to support infrastructure, so other parts of the state hold the line on strangling the North while profiting from our input to the state's tax benefiting them disproportionately. I am fine with supporting Appalachia, just let us self manage.

I think we can thank California for pioneering that. Traffic flow dictates cost. There are a few in the DC area, but I have seen it in SoCal years before. There is a toll lane in the VA portion of the beltway, and then down I95 from Alexandria/Springfield to like Woodbridge or Stafford. That southern leg hit +$60 once! Its a long way, or maybe it seem really long because of the traffic.

Just playing the game, you could do a Woodbridge around the beltway and down 66 to the Pentagon route on a bad day for $100.

There is an out. If you have 3 people in the car, and the right EZPass, it is free. That is not a change for the 66 leg that just opened as it was HOV-3 at rush hour. The way you work that on the Woodbridge to Pentagon route is a feature called a slug line where a drive picks up "slugs" to meet the HOV requirement. People line up and climb in a car when the driver calls out number and location. There are some good, and some very crazy stories about how this works, and the "rules". stories
