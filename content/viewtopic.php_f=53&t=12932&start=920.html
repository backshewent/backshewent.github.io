by dajafi » Sun Feb 05, 2012 13:26:53

Just saw two things on MLBTR that were kind of interesting:

--Angels are thinking about trading Peter Bourjos to the Nationals for John Lannan
--Oswalt remains interested in coming back to the Phillies

I'd hate to see Washington get Bourjos (and to see Lannan, whom we reliably tune up a couple-three times every season, follow Chris Volstad out of the division). And we know the Phillies need to get younger, though this isn't as big a problem in the outfield as it is in the infield.

Would it be worth trying to figure out a deal with LA of A with the key pieces Worley from the Phils and Bourjos from the Angels? If Oswalt at this point would come back on a one-year deal for $5-6m, that's probably an improvement for the rotation given Worley's likely regression. Given our glut of relievers and their infield corners logjam, maybe there's even a really big deal to be struck in which we also could get a Callaspo or Trumbo.

Bourjos either pushes Victorino to left field or goes there himself, with Mayberry taking the bulk of 1b duties until Howard gets back and then serving as a three-OF/1b swingman, freeing up Wigginton to spot Utley and Polanco once or twice a week. Having Bourjos gives the Phils a viable replacement for Victorino after 2012.

Losing Worley hurts a little, but between Hyatt and the May/Rodriguez/Pettibon/Colvin group plus the veteran depth (Piniero, Misch) at triple-A, they should be okay even if Oswalt gets injured again.

Long shot, but I think there's some logic to it. I guess one problem would be that Oswalt at $5-6m would push them over the luxury tax, unless they could clear money elsewhere. Hell, maybe the Angels would take Blanton; if healthy, he has to be about as good a bet to perform as Lannan, and the Phils could pay half his tab and probably still stay under the limit. Of course given his one-year deal, the Phils would have to put more in on their end--maybe one of the good young relievers. I could live with that.
