Tell me how I'm wrong--the Eagles could keep Wentz and Foles in 2018, but after that, Foles is a free agent and gets a new contract. At that point, the most plausible scenario is Foles leaves the Eagles. The NFL is designed to keep teams from stockpiling talent, and in a world where many teams lack even a competent QB, there's no way a team gets to use one as a back up.

So, really, the thing to do is to trade Foles now--you can't really sell any higher.

Here's my prediction: Foles is worth more than Bradford. Unless they get blown away before the draft (like this year and next years first round pick plus a bunch of other stuff) they will wait until a team has their starter go down during preseason. By then, they'll know how Wentz's recovery has gone, and they will be able to demand a bunch. Could the Eagles use a pro-bowl d lineman or something like that?
