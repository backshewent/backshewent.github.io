Bill McNeal wrote:The government allows you to get one free credit report a year by visiting: Annualcreditreport.com

http://www.ftc.gov/bcp/edu/pubs/consume ... cre34.shtm

This is what I did and it took me to the TransUnion website for the credit score(which was also free)

There are multiple credit scores. The score you want is developed by FICO, and is the score that is used by most underwriters. This is the score that matters. Each of the individual credit bureaus, seeing how lucrative it would be to sell a score, have developed their own scores. These are based on their own credit file, and are usually free. They get you pretty close to your FICO score, but they may be different. The various scores are developed based on their own proprietary algorithms, so they will be different to a certain degree, even if the source information is the same.

FICO doesnt give their score away for free, as far as I'm aware.
