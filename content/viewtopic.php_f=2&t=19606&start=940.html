JUburton wrote:
jerseyhoya wrote:Dunno why the Pod Save people are working with Change Research. Their work from what I've followed is absolutely horrendous.
Guessing the price is right. 538 has them as a C+ with a 0.8pt R bias so what I'm saying is Warren has already won the presidency.

They were mostly new, and 538 hasn't updated its ratings from last cycle yet. Looking at the gov polls they had 10 in the last week and had Dems winning the governors races in Ohio, South Dakota and Iowa, when they lost all of them. The only close gov race they got 'right' was New Hampshire, but were off by 6 on the margin. On the Senate side a bit better, but they had Tester losing in the last week though I guess only off by 6 on the margin there too, and Montana is a weird state to poll.

This is how they describe the methodology on their latest national Dem primary poll (which had Warren at 26, Bernie at 23, Biden at 23): Change Research surveyed 1,450 self-identified Democratic primary voters. The margin of error as traditionally calculated is ± 3% for the full sample. Change Research reaches voters via targeted online ads that point people to an online survey instrument. The Bias Correct Engine dynamically delivers large samples that accurately reflect the demographics of a population. Post-stratification was done on age, gender, ethnicity, and 2016 presidential vote.

I could write many thousands of words about why most public polling is bad, challenges polling faces in an era of low phone response rates, and I do think online polling off robust panels can be almost as good as phone polls. And trying new shit is good and maybe these folks are going to nail things. But I'm deeply skeptical of the amount of self-selection going on here. You need to click through to participate, self-id, etc. That is going to amplify the more 'online' candidates wildly. Also anyone who says they are using a 'Bias Correct Engine' is probably selling hocus pocus shit.
