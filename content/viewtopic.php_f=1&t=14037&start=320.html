by phorever » Thu Feb 19, 2015 05:55:37

reasons it is tough for any deal to match the RHF:
a) 2 years before necessary.
b) post-prime decline already evident.
c) bottom of the defensive spectrum player so value disappears if offense isn't superior
d) major platoon split issues with a proven tendency to create lineup construction problems.
all of these are mentioned frequently in the discussions above and elsewhere, but i would add
...
e) player now consistently a sub-replacement value, in other words hurting the team on the field just by playing, which is considerable less common than just poor return on a big contract hurting the team financial standing.
f) extreme organizational denial of the significance of the aging curve consistently leads to a false assumption of a chance of a rebound, leading to...
g) expecting too much in trade making him even more untradable
h) being too willing to trade singleton and santana, setting back the rebuilding process
i) continuing to run him out there as a starter, preventing the team from properly using and assessing the next generation of 1b options (brown, ruf, franco), still further setting back rebuilding.
