by Wolfgang622 » Wed Jun 17, 2020 08:51:00

This guy is doing some good stuff. This video on Bonds is well done.



The Gagne AB story is fascinating, but I think it's slightly less impressive than he seems to think it is for two reasons:

1. Just judging by body language, but Bonds doesn't seem to me to be "spitting on" the 0-2 slider; rather, he seems totally fooled by the pitch, and just resigned that the outcome will be whatever it is. Indeed, the pitch is close, and while there is no doubt Bonds would have complained were he rung up on it, nevertheless, again, just reading body language, I think he sort of knows he's lucky he wasn't. He had no chance of hitting it because he had no chance of pulling the trigger on it and had it landed another half inch higher he was a goner. Indeed, it wouldn't have been outrageous were it called - I am not sure it isn't, in fact, a strike.

2. And then after that, by way of the gentleman's agreement and the game situation, he knows he no longer has to concern himself with it even down 1-2. Sure, Gagne is pumping in 100+ MPH fastballs, but hitters - all hitters - look bad on those at least in part because they know they must simultaneously be prepared for whatever the "other" off speed pitch is from the pitcher. Take that away and let them gear up, and even 100MPH will look less intimidating. Granted, 100MPH is still 100MPH, but the false comparison the video is making, implicitly, is suggesting that Bonds is a god and all other players are Joe Schlabotnik. Bonds was the best of the best, no doubt, but if you took the top 10 hitters of any era and stood them in there and said "All you need concern yourself with is the fastball," the chances for the hitter would improve tremendously, even against 100MPH.

That said, Bonds was still other worldly, and this video is fun.
