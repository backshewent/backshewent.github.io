by swishnicholson » Sat Jun 08, 2013 02:38:34

Youseff wrote:trying to give Bruce Springsteen a chance here, guys, but I'm kinda bored. listening to The River for the first time and meh meh meh is my opinion.


Never really liked The River, to be honest and I'm a moderate fan. Heard the title song first I guess on the No Nukes concert and thought, well, that's awful, it sounds like a Springsteen parody, but I'm sure it will shape up before it appears on an album. But it didn't. Only songs I really like are the slighter rockers and pop songs like Hungry Heart, Sherry Darling and Cadillac Ranch. The Ties that Bind is absolutely awful, and a lot of the stuff just drones on.

I'm sure there are plenty of people out there that will say it's genius. But I guess my point is it's probably not the best place to enter at if you're trying to develop a taste. I'd work through chronologically, even if you aren't that into the first two albums. By the time you get to Born to Run it all seems to fit together though. After that, honestly I think it's kind of scattershot, except I love just about all of Nebraska, but there will almost certainly be songs you love if you have any taste for him at all.. But listening to The River is almost like reading Across the River and Into the Trees to see what all the fuss was about Hemingway.
