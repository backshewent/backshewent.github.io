what is wrong about leyland's point of view (and the opposite point of view) is that it assumes all pitchers are equal. verlander may not experience the same level of fatigue at pitch 100 that a guy like worley does. part of that is genetics, part of it is training and conditioning, part of it is age/development.

i think its pretty smart to monitor workloads of young pitchers, until they reach the age of, say, 24 or 25. at that point, they aren't still physically developing, they are what they are. still, some guys are capable of pumping 130 pitches per start, others simply aren't.
