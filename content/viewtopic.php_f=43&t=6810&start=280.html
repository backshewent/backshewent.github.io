I've recently started thinking that one of the main reasons that homefield advantage exists in baseball is the batting eye-- the hitter is used to picking up baseballs in their own stadium's batting eye. For instance, Ryan Howard might be rather good at seeing balls with the brick background in centerfield and may notice the spin on the ball quicker than on the road.

If this is true, perhaps a rain delayed start where Tampa Bay cannot take batting practice on the field will make it more difficult for them to pick up pitches against the CBP batting eye, and help our chances.

Am I a flailing optimist?
