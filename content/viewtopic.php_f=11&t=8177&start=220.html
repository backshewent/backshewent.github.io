by Wolfgang622 » Wed Jul 08, 2009 13:04:01

I don't know if it's possible, but am I the only one who would do what it took to get Halladay without giving up Happ? Getting Halladay is a right-now move that becomes more lateral than it otherwise would be if you are forced to give up Happ. A rotation of:

Hamels
Halladay
Blanton
Happ
Moyer/Whoever

is better than anything else the Phils are likely to be able to put together at this point. If, to get Halladay, you need to lose Happ, and replace him with Lopez or Pedro or whatever B-lister you can find, and move Moyer up to the four position, that's significantly less good IMHO. Not that Happ is the solution to all our problems, but he's a more-than-servicable number 4 I think. Relying on Moyer in that spot... scares me, to say the least.

At any rate, getting Halladay by giving up something off the ML roster doesn't really make sense. If you're finishing off the roster to win now, then you want to make this an all-propsects deal. I'd do Taylor, Kendrick, Donald, and, if I had to, Carrasco too, before I'd offer Happ. Maybe it's just me, but I don't really believe the latter will ever amount to much, as another poster has said.
