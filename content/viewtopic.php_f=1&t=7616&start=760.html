There is definitely a dearth in young talent coming up. The last guy I even remember watching and thinking he had serious game, and was below the age of 25, is the biggest ---hole in the sport right now, Nick Kyrgios. He has the size and the shots, but his mental game is arguably the worst I've ever seen in professional sports. He'll quit any time for any reason. There are plenty of stories of basket case types turning it around, but I'm very doubtful with him. He would also be a cancer to the sport and bring nothing but negative attention, and not in the John McEnroe kind of way.

Even worse than the overall lack of young talent is the American young talent outlook. We have nothing at the top already, but the next line of up and coming kids profile more as "hopeful" top 10-15 types rather than someone who can lead the sport after the big 4 (Federer, Djokovic, Murray and Nadal) retire. Keep an eye out for Taylor Fritz as someone who could have an Andy Roddick type of career though. That kid has skills, and is breaking a lot of "youngest" records in American tennis.
