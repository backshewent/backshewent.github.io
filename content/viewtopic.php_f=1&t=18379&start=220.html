From Sports Business Journal...Other changes coming to “Sunday Night Baseball”:

More on-screen graphics
ESPN will put win-loss records and the number of games behind on the screen next to the scoreboard. This graphic will remain on-screen about half the time.

“If you ask most viewers when they flip through and turn on the TV exactly where these teams are and what their records are, they probably wouldn’t know,” Orlins said.

New statistics
ESPN will continue to use technology that calculates exit velocity and distance on home runs, though now it will put those stats across the top of the screen. This season, it is trying to develop something Orlins calls “catch probability” for balls hit into the outfield, showing how far and how fast outfielders have to run to get to a fly ball.

“We’re working hard to develop it,” Orlins said. “It’s a simple concept, but I don’t know if fans have their brains around it yet.”

Expanded K Zone 3-D
ESPN, which is owned by Walt Disney Co. (NYSE: DIS), will roll out a virtual animated replay rendering of any given pitch, showing whether it caught part of the plate or not. It used this feature three times last year, all during its Yankees-Twins Wild Card game. Orlins expects that it will be used 12 or 15 times a game this season.


“It can be really effective as a reinforcement of where a pitch was,” Orlins said.
