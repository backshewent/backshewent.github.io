There's a delay due to some surprise defense witness. You should check out the Chicago Tribune wall to wall coverage.

As far as the other theory, I don't practice criminal law and the last encounter I had with it was the bar exam, but here's my thoughts while shooting from the hip: Child molestation is presumably a lesser included offense within statutory rape. Assault is a lesser included offense within murder. Lesser included offenses merge into the greater charges, so the lesser ones don't go away altogether.

Now, if I charge you with assault, but we know that the victim is now dead, can I say that since you weren't charged with homicide, you didn't commit the assault because if you did, they would've charged you for homicide? Remember, the evidentiary standards for the different crimes are different. The standards for a molestation charge are lower than the standards for a rape charge (presumably). So you would need more evidence to prove rape than you would for molestation. By making the argument you're proposing, you're assuming that the evidence requirements would be the same for both charges.
