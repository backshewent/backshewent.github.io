I'm not really sold that the primary is the test of electability. (I don't really like primaries in the first place, at least not publicly funded, but that's neither here nor there.) A primary will show which of a field of candidates is supported by the largest plurality out of voters from the same party, when they're all running against one another, not the actual candidate they would face in the general. I fail to see how this translates to electability in the general, when nearly all media attention will be focused on two candidates, with months for skeletons to come out of closets, negative attack ads to air, and so forth. And then you have independents and soft voters for the other party who might flip for the right candidate, all of whom cannot participate in the primary.
