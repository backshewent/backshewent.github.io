Interesting tidbit in the Inky today I haven't seen elsewhere:

Madson also cited a frank conversation he had with pitching coach Rich Dubee during spring training. Dubee questioned Madson's level of preparation. He challenged the pitcher and told him that he had too good an arm to be a pedestrian reliever. Dubee told Madson, a husband and father of three young children, that he had the potential to be one of the best setup men in baseball and enjoy the financial rewards that go with it if he improved his commitment and performance.

"I told him, 'You're cheating yourself and your family,' " Dubee said in October. "He hadn't stepped up. He has a total-package arm, but he hadn't pitched to his capabilities. I felt he should know."

"It had an impact, absolutely," Madson said in October.
