I cannot believe the Phils leadership (above Amaro) is so out of touch. They are last on baseball in so many modern techniques. There is no "plan" not one that anyone can believe. Amaro doesn't even SOUND like he knows what he's talking about. There is zero credibility.

I guess if you look at the club as an investment, they are doing fine. It's worth a lot. The good years and the new park, a World Series win, etc... and they can just do this -- but I think sadly, we are reminded that no matter how the stars align for a little spell - if its the same basic people at the top with same basic viewpoints and working styles -- not much will change. Need a TOTAL house-cleaning. This all started when Bill Giles got to be in charge. He was the beginning of the ruinous post 70's glory era.

The Bucks? How many still alive?

The Phils have done one major thing the fans cannot take them to task on -- have a high payroll.

But to admit, "we're idiots" and don't know how to build or sustain a competitive club in this era of baseball. Well...not going to happen.

Most hard core fans seems ready to take on some lean years to become the A's -- let young talent emerge and know its going in a direction. But this slow death with career Phillies...

Most likely, they will buy a few more players this off-season and meld them with the remaining core and take another run at it...

They know they are out of contention. Is there ANY reason at all, not to play young players every day to find out what you have? I don't get it. There is no vision, no plan and little to provide hope....
