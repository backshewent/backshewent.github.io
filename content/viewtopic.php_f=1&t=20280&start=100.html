by PSUPhilliesPhan » Sun Jun 14, 2020 13:25:12

Jake McKenna- BA #434
McKenna pitched well in Jupiter over the fall and threw well in front of scouts in January after not being high on the radar for many evaluators. A St. John’s commit, McKenna has a huge, projectable frame at 6-foot-6, 215 pounds, but he will have to work on cleaning up his operation from the left side. Throwing from a high overhand slot, he produces forced riding action on his 12-to-6 curveball that has a chance to be above average in the future. His fastball has sat in the high 80s, but he has gotten up to 91 on it at times—it’s likely a fringe-average pitch for now. His strike-throwing ability suffers due to his robotic delivery, but with his great makeup he should be able to fix any issues with continued development at the collegiate level.

Probably the biggest fallout will be potential travel restrictions and budgets for many of these college programs. The northern colleges play in numerous tourneys in warm weather states early in the year. Money might no longer be there to do it. Is every team in the conference going to field a team? There’s going to be uncertainty there. This is going to be an issue for college athletics across the board, with some sports definitely more affected than others. Football and basketball programs are the income generators for athletic departments, what if they can’t generate that same money?

In this specific case 20k, tuition, starting a career in baseball and concentrating on it vs. a reduced season/schedule with maybe local community colleges filling it out, not too mention some classroom restrictions with regards to even studying towards your degree.

Some might think it might be best for the smoke to settle over the next couple of years with regard to both college sports and academia and just spend a couple years in a baseball camp type setting.
