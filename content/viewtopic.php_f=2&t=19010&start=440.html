First Halloween at the new house. Our old place was in the center of Lansdale which is full of closely-spaced houses, many containing families with young children, so it was Halloween Mecca. (Not so much on our street [we were around the corner from all the action], fortunately; people in the thick of it used to start buying Halloween candy in August to spread out the cost.) People used to come from other parts of town or even other towns to trick-or-treat in our neighborhood. Several blocks were closed to vehicular traffic owing to the sheer numbers.

Now we're on a non-through street off a busy, not-pedestrian-friendly road, with larger lots, so it's very different. However, there's a cul-de-sac sprouting off our street, and a loop at the end; in total there are 51 houses in the neighborhood, with quite a few kids. We probably got about 25-30 total kids at the house, and our kids got a pretty good haul.
