I think that I am beginning to understand the strategy behind Trump's talking points. He can only operate in very few modes, so any numbers of scale are bad. Big numbers are scary and he represents a lot people and gets scared and confused by these. Lots of tests = lots of positive cases because lots of numbers. Lots of votes = lots of bad results (for him) because of numbers. It is the same train on the same tracks, just a different car in his limited view.

NPR wrote: "If we all anticipate that accurate vote counts, with a higher volume by mail, or for any reason, for a pandemic or for civil unrest, take longer because it takes longer to make sure the count is accurate, then that's the opposite of a disaster.


That is correct, more votes will take longer to count and more votes is representation of the population so that is a positive, but through the word blender it is something that is ominous and confusing when married with his standard fare rhetoric it becomes a threat.

His take is :

Trump wrote:"Must know Election results on the night of the Election, not days, months, or even years later!"


That is likely untrue at the extremes (it likely is a week or two), and sounds like an assault on the country. Factually the first United States presidential election was held from Monday, December 15, 1788 to Saturday, January 10, 1789, so we've been here before.
