as I was trying to organize my cash at home I realized I was totally incapable of figuring out what my plan was. so I ditched that idea and settled for commuting to my parents' new suburban house while tripping balls. It took me 90 minutes to get out of my apartment and another 90 to travel the mile or so walk to the train station.


One of the times when I was on mushrooms, I was inside listening to music and then had the urge to go outside into nature. When I got to the front door, I stood there for what seemed like an eternity staring at it trying to determine which of the two locks I had to unlock first to open the door. I was really sweating it and trying to figure it out before realizing that it didn’t matter which lock I unlocked first to open the door as long as both of them were unlocked. Like a lightning bolt, this insight lead to the epiphany that there are many paths to take in life that will lead to the same place. And as long as your intentions are true and you focus on your goal, you can get there no matter which of those many paths you take.

After conquering the challenge of opening the door, I went outside. Sat out by the pond watching light hit water, and feeling that the energy of all living things is one. Then there were some people out and about, and I got really paranoid that they knew I was tripping so I scurried back to my apartment. Except then I stood outside my apartment paranoid not knowing if it was really my apartment or not, and I didn't want to walk into someone else's place and get beat up or arrested. So I stood there for probably 20 minutes knocking on my own door to make sure nobody was home before opening it and being relieved to find that it was, indeed, my place.

But I still really wanted to see nature, so I decided to put on my Planet Earth DVDs. If figuring out how to open the door was a challenge, trying to operate the DVD player was like trying to understand quantum mechanics. I messed with for a long time before realizing I didn’t even own Planet Earth DVDs and never did. At that point I realized that doing anything other than lying on my bed listening to music was going to be damn near impossible Herculean task, so I just gave in and rode the wave. Many nice insights came after that.
