by Wolfgang622 » Wed Jan 19, 2011 10:27:10

TheDude24 wrote:Is Lenny still in line for the Phillies Wall of Fame?


Even if none of this had happened, I don't think he'd be a great candidate. Lenny essentilly played two complete seasons with the Phillies: 1990 and 1993 (and 1994, I suppose, if you count that strike-shortened season which he basically played all of). They were excellent seasons, but also Jim Eisenreich's career here was not that far off of Lenny's when you consider that he played 150 or less games, but managed to complete the seasons he played here. Lenny has a big edge in seasons played here over Eisenreich and ultimately in PAs, but the plain fact of the matter is that Dykstra was hurt too much. His 1991, 1992, 1995, and 1996 seasons were essentially destroyed by injuries.

Lenny Dykstra's career OPS+ as a Phillie: ~124 (doesn't count the last half of 1989). Eisenreich: 120.
