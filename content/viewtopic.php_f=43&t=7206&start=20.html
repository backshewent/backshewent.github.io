by smitty » Thu Nov 20, 2008 15:20:26

It's pretty clear that Utley's 2nd half power shortage (compared to his first half) was due to the hip injury. That Utley actually played with this at a very high level is amazing.

Utley is obviously a tough guy, which is good. He might very well be recovered by the beginning of the season. And if this thing is something that is truly fixed by surgery, he may have a monster year. He played so well with a bad hip, he might very well revert to his pre-injury master blaster form. (we might call this the Lidge effect). That would be cool.

Ideally, this opens the door for Donald and he does well. Then, when Utley comes back, you have a great 2Bman and perhaps a "super utility"/3Bman type who really strngthens the lineup and bench.

Of course, the worst case is that this injury isn't really fixable and Utley isn't the same again and Donald falls on his face. That would be a bad thing of course.

Brunt kinda proved last year he's not really suited for every day use. He hits lefties OK at times but he doesn't have enough bat or glove to be all that valuable as an everyday guy. I really hope they don't think Nick Punto is any kind of solution.
