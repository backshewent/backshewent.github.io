i feel like i should write an essay about chain restaurants. but this will suffice. chains have their place.

if you're out of town on business in a place you've never been, you're tired and you don't feel like taking a complete stab in the dark for a meal, you can go to olive garden or ruby tuesday or whatever. you know going in that the food will probably be bland unless you really doctor it up. but you can probably assure yourself you a.) won't get deathly ill and b.) won't have a truly awful meal, especially one that might cost you a decent chunk of change.

no one would ever confuse the olive garden with a fine, authentic italian joint. if you're home and you know your area, it wouldnt make much sense to choose olive garden over a local place.

but really at the end of the day, its a random meal. you eat, on average, 2.5 meals per day. Times 7 days a week = is between 17 and 18 meals a week. That's almost 80 meals a month. And almost 960 meals a year. Times 60 years is over 57,000 meals in your lifetime. For some, many more. Not every meal has to be at a trendy, cool location. not every sandwich has to come from joe's local deli. its food. some meals will be better than others. thats life.
