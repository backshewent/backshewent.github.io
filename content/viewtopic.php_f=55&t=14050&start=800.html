smitty wrote:
Howard is an RBI man. And he should get credit for that.


for fangraphs fans, this boils down to deciding whether to put a greater emphasis on WAR or on WPA.
howard sucks in WAR wins relative to his pay (paying for 5 wins a year, getting 1.5) , but in the WPA situationally leveraged wins he close to earning his pay when healthy, thanks to hitting better than most in the highly leveraged men-in-scoring position situations.
iÂ´ve been known to go with WPA for relievers and leadoff hitters. the former are brought in specifically to deal with highly leveraged situations, and the latter are supposed to pecialize adapting their on-base and baserunning performance to match the situation. in either case, performance in leveraged situations is supposed to be a matter of skill rather than luck. speed doesnÂ´t slump, so it seemed plausible to me that fireballing relievers and speedy leadoff guys actually can be consistent clutch performers.

i have been unwilling to believe that this applied to rbi specialists, because lots of people have tried and failed to find evidence of consistent clutch-hitting abilities. however, i am coming around to the idea that howard may be a special case. the extreme shift works consistently well, and is a big part of the decline of his overall hitting and WAR value. by extension, when having men on base takes away part of the shift it is not surprising that the hitting effectiveness consistently goes back up, making howard, effectively, that rarest of creatures: the true clutch hitter.
