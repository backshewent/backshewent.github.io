by phorever » Sun Dec 02, 2012 07:18:00

let´s review.

there was a glut of outfield on the market this offseason, but...
victorino, pagan, bourn, swisher, and hamilton will all be in their aging/decline periods.
span and fowler have home/road issues.
hamilton and bourn are seeking mega-contracts.
upton maybe has an attitude problem and worryingly declining plate discipline.
young has injury issues.
pagan and fowler and bourn are coming off of anomalously good seasons and will be overly expensive.
hamilton and bourn are lefty hitters. swisher has no power versus lefties.
hamilton and swisher play center. and a bunch of issues i´m sure to be missing.

oh, and by my best estimates, the phillies need to add about 6 wins of talent to become a leading contender again, even if halladay and utley and worley are healthy again, 3b isn´t going to help unless youkilis finds the greek god of health, so a defense-only cf could leave the offense dangerously weak, so we almost have to add a legit cf with a decent bat since there aren´t any 6-win corner outfielders on the market. (no, hamilton isn´t that good)

i think it is going too far to declare a pagan signing a disaster before we know the salary number.
at the right price, he could add flexibility rather than hurt it. if his salary leaves cash over to get youkilis and/or swisher and/or uehara, and.or a real starter to replace kendrick, it could be part of a good plan.
those pieces together can add up to my 6 wins and so what if pagan transitions to a platoon/backup role towards the end of his contract. that can be a piece that helps the phillies put together cheap and effective outfields... as long as he doesn´t get paid too much.

if he gets $7 million aav or under , that´s fine with me. if he gets more that about $11million, i´ll be pretty worried (i needed to adjust my earlier estimates by regression to league me, though that is partly cancelled by salary inflation), between those numbers is kinda gray for me.

in and above the gray zone, crisp and fowler look like better alternatives. the latter works for me if and only if we replace the traded pitching with smart choices on the free agent starter market. then we can deal with the gamble taken on fowler´s youth and upside.
